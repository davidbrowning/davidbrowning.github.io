                I got up and made egg omelets for breakfast.  I then removed the door lock of the door, where I store the lawn mower.  For some reason the Lock will not unlock.  I mowed the lawn and then searched for the sample of the siding on our trailer that I had taken off.  I wanted it so that I might find out how to paint the siding.  I was unable to locate the sample.  I took the lock back to Home Depot to have is repaired.  It took a little while, but they were able to repair the lock.  I next went to Wallmart where I bought some sweet potatoes, a watch for Mae and some cooked chicken for me as it was already after noon.  I next went shopping at some Paint Stores to price paint and to see if they could tell me how to re-paint our house and keep the same pattern as we presently have.  None of the ones I went to could tell me how to do it.  Irven and Sandy came and visited for a little while but mainly to pick up the Coconut Oil we had bought for them.  Mae and I went down to Brother and Sister Terkelson’s to visit and become acquainted.  It was an enjoyable evening and we were surprised to find out that they know June Moulton and others from the Mound Fort Ward. 

 

Saturday, August 27, 2016

 

                I woke with a dream reliving some of my employment experiences.  It almost seemed like a slow motion movie mainly consisting of why I resigned and changed employment.  When I woke, I was in a very depressed state and that feeling remained with me most of the day.  Also when I woke, I was depressed as to how my family has left activity in the Church.  I don’t know if the two are related or not.  Between my employment and my family, I am very depressed.  After Mae got our breakfast, I went outside and worked on the doors and riveted several places on the aluminum siding.  I got a call from Brother Okey asking if I could get together with Brother Fife and the two of us administer to Sister Diane Millspaw.  We did that after lunch, and then I took a thumb drive containing my Five Year Diary over to the U.P.S. store in Clinton and had them print them off.  When they finished I went to pay them, but I had left my wallet home and had to drive back home and back again to pay for the printing.  I stopped at Wall Mart and purchased a binder and some index divider sheets to put my Diary in.  I looked at some paint supplies at Home Depot before coming home.  I finished some work on the house and put my tools away.  Mae came out and helped me.  We ate dinner while watching the show, “The Great Escape” on TV.  I put my Diary in the binder.  Maybe I should work on transcribing my hand written Journals from 1975 through 2002.  That would be 27 years of journals to transcribe.  I am not sure if that time would be of value or not.  I have found where I have written on the computer from 2003 to 2010 but I can’t find where I have printed those years off on a hard copy.  I will have to check on that.  We are now going to cut Mae’s hair, take showers and get to bed.

 

Sunday, August 28, 2016

 

                I woke after 7:30 and Mae was not in bed.  I found her sleeping in the front room.  I let her sleep for a long as she could.  We just had cold cereal for breakfast and toast.  We watched the Tabernacle Choir Broadcast and then I watched another show on channel 21 dealing with finding out more about the earlier generations of our family.  I fell asleep before it finished, and Mae let me sleep for a while.  She woke me so I could take some water over to Jackie, across the street.  Of course I have to visit with her for a few minutes.  Meetings were very good and it is a special privilege to partake of the Sacrament.   The Sunday School class lesson was centered around Captain Moroni.  The subject started to turn to politics so I just kept my mouth shut.  After coming home and having something to eat, we went visiting first to Sister Leah Neilson (who had surgery on her teeth), then to Al and Blanch Ervin (Sister Ervin fell and broke her arm), then we stopped to visit Fred and Edith Amundson (they weren’t home), then to Wendell Siglin (he wasn’t home), and finally to Harold and Evie Hilton (neither of them are feeling well.)  We did go by Marianne Ray’s, but she wasn’t home.  After that we drove over to visit with Scott, Mishelle and their family.  Scott and Kylan were down to the air strip where Kylan was flying his Drone, so we went there to watch him fly it.  He sure enjoys his R.C. aircrafts.  We came back and visited with them and they showed us the pictures from their trip to Yellowstone Park.  It was late when we got home so we soon went to bed.

 

Monday, August 29, 2016

 

                Mae woke early so that she could make it to her dentist appointment.  They cleaned her teeth and repaired her denture.  She woke me at 7AM.  I read from my scriptures and then put the new mattress cover on our bed.  Mae and I had tried to put the cover on, but had problems, plus Mae had to leave for her appointment.  It took me a while but I was able to get the new cover onto the mattress and made the bed.  I figured out the materials that I need to build the carts that I want to put storage items on and put them under the house.  I filled my pickup with gas, got the pickup washed, bought steel at Bowman and Kemp’s and bought some bolts, nuts and lock washers at Nut and Bolt Supply.   Mae got home about 12:30pm and we had some lunch, after which I took a short nap.  Mae was busy with house work so I went shopping for tools and supplies that I needed.  This evening Bishop Jim and Sister Ravee Mullins stopped by and asked me to give them a Priesthood Blessing before they go back to Pennsylvania to visit Jims family for a couple of weeks. We then went to Wall-Mart for some rubbing alcohol and hornet spray.  Before going to bed we watched a western show with Jimmy Stewart staring in it.  I then filled out this entry and we went to bed about 12:30am.

 

Tuesday, August 30, 2016

 

                I woke up about 7am, but Mae didn’t get up until after 9am as she hadn’t slept well.  I made the bed and got cold cereal and toast ready for breakfast.  After eating, we went down to Gifford’s and picked black berries, a few tomatoes and carrots.  I then sprayed the bushes for insects that are causing damage to them.  I got tools ready and set up to cut the metal for the cart that will roll under the house.  The new tools, ear muffs and face shield, that I bought yesterday, was a great help to me.  I was tired when I quit and came in at 5PM.  After dinner, I watched some TV while Mae worked on the computer.  We played three rounds of Rummy Cube and Mae won two rounds.  I entered this entry before we went to bed.

 

Wednesday, August 31, 2016

 

                I woke early but then went into the front room and slept for another hour.  Mae made a nice breakfast of pan cakes and eggs.  After breakfast, I made a trip to Home Depot for some cinder blocks.  I used them as stands to lay the angle iron on to construct the fame for my storage carts.  When I got the welder out, I found that it did not have the male plug to hook into the 220 amp dryer outlet.  I went back to Home Depot, but they didn’t have just a plug, so I went to Whitehead Electric where I got what I wanted at a much cheaper price.  While I was gone, Mae inventoried the food storage under the bed in the 2nd bedroom.  That was really quite a job to do.  When I got back, Mae went shopping for food storage supplies.  I got the first cart finished, but need to make some modification to lower the cart so it will accommodate the standard size plastic totes.  Other than that, I think the carts will work great.  I do need to get a 40 amp breaker as the 30 amp breaker trips with the welder load.  I also need to find out where Bryce buys his welding rod as I don’t have enough to build two more carts, which I want to do.  After putting away my tools, I came in for dinner which we ate as we watched a show on TV.
