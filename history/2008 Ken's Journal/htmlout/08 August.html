Thursday, August 28, 2008

 

                I woke at 4AM still with concerns for each of our children and then to add to that I have one student on the bus that I am afraid may be a problem.  How to handle it?  On our rout this morning I took the time at the pickup of Matthew Williams to get of the bus and visit with Matt.  I ask him; “Where does a 2,000 lb gorilla sleep?”  When he didn’t know I replied; “Any where he wants.”  I then explained that my job as a bus driver is to get the children to and from school safely.  I explained the size of a school bus and the distance it takes to stop that bus at 30 MPH.  Yesterday because he and Brandon was horse playing and I look in the mirrors to see what was going on at the same time the traffic light changed yellow, I had to apply the brakes hard to stop.  Then a second time I had to call into the shop and while talking there, Pat my assistant said something to me and between the two things at the same time, I stopped just a second short of making a turn.  This made the turn much more difficult to complete the turn.  I was trying to empress on him the need for the students to remain reassembly orderly so that as the bus driver I could give my attention to handling the bus.  Well that seemed to help going to school but this after noon as we came into the bus loading lot we could see Matt hitting Brandon with his book bag.  Pat called Matt down about doing that.  That seemed to set it off and Matt became quite mouthy to everything Pat would say.  Pat finally asks me to pull the bus over, which I did as soon as I could do so safely.  We had to set there several minutes waiting for Matt to calm down.  At one point, Matt slammed his fist into the window.  When things settled down somewhat, we continued on.  When we got to the stop for Matt, Pat got of the bus and talked to Matt’s Mother.  His mother recommended that we write Matt up and turn it into the Principle.  Pat said that she would write it up this evening and I could read and sign it in the morning. 

                Mae was gone shopping, to a chiropractors appointment and to have her hair done, so she was gone all afternoon and evening.  I spent a lot of time trying to get maps and directions for my next weeks bus runs. 

 

Friday, August 29, 2008

 

                My assistant Pam, didn’t get the incident report filled out and this morning Matt was very humble so we will let it waite with just a verbal warning.  The run went well as did the afternoon run.  I did take a nap when I got home.  I helped Mae put up tomato juice before my afternoon run.  Mae was about ready when I got home and we drove to Mike and Becky’s so that we can watch David play football this evening.  Because we are over 65 years old we were able to get in the game free.  David did get to play quite often but there are only three players that are not seniors.  It was good to see him play but the team is not the best or at least didn’t play well tonight.  I felt that the quarterback had very bad passing ability with the exception of the one pass into the end zone and because of the ability of the receiver American Fork made a touch down.  When we got back to Mike’s home, Mike, Becky, Mae and I played a game of Rook.  Mike and I won that game.

 

Saturday, August 30, 2008

 

                We got up about 6 AM and came on home.  Mike got up early as he had to help his Father-In-Law move a table.  Jeff and Gail brought their children over and Mae tended them while Jeff and Gail did something together.  Jeff has been working long hours for several days now so it gave them an out.  Rich and Lois came and stayed with us today.  We sure enjoyed being with them.  This afternoon we attended Sherry’s wedding.  It was nice and we just hope they will be able to have a good marriage.  When we came back home Rich, Lois, Mae and I played more Mormon Bridge.  We also played a game of Knock.

 

Sunday, August 31, 2008

 

                I woke about 7AM and got up.  I went into the sunroom to read my scriptures and noticed that the yard was a mess of broken branches and leaves.  The wind must have blown quite hard last night.  I then noticed that the rabbit pens were blown over.  I had to get Mark and Bob to give me a hand to tip them up again.  The rabbits were ok.  Mae and I stayed home from church.  We cooked fish and eggs for breakfast.  Bob prepared dinner for all of us at noon.  Rich, Lois, Mae and I went to church at Devin and Betsy’s ward as their son Josh spoke prior to his mission call and their daughter Deviny spoke at her return from Twang Mission.  Both did very well but you can sure see the difference in a pre-missionary and a returning missionary.  We came home and got a dinner ready for shortly after we got home Scott’s family came over and a little later Jeff family came.  We had them for dinner and sure enjoyed being together with them.  Scott went out with me and we tied the rabbit pens to the fence to stop them from being blown over again.  It rained the rest of the evening.  After eating we played a game of Mormon Bridge before all our family went to their homes.  I loaned Jeff our projector as he and Scott wanted to use it tonight.  Mae and I watched a DVD on our TV in our bedroom before going to bed.
