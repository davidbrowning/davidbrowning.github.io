They will be going to The United Kingdom to serve.  The first thing on the agenda was to have our pictures taken.  I realized that I should have been in a dark suit and I felt improperly dressed the rest of the day.  We each introduced our self.  Kevin Pasker of Risk Management gave a presentation of farm safety.  Elder and Sister Bretzing presented a video on Serving on a FRI Mission.   At dinner time we were taken to the Joseph Smith Memorial building to the Garden Restaurant for a very good dinner.  This afternoon we continued with more training and wound up at 3:30PM.  I went to call Bishop Steadman but found that we had left both of our cell phones home.  I tried to reach Bishop Steadman but only got his answering service.  At 4:30PM we made contact with him and he soon came.  During the day I felt that I should have worn a dark suit.  Because my dark suit and to small I wore my light one.  While waiting for Bishop Steadman we thought about going across the street to Mr. Mack’s but realized that I wouldn’t have time for any alterations or the hemming of the pants.  When we got home we were also concerned about the renewal of our Temple Recommends.  I called Bishop Smith but he could not interview us this evening.  He had me call Brother Mark McPhie his 2nd Counselor, and he said that he could meet us at the office at 7PM.  We had time to go to Stake Executive Secretary and get a key to the red chapel.  I talked to him about getting a member of the Stake Presidency to sign our recommends and he said that President Mitchell would be at our chapel so we could get him to interview us.  It all worked out great.  When we mentioned that I needed a bark suit to Bob, he said he had one.  Most of the suits that Bob has had doesn’t fit me but this one was just perfect.  It looks like all our problems just worked out and we got everything done that we needed.  We went to bed late with Mae not feeling very well.

 

Friday, February 26, 2010

 

                We got up early to go to the Jordan River Temple to be with Bob and Cheryl Bottom as they go through a session for Bob to receive his endowments.  When we woke, I felt very rough, so I went back to sleep.  We later rode with Bob and Carroll to the Temple and were able to attend their sealing.  This afternoon Bob and I went over to Roy and picked up the rotor tiller that we purchased the other day.  It looks like it will be a pretty good one.  Mae got her hair washed and put up this afternoon and then went to Scott and Michelle’s.  She tended their children while Scott and Michelle went out to eat.  It was 9:30PM before Mae got home.  Bryce and Deanna came over and the two of us loaded most of our luggage into the two cars.  Bob cooked liver and onions for he, Carroll and I this evening.  After eating I went over to the Winegars to deposit our travel check and looked for some canned sliced apples but they didn’t have any.  Mae got her something to eat before we went to bed.

 

Saturday, February 27, 2010

 

                Bob fixed breakfast for us of bisques and gravy.  He also made us an apple pie, using our sweetener, and he made gravy for our family party.  I cooked chicken breast on the grill and put them in an electric oven.  I also finished packing the car.  We went up to the red chapel where all our family (except Shellie’s family and Logan) came and we had a Family birthday Party.  We did this so that we can give all the Birthday presents now instead of mailing them.  After a very great dinner, we opened gifts and then set up volleyball net and several enjoyed a game.  That wound up at 5PM.  Mae and I went to the Hail Playhouse and enjoyed Fiddler on the Roof.  It was 11:20PM before we got to Mike’s.  Becky was still up waiting for Taylor to get home from a Stake dance.

 

Sunday, February 28, 2010

 

                We got up at 6:30AM and got ready for church.  Mae and I were so tired that we only stayed for Sacrament meeting and then came home and took a nap.  When I got up Mike and I went over to Costco and purchased some appt cards for our IPod and a membership card for Mae and I.  Mike and I spent most of the day putting applications into the IPod for Mae and I.
