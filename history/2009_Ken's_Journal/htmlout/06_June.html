Tuesday, June 30, 2009

 

                We got up at 6am and took showers before going to Nauvoo.  We did return the DVD and get some salt, pepper and dish soap while we were at the store.  When we got to Nauvoo we went to the visitor center.  At the visitor center we got tickets for performance this evening at the Cultural Hall “Rendezvous in Old Nauvoo”.  We next went on a wagon ride around Old Nauvoo at which time homes, shops and special areas were pointed out to us.  The lot of Porter Rockwell was pointed out but there was not a home there. (In one of the books, we have read, on Porter Rockwell, it indicated that when the Saints were driven out of Nauvoo, Porter set his house on fire and burned it down so that no one else could have the privilege of living in his home.)  The Sister that narrated the area as we road around explained that Porter Rockwell was a childhood friend of Joseph.  Porter was one of the first to be baptized when the church was organized on April 6, 1930.  Porter became a fearless defender and body guard of Joseph.  It was said that Porter never missed his target whether he used a hand gun or a long gun.  In fact one of the evidences used in his defense when charged with the attempt to assassin Governor Boggs, was that the Governor was still alive.  Although Porter was not found guilty of attempting to kill the Governor, they kept him in prison trying to find other charges that could be brought against him.  He escaped once but was recaptured and brought back to prison.  This time they shackled his hands and feet in a very uncomfortable manner and the only way he could eat the little and pour quality food was like a dog lapping it from the pan.  Porter again escaped after several months, and they think he was allowed to escape because they could not find charges to hold him on.  He returned to Nauvoo where there was a Party that was being hosted by the Prophet.  When Porter tried to enter the building the guards wouldn’t allow him entrance because they could not recognize the dirty and unkempt man in such rags.  Joseph came to see what the problem was and when he saw Porter he looked into his still blue eyes and recognized him.  He embraced his dear friend, bringing him in and saw that he was cared for.  After Joseph and Hyrum were murdered, Porter became the body guard to Brigham Young. 

                Also we were told that Samuel Smith rode to Carthage to render help to his two brothers but encountered mob members that chased him for seven hours before he could elude them.  By the time he arrived at Carthage it was to late, Joseph and Hyrum we dead.  He cleaned up the bodies and returned them to Nauvoo and reported to his mother Lucy, at which he collapsed into his mothers arms.  Twenty three days later he died from injuries received during the chase.

                We were taken to a beautiful area by the Mississippi river named North Hill.  Joseph loved to come to this spot where he welcomed new arrivals, sent off missionaries and just be alone to meditate.  We were also told that you can see the shallow areas of the river because the lilies only grow in water up to 18 to 24 inches in depth.  There were about 30 outlying communities of Saints, West of the Mississippi River until, due to mob action; they had to come to Nauvoo for safety. 

                At one point in the wagon ride, it was explained to us, that at the time of the dedication of the Restored Nauvoo Temple, the Tabernacle Chorus put on two concerts at which they charged for emitting.  Normally there is no charge for these performances but for these two and admission was charged.  At the end of the second performance a check, for the total ticket sale, of $75,000 was written to the Mayor of Quincy.  President Hinckley, when presenting the check to the Mayor, said that when the Saints were driven from Independence Missouri in the depth of winter, with no place to go, it was the good people of Quincy that took them in.  For several months’ homes, food and jobs was shared with over 1200 suffering Saints.   He further explained; that the Church had never had an opportunity to thank those early citations of Quincy for their help and kindness.  We there for presented this check as a token of gratitude and sincere thanks, for you to use as you feel would be most appropriate for the community.

                When we came to Parley Street it was explained to us, that this street was referred to as “The Trail of Tears” and later changed to “The Trail of Hope”.  Also when we passed David Moore’s home on 20 acres of farm land, we were told that at the time the Saints were being driven out of their homes, David was offered $15.00 for nice brick home, farm, fruit trees and a brick shop.  With such an insult, David just walked out of his home, locked the door and never looked back.  I asked a question, at the Land and Records Office when we were there, as to who the deed for these property’s belong to being that the Saints were never paid for them.  It was explained that the Saints did not pay the property taxes after leaving and so the deeds were legally sold for those taxes.

                After the wagon ride ended we went back to the Visitor Center and saw the displays of sculptures by Amanda Johnson.  We were told that Amanda Johnson was an operetta singer that lost her voice because of a disease.  While recovering she took up sculpturing on her kitchen table.  Her works are remarkable with such talent.  We also went through a garden of sculptures called “A tribute to women”, mainly sculptured by Dennis Smith.  They too were beautifully done with great talent.  We went down town and got something to eat for lunch.  I then took a nap in the car while Mae walked around.  We went to the Nauvoo Temple and did a session.  Mae and I were asked to be the session witnesses.  That was an exciting and enjoyable experience.  I met a brother that worked at the Temple and visited with him for a few minutes.  Mae came looking for me and while doing so she got to see the Baptistery.  We asked and she was able to take me to see it also.  We also saw the saber that was owned by Joseph Smith and other artifacts related to the original Nauvoo Temple. One of the beautiful areas of the Temple construction was the spiral stair cases.  They are very similar to the ones in the Manti Temple.   It was a great experience.  The Temple wasn’t very busy today and that is one of the reasons that we got the extra privileges today.  Outside the Temple we took several pictures.  We got some dinner and then went back to the Cultural Hall for the evening show.  It was 10:30PM when we got back to our motel.  I entered this entry before we went to bed.  It’s been a wonderful day.
