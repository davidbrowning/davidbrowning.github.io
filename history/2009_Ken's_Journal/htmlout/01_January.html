                I slept most of the night in the recliner but did sleep ok.  It was after 8AM when we got up and probably would not have got up that early except Bob prepared fish for breakfast for us.  Mae spent most of the day cleaning and straightening up the storage room.  I kept busy but I’m not to sure just how much I accomplished.  There were many things I wanted to do but only got part of it done.  At 4PM I took a field trip, taking Davis High schools Girls Basketball Team to Viewmont High school.  I brought the bus back home with me and then at 6PM Mae went with me and we went to the Chuck-A-Rama in Bountiful.  After eating we went back to Viewmont where we watched the last ½ of the game.  Davis’s girls won the game.  It was very foggy this evening so it made it harder to drive but all went well and we got home about 10PM.  Mae is sure a special sweetheart and I sure enjoy being with her.  After filing out my journal entry I played a game on the computer before we went to bed.

 

Saturday, January 24, 2009

 

                We slept in until after 8AM and after breakfast we went over to Jeff and Gail’s house.  Gail met us there and I removed a CD and radio from the cabinets for Gail.  I then patched the mounting holes.  We next took the items, which have been in the back of my pickup, over to the storage shed.  After coming home Mae went over to Scott’s to help with the children.  I spent most of the day capturing 35 mm slides.  I watched a western show on Hall Mark this evening.  During the commercials I shaved my beard.  My face is healing quite well and it sure feels good to get rid of my scrubby beard.  When Mae came home we viewed the pictures that I had captured before going to bed.

 

Sunday, January 25, 2009

 

                We slept in until 8AM and the love that I feel with Mae is the most beautiful that I can possibly imagine.  I often wonder what I did to receive such a beautiful and wonderful wife and sweetheart.  We did turn the Tabernacle broadcast on as we prepared and ate breakfast.  The time went fast and we had to hurry to get to church on time.  The Sacrament meeting was very spiritual as we had the Special Needs Mutual present the program.  When we got home we had a turkey burger for lunch and then went up to David and Shauna’s for the afternoon and evening and for dinner.  We really enjoyed the time there.  I called Rich and Lois this evening and checked to see how they are doing.  Lois is having quite a lot of hip pain and discomfort.  I filled in my journal and also got on the internet and put my trip bids in for the first week of February.  It was almost 11PM before we got to bed.

 

Monday, January 26, 2009

 

                I went into the D.I. in Ogden this morning and worked as a volunteer from 8AM to 12 noon.  Mae tended Conner today for David and Shauna.  After getting home and eating lunch I took a nap.  Bob also called me and had me help him get his car from the repair shop.  Roberta called me and has a route for me to cover the rest of the week.  I will have to miss Thursday afternoon as we got word that Matt died yesterday evening.  They will have a funeral on Thursday for him.  I digitized quite a few more pictures this afternoon but the projector lamp burned out and I will have to locate another.  I have called all of our children, except Jeff, about Matt and they are each going to contributing money to be sent to Christine and the children.  We need to get to bed early this evening as I will be up early to take the route tomorrow.

 

Tuesday, January 27, 2009

 

                Mae woke me and said that I had over slept and she thought it was now 7 or 8 AM.  I flew out of bed and as quick as I could got my eyes to focus on the time and realized that it was only 2AM.  That woke us and neither of us got much sleep after that.  My bus run went well this morning.  On the way home I stopped and bought some rabbit pellets as I was completely out of them.  I don’t know if we are going to break even with this project or not.  We have got to decide if we should try to raise chickens or not.  I also took the measurements on the tiller motor.  Mark is trying to bid for a replacement motor for the tiller.  I see a lot of money going out for these projects.  The question is, are we being wise in these projects?  Mae went into the D.I. in Ogden and worked this morning.  From there she went over to Scott and Mishelle’s and helped there until almost 9PM.  After my last run I got home just before 5PM.  I got something to eat and then went to the Family History Center.  Mae got home just before I did.  I went out and took care of the rabbits.  We are late getting to bed again.

 

Wednesday, January 28, 2009

 

                I got up at 4:45AM to make my bus run.  All went well and I got back home about 8:30AM.  I fed my rabbits before breakfast.  After breakfast I went to sleep for over an hour.  Mae is tending Benji and Rachael for Gail today.  This afternoon’s run also went well and I was home by 4:30PM.  Bob had me go with him.  He bought a new used car.  It is a 2003 Mercury Grand Marquee and has only 40,000 miles on it.  It sure looks good.  After dinner this evening I again took care of my rabbits, did a little reading for Sundays Priesthood lesson and watched a show on Comcast.  Mae went Visiting Teaching to one of her Sisters but the other one wasn’t feeling well.  After the show I entered this journal entry before going to bed.

 

Thursday, January 29, 2009

 

                I got up early for a bus run this morning.  I got home by 8:30AM.  Mae fixed us some breakfast and then I changed clothes.  Mark had fed the rabbits for me this morning.  Bob, Carroll, Mark, Mae and I left for Providence shortly after 9AM.  we all rode in Bob and Carroll’s new car.  We arrived in time for the viewing before the funeral.  Christine had done the arrangements for all the music used in the program.  The talks were very appropriate.  David, Shauna and Conner were also able to come.  Many of Rich and Lois’s family came.  They always go out of their way to support family functions.  The internment was at the Eden Mountain Cemetery so it took a little traveling to get to there.  It did give us a chance to see the lot that we came so close to buying when we left Ogden.  I still can not help wondering what changes it may have caused had we been able to buy that property and build a home there.  It was 4PM when we got home.  We ate some dinner, I fed the rabbits and then we went over to Scott and Mishelle’s.  Today is Scott’s Birthday so we tended the children while Scott and Mishelle went out to eat.  Mae was the one that tended the children while I took a nap.  We stopped and bought some fried chicken to eat on the way home.  Although it was late I made this entry before going to bed.

 

Friday, January 30, 2009
