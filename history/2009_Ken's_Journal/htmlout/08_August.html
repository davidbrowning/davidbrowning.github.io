Saturday, August 01, 2009

 

                I got up before 7AM and read my scriptures and had personal prayer.  I sure enjoy starting the day in this way.  I am so greatful for the guiding influence that the Gospel has on my life.  With it I find purpose to life; many answers to the questions of life; guidance and direction for life; but especially hope for life after this life.  I have often thought that these reasons in and of them self would be a great reason to belong to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints; but then to add the personal testimony of the restoration of the Gospel with all its Authority and Priesthood Ordinances, that I have received; gives me great strength and happiness.

                Mae prepared breakfast while I printed off four months worth of my journals.  I then took care of the animals.  As soon as I finished that I went up and got the tire for the trailer.  I packed the bearings on the camp trailer and had just finished that when Mae called me for lunch.  After lunch I packed the wheel bearings on the boat trailer.  The right bearing was ok but the left had water mixed with the grease when I removed the bearings.  The inside seal was bad and the bearings looked stained.  I bought a new set of bearings and seal for the left wheel. 

                Mae has been busy all day with house cleaning and packing for our trip.  She went to town for some items.  After the close call of loosing the key to the boat when we were up to East Canyon with David’s family I had Mae make a duplicate key while she was at the store.  Mae came back from shopping about the time I finished with the bearings.  She came out and helped me vacuum and wash the boat.  It was very dirty inside and out.  We got that done and ate some dinner.  I then parked the boat and camp trailer in our driveway so that they are ready to pack and go.  We then showered and changed before going to a wedding reception for Jenessa Hubbard and Broadrick Horton.  It was held in the back yard of the Hubbard’s so we saw several from the old Ward that we know.  Scott and Mishelle were late getting there and so they came in one side of the yard as we left the other side.  Scott had tried to call me but I hadn’t heard my phone ring.  After returning home I cut Mae’s hair and then filled out this entry.  Mae took a shower and I played a game of Solitary before going to bed.

 

Sunday, August 02, 2009

 

                We woke about 5:30AM but laid and cuddled for a while.  After getting up and reading I took care of the chickens and rabbits.  I then set the tent trailer up but got a bucket of soapy water and the hose and washed it down.  It was really dirty on the outside.  When I came in Mae got after me for doing that on Sunday.  I started writing a thank you card to Garrett for the Fathers Day card he sent me.  (a little late!)  We attended meetings and I felt discouraged with the lesson in Priesthood meeting.  I just felt like somewhere I sure failed with the raising of my family.  We came home and I continued with the card for Garrett until Bob called us for dinner.  During dinner my tooth broke and came out.  That means that I will have to see the dentist before we leave tomorrow.  I already feel that I have to much to take care of in the morning with out this.  After dinner Mae and I took the boat out to Sandi’s.  Mae figured that we could get out to Sandi’s and back , take some things to Jeff and Gale’s and be at the Family History Center by 6:30PM.  I also let it get to me as we turned onto I-80 West as there was road constructions and I had to do some re-routing to get around.  Mae questioned where I was going and that set wrong with me.  We made it back just in time for the training meeting at the F.H.C. and so afterwards took the things to Jeff and Gale.  I felt overwhelmed and confused by the time the meeting was over.  I am discouraged this evening.  I checked to tent trailer and I had not set up the sink up therefore the power hadn’t came on.  Bob had taken the garbage cans out to the street but hadn’t taken the chicken cleanings out so I did that.  I finished the card to Garrett so we could get it mailed tomorrow and entered this journal entry before going to bed.

 

Monday, August 03, 2009

 

                I got up at 5AM so that I could get a early start.  I took care of the chickens and rabbits and with Bob’s help we skinned cut up and packaged the chickens that I didn’t get done Saturday.  Mae called Dr. Gray and he wants me to wait until I return for an appointment in as much as I don’t have pain with the broken tooth.  Mae and I got things packed up and left about noon.  We stopped to get gas in Clearfield and then drove to American Fork where we met Becky and gave her some vegetables from our garden.  We got natural gas at Spanish Fork and again at Richfield before getting to Fish Lake.  We found a camp site and setup the tent trailer.  We found that we had some broken water couplings.  I did get the water in the sink working.  We had bought Sub-Way sandwiches while in Richfield so that help us with dinner this evening.  We enjoyed being together this evening.

 

Tuesday, August 04, 2009

 

                I was up two or three times during the night.  The furnace didn’t work the best last night.  We slept in and enjoyed going for a walk along the lake.  It is sure beautiful country here at Fish Lake.  Sandi and Nick came about 1:30PM; after getting Nick and Sandi settled we took the boat out on the Lake.  We fished this afternoon and did fairly well and had a lot of fun.  When we came back to the boat dock area, Nick when over to the truck to put some things in it.  The first thing he realized is that the boat trailer was missing from the truck.  Nick had parked the truck and trailer after lunching the boat.  He did not see the three signs that said that you could not park trailers on this side of the road.  Someone had disconnected the trailer and moved it to the other side of the road in the area for boat trailers.  Nick was relieved to find it but also felt a little stupid that he hadn’t seen the signs.  After eating this evening we played Marbles until we went to bed.  Nick and I won two out of three games. 

 

Wednesday, August 05, 2009

 

                Nick and I went out fishing about 6:30AM.  We fished until about 1:15PM before coming in for lunch.  We had caught four fish that we kept and released ½ dozen others.  We cooked fish and eggs for lunch.  We played games this afternoon, because it rained all afternoon; until it was time to prepare dinner.  Sandi and Mae prepared the vegetables for me to cook in the Dutch oven.  I cooked Dutch oven chicken and rabbit with potatoes, carrots, onions and yams.  While I was cooking dinner Mae and Sandi went for a walk.  Nick stayed with me and in between checking the ovens, we played Rummy.  Nick won the game.  After dinner I cleaned up the oven and then we played more games.

 

Thursday, August 06, 2009

 

                All four of us went out fishing this morning.  It was quite windy from the start so I mainly drove the boat while the others fished.  We would drive to the North West end of the lake, into the wind and then just let the wind push us back to the South East.  We did quite well and all had fun.  It was quite rough when we quite and came back to camp.  The wind continued the rest of the day so we stayed in camp and played games.  Mae and I went to the store to the North end of the lake and used the public showers.  It cost $3.00 each but it was sure worth it.  Mae showered first and had trouble getting the water adjusted.  By the time I got in it was cool but I didn’t bother the adjustment.  This evening we again played games before going to bed.

quite bad but not as bad as it was yesterday.  We quite at 11Am with the three limits of fish, and came back to camp.  Mae stayed at camp and slept in a little later.  She wanted to do her hair so she tried to start the generator.  In the process she slipped and landed on a rock.  With all that she didn’t get the generator started, so she drove to the store where we had taken showers and used their finished packing up.  We left for Tropic about noon.  Mae had misplaced Keela’s address so I didn’t have it to enter into the GPS.  We stopped at the store to get some ice to pack the fish in.  While there Mae asked Sandi if she had the address.  Nick got out his map and said that he felt that we should go South through Loa.  I put a intersection in the GPS of Tropic and the GPS suggested that we go back to Richfield and then south along Highway 89.  Nick felt that was the wrong way so we went his way.  It was a much more beautiful and scenic way but it took us at least 1 1/2 hours longer that way.  We also stopped at a Sub-Way and got us something for dinner as we know that we would be late getting there and probably not have much that we could eat anyway.  It was 6PM when we got to Tropic and that was dinner time.  I did most of the setting up of the camp trailer.  There was a good turnout and it was a very enjoyable evening.  Before going to bed Sandi, Nick, Mae and I went over to David’s trailer and played games with David.  Shauna didn’t feel very good so we tried to be quite so she could sleep.

 

Saturday, August 08, 2009

 

                Most of the Family gathered for breakfast.  Keela and Pete have a very nice lodge for the gathering.  It was a very impressive setting and facilities.  On of the highlights for me was to have Aunt Iris (age 89) and Aunt Joy (age 85) there with us.  they both look very good and seem to get along quite well.  There were many activities for all ages to participate in.  Mae and I helped some of the children build bird houses.  Sandi, Nick and I went on a 3 ½ hour horse back trip down into Bryce Canon.  It was 49 years ago that Mae and I hiked down into Bryce Canon and still remember that experience.  I can tell you that horse or mule trip down and out the canon is a much better way for me to go.  Mae didn’t go with us as I was concerned about her sore bottom (from her fall yesterday) and he bad back.  this evening we had a very enjoyable talent program which many participated in.  Mae and I started it off by singing “There’s a Hole in the Bucket”.  We made it through it but I couldn’t remember the tune.  The evening was a lot of fun.

 

Sunday, August 09, 2009

 

                Most of the Family gathered for a Sacrament meeting although the Sacrament wasn’t passed.  Uncle Van conducted and had Bryce and Deanna report on their mission and gave an inspirational message.  Both of them did a very special job and left us with special thoughts of our Savior and what he did for us.  Bryce will always be Bryce but I am so impressed with his testimony.  I just hoped that David, Shauna and their children could feel the Spirit that I felt during their talks.  We packed things up and left a little after noon.  We stopped in Panguitch at Sub-Way and got a sandwich for lunch.  We filled the boat and Sandi’s truck with gas while there.  Mae and I then stayed on highway 89 to Ephraim where I bought a doe rabbit at Taylor’s Rabbit Farm.  We did stop at Richfield and get natural gas, on the way.  As we drove Mae read from the book “Love That Lasts”.  We talked about the thoughts that were expressed in the book and as we did I got the feeling that Mae felt that most of our moves was all my decision.  As we continued to talk about it I found that there are many things that I didn’t realize her feelings about.  I really had a depression come over me.  I gained a feeling that I have really made a lot of bad decisions and really have blown it.  We stopped in Springville for natural gas and we ate some dinner there at the Flying J.  It was about 9PM when we got home.  Bob helped us unload.  As soon as I came in to the house I could hear water running.  I soon found that the water softener was frozen in full discharge passion and I could not shut it off.  I am not sure how long that it has been that way.

                We slept in a little longer than I should have but then I had to hurry to take care of the rabbits and chickens before I went to the Dentist.  We decided to do a root canal on this tooth that has been bothering me.  When Dr. Gray drilled the tooth out and when he did he said that we had made the right choice as the root was almost dead.  After getting home I finished feeding the animals.  I hadn’t fed them any hay before so gave them some now.  After lunch I took a nap while Mae and Connor went over to the park.  I put my snowmobiles, boat and camp trailer under the canopy.  I had to have Mae come out and help guide me in order to get all three in.  After dinner I started this entry.  We were going to work on Family History this evening but when Jeff came home we played rummy.  It is now 10:20PM so we had better get to bed.

 

Tuesday, August 25, 2009

 

                I woke at 2:15AM and couldn’t go to back sleep.  I surfed the channels and found almost nothing except Wild World and that program was almost over.  At 5AM Mae came in and got me to come back to bed.  We were late getting up.  I took care of the animals and helped Mark cut and take away the corn stocks.  I planned a board for Scott and Mishelle but I don’t remember the length he wanted it cut.  Mae made dinner for Scott and Jeff to eat with us but neither could get away from work today.  I took a field trip today and didn’t finish until 8:30PM.  Mae had gone to the Family History Center so I beat her home.  After getting home I entered my field trip into the computer.  Mae got me something to eat when she came home.  We played a game of Rummy with Jeff to help him un-wind from work and hopefully be able to sleep tonight.

 

Wednesday, August 26, 2009

 

                I got up at 5:30 AM so that I could get things ready to leave with Nick this afternoon for Flaming Gorge fishing.  We will be home on Saturday afternoon or evening.  I did rotate the tires on the car and did some repairs to the rear bumper.  The rear bumper still doesn’t look right but at least it shouldn’t fall off.  I got some boards finished for Scott and Mishelle and showed Mae how to take care of the chickens. The red hen is setting and she doesn’t leave the nest even for food and water.  I therefore take her off the nest for a few minutes each day.  Mae has sure been a help to me in getting me ready to go today.  I was all packed and ready to go by 5PM but Nick didn’t get here until about 7:30PM so it will be a long drive tonight.  Nick and I arrived at Lucerne Valley Marina camp ground just after midnight.

 

Thursday, August 27, 2009

 

                When we arrived the office was closed so we just found a spot near the restrooms and through out the air mattress with out sleeping bags and went to sleep.  The stars were so bright that I woke several times and just looked at the sky in amazement at the beauty and wonder of the sky.  We got up, prepared some quick breakfast, set up our tent and went out fishing.  We stopped on the way to the boat dock and paid for our camp site.  We fished until after noon with out any success so we came in to get some lunch.  After eating we were going to take a nap but found that our tent was right in the hot sun.  We looked at a empty camp site close to our site and it had a shady area in it.  We decided to move out tent to that site and did but it was more work than I had anticipated.  After moving we had to go to the office and change our camp site.  The lady wasn’t too happy but made the change.  We went back out fishing and Nick did catch a nice coco salmon.  When we came in we cleaned the one fish and fillet it for breakfast tomorrow.  Nick and I played a game of Rummy before we went to bed.  The one trouble with a tent is that you don’t see the stars as well as just sleeping under the sky.

 

Friday, August 28, 2009

 

                I was up before 5AM so that we could get an early start on the day.  After preparing and eating breakfast of salmon, we met Hank _____ at the boat dock about 7AM.  Hank is a good friend of Nick’s who now lives in Manila and is a licensed Fishing Guide.  He supplied the boat and all the fishing equipment.  We just had to have our fishing licenses and snacks.  We fished until almost 2PM with out any of us even having a bite.  Then we changed to the forth location and as I dropped my lure and made the first jig I had a Lake trout strike.  He put up a good fight and after getting him in and having Nick take a photo, I released him.  That gave us some hope but that was our only catch.  We finally quite fishing for the day at 4PM, very hot and tired.  When we came in and Nick went to settle up, Hank refused and said that this trip was on him.  He said that Nick had spent many hours helping children, which included his sons, with sports and hadn’t been paid for his time.  He said that this trip was a way that he could say thanks in return.  After eating some dinner, Nick and I went back out fishing for coco salmon but didn’t have any luck this evening.  We were late coming in and had a little trouble finding the bay. 

 

Saturday, August 29, 2009

 

                Nick and I got up early to get out fishing.  As I forgot to bring the alarm clock I talked to Mae last night and asked her if she was up before 5AM she might give me a call to wake me.  She called at 4:03AM as she had woken to use the bathroom.  That was neat of her anyway.  We were on the lake by 7AM and fished until 11:30AM before quitting.  Nike had caught a couple of small lake trout but that was it.  We got some lunch and packed up and out of the campgrounds by 1:15PM.  Nick started driving so I could take a nap.  I woke shortly before we got to Evanston where we stopped for gas and natural gas.  I drove from there to home.  We quickly transferred Nick’s equipment to his car and he left for home.  I showered and shaved before Mae and I went out to eat at the Golden Corral.  We came back and watched a dumb movie on DVD but it was good to be together again.

 

Sunday, August 30, 2009

 

                I polished my boots and fed the animals before we had breakfast.  We had to hurry to get to meetings on time.  After meetings Bob had some dinner for us.  Jeff asked me if he could sleep in the tent trailer for a few days so he and I set it up.  About 5PM Gail and the kids came over and we grilled chicken and have dinner.  After Gail and the children left, Mae, Jeff and I played a game of Rummy; witch Mae won.  We were late before we got to bed.

 

Monday, August 31, 2009

 

                The bus chop called me at 5AM and asked me to take a route today.  I had to hurry and Mae got up and helped me get off.  The morning route went well, but there were some mechanical problems with the bus.  When I finished the run I took the bus to the shop to have them repair the windshield wipers and the horn.  When I got home I fed the animals.  There was a baby chick that hatched last night and I thought everything was ok when I left.  The one white hen is still looking quite sick but I let her be this morning.  Before lunch I took a nap but a call on the phone woke me.  Mae made a nice lunch and I went into work to take the afternoon run.  The bus had been repaired by the time I took it this afternoon.  This evening I took Mae out to see the chick and it is gone.  I don’t know what or why but it is gone. I also killed the white hen to put her out of the suffering.  I buried her as I didn’t want it in the garbage all week.  I got on the computer and got the plans for a Cornhole board and the rules for the game.  I then worked on The New Family History for a while.  I filled out this journal entry before we went to bed.
