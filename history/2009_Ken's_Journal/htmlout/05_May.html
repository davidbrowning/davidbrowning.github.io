Monday, May 25, 2009   MEMORIAL DAY

 

                We woke about 7AM and were soon up to get ready for today’s activities.  Mae and I want to go to several of the cemeteries here in Idaho to decorate love ones graves with flowers.  We had breakfast with Rich and Lois and some of their family that got up that early.  I did look at the railing, that they had replaced, and I have to admit that the person that did the job, either is a very poor craftsman or someone that would cheat and take advantage of an older couple & their daughter.  I would like to talk to him and tell him my feelings.

                Before we left, I got on Lois’s computer and did a search for some of the cemeteries, plus Elise helped me with some of the directions.  We went first to the Pocatello Cemetery and found Uncle Irven and Aunt Bernice’s graves.  There had not been an American Flag placed on Uncle Irven’s grave, which there should have been as he served in World War II. 

                After leaving there we went to Wal-Mart and bought some flowers for Mark.  I guess that we hadn’t told him that we were coming up to Idaho to decorate graves or he would have sent flowers up with us for his parent’s graves.  We next went to the Rigby Cemetery to find Uncle Myron and Aunt Lila’s grave.  As we entered the Cemetery I wondered where we should start looking for the graves.  Mae suggested that we just start walking but I felt impressed to drive in further.  I saw an equipment shed and drove to it.  On the door was the Sexton’s name and phone number.  I called it and his daughter said that he was out but gave me his cell phone number.  I called that but only got the answering message.  I left my number asking him to call me.  We then started walking backing and forth on the rows of graves reading Head Stones, Mae took one row and I took another.  On the third pass we found the markers.  It was remarkable to me that we could find them so fast.  Someone had placed some flowers on the graves.  I guess it would have been Aunt Lila’s family because with their and their daughter’s death, they would have no other posterity.  To the side of their graves was the grave of their infant son, which I had not known about until I did the Family History research.  

                We noticed that a military program was getting ready to start so we walked over to the main flag pole.  I spoke to one of the uniformed men about the placing of flags on Veterans graves and he told me that it was under the direction of the Sexton.  I told him that Uncle Myron’s grave did not have a flag on it so he gave me a flag to put on the grave.  The ceremony then started and we stayed for it.  It was very appropriate and fitting to honor those Veterans that had given their life’s and times to protect our freedoms.

                After leaving the Cemetery we went to a park and there had our lunch.  Before leaving Rigby we stopped at a store and bought some flags, so that if any of the other graves that should be honored with a flag on there grave did not have one, we could put one on it.

                We next went to the Rexburg North Cemetery.  We spent some time looking for dad’s grave.  I finally called Elise and she tried to tell me where it was.  After talking to her we went deeper into the cemetery than I had thought and we found Dad’s and his first wife Viola’s graves.  I was sure greatful to have found them as I had not been back to honor my Father since his death.

                We drove to Driggs and found the cemetery.  It didn’t take long to find the Hill plot.  I was especially pleased at the care that is now done for the cemetery grounds.  I remember that as a youth each year we would bring rakes and shovels and spend considerable time cleaning up the plot.  Now it is such a pleasant area with lawn and trees and it is kept clean and well cared for.  We then looked for Uncle Grant’s and Aunt Marjorie’s graves and soon found then further East of the Hill plot.  There to we found the grave of their first baby that I hadn’t known of until I started my Family History research.  I also took a picture of the head stones of Marjorie’s Brother and I think her Mother.  I will visit with Grant and Marjorie’s daughter about the possibility of doing their Temple work for them.

                At each grave we took pictures of the head stones and wrote a brief description of where the graves were located.  I will enter this information into my computer files so I will know where all these locations are.

                We left Drigg’s and went to Victor.  It took us a while to find the Cemetery there as I have not been there since we moved from Victor.  Plus the roads have changed a great deal since we lived there.  After finding the Cemetery I called and had Mark give me the location of Aunt Vida and Uncle Albert’s graves.  We placed flowers on each of them.  We then left for Pocatello but stopped at a camp ground on Pine Pass and ate a picnic lunch.  It was a picnic as we had the mosquitoes out after us.  It was fun anyway.  After getting to Idaho Falls, Mae drove and let me take a nap until we got to Pocatello.  We again stopped at Uncle Irven’s grave and placed a flag there, before coming on to Rich and Lois’s home.  We had a wonderful day and it was great to be together.   We love spending time together.  We stayed at Rich & Lois’s home again tonight.

 

Tuesday, May 26, 2009

 

                I woke up at 5:30AM with Mae’s phone beeping that it needed to be charged.  That woke me up so I stayed up thinking about the poor job that the fellow did in replacing the deck railing.  I would like to talk to him and let him know that I am very disappointed with his work.  Ether he deliberately cheated them or he is a darn poor craftsman.  Mae got up at 7AM and we soon had breakfast and started on our quilt.  We used Rich and Lois’s equipment and made a survival quilt.  We were not sure if their sewing machine would sew that thick of padding but it worked very well.  It took the longest for Mae and Lois to sew the edges.  It was about 3:30 PM before we left for home.  We stopped at home for just a few minutes and then went over to the Family History Center.  After getting back home I entered this entry.  I guess that we left some of Mae’s books at the center.  Time for bed.

 

Wednesday, May 27, 2009

 

                I woke at 3:45AM but then went back to bed and slept until 7AM.  After breakfast I took care of the chickens and rabbits.  I then went out front and started cleaning up the roots and tried to use the roto-tiller but there was too many.  I used the pickup and a chain and pulled roots.  Bob and Carroll came home and Bob helped me by driving the pickup.  I quite for lunch at 12PM as Scott came for lunch.  After lunch I laid down in the recliner and slept for almost an hour.  Bob and I again worked on pulling roots from the front of the house.  Mae left to help Scott and Mishelle this evening by tending children.  While she was there she did laundry to help out.  Bob and I took the load of cement from the curbing and the roots that we had pulled out to the dumps.  After getting back, Bob fixed some dinner for us.   After eating I went downstairs and took a shower and changed, then he and I went Home Teaching.  We stopped at Fallis’s but she wasn’t there, but told her son to tell us she would be back about 9PM.  We went over to Casey’s and found that Isaac has been laid off for about two months.  Also Becky is expecting a baby girl in two weeks.  We next went into the McKay Dee Hospital and visited with Patriarch Johnstun who is in there for bleeding Ulster’s.  We again stopped at Sister Fallas’s but she still wasn’t there.  I came home and started my journal.  Mae soon came home.  She got on the inter-net and reserved rooms for us when we get to Kirkland in July, but it would not process. 

 

Thursday, May 28, 2009

 

                I got up at 4:45AM to get ready to go to the bus compound.  I was one of two drivers that took youth from Clearfield High School to the Hale Theater.  The drivers also got to attend the show.  The show was “Pirates of the Caribbean” and the acting and stage props were great although the plot wasn’t anything special.  We got the youth back to school at noon.  When I got home Mae had just eaten so she got me something to eat.  I then laid down and took a nap.  This evening Mae and I went to the Ogden Temple.  After an enjoyable session we went out to eat at the Chuck-A-Rama.  I checked on the chickens and rabbits before coming for the night.  Mae got on the inter-net and read Garrett’s weekly letter, which gets forwarded to us.  Garrett is doing great and we sure enjoy hearing what he is doing.  I also filled in my journal entry for today. 

 

Friday, May 29, 2009

 

                I woke at 5:30AM and know that I had more time before I really needed to be up so I was sure that I could take d=care of the animals before I took my field trip.  At 5:45AM the phone rang and the dispatch asked if I could take a Special Ed run.  When Mae told them that I already had a field trip she said I should be back before my field trip.  I had to hurry and leave because the rout started at 7:15 AM.  I got back to the bus compound at 9AM and found my bus assigned to the field trip and made it to the school by 9:45AM.  There had been a especial request for a handicap life on one of the two busses so I brought that bus.  It turned out that the child for the lift was sick and couldn’t come.  We returned the youth to the school at 2:15PM and I started back to the bus compound when a request for a driver to take a Elementary class on a field trip.  I am not sure just what happened to their schedule but it had fallen through.  I called dispatch and volunteered.  Dispatch said that my bus was not large enough, so I said that I would return to the compound.  Dispatch then called back and asked my location.  They asked if I could stop at the Farmington compound and exchange busses and then return to the school.  I took the youth from Washington elementary to Draper where they went to a Family Activity area.  I was hungry so I went to the café and bought a Chicken Creaser Salad.  I waslked around the resort for a while and then went to the bus and took a nap.  It was after 5PM when the youth finished up and reloaded the bus.  After taking the class back to school I had to take the bus back to the Farmington compound, trade buses and then return that bus to the Clearfield compound.  It was after 7PM when I returned home.  I was very tired when I got home and so we went right to bed.

 

Saturday, May 30, 2009

 

                We slept in this morning and it was 9AM by the time I got out to take care of the animals.  Bob and I then planned the materials for the front lawn project.  We spent some time trying to find where the water lines are on the west side of the driveway.  It took two trips to Home Depot to get the blocks for the planters.  Bob helped me with the first load.  When I finished getting the second load Mae had dinner ready.  We watched a show on Hall Mark while we ate.  Mae got several phone calls while we watched that show.  Then before it ended Bryce call Mae and talked to her through most of the second show.  After that show ended I finished my journal entry and we went to bed.

 

Sunday, May 31, 2009

 

                I woke just after 4AM and thought that I might soon get a call to take a bus run.  When I again woke at 7AM I thought how happy I was that I didn’t get a call.  Then I realized that today is Sunday and I enjoyed that thought.  As I read the scriptures this morning my mind was continually on David and his family.  I don’t understand how someone can be so intelligent and yet be so blind as to the testimony of the Book of Mormon.  I find so much comfort and guidance as I read and study the scriptures and the directions from living Prophets and those who are called to lead the Church.  This morning I felt great anxiety for my children, my assigned families that I Home Teach and for the great evil that is sweeping the earth.  I often think of parents that I have known and that I now know that has or had children that have abandoned their teachings.  My heart aches under such thoughts.  Oh how I realize my need to trust in my Lord and Savior.  Bishop Smith gave the message in out combined Priesthood and Relief Society meeting.  His message was on the Family and he gave a very appropriate and timely message.  In Sunday School Sister Joyce Wilson gave the lesson on the Three Degrees of Glory and did an excellent presentation.  The Spirit of the Sacrament meeting was also very good.  Special Day!  After church Bob and I went over to Sister Fallis’s Home Teaching.  She wasn’t home yet so we came home and then went back a little later.  Mae and I then went to David and Shauna’s for dinner and to visit for the evening.  david took us down to show us their camper.  It is very nice.  After getting back to their home, Mae and David figured out a date for us to go with them to East Canon reservoir for a few days.  We watched a show on TV of a man that looks for monsters in the water.  It show him search for a large monster in a river in Africa that was eating children, young people and even a water buffalo.  When it ended up he found large cat fish that weighed up to 161 lb. And he estimated that there were some that weighted even much more than that.  It was 10PM when we got home.  I finished this entry and we soon went to bed.  Mae said that if I don’t get a call to drive bus in the morning we would try to go fishing.
