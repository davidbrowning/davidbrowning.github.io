                Again we were up at 6AM so we could get to Nauvoo.  We got there about 9AM and we first went on the carriage ride into the North side of Nauvoo.  It was a very inspirational ride as our guide talked about the people and trials they had as they settled the area of Commerce and turned it from a mosquito invested swamp into Nauvoo (The City Beautiful).  After that Spiritual and thrilling experience we saw the “Remembering Nauvoo” film in the visitor center.  What a beautiful film to see.  We next went to Land and Records, where we looked up some of our Ancestors that lived in Nauvoo and where they owned property.  The Sister there explained to us that when the Saints were driven out of Nauvoo they did not pay their property taxes and so their property was sold on tax auction and so they no longer had legal ownership to the properties.  I think it was a legal way to steal the properties.  We went to the Browning Home and Gun Shop.  As I am a third Great Grandson, we got rather special treatment there We got some lunch at a café and then came back and went to the Brigham Young home, Lucy Mack Smith home where she lived after the martyrdom of Joseph and Hyrum, next we went to the Brick Yard and then to the Joseph Smith Sites that are owned by the Community of Christ Church.  We sure recognized a difference in the Spiritual feelings there although we were excited to see the sites.  We then returned to the Nauvoo Visitor Center and saw the production of “The High Hopes and River Boats” production.  After that show we went for dinner at the Hotel Nauvoo where we had a full buffet and then returned to see the musical production “Sunset by the Mississippi”, that was a great evening of fun and talent.  It was after 10 PM when we got back to our motel in Keokuk.  We were very tired but had a wonderful day.

bucket.  The ashes were then processed to make, the lie used in the making soap, further processing resulted in potash and even more processing resulted into pearl ash.  Pearl ash was sent to England where they made china and other dishes, so they received best price (I believe it was $500 per barrel) for the Pearl ash.  These funds were used for the operation of the Church, with the majority of it going to for the building of the Temple.  After moving from Newell Knight’s home to John Johnson’s home and then for the safety of the Prophet, back to Kirtland to the remodeled apartment in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints, did a lot of restoration to the building.  She explained that during the dedications of the temple, there were over a thousand people and so 15 to 20 people occupied each pew.  As we set in the pew I estimated that only 8 to 12 people could set comfortable per pew.  The meeting took 8 hours and she said that no one left their seats even though there was some breaks.  I can’t imagine children especially going that long as they would be tired and have to go to the bathroom, eat, etc.  I asked the guide this and she said she didn’t know how they did it, but that was what she was told.   During the 2nd dedication session on Sunday, the curtains were dropped to give privacy to the First Presidency and the Savior appeared to Joseph and Sidney and accepted His temple.  The members had been in poverty and yet they gave their all to the building of that temple.  The sisters had also gave their china dishes which were crushed and put in the plaster for the exterior of the temple which gave the walls a glisten effect.  When the Reorganized Church renovate the exterior of the temple, because big chunks of plaster had fallen off, etc., they tried to re-plaster it the same way, but it didn’t work.  They later found out the reason why was that the early saints had plastered it by hand, but when they redid it, they used a power sprayer to put the plaster on the pressure caused the broken glass to seed itself deep into the plaster and so the glass didn’t show up on the outside.  They, therefore, just painted over the building with white paint and that is the way it is maintained today.
