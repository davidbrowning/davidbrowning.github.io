Wednesday, April 01, 2009

 

                I got up at 5AM to get ready for a field trip.  I had time to feed the rabbits, chickens and chicks.  I took the band members from Fairfield Jr. High to Utah State University in Logan.  The trip went well but the group was longer eating than was planned so we were 30 minutes later leaving for home.  I got to the school at 3:15 PM and finished the trip at 4 PM.  We had something to eat and then drove out to Sandi and Nick’s.  I looked at the assembly instructions for the Gazebo.  We also looked at the master bedroom shower, as it is leaking and causing mildew.  We stayed over night so before going to bed we played a game of marbles and as normal Mae and Sandi won. 

 

Thursday, April 02, 2009

 

                I woke several times thinking of how to put the gazebo together and also tried to think of what can be done with the shower.  I got up just before Sandi left for work and talked to her about some ideas.  Mas fixed us some breakfast while I shaved and made our bed.  After eating breakfast we came on home.  I stopped and got gas in the car and took the car through a car wash.  When I got home I worked on the electrical for the chicken coop.  I had to go to The Home Depot and exchange the electrical breakers that I had bought the other day.  I was sure they were wrong when the fellow in the electrical department gave them to me.  I told him that I didn’t think they were right but he insisted they were correct.  I got the 110v circuit working but will wait until later to install the 220v circuit.  I came in for lunch and after eating took a nap.  I got a call from the School Bus dispatch and asked if I could take a PM route?  I did and then as I finished that Roberta called me and ask if I could take an additional route tomorrow when I get back form the field trip.  That will make it a big day tomorrow.  I worked outside from 5PM until after 8PM getting a roust about built for the chickens.  Bob came out and helped me.  It rained quite hard on us.  Mae had a nice dinner ready when I came in.  After eating I entered my journal and took a shower before going to bed.

 

Friday, April 03, 2009

 

                I got up at 5:35AM to get ready for a field trip.  I took Creekside Elementary to the University of Utah.  When I returned and let the children off I took students from Layton Jr. High home and then took a rout from Northridge High.  I was tired when I finished up.  This evening we watched the movie “Funny Girl” before going to bed.

 

Saturday, April 04, 2009

 

                I got up at 7AM and after eating breakfast I went out and worked on the chicken coop.  I missed the first half of the morning session of General Conference because I worked to long on the coop.  After the morning session we ate and then I went back out and worked on the coop until the afternoon session.  Gail brought the children over for Mae to tend while she attended baby shower for her sister.  We took the children out to see the chickens, chicks and rabbits.  We did get to watch the afternoon session of General Conference.  I finished the work on the coop and helped Mark get started planting strawberry plants in the garden.  I picked up Scott and we attended the Priesthood session of General Conference.  After the session we went to Golden Corral and ate and visited.  It was a nice evening and I sure enjoyed being with Scott.  I just wish that I could have had more of my boys with me. 

 

Sunday, April 05, 2009

 

                I woke up early so went into the front room and slept in the recliner so Mae wouldn’t get disturbed.  I was up for a while thinking of Sandi’s project so I made a list of some of the items I would need to take with us.  When I went back to sleep, Mae woke me at 8:30AM.  I fixed us some breakfast and we finished eating just before conference started.  While the opening song was sang I went out and fed the rabbits and chickens.  Conference was sure good.  I am deeply impressed at the lives and devotion of the members of the Quorum of the Twelve and the First Presidency.  I wish that I could transmit my impressions to my children and especially David and Jeff.  In between the morning session and the afternoon session, Bob invited us up for dinner.  He cooks chicken especially for Mae and I.  Just after the afternoon session started Scott and his family came to be with us.  After another wonderful session of General Conference ended Mae had a nice dinner ready.  Following dinner we took the children out to see the rabbits, chickens and baby chicks.  Ashley and Kylan really enjoyed the baby chicks and enjoyed holding them.  The twins are sure growing fast and we enjoyed them also.  Mae and I took a walk in the park after Scott and his family left for home.  Bob sent us down a couple of slices of apple pie that he made especially for us with out any sugar in it.  He uses the Agave sweetener that is all natural agave fruit sweetener.  Scott and Mishelle informed us that Tina Linderman is getting married.  That is sure good news and we sure hope for the best for her.  Mae called Tina this evening to let her know how happy we are for her.  I had to make up some capsules so that Mae could get them ready for next week.  We sure seem to go through them fast but time goes by fast anymore.  Mae got things some what ready to go out to Sandi and Nick’s tomorrow.

 

Monday, April 08, 2009

 

                We got up early and were ready to leave about 8AM.  We had to go to the Nut & Bolt Supply to get some anchor bolts and then to the gas station for natural gas for the pickup.  It was close to noon when we got out to Sandi’s.  We went right to work and got a lot of the gazebo assembled.  I backed the trailer with the boxes of parts down to the patio pad but the dirt was quite wet and I got stuck.  I put on the tire chains but still couldn’t pull the trailer back up.  I decided to unhook the trailer and leave it where it was as it was close enough to get the materials we needed.  The trailer rolled back when I disconnected it and as I tried to control it I slipped and landed on my tail bone which bruised it and made it hard to work with out pain.  Sandi and Mae really helped me.  I assigned Mae as the supervisor and chief engineer on the job.  She read the instructions and kept the right part numbers supplied to us.  We quite after Nick got home form work and before he went to bed we played a couple of games of marbles.  The first game Mae and Sandi won but I realized that Sandi had not used a card that she could have used but had just discarded a card and drew another.  Kidding, I told her that it had to be forfeiture.  The second game Nick and I did win.  That is the first game in many that we have won.  After Nick went to bed Mae, Sandi and I stayed up and watched a video, “The Boy in Stripped Pajamas”.  It was a World War II movie and left you with a sick feeling.

 

Tuesday, April 07, 2009

 

                We got a good start this morning and worked all day on the project.  We used an Extended Fork Lift to lift the gazebo roof while we installed the eights legs.  It was after 4PM when we finished and left for home.  I took care of the animals while Mae unpacked some things and took a shower.  I took a shower and we soon left for the Family History Center.  We were a little late again this evening.  I worked mainly on Grant Hill’s family and JaNae and Brad’s records.  I did get a phone number for Jennie Lee Griggs, that is Uncle Grant’s daughter, but I tried to call her but didn’t get an answer.  I caught two day of my journal entries up this evening before going to bed.

 

Wednesday, April 08, 2009

 

                We were late getting up and finishing breakfast.  I took care of the rabbits and chickens and then went to town to get a meat cleaver.  Mae asked me to mail a package to Jeff and I also stopped at Dee’s garage to check on the charges for inspecting the car and getting it registered.  I bought the meat cleaver at Bed Bath and Beyond.  Scott came for lunch with us today and we sure enjoy having him.  I killed the five black rabbits this afternoon, cut them us and packaged them.  They averaged 3.6 lb. apiece dressed weight.  Mae cooked one of the rabbits for dinner this evening.  After dinner I took a nap and then went outside and sorted the white kits out into the four pens, separating the bucks from the does.  Bob and I went Home Teaching this evening.  We visited with the Casey’s and then went to Sister Fallis’s.  Sister Fallis wasn’t home but her daughter-in-law came out just as we got there and brought me the phone.  It was Sister Fallis, telling me that she had gone to Park City with her Sister-in-law and the windshield wipers quite working.  It is snowing very hard up there so they are just going to stay the night there.  We had a nice visit with Sister and Patriarch Johnston.  Sister Johnston fell last Sunday and really bruised her face.  There were no bones broken, so she should be ok.  Mae was watching a show when I got home so I watched the end with her.  We went to bed after I made this entry.  Of course I had to play a game or two of Solitary before turning the computer off.

                I got up at 4:30 AM and drove a bus route at 6AM.  I had taken this route last week so the route was familiar.  After the morning run I went over to Scott and Mishelle’s and put the final coat of sheetrock mud on the patch.  I got home in time for lunch.  Mae had lunch ready for Scott and I when I got back at noon.  After Scott went back to work I took a ½ hour nap before driving the afternoon route.  When I got home Bob and I loaded the tiller on the trailer and went to Sherries where I tilled her garden for her.  Mae had dinner ready when I got home.  After dinner I tried to find something on TV to watch but came out empty.  I made this entry and then we went to bed.

 

Thursday, April 30, 2009

 

                I was up early for a bus run again.  Mae prepares as much of my breakfast as she could last night so I let her sleep in.  She also fed the rabbits and chickens so that after I ate I took a nap.  Mae soon had dinner ready when I got home this afternoon.  We got ready and attended the wedding reception for Tina Linderman and Scott Richards this evening.  There was a special feeling to see Tina with Scott and to know that she has finally found someone to care for her.  It was also great to see all those from the old Ward there that we know so well.  The reception was held in a bowery at the Ward Building.  The weather was very pleasant for the occasion and there was a very large attendance.  Jola Craven, Deinise Hawks, Bonnie Benedick and Diane Lapervose had voluntary catered the refreshments and did an excellent job of it.  It all seemed so appropriate.  We found out, while there, that Tina’s father had passed away and his funeral was held on Tuesday.  We felt bad that we hadn’t known about that and that we hadn’t attended the Wedding Ceremony in the Salt Lake Temple this morning.
