To Pen Pal, Austin, at South Weber Elementary

December 23, 2015

Dear Austin,

                My name is Kenneth Browning.  I usually go by the nick name of Ken.

I am glad to receive your Pen Pal letter.  I still remember when I was about your age I also had a Pen Pal that lived in California.  I still remember the excitement of receiving those letters from California.

I was born in Sugar Idaho and started school in Victor Idaho.  A couple of years before I started school, our school house caught fire and burned down.  Mainly with volunteer help from men that lived in Victor, they built a three room log building for a school house.  When I told my children that I went to school in a log cabin they ask if Abraham Lincoln went to school with me.  I am not that old but I am much older than you.  When I was 12 years old my family moved to Ogden, Utah.  There I went to Lincoln Jr. High located on 28th street and east of Washington Boulevard.   From Ogden my family moved to Sunset, Utah and I went to Davis High School where I graduated.  I then went into the US Air Force.  Since then I have move several times. Married to wonderful girl and we have raised a family of 2 girls and 4 boys.  They have all married and we now have 20 grandchildren and 10 great-grandchildren.  My wife and I now live in Roy, Utah.

Thank you for your letter.  I hope you always enjoy school and do your best in your classes.

Ken Browning

 

P.S.  I hope you have a Marry Christmas also.

                I woke at 2.20am and didn’t go back to sleep.  My I pod was left on and so it was out of power when we went to bed, so I wasn’t able to read from that, plus we hadn’t brought the New Testament so I just laid in bed worrying about Sandi and the predicament she is in.  My mind can’t comprehend the pain, we people here on earth, give our Father in Heaven as I think about the sorrow our children are giving Mae & I.  Maybe someday we will.  We did enjoy being with David and his family as we opened Christmas gifts.  I was given two ties, a pant suit and a new white shirt from Mae, as well as Mike and David paid for Mae and I to stay overnight in the hotel after our family Christmas party.  Shellie, Roy & Sandi gave us a set of electric tooth brushes.  Jeff & Gail gave us a $50 Chevron gas gift card and Scott & Mishelle gave us a plaque that Mishelle made, “Christ is in the center of our home, A guest at every meal, a silent listener to every conversation” and some dish cloths which she had crocheted .  After enjoying the morning with David’s family, we went to see Scott’s family for about three hours and then to see Jeff’s family who are at Gail’s mother’s home.  From there we went to Mikes home.  We ate a wonderful dinner there and enjoyed visiting with them.  We also played Mormon Bridge before coming home.

Saturday, December 26, 2015

                We got up at 6:30am and got started for the day as Mae is having a Christmas party for our younger children.  I went over to the church house and helped clean the building.  I was the last on to finish up and as I went to leave I realized that the sidewalks had not been cleaned of snow, so I got a shovel and cleaned the walks.  I also put snow melt on them.  It was 10:15am when I got home.  Gail had already brought her children over and by 10:30am Scott and Mishelle brought the twins over.  I was just going to start helping Mae with the party when Richard Flint called with a frozen water line.  I took my heater down and we started thawing the inlet line but I could not find the main water inlet valve.  We went up to Jimmy’s and got him and he doesn’t know where the main valve is.  He called Scott Wayment who came up and he suggested that Richard leave the water running on slow until Monday and then he will install a heat cable on his water lines.  While visiting with Rich, he said that his son had informed him that Jimmy Cosper died yesterday morning.  When I got home, I called Bishop Mullins and he confirmed that Jimmy died at 8am in his home on Christmas morning.  We feel bad as we enjoyed getting to know the Cospers.  I am sure that it is going to be very hard on His wife Darma as they were very close.  Jimmy’s funeral will be held on the 31st and that will be the day we are planning on returning home.  Mae’s party went well and when our grandchildren left, I helped clean up the house.  We then went shopping for funeral flowers for Linda’s funeral.  We also got some sympathy cards, for Linda’ and for Jimmy’s spouses and family.  This evening we watched two good movies on the Encore Family channel.  I filed out this entry before we took our showers and went to bed.

Sunday, December 27, 2015

                We got up and had breakfast, then had to hurry so that we could pickup Brother and Sister Scheer’s for church.  When we got to their place, I realized that I still had the flowers in the trunk, so I took them back home and then came back so we could take the Sheer’s to church.  We have to put their walkers in the trunk.  Meetings were very good, although I was a little depressed after priesthood meeting as I don’t know why I have 4 out of my 6 children that are not really active in the church.  That is and has been a depressing thought every since we returned from our 2nd mission.  I love them all, but just don’t know how to help them return back with strong testimonies.  When we got home we ate some lunch, then David and his family came by to bring Mae her Birthday present.  They gave her a beautiful card with $40 so she can go shopping and get what she wants.  David and I also talked about the building of their climbing wall.  After they left, Mae and I went over to visit Sister Cosper and give her a small gift of our love.  We will miss Brother Cosper’s funeral as we will not arrive home from Linda’s funeral until late on Thursday night.  My big concern is with the drive from Fresno on Thursday, as we will be traveling through Las Vegas on the 31st .  I have tried to find an alternative way to travel but cost and connections just won’t work out.  I have also been trying to figure out the angles of David’s climbing wall.  Trying to figure the angles of the climbing wall, trying to figure out a way to get to Linda’s funeral, trying to figure out Ray’s stairs and trying to figure out the money that might have been given me for flowers, my mind has gone completely blank.  Good night!

Monday, December 28, 2015       MAE’S BIRTHDAY

                I woke at 6:30am but Mae didn’t sleep well so she slept until 8am.  I prepared breakfast of egg omelets.  After eating and cleaning up the dishes, I went up to David’s and figured the materials we will need to build their climbing wall.  I got back in time so that Mae and I could go out to dinner with Sandi.  Sandi treated us to dinner at Sizzler in Bountiful for Mae’s birthday gift.  When I came home, I went down to Wayment’s and got my heater as I want to take it with us when we leave tomorrow.  David came down and took my tool trailer up to his place.  I did some figuring on the material for David.  Irven and Sandy came this evening for dinner.  They will stay over tonight as we plan on leaving for California by 6am tomorrow.   Mae spent the day doing laundry, packing and fixing dinner for us, Irven, Sandy and their daughter, Andrea, other than going to lunch with Sandi and I.  Andrea had stayed with Irven & Sandy for the Christmas holidays.  After eating dinner, she left to go to her home in Orem.

Tuesday, December 29, 2015

                We were up early, had breakfast and we got off by 6:15am.  We stopped in Fillmore where we got a frozen yogurt.  With only stopping for fuel and rest stops, we drove straight through.  Traffic was really full and slow from Las Vegas to Barstow.  When we got to Tehachapi, California (I have never heard of the town nor can I pronounce it), Mae suddenly said turn off here.  I did, but when I asked her why, she said there was a sign that said “Food”.  We were all hungry.  It is kind of a strange town but we found a Chevron Gas Station.  The price of gas was $3.239 per gallon.  Although Irven and Sandy objected, I filled the tank.  They only wanted me to put in ten dollars and then find another station which had cheaper gas.  We also asked a police officer if there was any place where we could find food this late at night.  It was dark but must have been about 6:30pm Pacific Standard time.  He told us where to go and we did find a Burger King and got something to eat and then went on.  We are sure grateful for our GPS as we try to navigate our directions here in California.  We got to Rod and Norma Jean Smith’s, Brother and Sister-in-law to Russ and Barbara Smith.  They are away with their family on vacation and so they offered their home for us to stay in while we are here.   Heather and her daughter (Steve’s 2nd wife and daughter) also came for the funeral and stayed in the home with us.  Barbara and Russ and Russ’s Sister Cindy and Roy were also at the home when we got there.  We didn’t stay up very long before going to bed.

Wednesday, December 30, 2015

                We slept well last night and were up for breakfast of cold cereal and toast.  We went to the chapel at 8am and set up the flowers that we had brought.  We were very glad that we had brought the flowers, as with the exception of one lovely plant and the Family flowers for the casket and an easel display, there were no other flowers.  With what we brought, it did look enough and very appropriate.  The program was very fitting with Barbara’s Eulogy, Julie Ann’s solo “You’ll Never Walk Alone” and Bishop Wayne Green’s closing remarks.  Linda’s nephew also made some short remarks representing his Father, Linda’s Brother Mike, who could not be at the funeral, as he and his wife are serving a mission in Alaska.  Mae and Lind’s grandson, Jared, also gave some remarks as they had requested that anyone who felt they wanted to say something, could do so.  I felt that the service was very fitting and well performed.  We went to the Fresno Memorial Gardens, Fresno, California where Linda’s physical body was laid to rest.  Roy gave the prayer at the cemetery.  We returned to the chapel where the Relief Society Sisters of the Cedarview Ward served a wonderful lunch for the family.  After eating, we went to Roy’s where we visited and enjoyed being with Roy.  Roy had us pick some oranges to take home as well as some that we juiced and enjoyed.  This evening we played some games with Roy and as we were leaving Julianna came to visit with Roy.  We returned to the home of the Smiths.  After getting there, Irven and Sandy remembered that they had left their water bottle at Roy’s.  Mae and I went back and got it.  We decided to fill the car with gas so that we didn’t need to do it in the morning.  Again we were so grateful for the GPS.

Thursday, December 31, 2015

                We slept well and were ready to leave by 7am Mountain Standard Time.  I had suggested that we leave an hour earlier, but I misunderstood Mae and Sandy, for they also thought that we would leave an hour earlier.  We drove straight through without any traffic problems.  Even through from Barstow to Las Vegas it was fairly heavy traffic.  Mae drove a couple of times for me to rest but other than rest stops and fuel we drove straight throught.  We thought of stopping and staying over in St. George but before we got there we decided to go on.  We did stop at Fillmore for frozen yogurt and then arrived at home at 9PM.  We transferred Irven and Sandy’s luggage to their car and they left for home.  Mae and I stayed up and welcomed the New Year in before going to bed.
