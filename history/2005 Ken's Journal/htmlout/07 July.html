                I got up at 5:30 am so Jaren and I could go fishing.  We drove to Soldotna and fished there to start, but after a couple of hours, we tried to find a better area.  We came back to almost the same area that David, Brandon and I fished on Saturday.  We saw a black bear on the other side of the river.  We didn’t have any luck there and at 3:30 pm we left for home.  After getting home I took a nap before dinner.  Terry is a temple worker and it was his day to work there, so Mae & Angela also went and did a session.  They drove in another car since Terry would be going earlier.  Terry took them to lunch at Chilli’s afterwards.  When they got home, Terry showed Mae the family history he had either imported from other files that other people had worked on, or some he had worked on.  He had found many discrepancies so hopes that Mae and other family members will help him work on them to correct them.  Mae was impressed that on the Church Family History website, they have Paff 2 which we can down load and it has the pedigree charts and family history charts, then you can import other information onto those sheets.  Terry has done that for most of the Porter line.  He would like to do that with the Bushnell line also.  After dinner we spent the evening visiting and playing Mormon Bridge.  We watched some of an animated movie, but at 1 am we went to bed.

 

Friday, July 29, 2005

 

                We didn’t wake up until 9 am.  It was 11:30 am before we finished breakfast.  Mike called early but we missed getting it so I called him back later and visited with him for a few minutes about our plans at Randy’s cabin.  Terry & Angela took us to the Alaska Native Heritage Center.  It was very interesting and we spent all afternoon there until it closed at 6 pm.  Terry said it was patterned after the Polynesian Cultural Center in Hawaii.  They had a lodge with a stage where they had the native dancing, sports exhibitions, story telling, etc., at different times.  Then you could take tours going to all the different areas where they had the native homes and buildings, etc., and a person there would be dressed in the native clothing and tell you about their culture and show you the things they used to make a living or live with their families, etc.  It was very interesting and enjoyable and we took lots of pictures.  Terry and Angela are tending David and Amy’s children tonight, Shira and Ashur.  They are cute children.  They paid for David & Amy to be able to stay in a nice hotel and tend their children, for David’s college graduation.  Shira is 3 and Ashur is one year old.  We watched two shows this evening, the end of the animated show we started last night, and “Tuck Forever.  Tuck Forever gave some thoughts on the fountain of youth that really made me think of the purpose of life and how Satan would have frustrated the plan had Adam and Eve partaken of the tree of the “fruit of life”.  We were late getting to bed, and then Mae stayed up to shower and wash her hair while she alternated taking pills and medicine to kill the parasites she has which she got while on our mission to the Philippines.

 

Saturday, July 30, 2005

 

                We were very late getting up.  The weather was bad and we had Shira and Ashur to tend, so we didn’t do anything except relax most of the day.  I read 2 or 3 chapters in my book “Porter Rockwell”.  About 2 pm, Mae, Terry and I went to the Alaskan Museum.  It was very interesting and enjoyable.  They showed a film of the 1969 Alaskan earth quake that was very interesting.  They had many displays and life size models of different native homes, boats, etc.  David and Amy stayed for dinner this evening before picking up their children to go home.  Amy wanted to stay and play Mormon Bridge with us, so David consented.  They put their children down to sleep and we played until after midnight.

 

Sunday, July 31, 2005

 

                Before going to bed last night, both Mae and I took our showers.  This helped us when we got up to be ready for church.  Angela had invited a friend to attend church with her.  When we went to get her, Angela got confused as to the location of her house and it took extra time getting there.  We were a little late for church.  The theme for the talaaks today was on the Sabbath Day observance.  I visited with Robert Park and he would like to go out fishing with me tomorrow.  He later made the reservations for us to go on a charter tomorrow.  The rest of the day was spent relaxing with Terry’s family who came over for dinner about 6 pm.  Terry took Mae & I for a ride to where we could overlook the city of Anchorage.  On the way he stopped so I could buy a fishing license at Wal Mart for tomorrow.  We listened to talks about Sabbath Day observance and here I was breaking the Sabbath.  I had asked Robert if I could get my license there in the morning, but he said I would miss the charter if I tried to get it there, so I felt the ox was in the mire.  Terry and Mae didn’t want to come in, so they waited in the car.  Amy and Jenny helped Angela prepare a nice dinner of grilled pork chops and vegetable sis kabobs.  After David and Brannon’s families went home, I went to bed so I could get up early tomorrow.
