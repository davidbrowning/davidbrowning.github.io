Friday, January 01, 1971

 

                We (Larry & Tammy Braithwaite, John & Linda Willerton, Bob & Pauline Hien and Mae & I) left the dance and went up to Larry & Tammy’s home in Liberty.  We didn’t get to sleep until 4:30AM.  In reality Bob kept us up most of the night.  When someone would start to fall asleep he would jump on them or dump them out of their bed.  We got up about 8AM, ate breakfast and then the men went horse back ridding.  Mae and I got home about 5PM.  I killed (butchered and dressed out) five rabbits.

 

Saturday, January 02, 1971

 

                John, Bob, & I helped Larry & Tammy get ready to move to Arizona.  I got home at 7:45PM.  We had a Bishopric party that started at 7:30PM.  I was late but we had a good time anyway.

 

Sunday, January 03, 1971

 

                We were busy up at the church all day today.  Mike isn’t feeling good; he is running a high fever.  Mae wasn’t able to come out to Fast & Testimony meeting therefore.

 

Monday, January 04, 1971

 

                I got home from work about 7:30 PM.  Mae had everything ready for a real nice family home evening.

 

Tuesday, January 05, 1971

 

                I got home from work about 7:15PM.  I ate and then went up to the Church House (for Bishopric meeting), got home about 11:30PM.

 

Wednesday, January 06, 1971

 

                I worked from 8AM until 10:30 PM at Browning Arms.  I wish that I was getting paid overtime, we could sure use the money.

 

Thursday, January 07, 1971

 

                I didn’t have to work late tonight.  Maybe I should have though, because Mae and I got upset with each other.  We worked it out ok.  I guess we have got to turn to the Lord for help.  We gave the Relief Society President a food order.

 

Friday, January 08, 1971

 

                We were going to go to a show and take the children but decided that we couldn’t afford it, so we stayed home, popped corn and watched a show on TV.  We really enjoyed the evening.

 

Saturday, January 09, 1971

 

                I went over to Wayne Wadsman’s home to talk to him about doing some remodeling on his home.  I am going to have to start doing some side work in order to make ends meet.  I spent most of the rest of the day with my family.

 

Sunday, January 10, 1971

 

                Today was Stake Conference so we got to sleep in.  Mom Porter invited us to eat dinner with them.  At Sacrament meeting tonight, Mae received her Golden Gleaner award.  I am so proud of her.  (I am not sure just how this worked out as there isn’t Sacrament meeting on the same day as Stake Conference.)

 

Monday, January 11, 1971

 

                I worked until 6PM this evening.  When I got home we had Family Home Evening.  Mae gave the lesson and did very good.  Sandi played the piano and Mike said the prayer (this is his 1st time). 

 

Tuesday, January 12, 1971

 

                Mae came down with the flue during the night and hasn’t felt good all day.  I had to work late and with the snow storm I didn’t get home until 7:45PM.  Sandy stayed home from school to help Mae.

 

Wednesday, January 13, 1971

 

                Mae is feeling much better this evening.  I didn’t stay at work tonight because of the snow storm.  Brother Terry Rix and I went out visiting members of the ward to get 8 members to take the Teacher Training class.

 

Thursday, January 14, 1971

 

                I got home from work at 7:30 this evening.  Mae had super ready for me and after eating I went out visiting on Church work.  I got home about 9:30PM and we went to bed about 10:30PM.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife.

 

Friday, January 15, 1971

 

                I worked until 8PM and then called my Mother. (Browning Arm’s allowed employed to use the company’s long distant line after hours for personal calls.)  We talked for about 45 minutes, so it was about 9:20PM when I got home.

 

Saturday, January 16, 1971

 

                Mae took me to work at 10:45 this morning and came back at 4:30 this afternoon.  We used the Co. line and called Larry and Tammy.  We talked to them for about an hour.  This evening we went to John and Linda Wilerton’s for dinner and spent the evening visiting.

 

Sunday, January 17, 1971

 

                It has been a busy day at Church today.  We were able to get some new families in the Ward visited.  Dennis Jenson and his parents spoke in our Sacrament meeting.  Dennis will be going into the mission home next Saturday.

 

Monday, January 18, 1971

 

                I went to work early so that I could get home at 5PM.  Mae took David to the Doctors today and started his baby shots.  The Doctor said that he is doing just fine.  We had a very nice Family Home Evening.  Mae gave the lesson.

 

Tuesday, January 19, 1971

 

                I worked until 6PM this evening.  We got word that Linda Davis went into the hospital again today.  The pace maker on her heart stopped working.  We guess they will have to operate on her tomorrow.  Dwayne Jessop went visiting with me this evening.

 

Wednesday, January 20, 1971

 

                Today I got a $35.00 a month raise that will be affective on this check.  That will sure be a help.  (I had expected that with this raise I would be brought up to quite a bit higher wage. I was told that Mr. Browning “Val Browning Jr.” felt that the company was in financial problems now and so had set a 1% limit on all pay raises.  In reality, that really stinks.)  I had gone to work at 6:45 this morning and worked until 7:30 this evening.  We went to a Fireside this evening, anyway Mae was at the Fireside and I joined her on my way home.

 

Thursday, January 21, 1971

Friday, January 22, 1971

 

                I came home from work early this evening and laid down for an hour.  I then took Mae, Sandy and Shellie to a show.  After the show we stopped and got root beer freezes before going home.  It was a good evening.

 

Saturday, January 23, 1971

 

                I worked at Browning Arm’s from 11AM until 4PM today.  When I got home Mae and I got things ready for a Ward Married social program.  It was a pot luck dinner held at the church house.  After that party we went to the Stake House for a square dance.  It has been an enjoyable evening. 

 

Sunday, January 24, 1971

 

                It was a busy day but an enjoyable one.  Brother Andra Antistation spoke in Sacrament meeting, he was great.  Mike has been sick all day so Mae wasn’t able to make it out to meetings.

 

Monday, January 25, 1971

 

                I got home from work at 6PM.  I ate and then Brother John Ross and I were asked to administer to two people in our Ward.  I got home at 8:10PM and we held Family Home Evening.  We watched some of our slides after we finished with the lesson.  Later this evening I cut Kim and Karl’s hair.

 

Tuesday, January 26, 1971

 

                This evening our Ward held a very nice M.I.A. Music Festival.  Mae had been asked to play her accordion in the program and she did a very good job.  She was nervous but did well and I was very proud of her.  At 8PM we held a Family Home Evening work shop.  We were a little disappointed at the turnout.  There was only a 10 to 15% of what we had hoped for in attendance.  It went well anyway.

 

Wednesday, January 27, 1971

 

                I got home from work about 6PM.  I thought that I would be able to spend some time with Mae and the children but spent the evening up to the Church House.  The demands of being in the Bishopric along with long hours at work, plus the low income that I am getting, is really weighing on me.  I have some serious decisions that I need to make.  I am seeking the Lords help in making these decisions but am really struggling.

 

Thursday, January 28, 1971

 

                I stopped at John Rose’s about 7PM on my way home from work.  John was already gone.  I was tired so I came home and relaxed this evening.  I watched some TV with Mae for a while.  It seemed good to stay home and just relax.

 

Friday, January 29, 1971

 

                I got home from work about 6:30PM and felt quite sick.  I didn’t sleep very well and finally woke, making it into the bathroom where I threw up.  I was able to go back to bed and slept some better.  David didn’t sleep very well tonight.  So between me and David, Mae didn’t get much sleep either.

 

Saturday, January 30, 1971

 

                Brother John Ross and I had an interview at the Bishops office at 8:30AM.  Following that interview I went into work at Browning Arms, getting there about 10 AM,  I worked until 2:30 pm getting back home about 3PM.  This evening John and I again went visiting from 5 to 7PM.  Mae attended the B.Y.U. Education Day from 9:30AM until 9PM.  Mae had got Debbie Higley to tend our children today while we were gone.  Debbie is so good with our children and we feel very confident in her.  It has been a long day for both of us.

Monday, February 01, 1971

 

                This evening we held Family Home Evening and had a wonderful time.  I sure have a great family!

                I was called from work at 12:15AM with problems on one of my programs so I had to go into work.  I got back home at 12:30PM.  After getting back home I went to sleep and slept until 5PM.  I got up and had dinner with my family.  We then watched TV for a while before going back to bed.

 

Thursday, February 04, 1971

 

                This evening after returning home from work, I attended an Adult Aaronic Priesthood Seminar.  After returning home Mae and I talked for some time.  She is sure a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

Saturday, February 06, 1971

 

                I worked at Wayne Wademan’s today, finishing their basement for a shop.  I was tired when I got home.

 

Sunday, February 07, 1971

 

                It was a very busy day at Church, as Kent and Shirley had gone out of town for the weekend.  That left me in charge of things.  Bishop Murdock had an appointment with Leroy Webb and Jean Sessions to interview them prior to their marriage.  Bishop had told me that he would be back in time for that interview, however by the time they were to be at President Haslam’s office for his interview, he was not back.  I therefore had to hold those interviews and fill out there Temple Recommends.

 

Monday, February 08, 1971

 

                When I got home from work, Mae had dinner ready.  We then held our Family Home Evening. It was a nice evening.

 

Tuesday, February 09. 1971

 

                Work went well today.  After getting home and having dinner, I attended M.I.A. Opening program.  Bishop Murdock and Brother Ross met me at the Church.  From there we went to the Stake House for our Bishopric Training class.  It has been a very good evening.

 

Wednesday, February 10, 1971

 

                Mae and I attended a Married Fireside this evening.  Sister Goff, a daughter of Paul Dunn, and her husband, Brother Goff spoke to us.  They mainly reminisced about her father and their feelings of being in a family of a General Authority.  The first time I remember meeting Elder Dunn was at the second Stake Conference after Larry Braithwaite and I were called to serve as Counselors in the Bishopric of the Mound Fort Ward. 

                (I will insert that experience here.)

                Mae and I moved into Ogden in the fall of 1961.  We purchased a 2 bedroom home at 233 W 13th Street.  The first Sunday after moving there, I attended Priesthood meeting at the Mound Fort Ward.  When I entered the meeting house, I felt quite like a stranger.  No one welcomed me nor did I feel welcome at the meeting.  After Priesthood meeting I went home and got Mae so we could attend Sunday School.  Again we did not feel welcome but felt ignored.  In the evening when we returned for Sacrament meeting we left with the same feelings.  We were not used, to this feeling, as we loved our Ward in Sunset.  The next week we received the same feeling as before.  On the Third Sunday as I left for Priesthood Meeting I told Mae that if I received the same treatment when I went to Priesthood, that when I came home to get her, we would find another Ward to attend.  Well, when I walked through the Church house doors, I was met by the Bishopric, who Welcomed and introduced me to everyone there.  It has always been a wonder to both Mae and I as to what happened the first two Sundays.  Shortly after, I was given a calling to serve as an assistant to Brother Reed, the Aaronic Priesthood General Secretary.  The first two weeks has always been an amazement to both of us, for we found out that the Mound Fort Ward was always been recognized as one of the most Friendly Wards in the area.

                I served in the calling as Assistant to Brother Reed until the following spring, when the Reed’s moved to the Stake Farm where he became the Farm manager.  I then served another couple of years as the Secretary to the Aaronic Priesthood.  I was then called to be the Elders Quorum President.  This came as quite a shock to me as I had only attended the Elders Quorum the first three Sundays, when we had moved into the Ward. 

                During those three years, Sandy was born, I changed jobs from I.R.S., going to Hill Air Force Base and Then to Commercial Security Bank.  At the bank I rotated shifts from Morning shift, starting at 5AM, to Evening shift which started at 5PM, both shifts ended when all the work had been processed.  The calling of General Secretary of the Aaronic Priesthood had really fit well with my work schedule.  Then one day I received a call asking me and my wife to meet the next evening with the Stake President.  After a short visit President Wimmer extended a calling to me to be the Elders Quorum President.  I explained my unusual work rotating schedule, plus the fact that having been with the Aaronic Priesthood program I really didn’t know any of the Elders.  He still felt that the calling to serve in that position was appropriate and counseled that I should visit with my Bishop in choosing my Councilors.   I accepted the calling, and with Bishop Deamer’s advice, I called two Brothers to serve with me.   Well, over the next several months our Quorum went from low to bad to worse and finally was the poorest attendance in the Stake.  I was very discouraged with my calling and my rotating shift.  Then my 1st Councilor and his wife bought a new home in Roy so they would be moving.  My 2nd Councilor’s job required him to be out of town 5 days a week, so he suggested that I replace him at this time when I called for a replacement of my 1st Councilor.  By this time I had become acquainted with most of the men in our Quorum and so I recommended Bob Hein and Larry Braithwaite to be called as my Councilors.  Bob and Larry would meet with me whenever I had the time for a Presidency meeting and then they would carry out the assignments.  With their help, things changed and our quorum was soon at the head of the Stake.  My work schedule also changed and I received the position as the Data Processing Supervisor.  Although this position was still very demanding in hours, it fell in line with most working people’s schedule.  Also, during that time Bishop Deamer was released, after serving five years as Bishop, and Delmar Moulton was called as the new Bishop.  After a year, both of Bishop Moulton’s counselors moved from our Ward.  I was then called to serve as 1st Counselor and Larry was called to serve as 2nd Counselor in the Bishopric.  The following Sunday we were sustained during our Sacrament meeting, and following the meeting we went right to work, even though we were not set apart.  The next Sunday was our Stake Quarterly Conference and it would be presided over by Elder Sterling W. Sill of the Quorum of 12 Apostles .  The morning meeting of Conference went very well.  Following that Session of Conference, all Bishopric members and all High Counselors plus the Stake Presidents were asked to meet with Elder Sill.  In that meeting, which lasted over an hour, Elder Sill chewed us out severely!   It took me several years of pondering that meeting before I understood what he meant, but the sting of his message really hurt.  When we left the meeting Larry went to the Stake President and asked to be released, but President Wimmer told Larry he needed to get in line and that he, as Stake President was first.  The following Tuesday, President Wimmer had Bishop Moulton, myself and Larry come to his office.  At that time Larry and I were set apart as counselors in the Mound Fort Bishopric.  President Wimmer explained that knowing that a member of the Quorum of the Twelve would be presiding at our Stake Conference, he would have us wait for a week and then we would be set apart by a member of the Quorum of the Twelve.  However, Elder Sill told him that this was his responsibility, so we were then set apart in our new callings.  Our Stake may have been low at that time, but over the next three months it went clear to the bottom.  Also, at that period of time, a General Authority would only be assigned to attend Stake Conference every other quarter.  As our Stake gathered for the next Stake Quarterly Conference and the meeting was about to start, Elder Paul Dunn, assistant to the Twelve entered and walked up to the stand.  President Wimmer altered the planned program and gave Elder Dunn time to address our Stake.  After the morning session, again all Bishopric members and all High Counselors plus the Stake Presidents were asked to meet with Elder Dunn.  I don’t remember what was said during that meeting, but I will never forget the Spirit that came from the meeting.  We came out of the meeting ready to take on the whole world if that was what the Lord wanted.  Then following a spiritual afternoon Conference Session, just as the person that had been asked to give the closing prayer was coming to the stand, President stepped to the podium and asked all Bishopric members and all High Counselors plus the Stake Presidents to meet in the Cry room for just 5 to 10 minutes.  As we entered into the Cry Room, Elder Dunn met and shook hands with each of us.  He then complemented all of us and only said how privileged he felt to be able to meet with us.  When he dismissed us to take our families home, there wasn’t a dry eye in the room.  That was one of the most spiritual 10 minutes I have ever felt.  From that meeting our Stake just went up in everything.

 

Thursday, February 11, 1971

 

                After dinner this evening we took all our family and went to Salt Lake to see Linda Pool, Linda is my niece.  She is in the hospital again with heart problems.  We also stopped at the Viewing of Shirley Murdock’s Grandmother, who passed away the other day.  In the back of our home had been a large stack of old wooden pallets and somehow this evening the pile caught fire and burned.  The Fire Department came but as there was no other structure in danger, they just stayed and kept the fire under control.

                I would like to add a little history about Linda here because I don’t think that anyone has written anything about her history.  Linda was born in Rock Springs, Wyoming on March 10 1953.  My family had just moved from Idaho to Utah the summer of 1952.  Her parents were Margaret (my half sister) and Dodger Davis.  When she was born she had a hole in her heart.  The hospital in Rock Springs quickly realized that they could not do anything for her condition, so they had Margaret and Dodge take her to the Primary Children Hospital in Salt Lake City, Utah.  She was there for only a few days when the Doctors said that they had just as well take her home.  On the way home Margaret and Doge stopped at our place in Ogden.  The baby was quite blue and not doing well.  Mom called our Bishop, who brought with him his 1st Counselor.  They gave Linda a blessing.  After the blessing Mom told the Bishop that the Doctors did not think the baby had any chance to live.  I remember hearing the Bishop tell Mom that there was a power much greater than the doctors and he was sure that the baby would live.  Over the months Linda continued to grow, although she was often into the Doctors.  If she caught a cold or any other minor infection it was an emergency trip to the hospital.   Each time the Doctors gave the same diagnostics that her heart could not continue to work and that there was not any way to repair it.  The Doctor predicted that she would never reach the age of 1 and then when she did, Margaret and Dodge again brought her to the Primary Children Hospital in S.L.  When they got there the nurse could not find Linda’s record.  The nurse then looked elsewhere and when she opened the file she gasped.  Linda’s file had been moved to the death records.  I always remember that Linda’s lips were blue from lack of blood circulation but she continued to grow into a beautiful girl, young woman and then a young lady.  There were many priesthood blessing given and when I received the Melchizedek Priesthood I often was called to give those Blessings. Linda seemed to have a special Spirit and Faith in our Savior.  Although her family was not active in the Gospel, Linda always attended her meetings from Primary, Young Women and then she married a returned missionary.  Sadly that marriage didn’t last very long before her husband started stepping out on her.  That was a great blow to Linda and for a while she stopped her Church affiliation.  She married Jerry Pool and shortly after their marriage, she wanted to return to activity.  She met with her Bishop and with his guidance and then with Jerry’s approval she again put her temple garments back on.  Although Linda could not have a child of her own, she and Jerry adopted a baby girl who they named Kimberly.  Jerry, Linda and Kimberly were living in a trailer park in Salt Lake.  When Kimberly was about 3 years old, Jerry and Linda decided to move to an apartment.  While Linda was packing to move and Jerry was at work, Linda took sick and asked Kimberly to run to a friend to tell her that momma needed her.  Linda passed away before they could get back.  I had the privilege of conducting Linda’s funeral.

 

Friday, February 12, 1971

 

                Mae and I were able to go to the Salt Lake Temple to witness the marriage of Leroy Webb and Jean Sessions.  When we got to the Temple I was told that I had made an error on Leroy’s Temple recommend but the Temple President over looked it as that was my first Temple marriage recommend I had every done.  I had indicated that this was Leroy’s first Temple Recommend, but Leroy had served a mission and there for had gone through the Temple for his own Endowments before leaving on his mission call.   President Hasslem hadn’t caught my error when they were interviewed by him.  We were also invited and attended their Wedding Breakfast.  When we returned home we took our family into Ogden.  We then went up to Browning Arms in Morgan and used the phone to call and visit with Larry and Tammy.

 

Saturday, February 13, 1971

 

                As a Bishopric we attended a regional meeting today.  Bob and Pauline Hien came to our place for dinner this evening.  The four of us then went to our Stake Sweetheart Ball.  We had a very enjoyable evening.  Mae is sure a special Sweetheart and wife.

 

Sunday, February 14, 1971

 

                I didn’t feel well today so I didn’t make it to all my meetings.

 

Monday, February 15, 1971

 

                I had the day off work because it is the new President’s Day.  I am very disappointed that our country feels that it is more important to have a Martin King day than to celebrate both George Washington and Abraham Lincoln’s Birthdays.  I spent most of the day in bed with a cold.  We held Family Home Evening this evening and Sandy gave the lesson.  She did a very good job.

 

Tuesday, February 16, 1971

 

                I called Larry and Tammy after work, mainly checking on work.  Mom Browning was at our place when I got home.  Mom told us that Linda was doing ok now.  I attended M.I.A. and Bishopric training this evening.  When I got home from, Irven and Linda were at our home.

 

Wednesday, February 17, 1971

 

                After getting home from work and eating dinner I went up to the Church at 7:15PM and got home about 12:15AM.  When we left the Bishops office for tonight I still did not know who we should call to fill the Y.W.M.I.A. program.  (I will make an addition to this entry.)  It was not unusual for us to be in Bishopric meeting that late.  Whether it was tonight or another evening that this incident occurred but I will add this here. On a Bishopric meeting, it was getting very late when Bishop Murdock asked me to call someone.  I looked at the clock and it was after 11PM.  I reminded Bishop of the time and asked if I should call this late.  He just looked at me and responded, “We are still up, so call”.  I just rang the number and handed the receiver to him.  When the person answered he did apologize for it being so late but he needed to speak to them.

 

Thursday, February 18, 1971

                Mae and I went out to a show this evening.  About half way thru the show we got up and left due to language and ludness.

 

Saturday, February 20, 1971

Sunday, February 21, 1971

 

                We had our Ward Conference today.  It made a busy day but all went well.

 

Monday, February 22, 1971

Saturday, February 27, 1971

Monday, March 1, 1971

 

                When u came home from work and had dinner, we held our Family Home Evening.  I sure enjoyed being home and playing with the children.

                This evening I attended M.I.A. and then met as a Bishopric and took care some business there.  Mae came up to the Bishop’s office and we set her apart as the “Spiritual Living Teacher” in the Relief Society.  As a Bishopric, at 8:30PM we attended the Stake Training for Bishopric’s.

 

Wednesday, March 3, 1971

 

                After getting home from work and having dinner I went up to the Chapel.  Bishop Murdock had gone to Provo, this evening, to see their new grandson.  Brother Ross and I carried on and held Oral Evaluation with the Priesthood Leaders.  I got home about mid-night.

 

Thursday, March 4, 1971

 

                This evening I went to the Church at 6:15PM to conduct two interviews.  Bishop Murdock came to the office about 8:30 PM.  It was about 11PM when I got home.

                Sandy was sick this morning and stayed home from school.  At work I went into Roger’s office and talked to him about possibly getting a raise.  He said that he would see what he could do.  This is the first time I have ever had to go talk to my boss about needing a raise.  This evening Bishop Murdock and I went visiting.

 

Saturday, March 6, 1971

 

                Sandy is feeling some better this morning.  I worked 7 hours for Wayne Wadsman today.  When I finished I came home and showered.  I spent the rest of the evening with my family.

 

Sunday, March 7, 1971

 

                Last night Shellie came down with the same thing Sandy had.  She has been sick all day.  Mae and I were involved with Church work until 6PM today.  We had a nice Fast and testimony meeting this afternoon.  Bishop Murdock and I were going to go visiting this evening but Bishop Murdock wasn’t feeling very well so we stayed home.

 

Monday, March 8, 1971

 

Shellie is still quite sick so Mae got some penicillin for her.  This evening we held Family Home Evening and had a nice time.  Sandy had made customs for Shellie and Mike and they put on a parade for us.  Sandy’s rabbit had a litter of kits today so that was fun for the children to see them.

Saturday, March 20, 1971

 

                This morning we drove as far as Page Arizona.  We stopped there and took a tour through the Glen Canyon Dam.  From there we traveled on to Mesa Arizona.  One of the fellows that works with me at Browning Arm’s gave me directions when we got to Phoenix.  He said that we would come into Phoenix from the north by way of the Black Canyon freeway.  The freeway would turn east and we were to continue until it turned south and then take the next exit.  As we were going east the highway turned south as it crossed the Salt River and then it turned back east.  He hadn’t said anything about that dog leg so when it turned south and then east again we took the next exit.  Mistake!  We found our self going north in an unimproved area that we later found was the east end of the Phoenix airport.  I drove until we found city again and I stopped at the first service station.  I asked the attendant which way we would go to Mesa.  He looked one way, then back the opposite direction and then back again.  Finally he said I think is that way.  I drove to the next station and again got direction from someone that seemed surer.  We drove a little longer where we found a phone and I called Larry.  He had us stay where we were and he drove down and got us.  It was about 9:30PM when we got to their place.  As soon as we got to Larry’s home and my family in, lary asked me if I wanted to go with him to pick out a St. Bernard pup.  We drove over to Phoenix where Larry had already called to reserve the pup.  The female and her litter were in the front room.  Larry picked the one he wanted and paid for it.  the wife then said to her husband that Larry might want to see the father.  He said ok, I will bring her in, but for Larry and I to stand back.  When the dog entered the house he was dragging the man.  The man would brace his shoulder against the door frame to hold the dog back, then quickly move to the next door again holding the dog with all the strength he had.  Boy! I was for getting out of the house.  That dog looked like he would eat us up.  After returning home Larry and I took Mom Porter over to Uncle Van and Aunt Allison’s.  It was much later after visiting before we got to bed.

Tuesday, March 23, 1971

Thursday, March 25, 1971

Friday, March 26, 1971

Wednesday, March 31, 1971

Thursday, April 01, 1971

 

                After work I drove up to Liberty where I helped Larry and Tammy with some repairs to their house.  When I got home Mae said that Ron Shumway had called and wanted me to call him.  He wants me to come back down for an interview and to take a physical.  I had to think before answering as I know that I didn’t have any more leave time.  I told him that I would leave Saturday morning and would be there on Monday morning.  He said that I didn’t need to drive but if I would go to the Air West ticket counter in Salt Lake Airport he would have tickets waiting there for me.  He also told me that if things went as expected they would probably want me to start on work April 19th.

                In as much as I don’t know the actual date that this incident took place I will insert it here:

One of my co-workers was talking to me about my traveling home each weekend, asked about the travel time.  I remarked that it took 2 ½ hours to fly to Salt Lake but only ½ hours to fly back to Phoenix.  He questioned my statement so I pulled out my last flight itinerary which I just happened to have with me.  The itinerary showed the hour flight time difference.  Puzzled he asked why for the difference in time.  I explained that with Salt Lake being almost due north of Phoenix it was all uphill going but coming down south it was all downhill and the plane could really pickup speed. 

 

Monday, April 19, 1971  Started working for Hugh’s Air West

 

                Today is my first day of work for Hugh’s Air West airline.  The office is located at the Sky Harbor Airport in Phoenix Arizona.  This office contains the Airline Planning division, the airline routing and scheduling of aircraft maintances, the main aircraft maintances center for the Airline and the main Data Processing center.  I will be working in the Data Processing center as a programmer.  Ronald Shumway it the manager of the Data Processing divisions.  He is over both the Phoenix Data Processing Center which consists of the Programming staff, the computer operations staff and the data input staff; and the San Francisco centers, which mainly consists of a Data Input staff.  Under Ron in the Programming staff are three Senior Programmers Analyst, each with 1 to 2 Junior Programmers Analyst, Senior Programmers and Programmers.  I started as a Programmer.  My Jr. Programmer Analyst is Scott Hanselman and my Senior Programmer Analyst is Scott Fry.  It looks like my job will be challenging as well as interesting.  This evening I enjoyed being with Larry and Tammy and their family as they held Family Home evening.  I really do miss Mae and my children.

Monday, May 10, 1971

 

                I had made arrangements for Mae to fly to Phoenix with me, as well as I took the day off work.  Mae sure enjoyed the trip.  Larry and Tammy let us use their car today and we went looking at homes.  We looked at two homes, one that we really liked.  The stopping point is money.  I am afraid that we are going to have to just rent for a year or two until we can get better financially. 

                I didn’t record the actual date that Ron came and talked to me about our housing search.  I explained our financial problem.  The next day he called me into his office and offered to loan us a $1,000.00 if that would help us find a home.  Ron is a special person and has been so good to me.

 

Tuesday, May 11, 1971

 

                Mae and Tammy looked at homes most of the day.  When I got home from work she and I went out again.  We weren’t able to find anything.

 

Wednesday, May 12, 1971

 

                I went into work early this morning.  Mae, with the use of Tammy’s car, which they have made available whenever we need it, again went out looking at homes.  After I got home from work, I went with Mae to look at some of the homes she had seen.  She showed me a home at 233 West Hunter, in Lehi.  This area is south of the river where the early Mormon Pioneers first settled.  The settlement later expanded further south up on the mesa and the settlement then incorporated into a township and the town was given the official name of Mesa Arizona.  I had actually seen the house previously but at that time we didn’t have the down payment.  With the loan from Ron, we could now make the needed down payment.  At 9PM we signed the paper work on the house.

 

Thursday, May 13, 1971

 

                I went into work this morning.  Mae was going to fly home this morning on the 11:30AM flight but when she got to the airport, she had forgotten to bring her tick.  She went back to Larry and Tammy’s and then took the 5:30PM flight to Salt Lake.  This evening I went with Larry to fill an assignment to peel potatoes for an Aaronic Priesthood outing.

 

Friday, May 14, 1971

 

                I worked all day and then caught the 5:30PM flight to Salt Lake.  Often when I am flying home, I will ride to work with Ron and then I just catch a shuttle bus over to the airport terminal.  Mae and the children met me at the Airport in Salt Lake.  It is always good to be home with them.

 

Saturday, May 15, 1971

 

                We slept in this morning.  After breakfast I went up to Mom and Dad Porters and finished installing their air conditioner.  I repaired the lawnmower and then mowed our lawn.  This evening we took the family to the park where we ate dinner and played games.

 

Sunday, May 16, 1971

 

                We attended meeting as a family today.  This evening Mae and I went over to visit with Bishop and Sister Moulton.  Delmar is recovering for being operated on for 4 hernias.  We sure think a lot of those two special people.  Mae took me to the airport so I could fly back to Phoenix on the 10:10PM flight.

 

Monday, May 17, 1971

 

                Work went well today.  This evening I joined Larry and his family in holding Family Home Evening.  After the Family Home Evening lesson was given, I went to my room I wrote a letter to Mae and my children.  I then read for a little while before going to bed.

 

 

 

 

Tuesday, May 18, 1971

 

                The Credit Union approved a personal loan of $1,000 in my name with Ron Shumway’s co-signature.  I really didn’t do much this evening except watch TV.  I feel a little lost with having so much spare time on my hands.  I did call my sweetheart and talk to her, even though it is long distance.

 

Wednesday, May 19, 1971

 

                I took off work early so that I could get my Arizona Drivers License.  This evening Jeff Bushnell was graduating from Seminary, so I joined with Van and Allison at the graduation exercises.  Following the event Uncle Van stopped and bought all of us ice cream.  Uncle Van then went with me to see the home we are buying.

 

Thursday, May 20, 1971

 

                It was a good day at work and things went well.  This evening I joined Van and Allison at a Temple Session.  After the session we stopped at their Chapel, where we joined with other Ward members in having ice cream and pie.  It was a very enjoyable Temple Session and very pleasant evening with Van and Allison.

 

Friday, May 21, 1971

 

                I rode into work with Ron Shumway this morning.  This way I could leave to fly home by taking a shuttle bus to the terminal.  During the day I checked on the 5PM flight and it was ok.  I went from work to the terminal and caught the 5PM flight to Salt Lake.  Mae and the children met me at the Salt Lake airport.  It sure was great to get home.  Later this evening Mae and I drove over and got a ice cream Sunday.  I sure love that girl!

 

Saturday, May 22, 1971

 

                We slept in until almost 9AM.  Then Mae prepared a very nice breakfast for us.  It is sure good to be with my family.  I started working on our car after breakfast and finished with it about 5PM.  The Bishopric sponsored a steak dinner and party for Mae and I at Brother and Sister John Rose home.  (I wanted to insert all the names of those that were in attendance that evening but it has been too many years ago and I can’t remember the names).  There was, Bishop Kent and Shirley Murdock; First Counselor John and Amy Ross; the new Second Counselor and his wife; our Ward clerk, Brother and Sister Smith; our Ward Finance clerk, Brother McMillan and his wife Beverly; our Ward Executive Secretary Dwayne Jessop and his wife.  It has been a great experience working with these great people in the Clearfield First Ward.

 

Sunday, May 23, 1971

 

                After Sunday school were invited Tom and Kathy Gun over for dinner.  This afternoon Tom and I went to a Stake Priesthood meeting.  This evening Mom Porter and Mae took me to the Salt Lake Airport where I flew back to Phoenix.  I have sure enjoyed this week end and I am glad that there is only one more week to go until we all move to Arizona.

 

Monday, May 24, 1971

 

                Work went well today.  After work I went looking for an apartment as we will still be a month before we can move into our home.  I was sure tired when I quite but I was able to find a two bedroom unit about three blocks east and a block north of Larry and Tammy’s home.  It has a swimming pool just out front of the apartment.  It will cost us $200.00 for the month plus I have to put the power and water in my name.  It will be a little cramped but I think we can manage for a month.

 

 Tuesday, May 25, 1971

 

                I went over to the apartment before work and put a deposit on it.  I was told then that I needed to schedule the utilities to be turned on and put them in my name.  After getting to work, I called the Salt River Power Co. and asked them to turn the power on as we would be moving in the evening of May 31st.  The lady that I talked to in Customer Service assured me that it would be turned on for us.  I then reminded her that Monday 31st was Memorial Day and she again assured me that it would be turned on when we got there.  This evening Larry and Tammy asked me if I was going to be at home this evening.  I told them that I was.  They were going out for the evening and just wanted me to be there as Jody, their youngest daughter was going to be there and had a friend coming over.  Well as it turned out it was a Boy Friend and even though I was there, they got necking quite heavily.  Even when I went thru the room that didn’t seem so slow them down much.  When Larry and Tammy got home I talked to Larry about it but it didn’t seem to worry him.  I couldn’t help but wonder about the different ways people raise their children.

 

Wednesday, May 26, 1971

 

                Bob flew in from Alabama and I met him at the Air Port.  Bob is getting ready to retire from the Air Force and wants to see what the job and housing market is like in the Phoenix area.  I had talked with Larry and Tammy about having him stay a few days at their place.  They were very gracious in letting him stay there.  I took Bob over to the office with me and he just waited until I finished up for the day.  When we got to Larry and Tammy’s, they invited Bob and I to go out to eat with them.  They also invited Melvin (Larry’s brother), as well as their children, to go eat.  We went to the Lost Dutchman’s restraint.  Tammy had also arranged for some of Larry’s framing crew to meet us there.  This was all in honor of Larry’s Birthday.  It was a very enjoyable evening.

 

Thursday, May 27, 1971

 

                Bob and I both woke at 4:15AM.  We just laid in bed and visited.  I went into work at 6AM and then left work in time to get to the terminal in time to catch the 10:45AM flight to Salt Lake.  Mae met me at the airport.  On the way home from the airport, the battery shorted out in the car.  A Good Samaritan stopped to see what the problem and then towed us on home.

 

Friday, May 28, 1971

 

                We spent the day getting thing ready to load for moving.  I also got parts and repaired the car.  Mae has already got most everything packed into containers.  This afternoon I rented a Rider moving van and a hitch to toe the station wagon behind the van.  Shortly after I got back with the van, we had two ladies knock on our door. It seems that most of the time that I have been in Arizona, on Friday’s these two Jehovah Witness ladies have been coming to visit Mae.  Of course Mae has been very friendly and polite.  Well! They came again today and when they saw the moving van I think they were afraid that their fish was going to get away.  One started talking to Mae in the front room and wanted her to purchase her book.  Mae told her she would pay her for her book on condition that she would accept, at no charge, a book from her.  She refused to that agreement.  The other came into the kitchen where I was working.  I tried to be polite but if I tried to defend what she was trying to push down my thought, she would just shut it off by saying that they didn’t argue and change to something else.  I tried to get her to just open up the Bible and read, but again the same answer “we don’t argue” and change the subject.  After a half hour or more of this and I finally told them that we were busy and quite firmly invited them to leave. 

                Later this afternoon Larry Sanders came with a Model T Ford, which he and his father had restored.  Sometime ago the Ward had an auction to raise funds for the Ward Budget.  Mae had bid on and won an evening out in the Rumble Seat of a Model T Ford.  I also indicated that Mae and I went out to a show this evening.

 

Saturday, May 29, 1971

 

                We spent the day packing the moving van.  Tom and Kelly Gunn came down and helped us all day packing the van.  Bryce and Deanna also came and helped along with Mom Porter.  At noon Mom took the children to her place and also fixed us some lunch.  I had a lot of lumber that I had collected and stored in the garage, along with quite a few nails.  I called a brother in our Ward that is a carpenter and told him that if he would clean it all out of the garage, he could have it.  He was quite excited to get it.  It was after mid-night before we completed the packing all our belongings into the truck and also packed the station wagon full.  I got the station wagon connected to the truck.  I disconnected the drive shaft by disconnecting the rear u-joints.  I wrapped the u-joint in a rag and tied the drive shaft up to the frame. However when I started to pull the station wagon, the weight on the station had lowered the drive line and the u-joint hooked and through the bearings. It was too late to buy another set of u-joints at this point. I decided to wait until we get to Phoenix to purchase a new set.  Mom and Dad Porter had all my family stay at their place tonight.  Our plan is that Mae will fly back on Tuesday.  She and the children will then fly back to Phoenix on Wednesday.

 

Sunday, May 30, 1971

 

                I got up about 7AM and retied the drive shaft up higher.  Mom Porter fixed us some breakfast.  Mae and I left about 9:30AM.  Mom Porter had talked to us about going thru Las Vegas and then crossing the Hoover Dam, continuing from there to Phoenix.  She said that by going that way we would not have any mountains to cross.  We debated those directions for some time until we got to where Hwy 20 crosses over the mountains to UT Hwy 89.  Still undecided we stayed on I-15 to Las Vegas.  At 9PM we arrived at Glendale Nevada, having driven about 500 miles.  We got a motel in Glendale for the night.  We figuring that we would only have another 300 miles to Phoenix and should arrive there fairly early in the afternoon.  Another thing which we received advice on, Tammy had told us that we should put all plants in the front seat of the truck so that when we went through the Arizona State Inspection station, they could easily check the plants and not have to open up the van to go through it.

 

Monday, May 31, 1971

 

                We got up at 5AM and got under way.  We figured that we would be able to get to Phoenix by early afternoon.  Well that was a mistake.  From the time we crossed Hoover Dam it was rolling hills the rest of the way to Phoenix.  Going downhill we could get up 20 or even sometimes 30 MPH at top speed and then going uphill we would have to gear down to 1st gear and be going so slow that the speed-o-meter needle would just bounce of the bottom peg.  We held traffic up for miles as the road was just a two lane highway.  Often times, when the road shoulder would let me, I would pull as far to the right as I could and let traffic pass.  Often people would get upset and pass us when there was a solid line in our lane.  Before long a Highway Patrol would pull those drivers over and give them tickets.  Before long we would see him coming up behind until he worked his way past us and then remain hidden in front of us until some would pass on the solid line.  Then his light would go on and pull them over.  We must have seen over two dozen drivers get tickets this way.  I also said that Tammy had advised us to carry all of our house plants in the truck cab with us, so that when we went through the State inspection station they wouldn’t make us open up the van for inspection.  Having all of Mae’s house plants in front with us, made it quite close and uncomfortable ridding.  Well! When we got to the Inspection Station, they just waved us through without checking us at all.  My supervisor, Scott Fry lived in Tempe, had invited us to store what furniture we didn’t need, while in the apartment, in ½ of his double garage.  We stopped there about 6PM and unloaded those items.  We then drove to Larry and Tammy’s where Mae stayed there and Larry road with me.  We drove to the apartment at 431 N. Williams #B in Mesa.  When we opened the door to the apartment and turned on the light switch, there was no power.  By this time it was dark so by the street light Larry and I carried our belongings into the front room and just placed them in the center of the room.  We locked the door and went back to Larry’s where we stayed the night.

 

Tuesday, June 1, 1971

 

                I got up early and disconnected the station wagon in front of Larry’s house.  I then returned the rented truck and tow hitch to the Rider Rental in Mesa.  Tammy let us use her car again today.  Mae and I went over to the apartment.  With it being light, we put a few items in the rooms where they would go.  Mae took a cold shower and then I took her to the Airport to fly back and get our children.  As there wasn’t any power, there wasn’t much I could do so I went to the office and worked.  I called the power co. and asked about the power.  The lady on the other end of the phone just explained that Monday was Memorial Day so they couldn’t have anyone turn on the power on a holiday.  After work I stopped and bought a new set of U-joints for the car.  I went to Larry’s and installed the U-joint, then drove over to the rear of the apartment.  When I pulled up to the rear of the apartment, all I could see was a black burned out hole.  I walked around to the front and the Manager’s son met me.  He estimated that the power co. had came somewhere around 11:30AM and turned the power on, without going inside to check anything.  They believed that a burner on the stove had been left on.  The burner must have heated up and caused a box of kitchen items that I had set on the cupboard to catch fire.  The renters in the upstairs smelt smoke and called the fire department.  The fire was contained in the kitchen but did smoke damage to the items we had brought into the apartment.  The Landlord was so upset that the Dr. had given him a sedative and had him lay down.  They had another apartment for us to move into and helped me carry our items to that apartment.  I then went back to Larry and Tammy’s for the night.  I didn’t call Mae because I felt that it would just upset her and there was nothing she could do from Utah anyway.

 

Wednesday, June 2, 1971

 

                I made some calls and none of our items were covered with any insurance.  Had I taken content coverage when I rented the truck the items would have been covered, but I didn’t, trying to save a little.  I guess I learned the hard way.  I picked Mae and the children up at the airport at 11:45AM.  I told Mae what happened.  We took the children over to Tammy’s where she tended them while Mae and I went to the apartment.  It was quite a mess.  We sorted through the clothes and took what we could to a laundry-mat where we washed them.  We worked until after 9PM cleaning things and moving what we could to the new apartment.  It looks like we lost over $200 worth of food and kitchen items.  We were lucky that we had most of our furniture stored in Scott Fry’s garage. 

 

Thursday, June 3, 1971

 

                I went into work this morning.  When I got home Mae had the apartment looking quite nice.  We went over to Skagg’s and spent $70 to replace kitchen items that was burned, such as silver ware and other utensils, salt and pepper shakers, plates and glasses and such.  Larry and Tammy came over this evening and brought us a new clock radio.  They have sure been good to us.

 

Friday, June 4, 1971

 

                Work went well and as I was about to go home for the day, Jerry Jones called me aside for a minute.  He gave me an envelope containing $115.00.  All my co-workers had chipped in trying to help us in our loss.  I was almost in tears of gradated for their thought fullness and generosity.  After getting home I took the children out swimming.  The swimming pool is almost out front of our front door.  Mae and I then went shopping and the money that we had received sure helped.

 

Saturday, June 5, 1971

 

                Larry and Tammy invited us to go with them to a movie this evening.  Tomorrow is their 12th Wedding Anniversary.  We had a nice evening with them.

 

Sunday, June 6. 1971

 

                We probably attended meetings at Larry and Tammy Ward this morning.  We invited Larry and Tammy and their family over to our place for dinner.  Bob and his Family moved to Phoenix today.  They rented a furnished home on the west side of Phoenix as he has found work at a Smitties bakery in that area.  Bob drove over to see us for a few minutes.  I believe that they are staying in a motel for a few days before they can move into the home.

 

Monday, June 7, 1971

 

                The programming staff moved from the room downstairs to a much larger area on the second floor in the South East corner.  Ron also moved his office up there.  There were three smaller offices and then one large room that was sectioned off with portable portions so that each programmer had his own area.  Jerry Hanselman, a new programmer that was just promoted from an operator to programmer, and I were given the smallest office.  With two desks and a two drawer filling cabinet, we just had room to get in and close the door.  Although it is small, I quite like the arrangement.  Jerry and I not only were close physically but we soon became very good friends.  Bob, Carroll and their children came over and stayed most of the day with Mae and our children.  With the swimming pool, it gave the children something to do.  When I got home we visited for a while before they went back.  After getting our children to bed, Mae and I went out swimming about 10PM.

 

Tuesday, June 8, 1971

 

                After getting home from work this afternoon, we spent the evening together as a Family.  Sandy and I went swimming for, while Mae and the rest of the children popped corn.  All of us then watched a good show on TV.  It sure was a nice evening.

 

Wednesday, June 9, 1971

 

                Again this evening after I got home from work we all went swimming.  After getting the children to bed, Mae and I had planned on going for a walk.  However we couldn’t get David to settle down so we had to forgo the walk and stayed home with the children.

 

Thursday, June 10, 1971

 

                I worked late tonight.  After getting home I ate some dinner.  Then Mae, the children and I walked over to Larry and Tammy’s just to visit.  I had called our realtor Earl Taylor and he informed me that our loan on the house had been approved for $18,000.

 

Friday, June 11, 1971

 

                When I got home from work I wasn’t feeling very well.  Before leaving work Jerry Jones gave me another $10.00 that had been turned into him from one of my co-workers.  That makes a total of $125.00 that was given me to help out with our fire losses.  I went to bed early tonight.

 

Saturday, June 12, 1971

 

                I went into work this morning and was able to finish one of my programs.  I was there for 3 ½ hours.  When I got home, Mae and I went shopping.  This evening we watched a show on TV as a family.  After the show and getting the children down to bed Mae and I walked over and got ice cream Sunday and a milk shake.  There is a Jack-in-the-Box hamburger and ice cream shop about a block away from our apartment.

 

Sunday, June 13, 1971

 

                I missed Priesthood meeting this morning as I forgot what time it started.  We did attend Sunday school at the Mesa 15th Ward this morning, but this evening we attended Sacrament Meeting at the Lehi 2nd Ward.  This will be our new Ward when we get moved over to our home.  After the meeting we took the children over to let them see the home we are buying.  We also drove around the neighborhood and over to the Elementary school where Sandy will be attending.

 

Monday, June 14, 1971

 

                While I was at work today, Mae went into the Arizona Drivers License Department and took her Driver’s test and got her new Drivers License.  When I got home from work all our family went in swimming.  After a while we got out and then we went to Pioneer Park as Mae had a picnic lunch ready.  We ate and let the children play.  It has sure been a wonderful evening and we brought all the family home tired and ready for bed.  Mae is sure a wonderful wife and Mother.

 

Tuesday, June 15, 1971

 

                I worked until 6PM this evening.  After dinner and the dishes were done, we all went in swimming.  We are going to miss not having the swimming pool right out our front door, when we move.  After getting the children to bed, Mae and I watched some TV and read before we went to bed.

 

Wednesday, June 16, 1971

 

                I had a good day at work today.  After getting home we had dinner and then Mae went to the Laundromat to get our laundry done as we don’t have those facilities here at the apartment.  I stayed with the children and we watched TV.

 

Thursday, June 17, 1971

 

                When I got home from work we ate dinner.  We then went over to the store for some milk and eggs.  From there we went over to see Aunt Allison.  Uncle Van was up to Utah with the National Guard.  He is making the National Guard a second carrier.  His unit is in Cedar City where he had originally enlisted.

 

 

Friday, June 18, 1971

 

                When I got home from work, Mae and I were going to go to the Temple.  After leaving home Mae realized that she didn’t have her Temple Recommend.  We looked but couldn’t find it so we stopped at the store for milk and then went over to visit Ron and Audrey Shumway. 

 

Saturday, June 19, 1971 - 12th Wedding Anniversary

 

                We took our family shopping for Birthday Presents for Shellie.  I then went into work for four hours.  Shortly after getting back home, Bob and Carroll came over for a few minutes.  I don’t think they are enjoying living here in Arizona.  The heat is too much of an adjustment and so they feel locked up in the house with little to do.  The tempter is now well over 100 degrees even during the night time.   It is also too bad that they live so far away from us and I don’t think they are becoming acquainted very fast in their area.  This evening Mae and I went out to eat at the Pinnacle Peak restraint with Ron and Audrey.  It was quite interesting as when you go there, if you are wearing a neck tie, the waiters using large shears will clip the tie off and staple it to the ceiling.  We had a good time this evening with the Shumway’s.

 

Sunday, June 20, 1971

 

                Today we attended meeting in the Lehi 2nd Ward.  This will be our new Ward when we get moved over.  We found that it is a very friendly Ward and were welcomed by several of the members. 

 

Monday, June 21, 1971

 

                This morning Mae and I went into the bank and signed the papers on our new home.  After signing the papers, I went into work.  Our landlord’s came over to visit with Mae.  They have a privet pool by their home and she invited Sandy and Shellie to use that one.  We guess that the pool contained too much chlorine because when the girls got out, both of them had an earache; also Sandy’s hair was green instead of its normal red.  Mae took Sandy and Shellie into the Doctor’s to have them checked.  The Doctor told her that he was sure they would be ok and he felt that Sandy’s hair color would be restored after a few washings.  Sandy was very embarrassed about the color.  This evening Larry, Tammy and their children came over for Shellie’s Birthday.  After putting all the children down to bed, Larry, Tammy, Mae and I went for a swim. 

 

Tuesday, June 22, 1971

 

                When I went into work this morning I took a change of clothes.  After we finished our normal days work, Jerry Jones and I changed into work clothes that we could paint in.  Jerry and I then painted our office areas and we also painted Ron Shumway’s office.  He and I had planned on this for a few days and Ron was able to get us the pint.  They sure look a lot better.  It was 10PM before we finished and went home.

 

Wednesday, June 23, 1971

 

                When I got home this evening, Mae had fresh fish for dinner.  Larry and Tammy had given them to us.  Mae sure made a very nice meal with them.  Larry and Tammy came over this evening just to visit.  Sandy went home with them and slept over night with Lori.

 

Thursday, June 24, 1971

 

                Larry, Tammy and their family came over this evening just to visit.  We all went in for a swim and had a lot of fun.  We are going to miss this swimming pool when we move into our new home.

 

Friday, June 25, 1971

 

                After dinner this evening we all went over to Bob and Carroll’s.  It is quite a drive to their place.  We had a nice visit with them before coming back home.  This evening Mae and I lay in bed and talked until quite late.  David woke very croupy about mid-night.  I finally drove over to Larry and Tammy’s and borrowed their vaporizer. 

 

Saturday, June 26, 1971

 

                I went into work early this morning and worked until noon before returning home.  Mae and I then took the children with us and went down town shopping.  Our main goal was to buy a dress for Shellie but we ended up buying Sandy a dress also.  This evening after the children were in bed, Mae and I went in for a swim about 10PM.

 

Sunday, June 27, 1971

 

                This morning Mike cut himself trying to shave with my raiser.  He is only 2 years old!  It wasn’t serious but we found it quite amusing.  After returning home from Sacrament meeting Mae and I wrote letters to our parents.  We then took the children for a walk and stopped and bought use some ice cream at the little shop just west of us on University ave.

 

Monday, June 28, 1971

 

                After getting home from work, all my family went to a Ward Birthday Party, which took the place of Family Home Evening tonight.  We had a nice time but I was a little disappointed with it.

 

Tuesday, June 29, 1971

 

                I went into work at 5:30AM so that I could get some test time on the computer.  After dinner this evening, Larry and Tammy also came over for a little while.  The four of us went swimming but because Sandy has had an earache and is still not over it, the children didn’t come in.

 

Wednesday, June 30, 1971

 

                I went into work at 6AM so that I might have time to get some computer testing.  When I got home from work we attended a Mutual Married party with Larry and Tammy in their ward.

 

Thursday, July 1, 1971

 

                I took today off work at 1PM, so that we could move into our new home at 233 W. Hunter, in the area still referred to as Lehi, which is now annexed into Mesa, Arizona.  Aunt Allison came over and helped Mae clean and scrub cupboards, walls and floors this morning prior to moving in our belongings.  I rented a truck to haul the furniture that we had stored in Scott Fry’s garage when we first moved down to Arizona.  Brother Tryon, who is a Counselor in our Ward Bishopric, Larry Braithwaite plus Jeff and Kerry Bushnell, helped me move our things from the apartment and Scott’s garage to our home.  Uncle Van and Aunt Allison also came over this evening to again help us get settled.

 

Friday, July 2, 1971

 

                I went into work this morning.  Mae worked all day, putting things away in our new home.  Aunt Allison invited us over for dinner and then she went with Mae and I to the apartment and helped us clean there.  Both Mike and David have ear aches this evening so that kept us up some of the night.

 

Saturday, July 3, 1971

 

                Mae and I spent most of the day arranging and putting things away in our new home.  It is sure good to have our own home again.  Mae took time out to take Mike and David into to see the Doctor.  This afternoon Bob and Carroll stopped over to visit for a while.  Bob is going to quit his job and they are moving up to Utah.  They really haven’t enjoyed the Phoenix area, especially the summer heat.

 

Sunday, July 4, 1971

 

                Being the first Sunday of the month, therefore it was our normal Fast and Testimony meeting.  We found out that Bishop and Sister Price’s oldest daughter was killed in (I believe) an automobile accident, last Tuesday and her funeral was held yesterday.  Sister Price gave a beautiful Testimony that strengthened everyone.  Later this evening Mae and I went to visit them at their home to express our sympathy and our appreciation for Sister Price’s Testimony.

 

Monday, July 5, 1971

 

                Our Ward held a Flag Raising ceremony at 6AM in honor of Independence Day.  Following the ceremony a breakfast was served.  Mae and I spent the rest of the day working on the house.  Larry, Tammy and their children came over this evening for dinner and to share Family Home Evening with us.

 

Tuesday, July 6, 1971

 

                While I was at work, Mae got everything put away.  She sure has the house looking nice.  When I came home from work, I trimmed the trees, a rose bush and did a couple of small yard jobs.  We enjoyed a nice evening as a family in our new home.

 

Wednesday, July 7, 1971

 

                Mae took me into work this morning, so that she would have a car to run some errons.  When she picked me up from work, we drove over to the west side of Phoenix to Bob and Carroll’s.  Then Bob, Carroll, Mae and I went out to eat.  Today is Carroll’s Birthday.

 

Thursday, July 8, 1971

 

                Mae had the house all cleaned up when I got home from work.  I repaired a couple of items for Mae and then we just spent an enjoyable evening as a family at home.

Monday, September 6, 1971

 

                I had the day off work due to Labor Day.  After breakfast Mae and I worked in the yard.  I assembled the slippery slid and swings for the children.  They were sure excited.  I did go into work for 3 hours this afternoon.  Mae had our lunch ready to go, when I returned home.  We then joined with Larry, Tammy and their family as well as Ron and Audry and their family, at the Pioneer Park in Mesa.  Our families all enjoy being together and this evening was the same. 

 

Tuesday, September 7, 1971

 

                I had a busy day at work today.  Sandy started back to School.  She really seems to enjoy school and is doing well.  This evening I took Mae to see a Doctor (or maybe a therapist), but he reads the eyes and then works on the feet.  It seems to really help Mae.  I guess we will see.

 

Wednesday, September 8, 1971

 

                Before leaving for work, I gave Sandy a Father’s Blessing prior to her starting school for the New Year.  I tended the children this evening as Mae attended M.I.A.  Mae has been called to be the Laurel Advisor and I was called to teach the same age youth in Sunday school.  Tonight was the Opening Social for the M.I.A.  It rained tonight.  (You wouldn’t think that comment would be worth entering into a journal, but we see so little rain here since moving down, so it is a big deal!)

 

Thursday, September 9, 1971

 

                I went into work at 6:30 this morning.  We received work that my half sister, Geneva Thomas, died today from a heart attack.  I am sure glad that while we were in Idaho Falls, August 30 we went over to visit her.  Today was a very busy day at work and with problems I worked straight through, not quitting to go home this evening.  I had taken time to call for Airline reservations for me and my family to fly to Salt Lake on Saturday.

 

 Friday, September 10, 1971

 

                I never finished yesterdays work until 5AM today.  I went home and went to bed, sleeping until 1 or 2 in the afternoon.  This evening Mae and I went over to Larry and Tammy’s to visit for a little while.

 

Saturday, September 11, 1971

 

                My family and I flew to Salt Lake on the 7AM Air West flight.  Irven met us at the Air Port.  Georgia also came and got Mae and our children.  Irven, Bob, Roy and I then drove straight up to Rexburg, Idaho, where we attended Geneva’s funeral.  Mom and Dad Jensen had also come to the funeral so we had a little time to visit with them and other family members.  We then drove back to Clearfield, where I and my family stayed the night with Mae’s parents.

 

Sunday, September 12, 1971

 

                We stayed at Mom and Dad Porters last night but this morning I attended Priesthood meeting at the Clearfield 1st Ward.  All of our family also attended the Clearfield 1st ward for Sunday school.  We enjoyed visiting with our friends and former Ward members.  Bob and Carroll invited us over for dinner.  Irven was also at Bob and Carroll’s for dinner.  Roy and Linda came over to Bob’s later this afternoon so that we could all visit.  Irven took us to the Salt Lake Airport where we flew out this evening.  We arrived home at 11:45PM, quite tired but it was a good trip.

 

Monday, September 13, 1971

 

                Yesterday was David’s 1 year old Birthday but because we were in Utah Yesterday, we had a Family Birthday Party for him this evening.  We are about broke, so we couldn’t spend much as far as gifts but we did have him a couple of toys.

 

Tuesday, September 14, 1971

 

                Work went well today.  After getting home, we had a nice dinner as always.  This evening Mae went Home Teaching with me.  After returning home Mae studied for her Young Women’s lesson and I studied for my Sunday school lesson.  Mae is teaching the Laurel Y.W. and I am teaching the youth, both boys and girls, of the same age on Sundays. 

 

Wednesday, September 15, 1971

 

                This evening Mae will be her first  time to teach her Y.W. class.  She asked me to give her a Priesthood Blessing before she left.  Mae came home from M.I.A. a little discouraged as she said the girls were very route and showed very little respect.  Mae had also been getting things packed so she can fly to Utah tomorrow. 

 

Thursday, September 16, 1971

 

                I went to work as normal.  Mae went into Dr. Johnston today.  I hadn’t indicated what kind of Dr. he was or why, but possibly he was a David’s Predication.)  This afternoon Mae and David took the Air West 5PM flight to Salt Lake and will be gone for the weekend.  After having super with the children, I had Mary Wood come to our house and tend the children for me.  I met with Bishop Price and Brother Marsdon to discuss the Adult Aaronic program in our Ward.  I did not indicate why Mae went to Utah but that would have been the season for peaches to be on and Mae went up to help her mother with peaches.  She would take an empty suit case with her and bring it back, packed with fresh bottled peaches.

 

Friday, September 17, 1971

 

                I took my children over to Tammy’s for her to tend them while I went into work.  I went back and picked them up after work.  Before going home we went Grocery shopping.  We then stopped and got hamburgers and ice cream for dinner.  I put the children to bed about 8:30PM.  I sure miss Mae.

 

Saturday, September 18, 1971

 

                I went to work with Larry today.  Tammy tended the children for me.    We are putting furring strips on cinder block homes.  It really goes quite fast as you tack 1” x 2” furring strips on the 16” block joints.  Latter someone else will finish nailing the stripping solid.  We furred out 1 and ½ homes today.  We get paid by the home’s we do.  After quitting work for the day, I stopped and got a haircut.  I then went over and got my children and brought them home.  The children and I sure miss Mae but we are getting along ok.

 

Sunday, September 19, 1971

 

                The children and I attended meetings this morning.  I taught my Sunday school class this morning for the first time.  “Boy !”, that was a flop.  I have got to try something different next week!  We also attended Sacrament meeting this afternoon.  This evening we went to the Airport and got Mae and David.  The children and I sure missed Mae while she was gone.  It is sure good to have her home.  Mae and I talked until 2AM.

 

Monday, September 20, 1971

 

                Work went well today.  I started at 8AM at Air West and Quite at 4:30PM.  I went from there to the housing division where I continued installing furring strips.  I worked until dark.  Mae had some supper ready for me and then we held Family Home Evening.  Mae sure is wonderful and very supportive of me.

 

Tuesday, September 21, 1971

 

                After working at Air West, I again went to the housing division where I finished the house I started yesterday.  I got home a little earlier than I did last night so I had some time to play with my children.  After putting the children to bed, Mae and I went for a short walk.  The evening are getting so pleasant this time of year.

 

Wednesday, September 22, 1971

 

                I had problems at work and didn’t get home until 11PM.  Mae was a little upset as she needed a car to get to M.I.A.  Not only was the transportation a problem but the girls in her class were again very disrespectful.  She came home in tears.

 

Thursday, September 23, 1971

 

                I had to work late again this evening.  Mae went to a Stake Leadership meeting.  I tended the children and figured our bills.  That is always frustrating when we have more out go than in come.  After Mae got home from her meeting, she and I talked for some time about our circumstances.  It was about 10:45PM when we got to bed.

 

Friday, September 24, 1971

 

                I got several phone calls after we went to bed last night and finally at 2AM I decided that I couldn’t handle the problems over the telephone, so I went in to work.  I didn’t return home until 2PM.  After getting home I took a nap and slept until 5PM when Mae and I went to the Temple with Larry and Tammy.

 

Saturday, September 25, 1971

 

                I went to work this morning putting up furring strips.  I was able to complete two homes.  After getting home and having some dinner, I took care of some Oral Evaluations with Home Teaching.  Mae and I then took all the children to a drive in movie.  By parking the Station Wagon backwards we can all see the screen, plus we make a bed in the back and if the children get sleepy they can just fall asleep there.  It seems to work out pretty good that way.  After returning home and putting the children into their own beds we found that David didn’t sleep well tonight.

 

Sunday, September 26, 1971

 

                Mae and I took turns staying home from meeting as both Mike and David are sick today.  David is feeling a little better this evening, but Mike has quite a bad sore throat and is running a fever.

 

Monday, September 27, 1971

 

                I called Ron and asked if I could ride into work with him this morning.  He stopped by and picked me up.  Mae kept the car at home so she could take Mike into the Doctor today, but our Pediatrician wasn’t in today.  After I got home from work, I went to the lumber yard and purchased material so that I could build some shelves in the storage room.  Tonight we held Family Home Evening and had a very nice time.

 

Tuesday, September 28, 1971

 

                After getting home from work at Air West, I went over to the housing project where I put Masonite Siding on framed storage sheds.  I did not have to nail it off, but just cut the board to fit and tack it in place.  I was sure tired when I quit and came home.  Mike and David are feeling some better today but Shellie now has what they had.  David is finally starting to crawl and is even starting to try taking a step or two on his own.

 

Wednesday, September 29, 1971

 

                At work some of us went over to the Air Port Terminal to see the first of Hugh Air West’s newly painted air planes.  The company has been trying to brighten up Air West Airline’s by painting the planes a Sun Shine Yellow.  They tried to put a sparkle finish to it but when they sprayed the finish on, the finish and paint and everything down to the metal, slid off the fuselage onto the ground.  This plane was painted Sun Shine Yellow, but didn’t have the sparkling finishing to it.  We commented that it looked like a big yellow banana.  One thing about it is you can see it and distinguish it above any other Airline!  When I got home this evening, I stayed with the children while Mae went to M.I.A.    Each night when Mae returns from M.I.A., she is very upset with the girls and usually in tears.  One thing about her though is she won’t give up.  I have even told her that I would go to the Bishop and ask for her release.  She will not hear of it.  It rained most of the day today so that was welcome.

 

Thursday, September 30, 1971

 

                Work went well.  It rained all day today, so I didn’t go to my 2nd job at the housing sub division.  It gave me some time to spend with our children.  After getting the little ones to bed, we left Sandy in charge and Mae and I went over to Brother and Sister Wood’s to see their new baby and for a short visit.

 

Friday, October 1, 1971

 

                After work, I stopped at a store and bought myself a watch.  I need to be more accurate in my time keeping on my second job.  When I got home I took my family out to a drive-in for dinner.  We took the children back home and then Mae and I went grocery shopping.  Before returning home, we stopped and visited with Larry and Tammy for a few minutes.

 

Saturday, October 2, 1971

 

                We slept in until 8 this morning.  After eating breakfast I took the children with me to Brother Tryon’s home, where we loaded his pickup with manure from his corral.  We hauled it over to our place and put it on our garden spot.  We returned Brother Tryon’s pickup and then came home.  I dug the manure into the garden area and raked the area level.  This evening I went to the Stake Center where we were able to watch on closed circuit TV, the General Session of Priesthood Meeting.

 

Sunday, October 3, 1971

 

                We held regular Sunday meetings, as there aren’t any of the TV stations that will broadcast the General Conference Sessions in our area.  One Session of Conference was rebroadcast at 1PM that we listened to.  I am having the same problems with the Sunday School Class, as Mae is having with her M.I.A. class.  There were only 2 of the youth that came today and they weren’t interested in a lesson and paid little, to no attention.  I was also called into the Bishop’s office and called to be an assistant in the Adult Aaronic a Priesthood class in our Ward.  I accepted the calling and will be working with Dave Marsdon.  Our little David doesn’t feel very well this evening and kept us up most of the night.  I feel that there is something wrong with his breathing.

 

Monday, October 4, 1971

 

                I rented a rotatiller early this morning and tilled up a small area in our back yard so we can plant a small garden.  After finishing that and returning the tiller I went into work.  After getting home from work this evening we held Family Home Evening and had a wonderful evening.

 

Tuesday, October 5, 1971

 

                Mae took me into work this morning so she could have the car today.  She took the children to Primary this afternoon.  After Primary, Mae and the children came to my office at the airport to get me from work.  Mike was quite excited to tell me that Momma had bought seeds for the garden.  After dinner this evening we planted about ½ of our garden.

 

Wednesday, October 6, 1971

 

                Work went well.  This evening I tended the children while Mae went to M.I.A.  Mae came home at the end of the activity in tears.  The kids gave them a bad time.  Mae comes home every M.I.A. night in tears over the youth.  I have tried to get her to quite but she just keeps persisting and trying harder.

 

Thursday, October 7, 1971

 

                This evening Larry, Tammy and Lorie picked Mae, Sandy and I up and we went to Phoenix where we saw the Ice Follies.  It was very good and we sure enjoyed the evening.

 

Friday, October 8, 1971

 

                Work went well at Air West.  When I got home from work I started working on our car.  I pulled the rear axles from the car, as the bearings have gone out and will need to be replaced.  I also removed both front and rear brake shoes, as the brakes are also bad.  I didn’t have time to put it back together but plan to do that tomorrow after work.  This evening Sandy went over to Lori’s to spend the night.  The rest of us stayed home and watched a show on TV before putting the little ones to bed.

 

Saturday, October 9, 1971

 

                After breakfast I started working on the car.  I was able to get it put back together and all is working just fine now.  This evening Mae and I went to a Ward Relief Society social and enjoyed that.  After returning home, I got a call at 10PM from work and had to go back in for about 3 hours to fix the problems.

 

Sunday, October 10, 1971

 

                I gave the lesson in the Adult Aaronic Priesthood class today and that went very well.  My lesson in Sunday School class went some better today, but the youth don’t have very much interest in learning about the Gospel nor do they show any respect for the teacher.  Mae was asked if she would sing with the Ward Choir, which she accepted.

 

Monday, October 11, 1971

 

                For Family Home Evening tonight, we accepted an invitation to Brother and Sister Ashcroff’s home.  Also the Young’s, another family in our Ward, were invited.  The Ashcroff’s showed many beautiful and interesting slides they had taken while Hall’s work required them to live in Europe for a while.  They really did do a lot of traveling while there.  Molly also provided great refreshments that the children especial enjoyed.  It was a very enjoyable evening.  We are really enjoying the friends we are gaining in our Ward.  Molly is very English and we enjoy her accent. 

 

Tuesday, October 12, 1971

 

                I hurt my back last night so I went into L.B. Johnston to have it worked on.  This evening I went Home Teaching with Doug Wood.  When I returned home I took Mae for a short ride, just to have some time to be together and getting Mae out of the home for a little while.  I sure love that girl and enjoy just being with her.

 

Wednesday, October 13, 1971

 

                At work today Ron Shumway called me into his office.  He read to me a memo written by Scott Fry, complementing me on my work since I have been here at Hugh’s Air West.  He also added his complement as to my performances.  He next asked if I would be interested in going to a school offered by Honeywell.  The class would be for two week, given at the Honeywell Office in Boston Massachusetts starting on the 26th of October.   I told him that I would be very interested in attending the class.

 

 

Thursday, October 14, 1971

 

                After work this evening, Jerry Hanselman’s came over to our place.  I help him build some shelves for one of his closet’s.  When we finished, Mae had made Peach Cobbler for us.  It was sure good.  Mae is sure a Great wife!

 

Friday, October 15, 1971

 

                After work Mae and I went to get a treatment on our backs.  This evening Larry, Tammy, Mae and I went to a Drive In movie.  We enjoyed the movie and had a good time being with Larry and Tammy.  I also think Mae is about as perfect a wife and sweetheart as anyone could have.

 

Saturday, October 16, 1971

 

                We were able to sleep in and that was sure nice.  Later I took Mae and the children over to Hollie Braithwaite’s Birthday Party.  I then had to go into work at Air West.  I found some problems with the Tape Library system and it took until 9PM before I returned home.  Mae had some dinner ready for me.  She sure is a very patient and loving wife.  Sure love her.

 

Sunday, October 17, 1971

 

                When I got home from Priesthood meeting Mae told me that I had received a call from work and they needed my help.  I called Brother Waite and he said that he would cover my Sunday school class.  I never got back from work until 4PM, just in time to take my family to Sacrament meeting.  When we got home Mae had a nice dinner for us.  Mae had got her hair cut yesterday.  She sure is a beautiful wife and I sure love her.

 

Monday, October 18, 1971

 

                I took the day off from work at Hugh Air West today and worked for Larry.  I was able to fur out homes two homes for Larry.  I made about $68.00 for doing those two homes.  That will sure help our budget out.  This evening we had a nice Family Home Evening.  We played some games after we had a short lesson.

 

Tuesday, October 19, 1971

 

                It was cold enough this morning that I turned on the furnace.  Work went well at Air West.  I left work at 4PM so that I could get a haircut.  We took the children to the park for dinner and let them play for a while.  This evening I built Jerry Jones a set of saw horses.  Mike asked me what I was building and when I told him, he asked if I could build a wooden man also.

 

Wednesday, October 20, 1971

 

                I worked at Air West as normal and it went well.  I am really enjoying my job at Air West.  Larry and Tammy are going up to Utah for the deer hunt, so they have asked us to tend their children while they are gone.  They left today.  After I got home and had dinner, Mae had to attend M.I.A. this evening, so I stayed home with the children.  Tending the children went very well but Mae came home from M.I.A. very upset, as normal. 

 

Thursday, October 21, 1971

 

                I though thing were going quit well at work until 4PM this afternoon when the manager of the department of which I am designing a program, called and said that they were scrapping the system at this time as it wasn’t going to meet their needs.  I had spent quite a while trying to get the basic system planned and felt bad that it had been a waste of time.  This evening Mae served dinner outside on our patio.  That was kind of a different experience for us to be eating dinner on the patio at the end of October.  Anyway, it was a good experience.  This evening Mae attended a Stake meeting.  I tended the children and we had fun wrestling on the floor.

 

Friday, October 22, 1971

 

                At work today I got things caught up so I would be ready to take off to attend the schooling from Honeywell, for the next two weeks.  After work I bought two new tires for our car.  After getting the little children down this evening we left Sandy to tend them.  Mae and I then started for a dance but changed our minds and just went for a ride.

 

Saturday, October 23, 1971

 

                I got up this morning and finished planting our garden.  I then helped Ron Shumway pore some concrete at a side job he is doing.  After dinner I needed to go into Air West for some things I needed to finish up, so I took Mae and the children up to the park for them to play there.  I picked them back up when I finished at work.  After taking the children home where we let Sandy tend them while Mae and I went out Christmas shopping.

 

Sunday, October 24, 1971

 

                My Sunday school class did not go well at all today.  No matter what I tried I could not get the students attention nor did they show any interest in learning anything.  When we got home, from Sunday school, Mae helped me get my clothes packed.  I then attended a Teacher Development class and Mae attended choir practice.  The choir sang in Sacrament Meeting this evening.  When we came home this evening we relaxed and watched a show on TV.

 

Monday, October 25, 1971

 

                This morning Mae and the children took me into the Air West office.  I said my good-bys to them before going into the office.  I will be gone to Boston for two weeks.  Darcy McGulloth will also be traveling with me.  We will be attending a Class given by the Honeywell Computer Co. on programming the Honeywell Computer.  We boarded our Flight on a 747 Airliner at 11:45AM.  We had to change planes at Chicago, which was just to be a short stop.  However after unloading the Chicago passengers and loading those going from Chicago on the continuing flight, there seem to be some delay in leaving.  The caption then announced that there were mechanical problems and we were asked to depart the plain.  After everyone was off the plain and in the terminal we were asked to reload.  Then there were some further delays.  Our plain finally left the Chicago airport for Boston.  We were told that the problem was in the breaks of the aircraft but it was determined that if the runways were dry there would be no trouble landing the plane.  The next concern was that at the time we left Chicago, the Boston Airport was experiencing hard rain storms.  Hopefully those storms would be over by the time we arrived at Boston.  When we got there the storm was over and we landed successfully.  We didn’t land at Boston until 10:45PM EST.  The Car Rental in which we had reservations had closed at 6PM Eastern Standard Time.  The only Car Rental now open was Herts and the cost certainly reflected their name.  We had no choice but to rent a car from them as we were several miles east of Farmington, where the class we came to attend was to be held.  We were given direction to Farmington by leaving the Airport and going onto the Boston Turn Pike West.  No problem except that every few miles we went through a toll gate which cost us 2 or 3 dollars.  We got to our hotel, in Framington about 11:45PM EST and checked in.

                During the flying time Darcy and I had time to visit about the Church.  I did approach Darcy about lessoning to the missionaries and he didn’t seem to be against it.

 

Tuesday, October 26, 1971

 

                Darcy and I attended the class at the Honeywell Main Office.  The class was quite interesting but very different than programming for the I.B.M. machines.  During the day, Darcy and I discussed the car situation and concluded that we would have to return the car to Hertz Rental at the Boston Airport.  We could then get a car from Budget Car Rental, the one that we had originally reserved.  We just couldn’t afford the one from Hertz.  We picked up a map of the area and in studding it decided that we could take the road that ran along the side of the Turn Pike and save all the toll gates.  However that was a mistake as we found when we got to Boston, we were lost.  We got the map out and according to it, we were to drive along the Potomac River to the 3rd Right hand turn, go about 3 or 4 block and enter a tunnel, then take the third right hand Exit to the Turn Pike going east.  Well that sounded easy enough but when we took the third left we found our self downtown Boston, lost in traffic.  Somehow we re-found the Potomac River and again counted to the 3rd right but again found that we were downtown Boston, lost in traffic.  Again we found the Potomac River drive but somehow this time we found the tunnel.  We counted the 1st and 2nd exits but the next exit was the end of the tunnel and all traffic entered onto the Turn pike but going west.  We decided that we would continue to the next exit, make a left turn under the turn pike, make another left turn and get back onto the turn pike entrance and would be going west.  Well before getting to an exit, we had to go thru a Toll Gate.  When we took the next exit we found that there was no left turn to go back under the turnpike but we were again driving along the Potomac River.  I had been driving and Darcy was reading the map and giving me directions.  I pulled over and traded Darcy seats telling him that I would read the map and give him directions.  I then counted the intersections and told him where to turn.  Again we were downtown Boston lost but somehow we found the tunnel so again we counted the tunnel exits and there was not a third right hand exit but went directly onto the turn pike going west.  When we came to the toile gate, I told Darcy to ask the toile gate attendance for directions.  Trying to understand his Boston accent we understood him to say “Take a right and take a right”.  We took the next right hand exit and as before the next right took us back along the Potomac River.  Again by accident we found the tunnel and for the third time we were going west on the turnpike.  The 4th time we entered the tunnel we took the 2nd exit and somehow was downtown Boston but found the turnpike going west.  We finally got the car back to Hertz and rented a car from Budget.  As we left the airport we just stayed on the turnpike until we got to Framington, being glad to pay at each toile gate. 

               

 

 

Wednesday, October 27, 1971

 

                School went well today, but I still have real concerns about the Honeywell computer.  This evening Darcy and I went out to dinner.  We decided that we would like to go to a Fish House and so found one.  There was quite a waiting line when we got there.  There were two ladies in line behind us that Darcy struck up a conversation with.  I really tried to stay out of it.  About that time the waiter came to show us to a table.  He asked it if was for four.  I quickly replied that it was for two.  When we went to the table Darcy asked me why I hadn’t offered the two ladies to join us.  I just said “no thanks” as I didn’t want to get anything going that shouldn’t.  I have often thought about that choice and am still glad for my decision.  Both Darcy and I ordered a 2lb crab.  When it was served to us, it was served on a very large dinner platter and the crab hung over all sides of that platter.  The center of the crab had been cleaned out ant the cavity was filled with fried rice.  I think it took us over an hour to eat all of it, but it was sure good. 

                When I returned to my hotel room I called Mae to see if she had received her airline passes in order for her to come out on Friday Evening.  Somehow there was a mix-up and if she would come now, she would have to fly on Stand By, with several plane changes.  Though I was very disappointed, I told her not to come as she could be bumped at any one of the stops and that would leave her stranded by herself.  The chances of that happening were just too great.  Mae also told me that David and Mike are not feeling.

 

Thursday, October 28, 1971

 

                During morning break at school, I called Ron to see if there was anything he might do to get Mae a flight to come out for the weekend.  Classes went well and after finishing for the day Darcy and I went to dinner at a Chinese Restraint.  It was very good.  After eating we just walked around town to see what there was.  I then came back to the hotel room so I could work on some problems from school.  I also hoped for a call from Mae but that didn’t happen so I presume she will not be coming out.

 

Friday, October 29, 1971

 

                After classes today I took Darcy over to Boston where he rented a car.  He left from there and drove to New York City where he has relatives.  He will spend the weekend with them.  I stopped at a department store and purchased a swimming suite.  This is the wrong time of year to find swimming suites but I finally found one that would work.  After getting back to the hotel I went swimming for a little while.  It isn’t the most fun to swimming by myself but I swam for a while.  There sure isn’t much on TV to watch, in fact I really don’t like doing very much by myself. 

 

Saturday, October 30, 1971

 

                I got up and went down to the dining room and got some breakfast.  I then found a Laundromat where I washed out some garments and socks.  I then got the road map out and drove to Cape Cod.  There has always been a feeling of specialty about Cape Cod, maybe because of the song “Old Cape Cod” but when I got there I was disappointed.  It is really just a Rich Mans neighborhood but the homes are big, from what you could see of them as they set back with so much foliage around them that most of them you can’t see the houses from the road and most are gated off.  After driving around for a while I found a place where I could park the car and walk out to the beach.  I set on a sand hill and watched for a while.  I noticed a young boy, maybe 12 to 14 years old, walking along the beach.  He carried a bucket and would stop every few steps and kneel down and dig in the sand.  He would put something into his bucket and then walk a little ways and repeat the same thing again.  Finally curiosity got to me and I went out and asked him what he was digging for.  He said clams and showed me what he had in the bucket.  I asked him how he know where to dig and I hadn’t seen him dig without finding a clam.  He said that when the tide comes in covering the beach, the clams will come out of the sand and grab floating sea weed.  It then burrows back into the sand as the tide goes back out.  During the day the clam will continue pulling the sea weed down into the sand as it eats the sea weed.  You just look for a small piece of sea weed in the sand and dig down until you uncover the claim.  I followed his instructions and soon had a dozen clams.  I took them to the boy and asked if he wanted them.  Surprised he asked if I didn’t want them.  I explained that I was staying in a hotel room for a week and didn’t think they would last until I returned home.  He was excited to get the clams.  I then thought about finding me something to eat and went looking for a Friday, November 5, 1971

 

                Classes went as normal until after the 9AM break.  The instructor then asked if there were any questions that we wanted discussed.  When there was no response, he said that he had nothing else for us.  He gave us a Certificate of Completion of the class and said we could go.  There was no way for us to change our flight schedule for the earlier flight, so Darcy and I decided to sea.”  Then we thought that we would like to see the battle ship “Old Iron Sides”.  According to the map, we were to take the same roads into Boston as we had done previously, but as we drove along the Potomac River we would take a different left turn than before.  We would then make a right turn on “Charles Street” and cross the Potomac Bridge.  After crossing the bridge we would make the 1st left turn off the bridge and make the next right turn.  That should be easy enough.  Well, we found the correct turns to the Potomac river road but missed the left hand turn and ended up back downtown Boston and as before ended up on the Turn Pike going West.  The 2nd try we made the left turn off the Potomac drive and Darcy said that we should come to a turn-about and that I should take the Charles Street exit.  Well, the turn-about didn’t worry me as I had taken several turn-abouts last week when I went out to Cape Cod.  Only there was one problem this time, when I entered the Turn-About it was only ½ a turn-about.  We couldn’t find Charles street and had to exit and was headed back downtown Boston.  Just as we exited the turn-about I looked over my left shoulder and I saw a sign “Charles Street”.  I just stopped in the middle of traffic and made an illegal U-turn and came back to the turn-about, this time I stopped in the turn-about, holding up traffic, until I found the street sign ‘Charles Street” on a building corner underneath a fire escape ladder.  (People were honking, waving their arms at me and yelling, but I didn’t care by then.) I took that exit and we went upon the Potomac Bridge.  The bridge is 4 or 5 roads high and we were on the top road.  When we got to the end of the bridge, there was a sharp right turn and I took it.  It was a very narrow lane coming back down from the bridge.  As we were reaching the end of the lane, Darcy told me to take the first left turn, however, there was a large truck coming towards me, with another truck behind be that was so close that all I could see in the rear view mirror was the radiator grill.  Also, the right hand turn coming up, had a truck also approaching the intersection.  I panicked and just stepped on the gas and went thru the intersection hoping we wouldn’t be killed in the collision.  Darcy yelled at me that I was to take the right hand turn, but I turned left into a parking lot and stopped the car.  I said that we could walk from there.  It wasn’t but a couple of blocks and we found the “USS Constitution” or nick named “Old Iron Sides” ship.  It was very interesting touring through the ship.  Besides the beauty and majestic of the ship,  I believe the most interesting thing to me was when we saw the officers sleeping area.  The bunks were only about 5 feet long and were three bunks high with the floor to ceiling only a total of 6 feet high.  It would have been almost impossible for me to have even got into the bunks, let alone try to sleep that cramped up.  Also, as we walked around on the lower deck we had to duck under every beam.  After spending over an hour walking through and seeing the ship, we left.  Upon leaving, I suggested that we find someone that knew the area to give us driving instructions to our next destination.  Darcy agreed, and we went to a shop where we found a clerk who travels to and from this shop daily.  She gave us very detailed instructions before we left.  We found our way for a while, but then again found ourselves lost downtown Boston.  After a few minutes I saw a sign “Budget Rental Cars”, I just pulled into the lot and we turned the car back in.  We then had them drive us to the airport.  As we got back into the same car that we had used, the driver asked if one of us wanted to drive.  We both quickly declined.  Darcy took the rear seat and I took the front passenger seat.  What a ride that was.  The driver only knew one speed and that was as fast as the car could go.  At one point the driver was going to go straight through a intersection and a large U.S. Mail Truck was going to make a left turn.  I was sure that we were going to collide.  Both drivers were honking their horns and shouting at the other as each continued to jerk forward, challenging the other driver to give way.  I could hardly believe it, but our driver won out.  When I looked back at Darcy, he was actually laying on the floor.  When we got to the airport at 1 PM, we just spent the remainder of our time in the safety of the airport.  Our flight left about 5PM and we got to Phoenix at 10:45PM.  Our flight was a direct flight with no stops.  We had changed our flight to first class and so we were up in the front of the aircraft in very large and comfortable seats with few other passengers.  As a result, we had plenty of attention from the stewardesses, which was kind of special.  Darcy had his car at the office so we caught a shuttle from the airport to the office and Darcy drove me home.

Monday, November 8, 1971

 

                There were problems at work with the Freight Cargo System.  That is normal with that system.  It is a very poorly written system that runs twice a month.  It runs after the end of the month and then again after the 15th of the month.  The auditing and balancing of the input that come into the system daily, is very poor and so if anything is out or entered to the wrong account the system just fails.  The error or errors has to be corrected, all files have to be restored and balanced against the last run and then it is started all over.  The computer operators cannot make these corrections nor is there any way they can correct the errors.  I am the only one that know that procedure, so I just have to be available during those run times and be ready to go back into work or stay after work to correct the data and files.  Even after correcting one error, there may be other errors that won’t show up until later during the run.  The main run after the 1st takes 12 to 18 hours to complete while the mid-month runs take 8 to 16 hours plus under normal processing our computer can operate 2 systems at the same time but this system ties up the complete computer until it finishes.  My senior programmer and I have approached management about rewriting the system but the answer always come back the same.  NO! We can’t justify rewriting a system that works when we have so many systems to write now!  For the present I just have to Baby Sit the system.  I worked until 5:30PM this evening.  Larry, Tammy and their family came over this evening for dinner and Family Home Evening.  Mae had a very nice chicken dinner with all the trimmings.  Family Home evening also went well.  It was a good evening.  I got called back into work at 10PM, we had just gone to bed.

Tuesday, November 16, 1971

                As far as we can figure when Mae took David into the Doctor, the Doctor started to get the shot ready and when he mentioned that it was Penicillin and a double shot, Mae had the feeling that she should not let him give the shot to David.  The only reason she could understand for her feelings was that while up to Utah last week, is that her Mother had been telling all the bad reports she had heard about the effects that Penicillin has had on people.  She couldn’t understand her feelings and didn’t know what to tell the Doctor.  After she left the Doctor’s office and got to the car, she just broke down and cried, feeling that she should have lessened to her feelings.  David was screaming all the way home after the shot.  David continued to cry all evening and when anyone touched him from the waist down he screamed in pain.  He also broke out, in what we thought were hives.  You couldn’t change his diaper or even remove his socks.  Both Mae and I became quite concerned about it.  I recommended that tomorrow Mae take him into another Doctor for his opinion.

 

Wednesday, November 17, 1971

Saturday, November 20, 1971

we should see what there was there.  It was just a western town set in which western movies were produced.  We later found out that Old Tucson was one of the main western sets that John Wayne used.  Shortly after getting in, there a western demonstration put on, depicting a gun fight.  One of the actors was shot and fell from a roof top, rolled down the roof, fell onto the roof of a porch and then rolled off that roof and fell to the ground flat on his face.  We were quite impressed with the stunt acting.  Following that stunt, it was announced that for an additional charge we could go on a tour of the town including the inside stages.  The additional charges disappointed us so we just walked around the town on our own, looking at the town for a while.  We did see some scenery that was very fermium to us, that we had originally seen one some Western shows.  After seeing some of those scenes we decided that it might be interesting to take the tour.  That tour really made the trip worthwhile.  We have seen many western, since that shows in which we recognized the scenery.  They also showed us how many of the tricks are done in the stunts, which are performed during the filming of a show.  Our tour guide was the person that did the stunt of falling of the roof during the street fight.  Before the tour ended he opened it up to questions.  Someone asked him about the street fight and his falling off the roof to the ground.  He told us that that stunt was one of the first stunts that an actor must demonstrate when applying for a job as a stuntman.  He also told us that John Wayne always performed his own stunts, never using a stuntman.  We were sure glad that we had paid for the tour, it was worth it.   From there we left and drove back home getting there about 8PM.  We then had to get ready for Sandy’s Birthday tomorrow.

                I left work a little early so that Mae and I could take David into the Doctor.  He feels that the pain David is having in his legs was caused by a reaction to the shot David had received.  After leaving the Doctors office we went to the store Grocery shopping.  We then took thing home and left the children with a baby sitter.  Mae and I went out to dinner and then did some more Christmas shopping.

 

Saturday, December 11, 1971

 

                When we finished breakfast this morning and I went out into the back yard, I noticed that many of the blossoms on our orange tree had fallen on the ground.  I severally got after the children for shaking the blossoms for the tree.  They said that they hadn’t done it but I wasn’t convinced.  Later I found out that it was the frost the other night that caused the blossoms to fall.  I had to apologize to the children.  We got Christmas decorations out but I was unable to find the tree stand.  I went over to Larry’s and used his settling torch.  I cut the bottom off of a 5 gallon milk can, leaving it about 6 inches deep.  I then welded a large lag bolt through the bottom so that it stood about 6 inches high.  I could now drill a hole in the bottom of the Christmas tree and screw the stand onto the tree.  This also allowed us to keep water on the trunk of the tree, slowing the drying time down.  This afternoon Jerry Hanselman came over.  I built a wood fram and we covered it with a sheet.  Jerry then painted a Christmas Sean of the Star and the Three Wise Men.  We mounted it to the front outside wall of the house.  This evening Mae and I attended a Young Married Christmas party.  After getting home we set the tree up and put decorations on it to the point of hanging the Ice Sickles. 

 

Sunday, December 12, 1971

 

                I woke this morning with a headache and stuffed up head.  I went back to sleep for a while.  I did go to Sunday School and my class went quite well.  I did get time to visit with Bishop Price about the court summons.  He suggested that we visit with David Roberts, who it the Stake Executive Secretary and also an attorney.  I called him and he made an appointment for Mae and I to meet with him tomorrow afternoon.   Mae had Choir practice for 3:30 to 5:30 PM, so I tended the children.  Sacrament meeting was very good.  The program was put on by the Primary.  Sandy was able to participate in that.

 

Monday, December 13, 1971

 

                I took off work early so that Mae and I could meet with David Roberts in his Law office.  We explained our feelings to him and told him that we didn’t have the money to pay such a lawsuit.  I asked him what chance we would have if we didn’t have council but just represented ourselves in court.  He said, “None!”  I then asked him what chance we had if we hire him to represent us and he said “50/50”.  We then asked him if he would represent us.  He asked us “If you lose the suit would we take out bankrupts?  I said that I didn’t think we would have any other choice.  He thought for a minute and then said that he would still take the case.  We turned the case over to him.  After getting home and having dinner we held Family Home Evening and had a lesson on “Temple Marriage”.  After getting the children to bed, Mae and I finished decorating the Christmas tree and writing more letters and cards.

                I will but an insert here, as I haven’t been able to confirm the dates that these things took place, yet.  

Mr. Roberts gave us an affidavit to fill out and return to him.  It was questions concerning the fire.  I went through and answered each question to the best of my knowledge and sent it back to David Roberts.  Mr. Roberts called me and ask me to come back to his office.  He had some concerns about my answers to the affidavit.  I went into his office and we went over each question.  The questions were as follows:

                Question:  Where did the fire start?

                My Answer:  The Kitchen.

                Mr. Roberts then asked me how I know the fire started in the Kitchen.  I told him that I was told that it was where it started.  He then said: did you see the fire start in the kitchen?  I told him that I did not as I was at work, so he changed my answer to “I do not know where the fire started”.   Mr. Roberts explained that unless I saw the fire start in the kitchen then I could not answer truthfully that I know the fire started in the kitchen.  He explained that he was not trying to have me to lie, but if I answered as I had done in an affidavit, that became legal evident, but if I answered truthfully, I do not know where the fire started, then it was up to the other attorney’s to prove where the fire started.  We continued through more than a dozen questions similar to this and changed every one of the answers.  I believe the only question on the affidavit we did not correct was my mane and address.

 

Tuesday, December 14, 1971

 

                After returning home from work and having dinner, I took Sandy Christmas Shopping.  We also stopped for an ice cream before coming home.  Mae and I stayed up for a while this evening putting ice sisals on the tree. 

 

Wednesday, December 15, 1971

 

                Air West Mechanic Union went on strike this morning.  That meant that any non-essential employee that belonged to “a Union” could not cross the picket line, one union supporting the other.  As the programming staff is not union members, but are classified as management, we had to take over the duties of the computer operators and data entries personal that had to go on strike.  The airline had been expecting this strike and had tried to avoid it.  Airline Management had already tried to arrange negotiation meeting for a settlement, three times, but the Union would not meet with management.  We were told that the reason the Union would not negotiate, was that President Nixon had just put a freeze on wage increase.  This freeze stopped the Union Leaders to get the settlement they had hoped for.  All in all it made quite a mess at work.

                This evening Mae and I had been invited to attend a Hockey game with Scott and Norma Fry.  This was the first time that ether Mae or I have attended a hockey game.  I came away with the feeling that in order for the game to be exciting depended on the number of fights that occurred during the game.  It was a fun evening and we enjoyed meeting Scott and his wife Norma, on a social base.

 

Thursday, December 16, 1971

 

                Work was busy again today.  After getting home from work and having dinner I took our family over to Scott and Norma home.  We had been invited to spend the evening with them.  Jerry Hanselman had also been invited over.  Jerry had brought home movies and slides he had taken of Europe that he had taken while stationed there while serving in the army.  It was very interesting and good to meet Jerry on a social base.  Jerry is one of the Computer Operators at Air West.  He is also on leave, due to the Union Strike.

 

Friday, December 17, 1971

 

                Work went well.  At lunch time several of us went off the airport for lunch.  We went to a Pizza calf and if the table ordered a pitcher of beer, then refills were free.  Boy did most of the guys go for that.  Ron went with us and he just had his Pepsi’s and I had Root Beer.  I drove and on the way back the picket line wasn’t going to let us through.  I just stepped on the gas and they jumped out of the way.  After coming home and having something to eat, Mae and I went out Christmas Shopping.  We came home and Larry and Tammy came over.  The four of us played Rook until mid-night.

 

Saturday, December 18, 1971

 

                I got up early this morning and met Larry at the Sub-division that Larry’s Sub-Contractor Company are building.  Larry wants me to work part-time for him.  He showed me how they fur-out the block walls on a home in preparation for the dry-wall company to come in and install sheetrock.  I will be paid by the piece.  That should give us some help with our budget.  We were able to complete one home this morning.  On the way home I stopped and did some Christmas shopping.  This afternoon Larry, Tammy, Mae and I went to the Swap Meet.  It was interesting but I could sure see a place for people to get rid of hot items.  After getting home I took Mike with me and we went to a barber shop and got our hair cut.  I believe this was Mike’s first hair cut in a Barber Shop.  He was really good.  After getting home I gave David a haircut.  Mae and I stayed home with our family this evening.

 

Sunday, December 19, 1971

 

                We attended meetings today including a Stake High Priest meeting.  In that meeting (I believe it was that one) two of the High Council were to speak on new direction from the Quorum of the Twelve.  It had been suggested that we have should do more exercise activity, as to many men are working in offices and not getting proper amount of exercise.  The first High Councilor told that he exercised two to three times per week golfing and went into some extremes about golfing.  Then the 2nd High Councilor, who was a M.D. stood and said that unless we first carried our gulf bag, not using a cart , hit the ball and then picked up the bag and ran to where the ball had landed, continuing that pace for the complete course, then golfing wasn’t worth a dam as far as exercising was concerned.  He said that in order for exercising to be helpful for the heart the heart rate needed to be increased substantially and maintained for a given period of time.  The first speaker just kind of ducked his head.  It was quite funny!  Mae had Choir practice this afternoon so between the two of us we were gone most of the day.  This evening we had noted that “The Miracle 5th Avenue” would be showing on TV.  We therefore woke Sandy so she could watch it with us.

 

Monday, December 20, 1971

 

                Work went ok despite the union situation.  This evening we took our family to the Ward Family Home Evening Christmas.  It was a very nice evening and the children especially enjoyed it.  it ended with Santa Clause coming to give out treats to the children.

 

Tuesday, December 21, 1971

 

                This evening after getting home from work, we had dinner and then Mae went with me to visit some of my Home Teaching Families.  We only found one family at home.  after getting the clildren to bed this evening, Mae and I stayed up and wrapped more Christmas gifts.

 

Wednesday, December 22, 1971

 

                After dinner this evening we went over to visit Aunt Allison, Uncle Van and their family.  After a short visit we went to the Church House where we attended Tithing Settlement at 7:40PM.  After visiting with our Bishop, I brought the children home and put them to bed.  Mae stayed for Choir practice and road home with one of the other members of the choir.  After getting the children down I figured bills.  They sure do not look good.

 

Thursday, December 23, 1971

 

                At work almost everything that could stopped for a Christmas party.  I only stayed around for a couple of hours and then left and went to work for Larry.  I was only able to work for a couple of hours but every hour counts when we need every cent we can get to take care of my family.  After dinner Mae again went with me to visit some of my families, but we didn’t have any success.  I guess everyone is getting ready for the Holidays.  Mae and I went shopping for a few last minute items for the Holiday.

 

Friday, December 24, 1971

 

                Air West gave us the day off today.  I worked half a day for Larry and then went home and got Mae.  We still had to go grocery shopping for a few items.  We then took all our Family over to visit Sister Powers.  Mae thinks that this might have been a widowed Sister in our Ward that gave Sandy piano lessons.  Again lacking room in my Diary for detail! From there we drove around showing the children the Christmas Lights.  After getting the children down to bed, Mae and I were still up until after Mid-night getting ready for Christmas morning. 

 

Saturday, December 25, 1971

 

                We let the children get us up at 5:30AM.  I remember that I was all in favor of that time although Mae wanted to wait until at least 7:30AM.  We had a very enjoyable Christmas morning.  In my Diary I indicate that we got the children a puppy for Christmas, although neither Mae nor I remember that one. We named the puppy Jennie.  We went over to Larry and Tammie’s ay 2PM for dinner and to enjoy being with their family.  After dinner we watched 35 MM slides, played Rook and just enjoyed visiting. 

 

Sunday, December 26, 1971

 

                I had problems with my Sunday School Class today.   Mae sang in the choir this evening during the Sacrament program.  After meetings we drove to Jack-In-The-Box where we got hamburgers for dinner.  We then went over to the Groscost’s and visited with them. 

 

Monday, December 27. 1971

 

                I went into work early this morning.  I was able to leave early and go to work for Larry for a few hours this afternoon.  We invited the Ashcroft’s over to our place this evening for Family Home Evening.  After putting the children down for the night, Hal, Molley, Mae and I played games.

 

Tuesday, December 28, 1971

 

                This morning at work, seven Programmers were laid off, due to the Airline Strike.  We expect more will be let go in the next little while.  We just keep holding our breath that I am not one of them.  I took Mae out to dinner at the Golden Fork restaurant, for her Birthday, this evening.  She sure is a wonderful Wife and Mother.

 

Wednesday, December 29, 1971

 

                Work continues to get more and more uncertain.  We don’t know for hour to hour just how long it will last.  This evening I held Oral Evaluation with the Home Teachers under me.  Only two out of the four showed up to report.  Mae got things packed for her and the children to fly to Utah in the morning. 

 

Thursday, December 30, 1971

 

                I took Mae and the children to the Airport this morning.  I waited until I saw them on the airplane before I went to the office to work.  I tried to get a plane out this afternoon but there weren’t any openings.  I will have to wait until tomorrow morning and try for that.  Flying Stand By does have some disadvantages. 

 

Friday, December 31, 1971

 

                I was able to get a morning flight out of Phoenix via SLC.  Mae met me at the Salt Lake Airport.  From there we went into Ogden to do a little shopping.  I went with Hal and Bryce sleight ridding.  Kim went with others friends skiing.  Kim took a bad fall while skiing and hurt his leg.  His leg isn’t broken but he will be limping for a while.  This afternoon Bryce and Deanna invited Mae and I to have dinner with them.  After dinner the four of us went to a New Years Eve dance at Weber State Collage.  Again filling in from memory, I believe at this dance there was two dances being held, one upstairs for the youth that are not comfortable with “Dancing” but doing their own thing, and one for the older generation.  During the dance, Terry and his date came down stairs.  Terry made the comment that we couldn’t dance the way they do upstairs.  I took him up on the challenge and carried it one step further.  I told him that I could go out on the floor, without a partner and dance with at least two other girls at the same time, without ether of the girls ever knowing that I was dancing with them.  We went upstairs and I did go onto the floor and danced with not 2 but 3 other girls on the floor and none of the three even recognized that I was actually dancing with them.
