Added Summery

 

                I thought I might write a little summery of 2008 before finishing this month’s journal.  This past year has gone by fast as they all seem to be doing as we grow older.  I remember asking Dad Jensen if time just kept moving faster, to which he answered that it did.  Then he had his stroke and one day when we were visiting he called me to his side and asked if I remembered asking him about time.  I told him that I did and he said, “Well it does continue to go faster as one gets older, until it stops and then you will wish that it would speed up again”.

                This year has had many experiences both good and bad.  We have now been home from our mission almost four years.  Except for the death of our Son-In-Law Terry, those days were just great.  The greatest sorrow since returning home is to find out that two of our sons have lost their testimonies of the truthfulness of the Gospel of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints.  That has weighed very heavy on both Mae and I.  Many, many prayers and tears have been shed over that and we are still very concerned about that.  This summer Jeff has become so discouraged with life that he tried to end it.  We feel that divine intervention stopped the success of that decision.  He and Gail are now separated and Jeff put in for a transfer, with the company that he works for, and will go to Greenville Ohio in January.  We will miss him but feel that this is the best decision for now. 

                Another trial this year has been with my health.  The last of July and the first of August, I begin to have great pain and discomfort in my body and especially in my legs.  For almost 20 years I have had a health issue that I have been able to control through diet.  When ever I have started in pain I could think of what I have been eating that I shouldn’t and make adjustments.  With this, I just couldn’t think of what I was eating that would be the cause of the pain.  The pain became so intense that I started using crouches to walk with to relieve discomfort.  I finally made an appointment with Nancy Litchford and went into her.  She and Fran Morris have teemed up together and visit with people at the Harvest Moon Health store.  When we went in and talked to Nancy she first asked me what I was eating that I had not been eating before.  Mae and I have been taking a 8 oz. glass of water with a teaspoon of non-processed honey, a teaspoon of apple cider vinegar (with Mother) and three shakes of cayenne pepper, each morning.  This is a remedy recommended by Uncle Wayne to keep healthy.  During the 10 months that we have taken this remedy we have not had so much as the sniffles.  When we told Nancy this, she just looked at me and said “da, Ken.  You know that your body will not tolerate that kind of sugar intake. The honey has been building up in your body over those 10 months, plus the vinegar is a by-product of yeast.”  Mae’s health had also been deteriorating, she has yeast overgrowth too, so we are both back on my very strict diet.  The main problem is that we are people persons and it is difficult to be with others and have to explain our diet.  We do count it a blessing that we can control our problems with diet.  We know several that are having much greater problems.

                On the brighter side of things, this year has brought us a new Grand-Daughter by Gail and Jeff, named Rachel Lynn Browning, a Great-Grand-Daughter by JaNae and Brad name of Emma Jean Caries and Mishelle and Scott blessed us with twin Grand-Children, a boy named Kaden and a girl named Kaitlyn Browning.  I had the privilege of blessing Rachel and Emma Jean.  The twins have not been blessed yet.  That now brings our family to 6 children, 19 Grand-Children and 5 Great-Grand-Children.  We feel that they are all GREAT!

                In February we took a short vacation with Sandi and Nike to the Trail Creek Lodge in Soda Springs, Idaho snowmobiling.  We enjoyed it so much that we have planned on doing it again in 2009. 

                In April, Kip Michalles gave me a buck and a doe and that got me into the rabbit raising business.  So far we have put out quite a lot of money and time with very little return.  We hope that next year works out better.  We are also thinking of raising chickens and hope that the out lay is not too great.

                We did take several fishing trips this year.  Our snowmobiles have been fun but they are expensive.

                We took a trip to Zion National Park with Jeff, Gail and the children that was sure fun.  We also took a trip to Flaming Gorge with Nick and Sandi.  All my children had gone together and bought me a guided fishing tour on Flaming Gorge.  Nick also purchased one for him self.  It was a good experience but I only had one strike and missed and Nick caught three beautiful fish.  The other disappointment was that the guide made us sign a form stating that we would release all the fish.  We just got to take pictures and then kiss them goodbye.  It was a fun trip and we saw some beautiful country.
