superintendent on this job.  I had finished the interior finishing of 4 or 5 homes in this subdivision and Ken was very pleased with my work.  I am not sure just when Ken came to me but I will add this detail here.  Ken Osburn came to me with a concern about the last home I had finished for him.  He had sold the home to a couple, without children, and they wanted several changes to the home.  First they wanted the third bedroom converted into an office.  In the office they wanted the cloth closet removed and a book case built in place of the closet.  They then wanted an “Oak U shaped desk” built in and stained a light oak.  From the bottom of the U they wanted a Ecology Box designed wall, constructed out of rough sawn 4” X 6”s, stained a dark oak, from the desk top to the ceiling.   Next they wanted to change the Family Room by adding high gloss black painted chair rail around the walls.  They wanted to cover the lower half of the wall with a Bright Red wall text.  The upper wall and ceiling painted in a bright white.  The floor in the Family room carpeted with a black and white checker board design.  The carpeting in the living room was to be shag white and the covering in the rest of the home was to be in a blond carpet.  Although I didn’t agree with the changes it was ok with me to do the work.  I did make one suggestion that Ken agreed with me and that was that I just remove the closet doors, storing them in the back of the closet and then building shelving that would fit inside the door opening.  This way, if someone in a future date wanted to change the room back into a bedroom we would be able to just remove the shelving and reinstall the doors.  Ken agreed with that idea.  I came home from work today with quite a bad head cold.  We held Family Home Evening and then I went to bed.

                At that conference Oscar McConkie, a younger brother to Bruce R. McConkie, was the visiting authority and main speaker.  Prior to him speaking, he was introduced by our Stake President.  Our stake president said:  “You might recognize that Oscar McConkie is a brother to Bruce R. McConkie, who is a member of the quorum of the 12 apostles.  In the ward which Oscar McConkie & his family lived, he had been called to serve as Elders Quorum President.  The ward was then divided and his first counselor was called to be bishop of the one ward and his second counselor was called to be bishop of the second ward and Oscar McConkie was retained as President of the Elder’s Quorum.  Later, the ward was divided again and again both of his counselors were called to serve as bishops and again Oscar McConkie was retained as the Elder’s Quorum President.  A short time later, his stake president called him in for a personal interview at which time the stake president thoroughly interviewed him as to his personal worthiness.  As the interview concluded, the stake president opened his drawer and pulled out a letter signed by the president of the quorum of the twelve at which he had been directed that under no circumstances should Oscar McConkie be advanced to be a high priest. A short time later, Oscar McConkie was called to the First Quorum of the Seventies.  Had he been ordained a high priest, he could have never been called to this position.  And so, at this time, Oscar McConkie was in the presidency of the first quorum of the seventies.  

                At the conclusion of the introduction our Stake President asked Oscar McConkie, if he felt that it would be appropriate, that he might relate to us the story of his father.

                I will attempt to retell that story, to the best of my ability, as I remember after more than 38 years.  Therefore, this story, without deeper research, can only be taken for just that - a story.

                Father McConkie, as referred to by Oscar, had been called to serve as a bishop while quite a young man with a young family. While serving as Bishop for over 30 years, Mother McConkie passed away.  All of their children were by this time married and raising families of their own.  Quite regularly Father McConkie would still call the family together for a Family Home Evening, to which all would respond.  On one occasion, as the family gathered, father McConkie reminded the family that he had served for over 30 years in his calling and with the passing of his sweet wife, he had been released as Bishop.  He told them that he was now very lonely as he missed this dear companion and felt that he had completed his calling here on earth.  He, therefore, asked the family to join him in prayers asking that Heavenly Father might release him from this earth and join his sweetheart on the other side.  Oscar said that as a family they were a little reluctant but said there was no disagreeing with Father McConkie, and so they all joined in their prayers for this desire.  A couple of weeks later, Father McConkie, again called for a Family Home Evening, at which time he recalled his previous request.  He said that he had a dream and was visited by a massager that told him that he was bothering the Father with his request to pass on.  He was told that in the first place, he had not fulfilled the assignment he had been given to fulfill while here on earth.  The messenger continued telling him that he should have never have been called as Bishop, except the person who was to be called as bishop was not living worthy of the calling and so it became necessary to give the calling to him.  The messenger then told him to look, and as he looked, he saw a large group of spirits that were waiting for their Temple work to be done for them.  The messenger then said, “This was your assignment.  Now quit pestering The Father and do what you were assigned to do.”  So Father McConkie told his family to discontinue praying for him to go home.  In a couple of weeks, Father McConkie again called his family back together.  He explained that he had spent every day at the Genealogy Library but did not know what to do.  Father McConkie therefore asked his family to pray for help to be given to him that he could accomplish his assignment.  After some time had passed, Father McConkie again called his family to meet together.  He said that he just wanted to report as to what progress he was making with his assignment.  He reported that the following week, after he had asked for their help, that as he watched others searching thru the micro-film on the readers, he decided to give it a try and as he reeled through a film he noticed that there was a little green light that moved as he advanced the film.  He watched it until it stopped..  As he had passed the light, he paused and then reversed the film, and would go until he again found the green light moving along with the names.  Reversing again the light would move with the film and then suddenly stop again.  Reversing, he again moved until he found the light.  This time he looked closely at the record where the light had stopped; and he realized that that record was a record on one of his family’s ancestors.  He would then write all the information down and then again start advancing the film and the light would also advance.  Each time the green light stopped, he again repeated the process.  He told his family that following this process, by the end of the day he had found several of these records of those that were in the group he had been shown.  Following this procedure each day he had been able to turn in the records for several hundred names for the Temple work to be done.  Oscar McConkie then told us that Father McConkie continued this routine for quite a long period of time before he again called the family for another meeting.  Father McConkie then reported that he had received another visit from the same messenger.  This time the messenger told him that he had completed the work that he had been assigned and that if he would like to have his original request that it would be ok and his work would be complete.  Before making that decision he was to look; as he did he saw another group much more numerous than the first.  The messenger then told him to look and he saw another man and was told that this group was his assignment.  The messenger said to him that he could see that this person would not live long enough to complete his assignment.  The messenger told Father McConkie that should he want to, he could help him with his assignment.  Oscar McConkie concluded the story by telling us that Father McConkie was still going to the library each day when it was open he still continues to following the little green light..

                I am working on a project in Arizona City.  We are starting work at 6AM, so in order to be there at that time we have to leave Mesa at 4:40AM.  It is also 4:30PM before we get back home.  That schedule is really cutting into my study time as well as family time.  Although I was tired when I get home, we still had a nice Family Home Evening.  Mae had made some wonderful apple pies for our refreshments.

 

Tuesday, May 7, 1974

 

                I worked at Arizona City again today and was very tired when I got home.  After dinner I studied until bed time.  Mae has sure been busy today and by bed time she also looks very tired.

 

Wednesday, May 8, 1974

 

                Mom and Dad Jensen were here when I got home.  They are trying to decide if they should sell their home and then buy something here.  Our instability with my job is sure giving them some turmoil in their decision.  We had planned on taking our neighbors, the Premick’s, to a Open House at our church.  But they called to tell us that Hal had to work late this evening, so they would not be able to go with us.  I am not sure that if this is an excuse or not.

 

Thursday, May 9, 1974

 

                This evening I attended Missionary Training.  President Shaw told us that Terry Elmer was an inactive member of the Church.  At first I felt disappointed but then I realized that she is just as important to our Heavenly Father as any of his other children.  I am really thankful that I may have had just a little part in her life.  I may never know in this life, but I can hope that our lesson may have an eternal benefit for her.

 

Friday, May 10, 1974

 

                I was late getting home from work.  Mae has been on the run all day, getting things ready for the Primary Mom and Miss Party.  Mae didn’t stop until we went to bed at 11:30PM, very tired!

 

 

Saturday, May 11, 1974

 

                Mae was busy from 7AM until 2:30PM preparing for the Mom and Miss program.  I spent most of the day building booths for the Ward Flee Market.  Charles Shumway came over to help me.  We were too tired to go out this evening.

 

Sunday, May 12, 1974

 

                Today is Mother’s Day, so we held our missionary meeting at 6AM, so that we might have more time with our wife’s.  I attended Sunday School with Mae and the children.  We had Mom and Dad Jensen over for dinner, also we had the full time Missionaries, Elder Hill and Elder Ogden here for dinner.  It has been a wonderful day.  I sure love my wonderful Wife and the Mother of my five special children. 

 

Monday, May 13, 1974

 

                Larry and Tammy invited us over to their place for the evening for Family Home Evening with their family.  Everyone went in swimming for a while.  We then had grilled dinner, followed by a lesson.  It was a very enjoyable evening.

 

Tuesday, May 14, 1974

 

                Today Larry had all his lead men meet him at his house and told us that he just didn’t have the work load to keep us going and asked that we each take a dollar per hour cut in wages.  He let all the rest of the crews go, but will still try to keep the lead men working on what work he can find.  After work I went home and got Mae.  She went with me to Scottsdale for me to see Dr. Swanstrum.  As we drove it gave us time to talk about the future.  Things look a little dim right now.  When we got home, we had dinner.  I then laid down for a couple of hours and slept.  I got up for an hour and then went back to bed.  Mae had some Ladies over this evening.

 

Wednesday, May 15, 1974

 

                After work I laid down for a few minutes. Mom and Dad Jensen came by for a few minutes just to visit.  After we ate dinner I went over to Larry Packer home to look at some remodeling that he wants done.  When I got back home, Mae helped me study my missionary lessons for a few minutes.  After getting the children down, Mae and I started to bed.  We talked until quite late about our work and financial situation.

 

Thursday, May 16, 1974

 

                This evening I got a call from John Howard at Commercial Security Bank in Ogden.  He made me a job offer to come back to work for them.  We talked for some time.  I asked what the position would be that they wanted me to fill.  He told me that it would be the Supervisor of the 3rd shift.  I told him that I really wasn’t very interested in working mid-night shift.  He told me that they only wanted me there until I could train someone to fill that position and then I would go into the programming staff.  He said that something had come up and they were now in immediate need of someone that know the work and could step right into that spot with very little training.  He expected that I would only be on that shift for a year or two at the longest.  We talked about the housing market, which right now is not good in Arizona.  To that he assured me that if I would put our house on the market and made the move in two weeks, then if had problems selling the house, the bank would either help me out with payments or would take over the house and they would sell it.  I asked about the wage and when he told me what they were offering, I told him that I couldn’t accept that low of wage.  He said that the cost of living was higher in Arizona than in Utah, to which I told him that for our family it would not be that much cheaper.  He raised the offering amount some.  I asked about moving expenses, to which he asked me to get three bids and send them to him.  The Bank would pay the lowest bid.  I asked about the time frame that they wanted me to be there and he said that he know that I would at least require two weeks and that would be ok.  I ended the conversation by telling him that I would like the weekend to make my decision.  I have very mixed feeling about this decision.  I would like to move back to Utah, yet I would like to finish this Mission Call.  My main concern is that we do the will of our Father in Heaven.

 

Friday, May 17, 1974

 

                I started at 8am this morning getting booths set up for the Ward’s Auction and Flee Market.  Those that were to be in charge of the Fry Bread canceled out.  With Mae’s help we went ahead with the Fry Bread booth, which turned out very successful, thanks to Mae.  By the time we finished and got everything cleaned up for the night, it was 9:30PM when I got home.  I was very tired.

 

Saturday, May 18, 1974

 

                I went back up to the Flee Market at 9AM and worked until 4:30PM before we had everything cleaned up and put away.  Although it was a lot of work, overall I think that it was a successful project in raising funds for the Ward Budget.  After going home I took a nap.  Jim and Marrion Fraizer and Mae and I went out to eat this evening.

 

Sunday, May 19, 1974

 

                I started a special Fast this morning, trying to get inspiration as to our decision about moving back to Utah.  I visited with the Stake Mission President who said that he would support our decision, but hated to have me leave.  Then the First Councilor in the Stake Mission Presidency visited with me.  He felt that I had been called to serve this mission and he felt that it would be a mistake to leave.  I made an appointment and visited with Bishop Reese.  At that visit, he strongly felt that we should accept the job offer and make the move back to Utah.  When we went to bed we still felt uncertain as to what we should do.

 

Monday, May 20, 1974

 

                I stayed home from work this morning.  By the time I called John Howard, we decided to accept the job offer.  This means we will be in Ogden by the 30th of June.  This afternoon I mowed the lawn before we went to Mom and Dad Jensen’s for dinner and Family Home Evening. Before we left, Dad Jenson told me that he felt that we were making the right decision.  When we went to bed, I still had an uneasy feeling as to whether or not we are right.  I sure hope we are doing what the Lord would have us do.

                As I rewrite this entry almost 39 years later, I still wonder about that decision.  Things didn’t work out like we had hoped.  We lost our home in Arizona because it didn’t sell and the Bank only helped us with two house payments to offset our loss.  I found out after two years that there were never plans to move me off the mid-night shift.  John Howard and I parted with very bitter feelings towards each other.  It took me a long time before I could even hear a commercial on the radio about the bank that I didn’t get a sick feeling.  In talking with Dad Jensen about forgiveness, he asked me if I felt that if I were to meet John on the street, if I thought I could at least shake his hand and be friendly, to which I said I thought I could.  I have never had that opportunity but I still think I could.  I can say this much, it sure made some major changes in our life.

 

Tuesday, May 21, 1974

 

                After getting home from work, I went over to visit with Doug Caldwell about selling our home.  He feels that we should be able to sell it for $30,000.  I know that the housing market is really down in Arizona at this time.  I repaired my saw this evening.  Later Mae and I went out with Steve and Darlene Smith to Ferrill’s Ice Cream and then we played miniature golf.  We had a good time.

 

Wednesday, May 22, 1974

 

                I called and had three moving companies come out and give us a bid to move our belongings to Utah.  The lowest bid was for $1,395.00.  After considering the bids, I think I can make pretty good money if I rent a U-Haul truck and move ourself.  I decided to call the Bank and see if they will pay me to move at the lowest bid.  I sure do love Mae and appreciate all she does for me.

 

Thursday, May 23, 1974

 

                I sold my van today.  I sure would like to have been able to keep it, but we need the money.  I tended Scott while Mae and the other children went to Primary.  John called me and said that the Bank would pay me $1,400.00 for moving expences.

 

Friday, May 24, 1974

 

                I started work at 5:30AM and worked until 8:30AM finishing up a job for Larry.  I came home and changed and went to Dr. Swanstrum for a treatment.  This afternoon I started finding boxes for packing.  I also bought me a pair of Justin Cow Boy boots for $52.00.  This evening we invited Lois and DeLoy Porter, Rick and Debbie Berkland, Jim and Marrion Fraizer, and Lonnie and Margie Owens over to our home.  We sure had an enjoyable evening. We are going to miss these friends.

 

 

Saturday, May 25, 1974

 

                Jim Fraizer came over this morning to help me pack.  I offered Jim our house if he would just take over the payments.  He and Marion gave it some thought but later decided that they shouldn’t do it.  Mae took the children to a carnival this afternoon.  This evening we let Sandy invite some of her friends over for a Taffy Pull.

 

Sunday, May 26, 1974

 

                We attended our Wards meeting today as normal.  We had expected Jim and Marrion to come over for the evening to visit but instead of them showing up, Marrion Fraizer, Lois Porter and Helen Freeman had planned a surprise Open House for us.  There were many from the Ward that came.  It was sure a great evening.

 

Monday, May 27, 1974

 

                We spent most of the day packing.  Mae and I went down to a furniture store and bought a new table and chair set.  This evening we met the Braithwaite’s at the Pioneer Park for Family Home Evening.  We finished the evening by going to Farrell’s for ice cream.

 

Tuesday, May 28, 1974

 

                I rented a moving truck just before 7AM.  We spent most of the day packing our belongings into the truck.  We did have several interruptions of friends and neighbors wishing us well.  It was after 11PM when we finished and got to bed.

 

Wednesday, May 29, 1974

 

                I was up and started packing at 5:30AM.  Jim Fraizer came over and helped all day.  We finished loading the moving van at 2:30PM.  Mae had her hair done and when she came back both she and I went over to Dr. Swanstrum’s.  He asked me what my problem was.  I told him that I really wasn’t hurting anywhere but I had been used to getting a treatment at least monthly and now with moving I just wanted him to check my back.  He did and after going over my back he said, “Well it looks like you just wasted $20.00.”  At 6PM we went to the Stake President’s office and got our Temple Recommends signed.  We had been invited to eat with Tonnie Aldridge this evening.  After a very nice dinner and a nice visit we said our goodbyes to them.  Larry and Tammy invited us to sleep the night with them.

 

Thursday, May 30, 1974

 

                Although we got up at 5:30AM, by the time we ate, took some pictures and said our goodbyes it was 7:30 before we got away.  I don’t remember the details but the entry indicates that we ran out of gas in the moving van just before we got to Flagstaff, Arizona.  North of Flagstaff, as we went through the intersection to Tuba City,  I saw two Missionaries get out of the back of a pickup and wave to the driver.  It was too late for me to stop the van but I thought that Mae might see them and stop.  When she didn’t I pulled over and stopped and of course Mae stopped behind me.  I told her that there were two Missionaries trying to catch a ride, and she should go back for them.  She wasn’t sure so I had her stay with the truck and Mike and David who were ridding with me and I drove back and offered then a ride.  We drove up to the truck and I had them ride in the truck with me and Mae and the children road in the car.  I took them as far as the Gap turn off and explained that because of the moving van we were going by way of Page.  They said that they wanted to go by the way of Jacob Lake, as they had a contact there.  I stopped and let them out.  Mike and David again got in the truck with me and we continued on.  We did stop at dam when we got to Page for a few minutes before continuing on.  When we got to Kanab, I honked at the same two Missionaries that I had given a ride.  They had told me that they traveled by hitch hiking all the time.  They found it interesting that they would leave their apartment in Tuba City, seeing a hitch hiker already trying to catch a ride, so they walk on down the road a block, catch a ride and travel to several locations, as much as 300 miles before returning home in the evening and still see the same person in the same spot, still waiting for a ride.  We got to Kanab about 8:30PM so we stayed the night in a motel.

 

 Friday, May 31, 1974

 

                We weren’t very worried about time this morning so took our time getting away.  As you leave Kanab, there is a cave that is advertised so we stopped to see it.  It is a Tourist Trap, but we didn’t have any money to spend so we soon left.  We stopped a couple of times along the way just to get out and stretch our legs, but still arrived at Sunset by 8PM.  We just left the truck in the driveway and went to Mom and Dad Jensen’s in Layton, where we stayed the night with them.

 

Saturday, June 1, 1974

 

                When we got up and had breakfast we went over to the home in Sunset to unload the moving van.  This home was the home that belonged to Mae’s parents.  When we had made the decision to move to Utah, Mae called and told her parents.  They said that the family that had been renting the house were about to move and asked if we would be interested in renting it from them.  Mae was especially excited to move back to the home she had been raised in.  Many of our family came to help us move in.  There was Irven, Bob, Grant, Kathy, Bryce, Georgia and Rick as well as Mom and Dad Porter.  Deanna came and got all the children to tended them for us.  With all that help we soon had the truck emptied and most things put away.  Mae wasn’t feeling very well this afternoon so I had Mom Porter take her to their home.  Even though there will be many things for Mae to rearrange and put in different locations, we had everything ready so that we could sleep as a family in the home tonight.

 

Sunday, June 2, 1974

 

                Scott didn’t sleep last night and he kept both Mae and I up also.  Mae still isn’t feeling very well.  Mae and I slept in this morning.  When we got up we realized that we didn’t have the children’s clothes unpacked so we all missed the morning meetings.  Today is Hal’s birthday and Mom Porter invited all the family over for dinner.

 

Monday, June 3, 1974

 

                I started work for Commercial Security Bank this morning.  I spent the day getting re-acquainted with the changes in everything from management to the new building.  The processing system I am fairly well acquainted with as I helped with the development of it when I worked for the Bank before.  I didn’t get home from work until7PM as I stayed around to get acquainted with the night shift.  Mom and Dad Porter, Rick and Georgia and Terry joined us for Family Home Evening, even though Mae is still sick with the flue.  Mom and Dad helped me put the children to bed.

 

Tuesday, June 4, 1974

 

                Mae is sick with the flu this morning.  I got quite a bit done around house before I left for work.  Sandy help me quite a bit, especially with the baby.  I went to work at 5:30PM and worked 11 ½ hours.

 

Wednesday, June 5, 1974

 

                I got up at 10AM.  Mae still isn’t feeling the best.  I had to get a new coil put on the car.  I then got a few other things done around the house before going into work at 5:30PM.  We had a lot of problems at work this evening.

 

Thursday, June 6, 1974

 

                Mae still isn’t feeling very well.  She went over to Doctor Kilburn for a treatment.  He said that she has quite a bit of infection.  I didn’t get to bed until 6AM and so I slept until noon.  I got some Bank papers filled out and took them over to the main office in Ogden.  I then went into work.

 

Friday, June 7, 1974

 

                Mae still isn’t feeling well so went into the Doctor.  He said that she has infection in her eyes and in her mouth.  He feels that it was caused by the flu.  I got a few things done around the house.  We were invited over to McDonald’s for dinner this evening.  (I believe this is Melba Miskin’s daughter and son-in-law.)  I went into work at 8:30PM.  Mae cleaned house all evening.

 

Saturday, June 8, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home from work until after 7am and with just a little to eat I went to bed at 8AM.  I got up at 1PM.  Mae and I went over to see Georgia and Rick’s new baby boy.  They are going to name him Bryan.  Both Mother and Child seem to be doing well.  Mae and I then went grocery shopping.  This evening Bob and Carroll invited us to attend a movie with them.  After the show started, if Mae and I would have had a car, we would have got up and left the show.  The main character was so vulgar that we just set and cringed.  We were very disgusted and did not enjoy the show at all.

 

Sunday, June 9, 1974

 

                This morning we attended Stake Conference.  When we got home I called Mom and Dad Jensen in Idaho Falls to see how they are doing.  Bob and Carroll invited Irven, Sandy, Mae and I for dinner.  After dinner we attended our Sacrament meetings.  We then again gathered at Bob and Carroll’s and the six of us played games.

 

Monday, June 10, 1974

 

                Mae and I mowed and trimmed the lawns this morning.  I then assembled the kids slide and swings.  Bob and I went out to the Hill Field Golf course golfing.  I am not much at golfing but I enjoyed being with Bob.  Mae and the kids went over to see Georgia and their new baby.  Work did not go very well tonight.

 

Tuesday, June 11, 1974

 

                It was late when I got home from work and to bed.  I got up 1PM and then I helped Mae wash the house windows before I went back into work.  Mae and the children went to Mom and Dad Porter’s for the evening.  Work went much better tonight.

 

Wednesday, June 12, 1974

                I got up about 11:30 this morning.  Brother Jenson of the Clearfield 1st ward called and asked if I would speak in there Ward this Sunday.  I washed a couple more windows for Mae.  Mae got her hair done today.  I went over to Vern Killburn’s for a treatment before I went to work this evening.

 

Thursday, June 13, 1974

 

                I didn’t get much done today.  I got up at 11:30AM and figured bills.  Bob, Grant and I went up to Hill field golfing.  When I got home I finished figuring the bills.  I took a nap after dinner and then went into work.

 

Friday, June 14, 1974

 

                After waking up, Mae and I went over to Clearfield and bought a few things for the house.  When we got back home I repaired the front room drapes so that they opened and closed better.  Sandy and I then played ball for a few minutes before I went to work.  Work went well tonight and I had a fairly easy evening.

 

Saturday, June 15, 1974

 

                I got off work at 6:30AM and so I stopped at Bob’s bakery and visited with him, before going home.  He and I got visiting and I stayed to long which caused Mae to worry.  After getting some rest, Bob, Robert and I went fishing this afternoon.  I hadn’t recorded much about the fishing trip except that Bob caught 1 nice size fish.  While I was gone, Mae attended a shower and then took the children over to visit Georgia and Rick.

 

Sunday, June 16, 1974

 

                I got up early this morning to study for my talk.  I attended Priesthood meeting and then came home and got my family to attend Sunday school.     Shellie, Mike and David all had parts in the Sunday School’s Father’s Day program.  Mae had invited Dad & Mom Porter and Terry over for a Father’s Day dinner.  Mom and Dad Porter took our children to their home after dinner.  They did this as I had a speaking assignment in the Clearfield 1st Ward Sacrament meeting this afternoon.  It was interesting as there were three speakers before me on the program.  As the 1st speaker spoke he gave the 1st part of my talk, I thought that was ok I could just skip that section.  Then as the 2nd speaker talked she gave the 2nd part of my talk.  Again I looked at my notes and I still had the last of my thoughts to rely on.  Then the 3rd speaker used the last part of my thoughts.  When I got up I just had to rely on the Lord’s help and somehow I came up with a talk as I stood there.  I didn’t remember just what I said but I seem to have kept the attention of the congregation as they seem to all be lessoning and after the meeting I had several express appreciation for my talk.  After Sacrament meeting Shirley and Kent Murdock invited us to stop at their home and visit for a while.

 

Monday, June 17, 1974

 

                After getting up and having breakfast, Mae put a picnic lunch together and we took all our family up South Fork canyon to enjoy getting out in the beautiful spring weather.  We all relaxed and also did a little fishing.  It was very nice but no one caught any fish.  We got back home in time for me to take a short nap before going to work.

 

Tuesday, June 18, 1974

 

                It was late when I got home from work this morning.  I slept most of the day.  After getting up I washed a couple more windows and then it was time to go back to work.

 

Wednesday, June 19, 1974

 

                I again went to sleep for a little while after getting home from work.  Mae and I went shopping for Shellie’s Birthday presents.  We also bought a wedding gift for a reception we are invited to, plus I bought Mae a food grinder as Wedding Anniversary gift.  We went out to eat for our Wedding Anniversary.  When we got home the children (mainly under Sandy’s direction) had surprised us by decorating the front room for us.  We sure have special loving children.  I soon had to go back into work.

 

Thursday, June 20, 1974

 

                Mae and I went to the Salt Lake Temple this morning to see Melba McMillian and Earl Christenson married.  Larry and Tammy stopped into our place, just before I had to leave for work.  At 8:30pm I took the rest of the night off from work so that Mae and I could attend Melba and Earl’s wedding reception. 

 

Friday, June 21, 1974

 

                This morning Larry, Tammy, Mae and I went through a session at the Ogden Temple.  This afternoon we took all our family plus Cindy (Hal and Jean’s daughter) and Sherrie (Bob and Carroll’s daughter) to Como Springs in Morgan, swimming for Shellie’s Birthday.  We all had fun and didn’t get home until 8PM.  I then had to go straight into work.

 

Saturday, June 22, 1974

 

                I got up at 1PM today.  I went back over to the bank for a little while and then got a haircut.  After getting the kids to bed, we left Sandy to baby sit for us.  Mae and I then went over to Bob and Carroll’s.  From there the four of us went over to some other friends of theirs.  When we got home Mae and I stayed up talking until 1:30AM before going to bed.

 

Sunday, June 23, 1974

 

                We attended Priesthood meeting and Sunday school this morning.  After getting home I studied for my talk and then took a nap.  Mom and Dad Porter and Bob and Carroll attended our Sacrament meeting as Mae and I spoke in Sacrament meeting today.  Both Mom and Dad Porter and Bob and Carroll came over to our place just to visit for a while after the meeting.  Dad Porter isn’t feeling very well today.  Scott got his first tooth in today.

 

Monday, June 24, 1974

 

                This morning Robert, Bob’s boy and I went fishing.  We went up on South Fork of the Ogden River.  I caught one fish and that was about all the luck we had today.  I went into work this evening.

 

Tuesday, June 25, 1974

 

                When I got up, I went over and bought the stain and varnish so I can finish the corner table I have built for Mae.  I also tended Scott while Mae and Shellie went shopping. 

 

Wednesday, June 26, 1974

 

                I didn’t get up until noon.  Hal had called and wanted to know if we could go swimming with them this afternoon.  Mae was at Relief Society meeting and so when she got we talked it over and called Hal.  We all went up to Como in Morgan.  We all had a good time being together.  When we got home I had to hurry and leave for work.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart.  I put in my Diary a short note that Mae bought a coat today.

 

Thursday, June 27, 1974

 

                After getting up today, I worked on Mae’s corner table.  Mae had a P.T.A. meeting to attend this evening.  Mae also got her hair done this afternoon.  Tonight at work I ran the shift by myself, for the first time.  We did have some problems and was therefore late with the delivery schedule.

 

Friday, June 28, 1974

 

                I got home from work at 5AM and then got ready to go fishing with Mr. Turnner.  We didn’t have any luck.  I got home from fishing and went to bed about 10AM.  Irven and Sandy came over as well as Bob to talk to me about a business opportunity.  Irven had seen some property west of Ridge Road in Syracuse for sale at quite a low price.  I brought up a couple of concerns.  First: it is in a swampy area almost Lake level, with no place for drainage.  Second: there is no utility west of Ridge Road in that area.  Irven felt that in time utilities will probably be brought in.  I guess I squashed the deal with my concerns.  (Even today in 2016, Irven still brings up that opportunity that we passed up.  If my memory is correct of the peace of property he was talking about, it is still only a piece of property where a few horses are pastured, even though some of the property around that are is now in housing.  Maybe someday, someone will find a way to drain the area and we might find that we missed out on a great deal!)  Mom and Dad Jensen bought a home in Layton today.  While we were in Arizona they came down there a couple of winters.  They would stay with another couple from Idaho Falls that had a winter home in Mesa.  I guess that with us being down there they had got talking about moving down.  One day while working at the Idaho Falls temple Dad had mentioned to another worker that he was thinking about the move and would probably sell their home there in I.F.  The next day the fellow ask Dad what he would be asking for the home.  Dad hadn’t put much thought into it and quickly figured what the home had cost him to build, not considering the inflation.  He just came up with a rough figure.  The next day the man said that he would like to buy it and would give him what he had said.  Well, Dad wouldn’t back down or refigure once he had given a price so he sold the house.  They then found out that we were moving back to Utah.  With us moving, they really didn’t want to be in Arizona alone.  They stopped in Layton and looked at a home and so bought it.  Over all it worked out fairly well for them in the long run but there is quite a story about that.

 

Saturday, June 29, 1974

 

                I stopped on the way home and visited with Bob.  I don’t think that we are interested in Irven and Sandy’s business proposal.  Mom and Dad Jensen stopped over for a few minutes but decided that they needed to go back to I.F. and start getting thing finished up there.  I got up at 12:30PM.  Bob, Irven, Craig (a friend of Bob) and I with our families all went up on the Weber River fishing.  I think that we took our families to the Morgan City park where they enjoyed the park while the four of us men enjoyed fishing.  We had a good time.

 

Sunday, June 30, 1974

 

                We woke up to late for me to attend Priesthood meeting.  So we had breakfast as a family and then Sandy, Shellie and I attended Sunday school.  We attended Sacrament meeting with Georgia and Rick as they are blessing their baby today.  I had the privilege of standing in the in the circle as Rick gave Bryan a name and pronounced a Blessing on their first son.  Mom and Dad Porter invited all the family to their place for dinner after the meeting ended.  This evening we got together with John and Linda Willerton and Bob and Pauline Hein just for visit.

 

Monday, July 1, 1974

 

                I slept most of the day, being quite sick with a cold.  I did get up at 11AM as we need new tires on the car.  I bought a set of 4 Pirelli Steel Belted all season tires for our car.  I sure hope they are worth is as they cost us $300 for the set.  I did go into work this evening although I still fill a little under the weather.

 

Tuesday, July 2, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home from work until 10AM.  I slept for a little while and then Mae and I drove to Salt Lake to see about buying a table saw.  We didn’t find anything that I liked that was within our budget.  I had suggested to the bank that we build two special cabinets for the sorting of check that were rejected by the check reader.  What we are doing now is too disorderly and many errors are occurring.  I had drawn designs and they were approved for me to build.  The bank will pay me for them.  I had figured the material costs and added some for my labor.  I figured my labor would cover the cost of a table saw.  I have also visited with our neighbor Tommy Thompson and got permission to use his garage as a working area to build in.  After returning from Salt Lake I again laid down and slept for a while before going into work.

 

Wednesday, July 3, 1974

 

                This morning I got home from work a little after 8AM.  I slept until 2PM and then got up and went into Ogden to purchase the materials for the cabinets.  I then went to Mc Entire and Hilton Lumber, in Clearfield, where I purchased a table saw that they had on a pretty good sale.  This evening Mom and Dad Jensen stopped in to visit.  They have decided to purchase a home in Layton.

 

Thursday, July 4, 1974

 

                I never got home from the work until 9:45AM.  When I got home Mae announced that Scott’s second tooth had came through.   I slept until noon and then all of us went to Uncle Verl and Aunt Beth’s for a Porter Family gathering.  We had a very enjoyable afternoon.  This evening Georgia and Rick came to our place.  The four of us played Rook and enjoyed being together.  After going to bed tonight, Mae and I laid and talked for some time.  I sure love her.  I didn’t have to go into work this evening as it is a holiday and the Banks are closed.

 

Friday, July 5, 1974

 

                I went into town this morning to take care of some business.  After returning home I put my new table saw together.  I then put another coat of finish on the corner table I built for Mae.  I went into work at 9PM.

 

Saturday, July 6, 1974

 

                I worked until 11am, as we had several problems during the shift.  Mae tended Irven and Sandy’s children today for them today.  Even with the added noise of the children I was still able to sleep.  Mae sure tries everything to keep the children quite.  We have found that if we put a fan inside our bedroom that it not only helps cool the room down a little but the noise also drowns out the children’s playing.  After getting up and having dinner, Mae and I went to John and Linda Willerton’s for the evening.

 

Sunday, July 7, 1974

 

                We were able to attend all our church meetings today.  Fast and Testimony meeting was especially enjoyable today.  Mae had a lot of phone calls today, plus we called and talked with Jim and Marion Frazer for quite a while.  We then took our family on a walk through the park and enjoyed them running and playing.  Later Mae and I visited with our neighbor’s two homes south, Rod and Linda Taylor and Linda’s mother Sister Miskin’s.

 

Monday, July 8, 1974

 

                To day I finished the corner table I have built for Mae.  I then mowed our lawn.  I also got about ½ of the cabinets cut out that I am building for the bank.  At 6 pm RaNae, Richard Barney and their family came over.  We ate down to the park and then held Family Home evening with them.  We had a very nice evening.  I went into work at 11:30PM. 

 

Tuesday, July 9, 1974

 

                It was after 10 am when I got from work.  After getting some sleep, I worked on the cabinets for the bank.  At 8pm I set down and watched a show on TV.  At 9 pm Mae joined me for a little while and then at 10pm I laid down for another hour of sleep before going in to work at the bank.

 

 

Wednesday, July 10, 1974

 

                I got off work this morning a little after 8 am.  I went to bed and slept until 3pm.  I worked on the cabinets for the bank most of the evening and then I got a couple more hours sleep before going back to work.

 

Thursday, July 11, 1974

 

                I got home from work at 8am and soon went to bed.  I slept until 2pm but when I woke I was sick to my stomach.  I was able to go back to sleep and slept until 6pm.  Mae and I got things packed to go to a Hill Family reunion tomorrow.  I went into work at 11:30pm.

 

 Friday, July 12, 1974

 

                I got off work this morning at 6am.  When I got home I slept for a couple of hours and then we got things packed in the car and started for Idaho.  With the car packed so full of camping gear we had little room for the children and soon they were fighting and we were all getting upset with each other.  By the time we got to Brigham City I stopped knowing that we had to make some changes or return home and not attend the Reunion.  There was a U-Haul dealer there and I checked about renting a small trailer.  The price was very reasonable, especially when I rented it in Brigham City and would return it to Ogden.  I questioned the attendant, telling him that I was going to take it clear to Idaho and back.  He said he didn’t care where I took the trailer but the charge was for picking up the trailer in Brigham City and returning it to Ogden.  We transferred all our camping equipment to the trailer, giving us whole car for our family.  It sure changed the feeling and we all really enjoyed the trip.  We got to Idaho Falls about 4pm and then went on to Meadow Lake, getting there about 6:30pm.

 

Saturday, July 13, 1974

 

                I couldn’t sleep so I finally got up, at 4am, so that Mae could sleep.  A little later I went out fishing but had very little luck.  It was a very enjoyable day being with the extended Hill family.  During the day I took the children out fishing.  Sandy caught 2 fish and David caught 1 fish.  This evening all the Hill Family gathered for a family program around the campfire.

 

Sunday, July 14, 1974

 

                We got up about 7AM.  Everyone put their fish together and had a great breakfast.  We enjoyed visiting with family members until we left to go back to Idaho Falls.  We attended Sacrament meeting with Mom and Dad Jensen.  Dad Jensen had been asked to speak in Sacrament meeting today and did very well.  We enjoyed visiting with Mom and Dad while we played a game of Rook before we went to bed.

 

Monday, July 15, 1974

 

                Dad Jensen had to go into the Hospital to have some test taken this morning.  We stayed until Mom and Dad returned home.  We then left for home getting there about 4PM.  We unpacked and put things away.  After some dinner I laid down and slept for a while before going into work at 11:30PM.

 

Tuesday, July 16, 1974

 

                After getting home from work this morning, I went to bed.  Mae has been very busy today.  She cleaned the house and with Sandy’s help she did the washing.  She and Shellie picked some cherries and with the girls help they got them canned along with many other little jobs.  I got up at 2pm and after having something to eat; I went out in Brother Tompson’s garage and worked on the cabinets I am building for the bank.  I worked until 9pm on that job.  At 11:30 I went to work at the bank.

 

Wednesday, July 17, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I got home shortly after 8am.  I slept until 2pm and then worked on the cabinets until 5pm.  At 6pm I joined the management and supervisor staff of Commercial Security Bank’s Data Processing Department for a dinner and seminar with management from Idaho Bank and Trust. I B & T is a sister bank with C. S. B. and we do all their Data Processing for them.

 

Thursday, July 18, 1974

 

                We had problems at work last night and I didn’t get home until after 10AM.  I went down to Mom and Dad Porter’s to sleep so that the children wouldn’t wake me.  After waking I spent the rest of the evening with Mae and my children, until I had to go to work again.

 

Friday, July 19, 1974

 

                Work went better last night and I got home at a reasonable hour.  After getting some sleep, I worked on the cabinets for a while.  Mae and I then drove down to Bob and Carroll’s to visit with them.  We came home and I got some more sleep before going into work.

 

Saturday, July 20, 1974

 

                I got off work at 6 this morning.  When I got home I laid down and slept until 9am.  At 10:15am Bob, Grant and I left for Idaho Falls.  We got to Mom and Dad Jensen’s just before 2PM.  We went right to work and had all their furniture and belongings loaded and left for Utah by 5pm.  I drove the moving van back.  We stopped in Downey and got something to eat.  We finished unloading the van and I got home about 10PM.  Mom and Dad bought a home at 419 West Paul, in Layton.

                What happened is that Mom and Dad got thinking about the cold weather in Idaho.  We were living in Arizona and while I worked for the air line, they enjoyed flying down to the warm Arizona weather.  When I left the air line it was a long trip to drive down.  They came down to Arizona a couple of winters and stayed with some of their friends that lived in Mesa during the winter and in Idaho Falls during the summer.  They had talked about selling their home in I.F. and buying a nice trailer home in Mesa.  One day while at the Idaho Falls Temple, where both of them were working as Ordinance Workers, Dad Jensen was visiting with another Brother and expressed their thought about selling their home.  The Brother asked Dad what he would ask for his home.  Dad, without really thinking about the inflation facture, gave a figure, really way below market value.  The next day the Brother came back and told Dad that he would give him cash for the house.  Well, Dad wouldn’t go back on his word so he sold their home to this couple. They called a trailer company in Mesa and made an offer on a home and put a down payment on it.  Almost the next day after the closing of the home in I.F. and the agreement to purchase the mobile home, I called to tell them that we were moving back to Utah.  When I called them, they were ready to get in their car to come to Arizona.  As Mom and Dad drove south, they decided that they really didn’t want to move to Arizona, if none of their family weren’t going to be living there.  As they were driving they discussed their options. When they got to Layton they stopped at a Real Estate office to see what was available in the area.  I think they were only shown a couple of homes when they decided on the one we moved their things into.

 

Sunday, July 21, 1974

 

                I attended Priesthood meeting and then came back to get the children for Sunday school.  Mae worked in the library this morning and so had gone in earlier.  I wasn’t feeling very well during Sunday School so I came home after the opening exercises and laid down.  I still wasn’t feeling the best so after dinner I again laid down for a nap.  Mom and Dad Jensen stopped over this evening for a few minutes.  I believe they are feeling ok with the way things have turned out.  After they left and the children were in bed, Mae and I took a walk, just to have some time together and talk.

 

Monday, July 22, 1974

 

                I worked all day at Mom and Dad Jensen’s, getting them moved in and everything arranged in their new home.  I came home at 4PM and was so tired that I went to bed, so that I could get some sleep before going into work.  Mae and the children joined with Mom and Dad Porter, Rick, Georgia,Bryce and Deanna and they all went up the cannon for dinner and Family Home Evening.

 

Tuesday, July 23, 1974

 

                Larry and Tammy came by today while I was sleeping.  They left Lori to spend a few days with Sandy.  I worked on the cabinets after getting up at 3PM until 9:30PM when I quite to go to work.

 

Wednesday, July 24, 1974

 

                I got home this morning at 8:30.  After some breakfast I went to bed and slept until 4pm.  Mae and the children went into Ogden to see the Pioneer parade.  They then went down to Mom and Dad Porters.  I went back into work at 7 this evening.

 

Thursday, July 25, 1974

 

                I worked most of the day on the cabinets.  I did call the Real Estate agent in Mesa I am trying to work with.  She told me that the appraisal on our home in Mesa, only came back for $25,000.  At that price we will not get anything for our equity in the home.  I am not sure what we should do.  Mae and I went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s to visit for a while.  They have really been working hard on their home and it sure looks nice.

 

Friday, July 26, 1974

 

                I figured our bills before coming home from work.  I went to bed after getting something for breakfast and slept until 3PM.  I went into Ogden to take care of some items.  I had planned on getting back home so that I could get some more sleep.  However when I got home Jerry and Linda were there at home.  They stopped just to visit.  It was 11:15PM before they left and I had to leave for work at 11:30PM.

 

Saturday, July 27, 1974

 

                I was late getting home from work this morning.  I slept until 5:30pm.  After super Mae and I drove over to look at a home that Georgia & Rick are interested in.  We then picked up the children and drove over to see Mom and Dad Jensen.  After getting home and putting the small ones down to sleep, Sandy, Mae and I played some Rummy.

 

Sunday, July 28, 1974

 

                I didn’t sleep very well last night.  I attended Priesthood meeting, Sunday School, Stake Priesthood meeting and Stake High Priesthood meeting, along with Sacrament meeting this evening.  They were all very good but by the end of the day I was tired.  This evening I called and visited with Jim and Marion about the possibility of them buying our home in Mesa.  They said that they would like to but they couldn’t offer us anymore than $2,000 for our equity.  (I really felt that it was almost a slap in the face, but as thing later worked out we would have been better getting that much out of it compared to what we ended up losing.)

 

Monday, July 29, 1974

 

                I woke up at 4am and couldn’t go back to sleep.  I read from the Pearl of Great Price and from the Book of Mormon.  At 8am I went out and started working on the cabinets, working for about 5 hours.  Sandy graduated from Primary today.  We had a nice Family Home Evening before putting the children to bed.  I went into work about 11pm.

 

Tuesday, July 30, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home from work this morning until 10:15.  I ate and went to bed.  This afternoon Mae and I went with Bishop Delmar and Sister June Moulton to the Ogden Temple.  Following the session we went out to eat at the China Night.  It was sure an enjoyable time being with Bishop and Sister Moulton.  Although they are old enough to be our parents, they are very special friends.

 

Wednesday, July 31, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I got home early this morning.  However, I didn’t get much sleep, as I had to go back into work at noon.  The bank is hosting a lunch and meeting with officers from Idaho Bank and Truss, IB&T.  After returning from that meeting, I went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s and hung the large mirror that we had bought them for their wedding.  I came back home and got a little sleep before going back into work.

 

Thursday, August 1, 1974

 

                I was late getting home from work because of a bad night.  I slept until 3pm.  I was going to go down to the Stake farm to weed, but I couldn’t find it.  Mae and I went over to visit the Taylor’s.  I cut the boy’s hair before they went to bed.

 

Friday, August 2, 1974

 

                We had problems at work again tonight, so I was late getting home.  This evening Mae and I went with Dennis and Eleanor Ewing to Mae and Eleanor’s 15 YEAR, Davis High school class reunion.  It was nice and being with Dennis and Eleanor was a lot of fun.

 

Saturday, August 3, 1974

 

                I was late getting home from work this morning.  After getting up, I spent most of the day working on the cabinets.  This evening Bryce and Deanna stopped in just to visit.

 

Sunday, August 4, 1974

 

                I woke up at 4:30 this morning and wrote my talk for Sunday school.  Mae, Sandy and I were asked to speak in the Opening Exercises of Sunday School today.  This evening Bob and Pauline Hine came up to visit with us.  They are now living in Salt Lake.

 

 Monday, August 5, 1974

 

                I got up at 4:30 this morning and started working on the cabinets.  I finished the first set of cabinet, before I went into the office for a meeting.  The meeting lasted from 8AM until 11AM.  Jim and Marion Fraizer got to our place about 10AM.  Mae had lunch ready when I got home.  After eating lunch we took both of our families and went to Hogal Zoo and to Liberty Park.

 

Tuesday, August 6, 1974

 

                I never got home from work until after 10am.  I slept until 5:30 pm.  After dinner, Jim, Marion, Mae and I went into the Ogden Temple but got there too late to go on a session.  We went over to Farr’s Ice Cream before coming back home.  It rained this evening.

 

Wednesday, August 7, 1974

 

                I got home from work late again today.  Jim, Marion, Mae and I went to the Ogden Temple again today but a little earlier this time.  After the session, we went out to eat.  We had a very nice evening before I had to go into work.

 

Thursday, August 8, 1974

 

                I got home from work late again.  We took the Fraizer’s, our family and went up South Willow cannon on a picnic.  We had a very nice time.  Mae asked me if we could get a house dog.  When I started to object, she said that Sandi needed a pet, then she added, saying that she didn’t feel safe with me being gone nights and leaving her and the children to be at home alone.  How could I argue with that?  I told her that if she could find a dog, that was over 1 year old, was a female, had had a litter of pups and then had been spaded, had all her shots, didn’t cost very much, didn’t shed hair all over, was good with children, (then to make it impossible) was white, she could get it.

 

Friday, August 9, 1974

 

                Work went some better last night, but there are some real moral problems with some of the night shift employees.  Mae had gone to have her hair done before I got home this morning.   Jim and Marrion Fraizer left to return to their home about 10:30AM. They stayed long enough for me to get home so they could say good bye before they left.  Before I laid down to sleep Mae told me that she had found a dog.  I questioned if it met all my specifications.  She said that it did.  She said that the lady that did her hair had just the dog.  Mae took Mike into the Dr. for a physical examination before he starts school.  I went into a meeting at work from 4 to 8PM.  In that meeting I brought up the problem that was occurring with my employees.  As I saw it the problem, it mainly stemmed over two employs that had not received a raise in pay, for over a year.   I was then told that these two had found that if they were a little short of money before payday, they could cash a check, make a tear in the routing number, the check would then reject as it went through the check reader.  Because they worked as “batch balancers” they would see their check come through the bank.  They then would simply put the check into another Bank’s work, and the check would take another two or three days before returning to the bank for clearance.  One of the banks recognized the seam and reported it back to our bank.  The two were called in and confessed their action but didn’t think it was wrong.  They could have been fired over it but it had been decided to let it go with a warning.   This incident took place almost a year before I returned to work for the bank.  Management also decided that they would also forgo any raises.  I expressed my feelings that they were two of my best workers.  I felt that it was wrong to continue to punish them by withholding raises and if they could not make amends then they should be terminated.  After returning from the meeting, I had to go back to work by 11:30PM.

 

Saturday, August 10, 1974

 

                Work went some better last night.  I think talking to the employees about the results of the meeting yesterday help quite a bit.  I didn’t get home from work this morning until 11:30AM.  I slept until 5PM and then Mae and I went with Deanna to pick corn.  This evening Mae and I went to a movie, “Where the Lillie’s Grow”.  It sure was good to spend an evening with Mae.

 

Sunday, August 11, 1974

 

                After attending Sacrament meeting, we had a light lunch, before going to Mom and Dad Jensen’s Ward where we attended their Sacrament meeting.  I felt very impressed with their Ward’s Sacrament meeting.   Following the meeting, Bob’s family, Irven’s family and our Family got together at Mom and Dad’s place for dinner.  We had a wonderful time being together.  Before going home we went to Mae’s hairdresser and got the dog that Mae had bought for Sandi.  She is a 1 ½ year old Spits.  She really met every condition that I had set.  Her name is Casper and the whole family fell in love with her.

 

Monday, August 12, 1974

 

                I stained and put the 1st coat of varnish on the cabinets I am building for the bank.  We had the kids shuck the corn that we picked on Saturday.  The whole family helped as we cut the corn from the cob, washed it, blanched it in hot water and then cooled it down in ice water.  We then bagged the corn in plastic bags and put it into the freezer.  We sure enjoy the corn put up that way and it is so much cheaper than purchasing it from the store.  After finishing up that job, we went down to Mom and Dad Porter’s where we held Family Home Evening.  I am sure that Mom had a nice dinner ready when we got there.

 

 Tuesday, August 13, 1974

 

                I got off work at 8:45AM.  After I woke up and had my breakfast this afternoon, I sanded the cabinets down that I am building for the bank.  At 5PM I went with Brother Ben Sisinaro to weed potatoes at the Stake farm.  After returning home I relaxed and watched a TV show with my sweetheart and Sandy.

 

Wednesday, August 14, 1974

 

                Mae and the children went to Lagoon with Mom and Dad Porter.  I was going to go with them also but I was late getting home from work and by the time I woke up it was too late.  With no one else at home I went out in the garage and put the finishing on the cabinets.  Mae was disappointed that I hadn’t been able to go with them, because Mom Porter had to return home earlier than my family wanted to return.  Being that they had gone in Mom’s car it meant that my family also had to come home.

 

Thursday, August 15, 1974

 

                After getting home from work and getting some sleep, Mae and I went over to Dr. Kilburn’s and each got a treatment.  This afternoon we took some tables and chairs from the church house to Sister Miskin’s, where we are holding a Ward party tomorrow evening.

 

Friday, August 16, 1974

 

                Work did not go well last night and so I was late getting home this morning.  I woke up at 2:45PM and was still upset about work.  Our Ward asked everyone to set today aside for a Temple Day.  Therefore, this afternoon we went to the Ogden Temple with other members of the Ward and went through an Endowment Session.  Following the session everyone went to Sister Miskin’s back yard for a party and get together.  We had a good turnout from the Ward and everyone seemed to have an enjoyable evening.

(From my entrance on Tuesday, August 20th, I believe I can and should add some insight to this entry, as to some of the reasons why work didn’t go well!)  When I got to work last Thursday evening, the Senior Programmer met me and said, “I heard that you were going to come into the Programming Staff and if so when?”  I told him that I was not sure, but that is what John Howard had told me when he hired me.  He said that he had never heard anything about that move until he was told a rumor this morning.   This conversation caused me to reflect on this, through the night.  Sometime ago I had met with John, asking him about the promise to move me into the programming staff.  He had told me that we needed to first train someone to take my place.  I should, therefore, select someone to train.  I suggested Bobby (don’t recall his last name) to which John agreed, pending Bobby’s willingness.  I met privately with Bobby and asked if he would be in favor of that position and he had indicated that he would.  For some time I have been training Bobby and I believed that he was now well qualified to take my position.  In recent meetings with John, I have told him that I felt that Bobby was now qualified to take over the Night Shift Supervisor position.  However, nothing has ever happened.  Thinking of these past events, I determined to again confront John, but John is now on a two week vacation and will still be gone for another week.)

 

Saturday, August 17, 1974

 

                After getting home from work, I had a little breakfast and went to bed.  I slept until 5PM before getting up.  We got things gathered up to go on an overnight camping trip.  We plan on leaving Sunday evening and staying over until Monday evening.  We had a relaxing evening together as a family.  Mae and I didn’t get to bed until almost 11PM.

 

Sunday, August 18, 1974

 

                I attended Priesthood meeting and then came back and got my family.  We attended Sunday school and then came home to a wonderful chicken dinner that Mae had prepared.  This afternoon we all attended Sacrament meeting and enjoyed the Spirit we felt there.  We finished packing and left for the Meadows, up South Fork canyon of the Ogden River.  We had let Sandy invite Julie Cisneros’s to also go with us.  We enjoyed a very nice evening.  Scott didn’t sleep very well and had us up some during the night.

 

 Monday, August 19, 1974

 

                Mae and I didn’t sleep very well last night as Scott and David kept getting uncovered and getting cold.  We didn’t do much, but just had a relaxing day.  Sandy and Jullie wanted to go for a walk so I went with them, also taking the little children with us.  We only walked a little ways and then Mae drove up in the car and picked up the little ones.  Sandy, Jullie and I continued the walk.  As Mae was returning to our camp site, she saw that the gate to Camp Beaver was open so she drove into the camp to show the children where she went camping with the Girls Camp when we lived in Ogden.  Well, when she went to leave the gate had been closed and locked.  (Because this entry was written in a Five Year Diary, I ran out of space to write more.  Neither Mae nor I can remember how we got the car out.)

 

Tuesday, August 20, 1974

 

                Work went pretty well last night (Add in: however I continued thinking about my promises that John Howard had made me when I accepted the offer to come to work for the Bank.)   Before leaving to come home, because John is still on vacation and won’t be back for a week, I tried to call Ross Kendale that I might set up an appointment with him, concerning the agreements John had made with me when I accepted this job.  To my disappointment, Mr. Kendale is out of the bank today.  I arrived home by 9AM and after taking a nap, I went to the Stake farm and pulled weeds in the potato field.

 

Wednesday, August 21, 1974

 

                This evening after getting up I just relaxed being home.  Terry, Mae’s brother, came over and had dinner with us.  Terry and I visited after dinner and it may have been at this time as we were talking about marriage, and Terry was dating some very pretty nice girls, that he said that he would not get married before he was 24 years old.  It ended up in a bet for a steak dinner that he wouldn’t wait that long.  As it ended up I had to pay that bet.  I received a call asking me to meet with our Stake President this evening.  I went over to the Stake Center and met with President Peterson of the Stake Presidency.  He extended a call for me to be the Ward High Priest Group Leader.  Wow!  I felt over whelmed, but accepted the calling.  After returning home, I continued the visit with Terry until 10:30PM when I had to excuse myself to go into work. 

 

Thursday, August 22, 1974

 

                I got up at 3PM and went down to Mae’s parents.  I visited with Dad Porter about putting in a concrete driveway.  He felt ok with that and said that I could take the expenses out of our rent payment.   Mae and Mom Porter canned pickle relish today.  Mae and Sandy went into Ogden shopping for school clothes.  Mom Porter invited us down for dinner, with them, this evening.

 

Friday, August 23, 1974

 

                After I got up, Mae and I went to buy a wedding gift for Bishop Sly’s daughter and her new Husband.  Mae picked out some crystal dishes that she really liked, so I bought two sets of them, one for the wedding gift and one for my Sweetheart.  This evening we took our dinner and all our family to the park where we ate and let the children play.  Later Mae, Mom Porter and I attended the Wedding Reception of Sister and Bishop Sid Sly’s daughter and her new husband.

 

Saturday, August 24, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home from work until 11AM and then was called on the phone twice during the day with problems at work.  Mae and I went with Rick and Georgia to look at a home in Clearfield.  This evening Rick, Georgia, Kim and Luana came over to our place and we played Rook.  We had a very enjoyable evening.

 

Sunday, August 25, 1974

 

                This morning during Priesthood meeting I was sustained and set apart as the High Priest Group Leader in the Sunset 2nd Ward in the Sunset Stake.  After getting home from Sacrament meeting, we watched a TV show as a family.  Then Sandy tended the children while Mae and I went down to Mom and Dad Porters and played games with some of the family.

 

Monday, August 26, 1974

 

                After breakfast, I went down to Mom and dad Porters and picked two bushels of tomatoes.  While I was there, Mae registered the children in school.  Mike is starting Kindergarten, Shellie will start the 2nd Grade and Sandy will be in the 7th grade at Sunset Jr. High.  Mae and I then canned the tomatoes into Tomato Juice, getting 40 quarts of juice.  This evening we had a nice Family Home Evening with all the children reporting about their first day of school, along with a good lesson.  I went into work on my normal schedual.

 

Tuesday, August 27, 1974

 

                I was late getting home from work this morning due to some problems.  Mae went over to the Ward Library this evening.  I met with Herman Udink for a while.  Brother Udink was the previous High Priest Group leader.  I appreciated his input and information that he could share with me concerning the members of our Quorum.

 

Wednesday, August 28, 1974

 

                Before leaving work, I visited with John Howard.  He told me that the Bank is going to reimburse us 4 months back interest on our home in Mesa.  That will really help us out, as so far we haven’t had any interest in purchasing the home.  This evening Mae and I took Terry, Mae’s brother, and his date out to dinner.  Some time ago, I had bet Terry that he would be married before he reached his 25th Birthday.  He still isn’t married, so I decided to pay up.  We had a nice evening.

 

Thursday, August 29, 1974

 

                Before coming home I figured the bills.  They don’t look very good.  Sandy started taking piano lessons with Mr. Pingree who has his studio in Roy.  He seems to be a very good teacher.

 

Friday, August 30, 1974

 

                After getting home from work and going to bed, I got up at 3PM.  I then went to Farmington and registered our car and got Utah license plates.  This evening Mae and I attended the Ogden Temple and enjoyed a nice session.

 

Saturday, August 31, 1974

 

                I came home at 6:30 this morning.  After getting a little sleep I worked on the driveway and a retaining was on the west end of the driveway.  I went to the Barber Shop and got a haircut.  I then went over to Mom Jensen’s and cut our turkey in half.  (I don’t remember the circumstance about that.)  This evening we had invited Irven, Sandy, Bryce, Deanna, Mom Porter and Mom Jensen over to play games.  (I guess Dad Porter and Dad Jensen didn’t feel like joining with us this evening.)

 

Sunday, September 1, 1974

 

                After attending our meetings, my family went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s for a family dinner.  Irven and Bob’s familys were also there.  After returning home this evening, we put the children down to bed.  Mae and I then set up and watched a show on TV.  Mae is sure a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

 

Monday, September 2, 1974

 

                Today being Labor Day, I won’t have to go into work tonight.  We took our family to Uncle Dale and Aunt Ester’s cabin that is up in Morgan County.  Beside their family, they had invited Uncle Verl’s family and Mom and Dad Porter’s family to come up for dinner.  (Addition to my Diary entry.  As we started driving up the dirt road I realized that we had been here before.  Shortly after we moved to Utah, Bob and Pauline Hien stopped at our place and asked us to go for a ride with them.  Bob drove up to Peterson, then turned west and at a dirt road drove about 1 ½ miles south, up the cannon to this open meadow, with a small pond fed by a spring.  Bob stopped the car and asked me what I thought.  Questionably, I ask what he meant.   Bob said that he had a chance to buy this area.  I guess I discouraged him for as I couldn’t see much he could do with it, being 1 ½ miles of paved roads with no utilities to the property.  I guess that shortly after that Uncle Dale purchased this property and put a cabin on it.  I have always wondered if I was wrong in my advice to Bob.)   I took Mike and David out away from the cabin and we shot did some target practice with my 22 rifle riffle.  We had a nice dinner, played games, visited and just had a good time.  It was a very enjoyable time.  I sure love Mae.

 

Tuesday, September 3, 1974

 

                After getting up, I enjoyed helping Mae around the house.  I ordered some road base to be delivered, for our driveway.  I really enjoyed the day being with Mae.

 

Wednesday, September 4, 1974

 

                Last night was a very bad night at work.  I didn’t get home until after 1:30 PM.  I slept until 5:30PM when I got up.  We had invited Hal and Jean over for dinner.  After dinner Hal and I played a game of chess.  As normal our chess games will last a couple of hours or more until I become mentally executed and I make a mistake and Hal then wins.

 

Thursday, September 5, 1974

 

                After getting home from work this morning, I helped Mae and Mom Porter put up some peaches for a while.  A few days ago I had a couple of dump truck of road base delivered to the house.  Today, one of the workers from Sunset City came over with a grader and level the road base in our driveway for me. I believe it was Margie Nelson’s brother or Brother-in-law that did it for us.  That sure saved me a lot of hand labor.  After that was done, I laid down and slept the rest of the afternoon, until I had to get to go into work.

 

Friday, September 6, 1974

 

                After getting home from work and sleeping for a while I got up.  I didn’t do much this afternoon except visit with Brother Thompson.  This evening we took our family out to eat at McDonalds.  After returning home and getting the children to bed, Mae and I watched a good show on TV about Abraham Lincoln.  I then had to go to work.  At work this evening I became very tired and so after finishing what I had to do I turned the shift over to Bobby and I went home.

 

Saturday, September 7, 1974

 

                When I got home I went straight to bed.  I woke up at 10AM and figured materials for our driveway.  I then went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s and figured materials for a project they want me to do.  They would like their carport turned into a garage.  From there I went back to work and finished up a couple of projects I had left undone when I went home.  Mae had a display she was working on this evening.

 

Sunday, September 8, 1974

 

                After Sunday school we went down to Mom and Dad Porters for a Birthday dinner for Georgia and Kim.  After dinner, Hal and I started a game of chess.  I indicate in my Diary that I won.  To add a little more detail, Hal made a bad mistake and I took advantage of it and put him in serious trouble, with his next move I would have his king in check mate.  About that time Mae came in and said that I needed to get ready to attend Sacrament meeting.  Hal jumped on that and said that we would just call the game a draw.  I responded that he needed to make his move first and that I still had time to get ready for Church.  He kept stalling until Mae came and said that we needed to leave.  I told her to take the children and go.  I would catch the next Wards meeting.  Hal of course encouraged me to attend the meeting.  I refused to call the game a draw so he could stall as long as he wanted but ether he made his move or concede the game.  Hal finally tipped his king over and conceded.  I quickly changed my clothes and made it to Sacrament meeting.  Following Sacrament meeting Deanna came over to our place.  We ate super and then played some Rook.  Larry called me to let me know that he had two people, the Allen’s and the Elis’s, that are interested in buying our home.  The problem being, that both of them want it for nothing.  Maybe if I could have seen the future, we would have been better off just getting out of the house.  However I turned the offer down.

 

Monday, September 9, 1974

 

                After breakfast I worked on the driveway for about 4 hours.  I then helped Mae put up fruit away.  We then drove out to look at a home for sale in West Warren, it was a disaster!  After dinner I laid down for a short nap.  We then held Family Home Evening with our family.  I laid back down for a little more sleep before going into work at 11:30PM.

 

Tuesday, September 10, 1974

 

                I never got home from work until 10:30AM.  After getting some sleep, I repaired the power steering on our car.  I also talked to Larry on the phone about our home in Arizona.  Larry feels that we need to install Shutters on the home to dress it up some.  He is also concerned as the back yard is not fenced and almost all homes in the Mesa area do have fenced yards. 

 

Wednesday, September 11, 1974

 

                I worked late this morning before going home.  Mae went shopping today for David’s Birthday presents.  When I got up I played with my children until it was their bed time.  Larry called me again this evening about a lady that is interested in buying our home.  Mae and I talked until time for her to go to bed.  I slept with her for a little while before going into work.

 

Thursday, September 12, 1974

Wednesday, September 18, 1974

Thursday, September 19, 1974

Saturday, September 21, 1974

Center where we loaded all the materials that needed to be delivered into the Bank’s station wagon and left for Grantsville.  We made a drop at the Grantsville Branch, and then went to Tooele; dropping off materials at the Tooele Branch and from there we went to the Murray Branch to deliver the last of the items.  The reason that I had to make these drops; it’s that our regular employee that does this, was not at work last night.  While already in the Salt Lake area, we stopped and visited with Irven and Sandy for a little while.  We were late getting back home.  Mae and I did enjoy being together, along with the visit to Irven’s.

Wednesday, October 2, 1974

 

                We had another bad night at work and I was late getting home this morning.  This evening I stayed home with our children while Mae attended a Laural Program in West Point.  She had been asked to speak at the program.  I enjoyed being with the children this evening.  After Mae returned home we talked until it was time for me to go to work.

 

Thursday, October 3, 1974

Saturday, October 5, 1974

Sunday, October 6, 1974

Monday, October 7, 1974

 

                I worked all day at Georgia and Rick’s home, building their kitchen cabinets.  After returning home and having dinner, we held a very enjoyable Family Home Evening.  Scott still isn’t feeling very well but the rest of us are doing much better this evening.  I did get a little sleep before going into work.

Wednesday, October 9, 1974

 

                We again had a lot of problems at work and I didn’t get home until 11:30AM.  I guess that was better than yesterday.  I slept for a little while and then at 4PM Mae and I took Shellie, Mike and David with us and went to the Stake Welfare Farm and picked potatoes.  For those that worked there, we were able to purchase what potatoes we wanted.  We purchased three sacks of nice potatoes.

 

Thursday, October 10, 1974

 

                I left work at 8:15AM today.  That was better than the past few days.  After getting some sleep I went into the Ogden Temple where I worked in the Baptistery.  When I got home we spent some time still trying to decide what to do with our home in Arizona.  I got a call from the office and had to go into work at 9PM tonight.

 

Friday, October 11, 1974

 

                After getting home I tried to sleep but wasn’t able to as I am so concerned with what to do with the home in Arizona.  After giving up on sleep I wrote a memo to Ross Kendall.  (I do not know what the memo consisted of as I hadn’t enter enough detail in my Diary)    This evening Mae and I went into Ogden to attend the square dance.  That always helps to get our mind clear for a little while. 

 

Saturday, October 12, 1974

Sunday, October 13, 1974

 

                We attended Stake Conference this morning.  The Conference was very good.  After returning home I got my High Priest’s reports filled out and turned in.  This afternoon we attended Sacrament meeting at the Mound Fort Ward.  I was asked to speak for a few minutes during the meeting.  Following the meeting we visited with Pete and Grace Mooney for a little while.  Although we enjoyed the visit, we were late getting the children to bed in as much as they will have school tomorrow.

 

Monday, October 14, 1974

Tuesday, October 15, 1974

 

                After getting home from work I went over to Georgia and Rick’s where I worked all morning.  I did quite at noon and came back home.  Mae and I then went to the Sunset Junior High where we ate school lunch with Sandy.  After dinner this evening I went to bed for a little while before going into work.

 

Wednesday, October 16, 1974

 

                Work did not go very well again last night and it 10:30AM before I got home.  I slept until 5PM when I got up and ate with my family.  After dinner, Mae took the children to the Ground Breaking ceremony of our new Chapel.  I stayed home and relaxed as I am not feeling the best.  I went into work anyway about 11PM.

 

Thursday, October 17, 1974

 

                Work went much better last night and I was able to get home about 8:20AM.  I was able to sleep until 3PM when I got up and worked on my Honda motorcycle.  This evening at 6PM we attended a fund raising dinner for our new chapel.  When I got home I got things ready to go Deer Hunting.

 

Friday, October 18, 1974

Saturday, October 19, 1974

 

                Work went very well last night and I was able to leave work at 4:45AM.  I then met with Irven, Kim and Robert and the four of us went up Black Smith Fork canon.  We hunted all day but didn’t see anything except doe’s, and this area is a “buck only” hunting this year.  We finally gave up and came home, getting back home after 6PM.

 

Sunday, October 20, 1974

 

                This morning in Priesthood Quorum meeting Brother Herman Udink and Brother James Popham were set apart as assistance to me in the our High Priest Group.  After we attended Sunday School, I and my family all went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s.  Both Bob and Irven’s family also were there.  We attended Sacrament at Mom and Dad’s Ward and then came back to Mom and Dad’s where Mom had a nice dinner for us all.  It was a good time being together.

 

Monday, October 21, 1974

 

                Irven, Bob, Grant, Robert and I left at 4:30AM this morning and went down to the Pleasant Grove area deer hunting.  Most of the lower lands were all fenced off with no trespassing signs posted.  We found one area where the gate was open and no signs posted so we drove up it to the foot hills.  We then got out and hiked up a canon, until we got to a fence that was posted.  We then turned south and walked until we came to the next draw and then hiked back down the mountain.  When we got almost to the bottom of the draw, Irven saw a doe and shot at it.  Soon a group of men came ridding up on horses.  They demanded to know what we were doing trespassing on privet property.  I told the one that seemed to be the leader of the group that we had not crossed any property that was posted.  He called me a liar and demanded that we retrace our path and go back over the mountain in which we had come.  He said that we hadn’t crossed the fence at the top of the draw we came down, as it was posted.  I told him that we had came up a draw to the north.  Until we came to a fence that was posted and so we turned south.  We follow along that the fence then came down the next draw we came to.  He again called me a liar and indicated that he was going to force us, with his horses’ and rifles and to make us go back to the top of the draw and over that fence.  I know that going over that fence would put us completely out of the area we had left the car.  I wasn’t sure about his bluff but I was too tired to retrace our path and I refused.  At that point a younger person road up to the older man and said that the direction I had indicated we had come would not have been posted.  He then back down and told us that we could continue to our car and then to get off his property.   We did that and went on home, very tired, getting home about 9:30PM.   I got a little sleep before going to work.

 

Tuesday, October 22, 1974

 

                Work went very well last night and I was able to get home at 5AM.  That is probably the first time and may be the only time that has or will happen.  I went to bed with Mae still in bed.  After I woke up and had breakfast, Mae and I worked on our budget.  I then washed and vacuumed the car.  I then put away my hunting equipment.  Mae and I then went to Barlow furniture in Clearfield, where we bought an upright freezer and a vacuum.  (Note: as I am editing this Diary entry, on April 17, 2016, we are still using that freezer.)

 

Wednesday, October 23, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home as early as I did yesterday.  I slept until 3PM before getting up.  I was going to go over to Georgia and Rick’s place to work there but instead Mae and I decided to attend the Clearfield High School Football game last game of the season.  Bob had called Mae and told her that Grant would be playing.  We got there just a little late so the game was already started.  We didn’t see Grant on the field but by the time we found our seats we could see him sitting on the bench with his leg up and an ice pack on it.  When we saw Bob he said that he had told Grant to have his ankle wrap tight with tape before he started the game.  He didn’t and the first play he sprained his ankle, which put him out of the game and also out the rest of the season.  This evening I relaxed and watched some TV.  Our freezer was delivered this afternoon.  This is our first freezer we have owned, so Mae is really excited about getting it.

 

Thursday, October 24, 1974

Friday, October 25, 1974

C. Willies where we looked at some carpeting.  A few days earlier there had been an ad for Don’s Meats in Sunset, of a sale on ½ a beef cut and wrapped for the freezer.  Mom and Dad said that they would like to go in on it with us.  Don’s Meat’s called us this morning and said the beef was ready for us to pick up.  So from R.C. Willies we went to get the meat.  Mom and Dad took us home where we took our meat and put it into our freezer.  They then took their meat home to put it into their freezer.  Later this evening Mom and Dad came back to visit and play Rummy.

 

Sunday, October 27, 1974

 

                This morning we attended the regular Sunday morning meeting.  After Sunday school, as a High Priest Group leadership, we went to Darrel Drapers to visit him.  We were given the report that he has been quite sick.  Following Sacrament meeting we took our family down to Mom and Dad Porter’s for a visit.  Of course when we got there we have to have a game or two or more with Mom.  She really enjoys visiting and playing games.

 

Monday, October 28, 1974

 

                I went over to Rick and Georgia’s home in Clearfield where I worked from 7am until 6pm.  I came home where we ate dinner and then held Family Home Evening.  While Mae was getting the children to bed I fell asleep on the floor.  Mae got me up and into bed where I slept until 11pm before getting up and going into work.

 

Tuesday, October 29, 1974

 

                Work went well and I went straight over to Rick and Georgia’s where I worked from 6:15am until 9:30PM.  I believe that Mae brought me over some lunch.  I got about 1 ½ hours of sleep before going into work.

 

Wednesday, October 30, 1974

 

                With only getting 1 ½ hours of sleep before going to work last night I was really tired when I came home.  I slept most of the day.  Mae told me that their Relief Society Opening Social went well today.  This evening I held Oral Evaluation with some of the High Priests.

 

Thursday, October 31, 1974

 

                I got home from work at 9:30am.  This evening I took the children out Trick or Treating.  After we returned Mae took them to both Grandparents homes so that they could see the children.  I held Oral Evaluation with Mel Wood while they were gone.

 

Friday, November 1, 1974

 

                I got home from work about 10am this morning.  After some breakfast I went to bed.  Mae has been sewing on a Square Dance dress most of the day.  I went into the Ogden Temple at 3pm to work in the Baptistery.  This evening Mae and I went Square dancing with the Double J dance club and had a very enjoyable time.

 

Saturday, November, 2, 1974

 

                I got home from work at 9:30am.  I ate and then went over to Rick and Georgia’s where I worked until 4PM.  Mae came and got me, as she decided that I had worked long enough for today.  I noted in my Diary that we rented a TV, but neither of us remembers doing that.  I fell asleep on the couch while Mae was getting the children ready for Sunday and down to bed.

 

Sunday, November 3, 1974

 

                We held a High Priest Group Leadership meeting at 7:30am.  I feel quite pleased with the Group Leadership.  Brother James Popham is keeping us up on the Temple work and Brother Herman Udink is keeping us on track with the Home Teaching.  Following Sunday School the three of us went visiting some of the Brethren and finished our Oral Evaluation on Home Teaching for last month.  After getting back home from Sacrament meeting I helped Mae with a few items and then as a family we watched TV together.  I sure was wonderful to spend some time with my family.

 

Monday, November 4, 1974

 

                This morning I went over to rick and Georgia’s and worked from 8an until 4:30pm.  After dinner we held a nice Family Home Evening.  I slept for a little while before going into work at mid-night.

 

Tuesday, November 5, 1974

 

                Tonight things went wrong at work and then at 4AM it really snow balled.  I didn’t get home until 4PM.  What a night that turned out to be.  I got a little sleep before going back to work at Mid-night.

 

Wednesday, November 6, 1974

 

                I got home from work at 9:54am.  I ate a little breakfast and then went to sleep.  At 3pm I got up and spent a little while with the children.  I then went over to Rick and Georgia’s and cut the cabinet top for the stove and sink insets.  Back to work at Mid-night.

 

Thursday, November 7, 1974

 

                We had problems at work again last night and so I didn’t get home until almost 10am.  I took a short nap and then went to the barber to get a haircut.  I came home, took a shower, shaved and went back to work at 4PM.  We had a meeting with some of the management of IB&T.  When I got home, I only got a short nap before going back to work.  Today has been a long day.

 

Friday, November 8, 1974

 

                I got home from work about 9am and slept until 1:30pm.  After eating I went into the Ogden Temple where I worked in the baptistery.  This evening at 6:45pm we took our family to a special program in honor of Kent and Shirly Murdock.  Following that program we took our family back home where Sandy took care of our children while we picked up Don & Dorthy McNee.  We went Square Dancing with the Double J Dance club.  We had a very enjoyable evening coming home about 11pm and I went into work at mid-night.

 

Saturday, November 9, 1974

 

                I didn’t get home from work until 10am.  I was so tired that I slept until 6pm.  Today Mae and the children went to Cindy Porter’s Birthday.  In connection with the party they went on a nature hike.  This evening we watched a show on TV as a family before we went to bed.

 

Sunday, November 10, 1974

 

                We attended our regular Sunday meeting today.  For dinner we invited Deanna and her children over to eat with us.  This evening Mae and I attended a special fireside.  I sure have a special wife, sweetheart who is also a very special Mother to my children.

 

Monday, November 11, 1974

 

                I worked at Rick and Georgia’s for about 8 hours today.  After dinner this evening we had a very enjoyable Family Home Evening.  I went to bed at 8:15pm and slept until Mae woke me at 11:15pm for me to go to work.  She hadn’t gone to bed but stayed up doing house work, but after I got off to work she went to bed.

 

Tuesday, November 12, 1974

 

Work went well last night and I got home early, so only slept for a short while.  When I got up I ate breakfast then went over to Rick and Georgia’s where I finished their cabinets and door’s.  Mae has been busy all day and evening, working on a Road Show.  Again she went until she woke me to go to work and then she went to bed.  She told me that she had fallen down the stair but felt that she was ok and hadn’t seriously hurt herself from the fall.

 

Wednesday, November 13, 1974

 

                I got home from work at a reasonable time and was able to sleep quite well today.  This evening the Stake M.I.A. held their Road Shows.  I believe that there were 2 Wards that put their show on at our building and 2 wards at another chapel.  Mae was involved with our Wards show so she was busy with that this evening.  I took the children and we enjoyed watching the two shows at our building.

 

Thursday, November 14, 1974

 

                Work must have gone ok last night as I didn’t indicate anything about it.  This evening I stayed with the children while Mae again was involved with the Road Shows.  That will end the Road Shows for this year.

 

Friday, November 15, 1974

 

                Again there was no entry as to work last night, but I got up at 12:30pm planning on going into Ogden to look for some paneling for our living room before going into the Temple to fulfill my assignment in the Baptistery.  However when I got up I found that Mae had gone with the car and my wallet.  She did return in time for me to attend the Temple.  After returning from the Temple Mae and I attended the Square dance.  We sure enjoy and look forward to going Square Dancing.  Also I didn’t make any indication as to why but I didn’t have to go into work tonight.

 

Saturday, November 16, 1974

 

                I got up this morning and textured the ceiling in both our living room and Sandy’s bedroom.  I then went into work to process the Idaho Bank and Trust work.  I did not indicate in my Diary why we had yesterday off but it must have had something to do with Veterans Day.  This evening Bob and Carroll invited Mom and Dad Jensen and Mae and I over for the evening.

 

Sunday, November 17, 1974

 

                We attended our regular meetings.  After Sacrament meeting took our family and went for a walk down in the park.  When we got back home we had a light super and watched a show on TV together.  I also called Roy just to see how they are doing.

 

Monday, November 18, 1974

 

                I got up at 6am this morning so that I could get an early start on fixing up the front room.  I worked most of the day on it and had it done by 11PM.  At 11:30PM I went into work.

 

Tuesday, November 19, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I got home at 9:30AM.  When I got home I started back to work on the front room.  I finished up the work I needed done on the front room just before the carpet layers came at 10AM.  The room sure looks nice as they finished laying the new carpeting.  I then went to bed and slept the rest of the day.  I went back to work at 11:30PM.

 

Wednesday, November 20, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I was home shortly after 8AM.  I ate and went to bed, sleeping until 2PM.  I then started redecorating Shellie room.  As I typed this information I cannot remember how we all fit in this home.  We must have had Scott in the bedroom with Shellie, downstairs in the room which the stairs came down into.  Mike and David were in the 2nd bedroom upstairs.  Sandy had the North West bedroom downstairs by herself.  It is really interesting how times have changed in today’s world.  Mae has been very busy as she prepared for a Birthday party for Sandy tomorrow, held an Open House for the Ward Library and helped me with some preparations for the upcoming High Priest Social.

 

Thursday, November 21, 1974

 

                When I got home from work I was feeling quite ill with a cold.  I therefore soon went to bed and I slept most of the day.  Mae hosted the Birthday Party for Sandy and that went well.  This evening Mae and the children went over to the Ward Dinner.  I went into work at 11:30PM.

 

Friday, November 22, 1974

 

                I got so sick at work that at 2:30AM I left work and came home.  I went to bed with a fever and bad cold.  I woke and got up at 8AM feeling much better.  Mae and I joined with others to attend the Wedding of Bruce Young and Jody      in the Salt Lake Temple.  Following the Wedding and Sealing at the Temple we joined with other guests for a Wedding Breakfast.  From my Diary entrances I gather that Rich and Lois were also at the special event.  They probably stayed over with Mom and Dad Jensen.  When we came back home I went to bed at 5PM so that I could get some sleep before going into work at 11:30PM.  Mae was busy with the Library open house as well as preparing for the High Priest social.

 

Saturday, November 23, 1974

 

                I got home at 7:30am from work.  I started working on Shellie’s room but got a call from work requiring me to go back in for a while.  When I got back home, with the help of my sweet wife we finished preparing for the High Priest Social.  We then attended the social and had a good turnout.  Mae and I then attended the reception in honor of Bruce and Jody and then went back and cleaned up from the High Priest Social.  We visited with Rich and Lois until quite late.  I did not indicate as to where we visit with them.

 

Sunday, November 24, 1974

 

                I attended a High Priest Leadership meeting this morning and when it finished I came back home.  We visited with Rich and Lois until they left to return to their home.  We got the children ready to attend Sunday school and had Sandy take then there.  Mae and I then went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s where we visited with Uncle Irven, Aunt Bernice, Aunt Welma and Uncle Lee.  We left there and came home planning to attend our Sacrament meeting but when we got there Pat and Ron Bradfield had just arrived, so we visited with them and missed our Sacrament meeting.

 

Monday, November 25, 1974

 

                I worked most of the morning in Shellie’s room.  This afternoon Mae and I went shopping.  This evening we held a fairly nice Family Home Evening.  Of course as normal I went into work at 11:30PM.

 

Tuesday, November 26, 1974

 

                When I got home from work this morning I was quite tired.  I slept most of the day.  This evening I worked on Shellie’s room.  I went into work at 11:30 PM.

 

Wednesday, November 27, 1974

 

                Work must have gone fairly well last night and I got home at a decent time to get some rest before getting busy around the house.  When I got up, I cleaned up my tools and put them away.  I cleaned up the basement so that the children would have room to play down there tomorrow.

 

Thursday, November 28, 1974

 

                Work went very well last night and I was able to get home by 6AM.  I slept until 10AM and got back up.  I raked and cleaned up the yard and then went in and help Mae with Thanksgiving preparations.  We had Irven and Sandy, Mom and Dad Jensen, Bob and Carroll and their family join us for Thanksgiving Dinner.  We had a very enjoyable time together.  This evening Larry, Tammy and their children stopped in to visit for a while.  They had come up to be with Larry’s family for Thanksgiving.  They are special friends and we sure enjoy having them come by.

 

Friday, November 29, 1974

 

                (My Diary doesn’t explain why I did not have to go into work at 11:30PM but maybe the banks took a two day Holiday for Thanksgiving.) 

                Larry, Tammy and their family came back this morning and visited with us until 10AM when they left to drive back to Arizona.  We sure enjoyed what time we had to visit with them. 

“Whenever I enter anything about Larry and Tammy I sure have a sick feeling over our friendship been severed.  I just hope that some time, some place feelings can be mended.” 

After they left, Mae took the children over to the Mall to see Santa.  I went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s where I measured their carport area as Dad would like to turn the carport into a garage.  From there I went into the Ogden Temple where I served in the Baptistery.  This evening Mae and I went into Ogden where we danced with the Double J Rustlers square dance club.  As normal we had a very enjoyable evening.

 

Saturday, November 30, 1974

 

                 After getting home and a little sleep, I went into Ogden to order some materials for Mom and Dad’s garage.  I wasn’t able to get all that I needed.  This afternoon we took our family and went to Salt Lake for a family activity.  We mainly visited Trolley Square and had an enjoyable time.  This evening after getting the younger children to bed, Mae, Sandy and I watched a show on TV before going to bed.

 

Sunday, December 1, 1974

 

                I attended an early Stake Priesthood Leadership meeting this morning.  When I came out to go home, I had a ticket on my windshield for not having Utah License Plates on my car.  I did have the new plates in the car on the back floor but had not got them installed.  We attended our regular church meeting today.  This evening Mae had prepared w very nice meal.  After eating I helped Mae do up the dishes while the children played.  I laid down on the carpet and fell asleep.  Mae let me sleep there until I got up at 10:30PM and got ready to go into work.

Monday, December 2, 1974

 

                Work went well last night but this morning I had to attend a Management Training Seminar before going home.  I got a little sleep and then Mae and I went Christmas shopping.  I ordered the materials needed for Mom and Dad Jensen’s job.  This evening we enjoyed a nice Family Home Evening.  I got a little sleep before going into work.

 

Tuesday, December 3, 1974

 

                When I got off work this morning I went over to Bob’s and helped him get his car started.  I went home and had got a little sleep when Anderson Lumber called me to tell me that they didn’t have the siding that I had ordered.  This afternoon I took David Christmas shopping, mainly trying to find out what he wanted for Christmas.  Bob also went with us, just to tag along.  We also stopped at Anderson Lumber and I picked out another siding for Mom and Dad’s job.  I got a little more sleep this evening before going to work.

 

Wednesday, December 4, 1974

 

                I was able to get home from work at a good time this morning and so got to bed and slept.  When I woke I ran several errands.  This evening after dinner I held Oral Evaluation with some of the Brethren.  Mae and I then attended the Wedding Reception for Mark Ross and his new bride.  I sure enjoy being with Mae and love my wonderful wife.  It is hard leaving her to go to work each night.

 

Thursday, December 5, 1974

 

                After getting off work I picked up Bob and we went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s and started working on changing their carport into a closed in garage.  As I started laying out the wall, I laid it out the full length.  Dad Jensen came out and asked why I was building it that way.  He felt that I should just build individual framing that would go between the already existing 4 X 4 posts.  I tried to explain that the siding would not alien with the existing post settings.  He got a little upset with me and went into the house.  Mom came out and told me not to worry about it but to continue the way I planned.  We continued and soon had the new wall up in place.  Later Dad came back out and looked at the wall, then made the comment that he was sure glad that I hadn’t listened to him because he could now see my reasoning in the design.

Friday, December 6 1974

 

                Work went well and I got home in good time this morning.  This afternoon I worked at the Temple in the Baptistery.   Working there is one of my weekly highlights.  This evening, instead of going Square Dancing, Mae and I attended the Ward Relief Society party and sure enjoyed that.

 

Saturday, December 7, 1974

 

                It snowed most of the night, so when I got home from work this morning, I built the children a Snowman.  I then picked up Bob and we went back to Mom and Dad Jensen’s and worked more on the garage.  After returning home I slept for a couple of hours.  We then took the children out to buy a Christmas tree.  This evening Mae and I went to the Bank’s Christmas party.  At the party we won a $25.00 prize.  We were very excited at that, as it will help at this time of year.

 

Sunday, December 8, 1974

 

                This morning I attended Priesthood and Sunday school but then I got a call informing me that Margaret is having problems again.  Bob and I went over to Mom and Dad’s to try to help.  As normal when Margaret has problems, she turns to Mom to help her out.  Dad has different feeling on how you help someone in her situation.  I sure hope that Margaret’s problems, doesn’t cause problems between Mom and Dad.  When I got home I was exhausted and fell asleep on the couch.  I didn’t get to spend much time with my family today.

 

Monday, December 9, 1974

 

                I attended a Management Training seminar this morning.  I then went over to Mom and Dad’s and continued working on their garage.  This afternoon I had to attend court because of the ticket I got Sunday, December 1st for not having my license plates on my Car.  When I told the Judge that we had bought the Utah plates the previous week and that I did have them lying in the back window of the car, he dismissed the charges, telling me that I should get them installed.  I reported that they were now installed.  This evening, as a family, we installed and decorated our Christmas tree.  Rich and Lois came down to pick up Lois’s parents, and they stopped in for a few minutes.

 

Tuesday, December 10, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I got home in good time.  After getting up this afternoon I just relaxed and spent time with my family.  This evening Mae and I watched a couple of show on TV before I went into work.  I sure love that gal!

 

Wednesday, December 11, 1974

 

                As I am retyping my Diary entrances from my old hand written entries, I have noted that there very many events missing or incomplete and without person recollection the reader would not be able to understand what is going on.  I have therefore taken the liberty of adding to such entries, so that future readers might have a better understanding of what I was trying to say.

                Work must have gone well last night and I returned home this morning in time to get some sleep.  Working grave yard shift never seem to quite fit in with the normal life style!  After getting up I took some Christmas items over to keep at Mom and Dad Jensen’s evidently to keep them secrete from my family for now.  While there I also picked up some of my tools that I have been using there.  After getting home, as a family we put up the outside Christmas lights and decorated our front room window.  Mae popped some pop corn for a treat.  I took the children over to the church where we used some sidewalks that were covered with snow and used them as a slay hill.

 

Thursday, December 12, 1974

 

                After getting up this afternoon, Mae and I went into the Temple. We must have been later getting home than we had planned, as I had noted that Sandy was late getting to her piano lesson.  (addition) Sandy is doing quite well with the piano and though we are tight on finances we feel her lessons are of great value.  We are having her take lessons from an instructor who has his studio in Roy.  We feel that her achievement on the piano is such that she needs someone more than just the average teacher.

 

Friday, December 13, 1974

 

                It started snowing about 1am and has continued through the night and today.  Mae had a Stake Relief Society meet that she needed to attend this afternoon.  Because of her concerns about driving on the snow covered roads, I got up and drove her to her meeting.  I believe that she road home with another Sister in our Ward.  I shoveled the driveway this afternoon before I went into the Temple to officiate in the Baptistery.  This evening Mae and I went Square dancing.  We look forward to this great activity and so snowy road was not going to stop us from going.  After the dance the night was so beautiful and earth covered with a blanket of snow, we went for a ride to see the Christmas lights.  I have such a beautiful wife and sweetheart.  Although I didn’t want to I went back into work tonight.

 

Saturday, December 14, 1974

 

                The following information was not in my Diary but I want it preserved and so will enter it here! (In our Square Dance group we have became acquainted with Margie Nelson’s Sister and Brother-in-law, Lois and Jerry Ellsworth.  Of course Mae has known Lois through Margie but we have become closer acquaintances with them being in the Square Dance Club.  Jerry was elected president of the Double J club and it was then that invited us back into the club, when we moved to Sunset.  Jerry works for Sunset City and so whenever he is in our area with the snow plow, he will swing in and clear out the heavy snow from my driveway.  This has really helped as we live on the State Highway and when the State plows clean the highway it really packs our entranceway.  He did this until someone reported it and before the winter was over he had to stop helping me out.  I will also add this experience in.  When we first moved into the home in Sunset, I decided to raise the driveway so that it would not be so steep, plus I wanted to pour concrete for the driveway.  I had 2 or 3 dump truck loads of fill delivered and was starting to spread it out by hand with a shovel, when I had the time.  One morning when I got home from work, the fill was all leveled out and packed down.  I never found out who did it but I am sure that it was Jerry using the large front loader or the road grader of the city.  With that it would have only taking him a few minutes but it saved me many, many hours with the shovel.)

I got home in good time this morning and after sleeping for a little while I got up.  After some breakfast I went out and cleaned the loose snow of the driveway.  The heavy snow had been pushed off for me. I then took the children Christmas shopping and while at the stores I bought a new snow shovel.  When we got back home I used the new shovel to again clear the driveway.  After eating dinner, I put vies-queen over the boy’s bedroom window, trying to keep some cold out.  We then took all the family and went into Ogden to see the lights and decorations at the park.  After getting the children down for the night Mae and I stayed up longer to talk.

 

Sunday, December 15, 1974

 

                We attended Sunday meetings, as normal.  Mae prepared a wonderful dinner and we had invited Mom and Dad Porter as well as Rick and Georgia and Kim and Terry.  It has been a very enjoyable day.  This evening Mae and I filled out Christmas cards.  Scott started walking between Mae and I as we held out our hands to him.  He will soon be running.

 

Monday, December 16, 1974

 

                I attended a meeting at the Bank at 8am that lasted until 10am.  When I got home I figured my bills from Anderson Lumber.  I then went over to Mom and Dad Jenson’s and installed the back door to the now garage, for them.  When I finished that I went into the hospital where I donated blood.  Mae and I then went shopping for a few minutes.  When we got home we found out that Hal had tried to take his life and was in the hospital.  As I had to get some sleep before going to work, Mae went into the hospital to be with the family.  Hal had taken an over-dose of sleeping pills.  Mae was home when I got up to go into work.  Mae told me that Hal is stable now but will remain in the hospital for a day or two.  I went into work.

 

Tuesday, December 17, 1974

 

                Work went well last night and I was able to get home at a reasonable time.  I slept for a while and then when I got up Mae told me that Hal was released from the hospital.  Mae and I went over to be with the family and spent most of the day there.

 

Wednesday, December 18, 1974

 

                After getting home from work and getting some sleep, Mae and I went Christmas shopping most of the afternoon.  We then went out to eat at Sizzler for dinner.  It has sure been a lot of fun being with Mae and shopping together.

 

Thursday, December 19, 1974

 

                Work went well again last night and I got home and to bed at a decent time.  After waking up I took Mae to get her hair done.  While she was there I went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s and picked up my tools.  This afternoon I cut out the material to build Mom and Dad Jensen a rack to hold their pool cues and balls.  We decided that we would build that as a Christmas gift for them.  I took Sandy to her piano lesson this afternoon.  This evening Mae took the children to our Ward Christmas party.  I stayed home to get some more sleep before going into work.

 

Friday, December 20, 1974

 

                When I woke up this afternoon and had some lunch, breakfast or whatever you call the first meal you eat when you wake up from working the midnight shift.  This afternoon I went into work at the Temple on my assignment to officiate in the Baptistery.  This is always a very enjoyable experience and I look forward to it.  This evening we took all our children, except Scott who we left at home with a baby sitter, to the Family Square Dance Christmas party.  We all had a wonderful and fun evening.  After taking my family home I soon went to work.

Saturday, December 21, 1974

 

                When I got home from work, Mom called me to tell me that Dad Jensen’s grandson died this morning.  They are going up to be with the family and so I went over and helped them get ready and leave for Idaho Falls.  We had bought a bicycle for Christmas to be given to Sandy but as I checked it out, it was too big for her.  I went to the store and bought her a new one.  Mae has been sewing clothes for the children for their Christmas, so that keeps her busy.  I assembled Fruit Baskets to be taken around to the widows in our Ward.  Irven and Sandy invited Bryce, Deanna, Mae and I over for the evening.  We had a very enjoyable time visiting and playing games.  It was after mid-night before when we got home.

 

Sunday, December 22, 1974

 

We attended our regular Sunday meetings.  All our family, except Scott, participated in the Sunday School program today.  This evening we stayed home and spent time together as a family.  After putting the children down for the evening, Mae and I spent time wrapping Christmas presents.

 

Monday, December 23, 1974

 

                I went into a meeting at work this morning.  Following the meeting I came home and assembled, stained and put a finish on the cue rack that I cut out last Thursday.  We want to give this to Dad Jensen as a Christmas present.  This afternoon we went to Mom and Dad Porters for dinner and a Family Christmas party.  All members of the Glendon and Mildred Porter family were in attendances.  This is the first time we have had all the family together for many years.  It was a very enjoyable evening.

 

Tuesday, December 24, 1974

 

                Work went terrible last night and so I was late getting home.  After getting some sleep I got up and we wrapped more presents.  This evening, as a family, we went visiting.  We visited the Errickson’s, who lived next door to the west of our home on 13th street.  Then we visited Sister Thompson and Brother & Sister Peterson, these are special people that we loved while living in Ogden.  After visiting we came home and all the children hurried to bed with excitement for tomorrow morning.  Mae and I didn’t finish getting ready for Christmas until 2:30am.  That wasn’t so hard for me because that is my work schedule but for Mae, she was tired.

 

Wednesday, December 25, 1974

 

                We got up at 5:45am as Mae couldn’t keep me and the children in bed any longer.  We had a wonderful Christmas morning.  After opening gifts we had breakfast.  Roy and Linda called us from California to wish us a Merry Christmas.  Of course they don’t have snow for Christmas but they seem to enjoy that.  Then Sandy and I rode our new bikes over to Bob and Carroll’s.  I felt that I need more exercise and so when we bought a bike for Sandy, we also bought one for me.  After a visit with Bob’s family we came back home.  We then went down to Mom and Dad Porter where Mom Porter had made a wonderful Christmas dinner and we enjoyed visiting with the family, most of them came out also.

Thursday, December 26, 1974

 

                We are having problems with our TV and so I asked Brother Brandenburg to come over and repair it.  When he finished the TV sure works much better.  We enjoyed having the day fairly free from other responsibilities.  This evening Mae and I took the three boys and went over to Mom and Dad Jensen’s.  While there I hung the pole cue rack up for them.  Shellie had gone with Uncle Hal and Aunt Jean to play with Cindy.  Sandy went with the Sisneros, who took their family ice skating.  I had to go to work tonight at 11:30pm.

 

Friday, December 27, 1974

 

                After returning home from work and getting some sleep, I went to the auto parts and got some pars for the car.  Mae laid down and tried to get a little sleep because she didn’t get much last night.  This evening Mae and I attended a wedding reception in honor of Terry and DeAnn Jessop.  We then drove over to Ogden to visit Mike (Myrene) and Ed Dransfield. When we lived in Ogden, Mike called Mae to be her 2nd councilor in the Stake Young Women Presidency.  They became close friends and we also became friends with their family.  Sandy attended a Ward Young Women’s slumber party.

 

Saturday, December 28, 1974

 

                Work must have gone pretty well last night and when I got home I slept for a little while.  This afternoon Mae and I took Scott with us and drove up to Pocatello, Idaho to Rich and Lois’s home, so we can attend Phillip and Lisa’s wedding reception.  Irven and Sandy were also there when we got there.  Having a little time before the reception, Irven and I took a couple of Rich’s snowmobiles and went for a ride.  This evening we attended the reception.  We then went to Uncle Irven and Aunt Bernice’s home where they let us stay the night.  From what I can make out in my Diary, Irven and Sandy must have also stayed the night with Uncle Irven and Aunt Bernice.  Today was Mae’s Birthday but we didn’t do much celebrating for her.

 

Sunday, December 29. 1974

 

                This morning Uncle Irven, Irven and I attended Priesthood meeting.  When we returned from meeting, Aunt Bernice prepared breakfast for us.  Mae woke up this morning with a bad headache.  We ate breakfast and visited.  It also gave s a little time to visit with Lesley.  Rich and Lois invited us out to their place for dinner.  After dinner we left about 2pm for home.  Mae was sick with the flu and so she slept most of the way home.  After getting home Mae just went to bed, while I took all the children and went down to a family party at Mom and Dad Porters.

 

Monday, December 30, 1974

 

                Mae was in bed most of the day with the flu.  Sandy and I cleaned the house.  Sandy tended the children, while I put weather stripping and new thresholds on the exterior doors.  I took the children to a show this evening.  Mae stayed home in bed.  I went into work at 11:30pm as normal.

 

Tuesday, December 31, 1974

                Work went fairly well last night although, one person called in and quite without any notice.  Another person got sick at work and had to go home early. When I got home from work, Mae was still not feeling well but was up with the children.  When I woke up this afternoon I wasn’t feeling very well.  Although tonight is New Year’s Eve, we just stayed home and went to bed.
