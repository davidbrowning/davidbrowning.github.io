Saturday, January 01, 2011

Morro Bay, California          (Rain)

 

                We woke early this morning, dressed and ate breakfast.  We then drove to Elder and Sister Shaw’s where they were waiting for us.  The four of us drove to the west coast.  We were able to find the Creek Canyon road, in which we first drove down when we went to Morro Bay the first time.  Spring is starting to show although it was not as beautiful as it was first time we went this way.  It still is a very pretty and enjoyable way to go..  It rained on us all day but it did not stop us from having a wonderful day.  We first went for a walk to the beach at Morro Bay, we then took a ride up the East side of town in the residential homes and where you could get a wonderful view of the ocean.  We next found a McDonald’s and got something to eat.  From there we went to the wharf area and went to the shops to get out of the rain.  The first store we went into we bought warm jackets and hats.  It felt so good to have on warmer clothes for thou we had brought jackets it was to cooled and wet for just what we had.  We spent some time just shopping but buying very little.  This afternoon we drove north to where we could view the elephant seals.  While there we visited with two park rangers that explained that in the early 1900’s these seals were thought to be almost extinct, then they found a small group of the elephant seals and through preservation programs there are over 200,000 that live along the west coast from Mexico to Alaska with 17,000 to 20,000 that resides on the west coast about 7 miles north of Hearst Castel.  Some of the large bull seals may weigh 3 ton with the mother seals averaging 1,500 lb to 2,000 lb.  After giving birth to one pup which weighs 7 to 8lb.  The female will lose 25 to 30 lb per day in feeding the pup.  She will nurse the pup for 28 days at which time the pup will average 300lb.  The female milk is about 65% milk fats or about like Mayonnaise. At that time she will go into the water and head for Alaska to the feeding grounds.  She will travel as far off the western coast as 2,000 miles and very deep so as to avoid the Orca (killer whale), where as the males will swim much closer to the western shore and only 6 to 8% of them will get past the Orca and survive.  The pups will remain on the shore for about 3 weeks by them self for they don’t know how to swim.  After 3 weeks they will begin to venture into the water and will soon swim off to Alaska and then will return next year to the same beach in which they were born.  Although it was raining very hard while we were at the beach, we got some very good pictures and really enjoyed learning about the seals.  We were there about an hour and all of us were wet when we got into the car.  We came back to a Home Town Buffet and ate.  I had Sister Shaw drive for about ½ hour while I took a nap.  I then took over and drove on home.  It was about 8PM when we got home.  I got on the computer and typed this entry.  Mae and I then took showers and went to bed.  GREAT DAY!
