                I got up at 3:15AM and got ready to go fishing.  I was at Randy’s at 5AM and Randy was waiting for me, however there was a car, trash receptacles and a motor scooter that was in the way.  I thought that we could maneuver the boat trailer around the car but with the weight of the boat and the trailer having duel axels we just could not get the trailer to turn.  We had to get Randy’s son up to move the car.  By the time we got my items and Randy’s items into the boat it was almost 6AM before we got away.  I asked as we started out if the boat was full of fuel.  He assured me that the boys had filled it after their trip.  We got to Strawberry about 7:45AM and the lake had a mist over it but as the sun started to worm the mist was leaving and was gone from the west end of the lake by the time we got launched.  As we left the marina I looked at the fuel gage and it registered empty.  I asked Randy and he assured me that the boys had filled the tank and so the gage must not work.  We got to the area where I wanted to start fishing and the boat ran out of fuel.  There we set in the middle of the lake out of gas.  We spent until noon getting gas delivered out to us and back to the marina to get more gas.  It was good that we had cell phones with us as I finally had Mae reach the marina and get them to send us help.  Anyway we started fishing just after noon, the poorest time of the day to fish.  We did catch some fish but had to release them because of the slot limit and they were all rainbow trout, where as the last time we fished this same area I caught all rainbow trout.  I did catch one large cut throat that measured 22” and so we kept it. We moved to another area of the lake but still had to release everything we caught.  At 5PM we called it enough and came home.  It was after 8PM when I got home, very tired.  With Randy being blind it really causes me to work.  Also with the starting stress that took its toll.  Then to add to the problems I hadn’t got all my gear out of my truck when we left this morning and so I didn’t have the fishing equipment I needed to fish with.  After getting home I cleaned my fish after taking some pictures of it.  I re-measured it and it only measured 11 ¾”, which if that was what it would have measured when I caught it is should have been released.  I really think it did shrink during transportation as I had checked the length three times before keeping it.  I was so tired that I fell asleep in the recliner as Mae was talking to me, and that is where I slept most of the night.
