Here is Cheryl’s talk that we missed and asked her to send to us:

“I’m so happy and grateful to be here today for Emmah’s baptism.  It is such a special day.  It has been five years today that I went through the Salt Lake Temple for my own endowments.  I also was able to be proxy for the baptism of my daughter, Sarah, in the Jordan River Temple with the help of Sister Linda Bonella.  It was such a special experience.  That particular day was fifty years after my own baptism on September 29th.  The Lord has blessed me in so many ways and I am thankful.

I have been asked to talk about the Holy Ghost.  I want to start my talk by asking some questions and hopefully give some answers and ideas for Emmah.

What valuable gift can you receive after you are baptized?

I think Emmah already knows the answer because when Bishop Skidmore asked her on Sunday “Why do you want to be Baptized?” She said “To receive the Holy Ghost.”  The gift of the Holy Ghost is one of the great blessings we receive.  In Moroni chapter 10 verse 5 it says “and by the power of the Holy Ghost ye may know the truth of all things.”

Who is the Holy Ghost?

A being who helps Jesus and Heavenly Father, a spirit, someone who can comfort us.  The first article of faith “We believe in God, the eternal Father and his son, Jesus Christ, and in the Holy Ghost.”  The Holy Ghost does not have a body of flesh and bones like Heavenly Father and Jesus.  In 1995 the prophet Gordon B. Hinckley said “I believe in the Holy Ghost as a personage of spirit who occupies a place with the father and son, the Holy Ghost is the testifier of truth.”

When are we given the Holy Ghost?

After we are baptized; when we are confirmed members of the church.  Part of the fourth article of faith says “Laying on of our hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost.”

Where can we feel the promptings of the Holy Ghost?

In our hearts and minds, at home, at school, at church or wherever we are when we choose the right.  We can not see the Holy Ghost, but he can help us to do what is right.  The Holy Ghost doesn’t always speak to us in a voice we can hear.  Many times he gives us feelings of what we should do.

Why did Heavenly Father send the Holy Ghost to be with us?

To help and comfort us; to guide and warn us; to tell us what is true; to give us feelings of love and peace.  The Holy Ghost can help us before we are baptized and confirmed but after we receive this gift we may have the Holy Ghost as a constant companion.  This means as long as we do what is right, his influence can be with us always.

This is a gift for you, Emmah.  (Give the vase of “W” Flowers)
