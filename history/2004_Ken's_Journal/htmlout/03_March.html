                We went out to Guimba District Primary Sports Fest. held at Guimba 3rd Branch building.  They had a good program and good attendance.  Elder and Sister Clark also came out.  It was a good day and we enjoyed being with the Clark’s.  We stopped at President Cabuyaban’s and watched them transplanting the Pechay plants.  Yesterday I was concerned at the looks of the transplants, but today they are looking much better.  We just hope and pray that this is helping them.  We came home planning on relaxing the rest of the evening, but right after we got home President Neito called us and invited us to a Branch activity.  We went and enjoyed it but was glad to come home, very tired.

 

Sunday, March 28, 2004

 

                We didn’t get up until almost 7 AM.  I started on my presentation for today.  Jo Jo Pasardan called to see if he could come over for a few minutes.  We had him come over.  He needs to print some work of, but we couldn’t figure how to do it as my printer will not hook up to Roger’s computer.  We left for Moncada with plenty of time, and so when we got passed Guimba I decided to take a road that Elder Clark told me was a shorter way to Moncada.  We took a wrong turn some where and got lost.  After asking several people, we made it to church 10 minutes late.  That was a mistake.  We attended Moncada 1st Branch.  After the block program, we held training for the leadership.  Mae took the Sisters and trained them on Visiting Teaching.  I took the Priesthood Executive Committee and trained them all together.  That training usually takes four or five sessions at 1 to 2 hours each.  I took 1 ½ hours this evening.  I think it went fairly well.  We then picked up Elder Elison and Elder Marks , and we went to visit Brother and Sister Roger Tabbilos.  It was 9 PM when we got home.

 

Monday, March 29, 2004

 

                We were not sure when Sister Nida Valenton was coming to give us a treatment.  We didn’t go walking and took quick showers, so we would be ready when she came.  I prepared breakfast of egg omelet but was not sure if we would have others here and how many if they came.  We did finish our breakfast and started on the family letter, before she got here.  While she started on me, Mae got on instant messaging with our children.  After she finished with me, I took over the computer while Mae got a treatment.  It was good to be on with our children.  All of them were on with us except for Shellie.  We visited there until almost noon.  After lunch Mae got on the computer and sent our family letter.  I took a nap.  At 3 PM Sister Rubio came over and visited with Mae.  She stayed until 8 PM visiting.  She informed us of many things that are going on in the branches that are wrong.  I am not sure just what to do about them, but it makes you wonder about the strength of the people.  It is no wonder that the Lord withholds his blessings.  We got some dinner, read an article in the Ensign and got ready for bed.  It was late when we got to bed.

 

Tuesday, March 30, 2004

 

                We went walking this morning at the collage.  There is a termite hill that someone is cutting away.  It was interesting to us as we have heard that they are very superstitious that little people lives in those hills and if you disturb the hill, you will be cursed.  A couple of weeks ago, we saw where some men had to dig a termite hill away in order to run water lines.  The next time we went by there, there was a small cross and some flowers similar to what you would put on a grave.  We found it very interesting.  After breakfast I went to San Jose and met with President Apao about the needs to Guimba 1 & 2 chapel.  I apologized to him that the communications had went the way they did.  He was very receptive and explained all the procedure.  I took the Zl’s a letter for the Paniqui 1st branch and a letter to Sister Clark containing the reimbursements for March and the charges for April.  I then went out to President Cabuyaban’s and we went over his farm project.  I brought President Cabuyaban back to Munoz with me.  On the way I mentioned that I had seen a palay cutter and it cut more than a dozen men could cut.  He said that the government would not let them use it because it would put people out of work.  It was almost 2 PM when I got home.  Mae fixed some lunch.  I took a nap after lunch.  This evening Mae and I went to visit the Pasardan’s to see if everything was settled with the tithing problem.  President Brimhall had visited with them and things are looking better now.  While visiting, Brother Pasardan told me he was teaching an auto mechanic’s class on Saturdays.  He said that he has a 68 year old lady taking the class.  He asked her why, at her age, she wanted to take a class like that.  She told him that she has two vehicles and her chauffer was always telling her that he needed to repair them, but she doesn’t know if he is doing it or not, so she wants to know what he is doing.  We had a nice visit with them.  We worked on the budget after getting home, ate and did my journal.  Was late going to bed.

Mae worked on the computer getting some songs printed for the Primary’s.  I took a nap.  Brother Sylvia came over and they would like to take the hard drive from their computer to repair another.  They will get paid on the 15th and plan on paying us in full at that time.  We said to go ahead.  I worked on a presentation for the Guimba 2nd branch for this Saturday, but became a little frustrated with it.
