I love you for so many reasons.

I love you for the things you say

that brings such special meaning

to my life,

like praising me for

a job well done,

complimenting me on how I look,

and whispering sweet nothings

at the most unexpected times.

I love you for the silent times

when your eyes and your arms

tell me all I want to know…

that I never have to be alone

for as long as you’re around.

I love thinking of all

the adventures we still have

to experience,

the places we have yet to see,

the things we have yet to discover.

I love you…

because now, in a place

where there was nothing before,

in the deepest part of my heart…

there is love.

 

That love has been there

for over 45 years and I

hope and pray that it will

be eternally.

 

Mae

We woke early so we could leave at 6:45 am to go to Guimba 1st Branch meetings.  My back is really hurting so I was slower than normal, but we still got away in time.   We texted Pres. Cabuyaban that we would be there to pick him up at 7:15 on the highway, but we got there at 7:10 so decided to go to his house to pick him up.  His wife and family were coming with us too and they were not ready.  PEC Meeting is supposed to start at 7:30 am, but it wouldn’t be starting at that time this morning.  We wish his wife and kids would come with his mom and come later and just let President Cabuyaban come with us.  Sister Cabuyaban doesn’t seem to like his mom, so that is a problem.  Sister Cabuyaban, Pres. Cabuyaban’s mother, said the closing prayer after Sacrament Meeting was over, and she started crying during the prayer.  I think she is feeling bad that Sister Cabuyaban treats her badly.  They are living with his Mom, while they are getting their home built, and Annie doesn’t want to.   Both Mae and I spoke during Branch Conference. Christopher spoke first and he took 20 minutes where he was supposed to take 5 to 8 min., so I cut my talk short.  The youth sang a song and also the Primary Children sang a song.   The District Presidency spoke.  After the block, I visited with Brother Matal in the Branch President’s office and hoped I helped him realize the need to pay tithing.  That is the only problem stopping him from getting a temple recommend to go with his daughter, Annalisa, to the temple next month when she marries William.  Him and his wife could be sealed and then have their family sealed to them and then be a witness for Annalisa’s marriage.  By the time I finished helping the clerk count the money, I was hurting so bad that I could hardly make it to the car.  We came home and I have been in the bed most of the time.  I did call and talk with both Elder Clark and Elder Maharry.  We texted Nida to see if she could come in the morning to give me a treatment, but she is in Manila for the week.  Not sure how I will manage, but somehow I guess I will – with the Lord’s help.

 

Monday, July 19, 2004

 

                I am really having problems with my back so we didn’t try walking this morning.  Mae rubbed my back several times to day.  We did go to the bank and make the deposit for Branch 1 got the copies and went to San Jose to mail the reports.  While there we did our shopping and got a new battery for our cell phone.  This evening we got texts from Christopher wanting to borrow some money.  We are not sure why, how much or when.  We met the Elders in Munoz 1st branch at the Tabalino family and had a F. H. E. with them.  It went well and I hope we gave them something to consider that will help them.  I did figure the bank statement today and figured our budget and we are in balance on both.

 

Tuesday, July 20, 2004

 

                We didn’t go walking as my back was still hurting.  We took the four Munoz Elders out to breakfast as this will be Elder Abao’s last day with us.  He is being called to serve as a Assistant to the President.  We ate at the Western Food and Coffee Shop.  We paid the bill and for the six of us it was P480 and I left a P20 tip.  The ZL’s came back home with us and we held our correlation meeting.  Mae and I had some problems today in communication.  I put a program flyer together for the rebroadcast of the 1st Philippines Area conference.  We also sent some pictures by e-mail to Brother Nuque of the Medical Mission / Community Service Project.  I went out to Guimba and met with President Cabuyaban.  We did a lot of visiting and I guess it was worth while although I feel like a taxi driver.  Everything is all spoken in Tagalog so I just sit like a bump on the log.  I was quite discouraged when I got home at 9:45 PM.

 

Wednesday, July 21, 2004

 

                We did go walking at the collage this morning and though I got tired it seemed to help my back.  We keep busy with our spare time with e-mails and letters.  Mae is preparing letters for all R. S., Y. W. and Primary Presidents in the two districts.  I wrote a rough draft for a letter to President Mercado and sent a short e-mail to President Brimhall.  I also took reports to Brother Damacio, cashed the LUDA check and got flyers copied for the 1st Philippines Area Conference.  We went out to Activity Night and Branch Presidency meeting this evening.  Mae also went visiting with Sister Villanueva to several Sisters.

 

Thursday, July 22, 2004

 

                I woke at 4:30AM and Mae at 5AM.  We hurried and left for Angeles at 6:30AM.  We stopped in Tarlac and got breakfast at McDonalds before going on to Clark.  We helped the Clarks pack using both their car and our car to get all their items ready to move.  About that time the new missionaries arrived so we got to meet them.  After their orientation we were invited to have lunch with them at President and Sister Brimhall’s.  We then attended the transfer meeting in the Mabalacat  Chapel, which was good to see how that goes.  We then followed Elder and Sister Clark to  Palugrin to their new home.  It is really beautiful and sits on several acres of land.  It has a gazebo with pool table, a nepa hut and is landscaped really beautifully, but Mae and I decided we are still happy with our home and wouldn’t want to swap with any the couple missionaries.  We are thankful for ours.  There are some things which are better than ours in their homes, but some things which are not as nice..  The landlord was there along with 2 hired help and they helped bring in the items from the two cars.  That was nice.  I hooked up their computer and printer, while Mae and Sister Clark put the food away.  The landlords brought food for all of us.  It was rice, shredded coconut, rolls, some kind of a rice steamed dish which is put in bamboo and steamed and afterwards wrapped in banana leaves.  She also had some vegetables with lots of squid in it.  She served goat meat with onions, and another dish which looked like a egg omelet, but didn’t taste like it.  Anyway, Mae and I didn’t enjoy the meal very much.  Don’t think the Clarks did either.  It took us about an hour to get home.  Pres. Brimhall texted us that Elder Neal A. Maxwell of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles passed away today after a lengthy battle with leukemia.  He was 78 years old.

 

Friday, July 23, 2004

 

                Although we were in a hurry to leave this morning I took the shower apart and flushed out the water heater and we now have better water pressure.  I should have done that months ago.  We left at 7:15am to pick up the Elders, drop them off in Guimba and go on to Paniqui District Development Meeting.  After meeting, we went to Moncada and visited with Imedla Baiola and corrected our scheduled for Noemi’s checkups and medications.  We received a text from President Brimhall informing us that Elder Neil A. Maxwell of the Quorum of the Twelve passed away after his long battle with leukemia.  He will be missed.  We got home about 1:30 and Marlyn soon heated up our food for us.  Afterwards we took a nap and then I got ready to go out to Guimba again to go visiting less-active priesthood brethren with President Cabuyaban.  We visited 3 families.  We showed the video “Windows of Heaven” to the Mactal family.  President Cabuyaban had to have an interview with Sister Emmy.  Afterwards I took President Cabuyaban and William to their homes.  I arrived home about 10:10 pm.  President Mercado, his wife and daughter visited with Mae this evening, plus she said she had other visitors, but still typed part of the family weekly letter.  She also watched the DVD that Mike had made for us entitled “Our Mom”.   We got to bed at 11:00 pm.

 

Saturday, July 24, 2004

 

We got up about 6am and at 7:30, the carpenter and his helper were here to fix the ceiling in the cooking kitchen.  Water had seeped in and causing some problems.  It took them all day to take out a piece of the ceiling and put in a new sheet of plywood, seal it and paint it.  That isn’t the way you fix a roof from leaking, but I didn’t say anything.  We had to stay home all day since they were here.  Marlyn had wanted us to come to their branch party this morning, but we couldn’t leave the house.   I worked on my Elder’s Quorum presentation, part of the time I was looking through the Church magazines for pictures to use in it.  Mae finished the family letter and sent it off to the kids.   She also read some articles from the magazines, to us while I worked on the computer.  They finished soon after 4:00pm so we were able to grab a bite to eat, Mae fixed brownies and I popped pop corn, we put the multi media equipment in the car and headed off to the Munoz Chapel for the baptism of Lesly Ann Parrenas Merto, Brother Nuque’s 8 year old niece.  He baptized her as her father isn’t active in the Church.   It was nice and we were happy to be there.  It was raining hard when we got there and they are not finished with the parking lot so it was like a swimming pool again this week.  They did have a plank to walk to get across the water.  There were a lot of children and youth, but not many adults, so we were happy we were there.  Brother Nuque had texted us inviting us to come.  Bishop Neito was glad we were there also.  The zone leaders also attended.  Afterwards, we went over to Denny’s house to watch the video “The Book of Mormon” which President Mercado had borrowed from the PBO.  It was very good.  We all enjoyed it.  There were about 20 people there including President Mercado’s 2 young children.   We had just gotten home and I was about to go to bed when the cell phone rang.  It was Elder Hulbert, the zone leader.  He said the one new Filipino Elder wants to go home.  He doesn’t speak either Tagalog or English very well and told President Brimhall that he can’t speak or teach so he wants to go home.  He has only been here 2 days.  President Brimhall wants the new Filipino zone leader to go out and work with him to see if he can get him to change his mind, so they needed me to take them out to Guimba.  I was really tired, but, of course, I went.  We brought Elder England back with us.  It was 12:30 AM when I got to bed so soon went to bed.

 

Sunday, July 25, 2004

 

                I woke at 4:30 AM and couldn’t go back to sleep for a while so Mae had me sleep in a little later.  We had to hurry to get out to Guimba by 8 AM.  With others help we got the projector, Screen and VCR set up.  Mae had help and put curtains over some of the windows to darken the room.  We were able to start the showing of the 1st Philippines Conference on time, but then about 20 minutes into the conference the power went off.  Brothers went to borrow a generator but before they could get back the power came back on and we were able to finish the conference.  One other problem occurred when the first tape ended and we had to start the 2nd tape, for they had double recorded about 10 minutes of the tape, so we had to fast forward it to find where to start.  Over all it went well and we had about 200 plus in attendance.  It was 2:30 PM when we got home.  We took a nap and then had a nice baked potatoes and pork chop diner.  Mae and I played Rook for a while this evening.  It was nice to relax and get some rest.  Besides the work in the cooking room that the Land Lord had done yesterday, when we got home this afternoon, they had removed the plants in front and redecorated it with new plants.  We were enjoying the plants and shrubs that was there but I guess it is there place so it is up to them. 

 

Monday, July 26, 2004

 

                We slept in until 6 AM and then had to hurry to get away to go to the Clark’s.  We stopped in Cabanatuan at Blas Edwards and bought some DC’s and a AutoCAD 2004 program.  It cost me P150 or $2.74 for the program, the CD-RW disks only cost me P75 or $1.37 each.  It was about 10 AM when we made it to the Clark’s.  I transferred several of our files onto CD for them, what should help them in preparing training outlines.  They had a nice lunch for us.  This afternoon we took a ride and saw some beautiful country.  It was 6:30PM before we started home and it was almost 7:45PM when we got home. 

 

Tuesday, July 27, 2004

 

                We planned on getting up and going walking but were to tired and fell back to sleep.  When we woke it was too late and we had to hurry to have breakfast ready for the Zone Leaders before we had correlation meeting.  We were ready when they got here.  We had French toast and scrambled eggs.  We enjoyed having the correlation meeting and planned on a Home Teaching Visiting Teaching fireside.  After they left we got reimbursement ready for the ZL’s to take to the mission office.  We then went to town for gas and to take the letters to the ZL’s.  We went to San Jose to transfer our money, got some items at the hardware store and the market and then did our grocery shopping.  While at CVC we picked up the painting that we had done of the two of us.  Our power has been out all day so it was good to be going in the car most of the day.  We had lunch at Jollibees while in San Jose.  Mae did a I. T. L. while we sere at the bank and have a referral for the San Jose Elders and also while at the Palanki we met a young man that offered to take our blood pressure and he ask us how he could join our church.  We got his name, phone number and address and will send in a referral to the mission office as he doesn’t live here locally. I went out to Branch Presidency meeting this evening but President Cabuyaban didn’t feel well so didn’t come.  I visited with him at his home and then went to the office.  I took some money to Sister Villanueva for their Relief Society Work Day tomorrow.  We were on the computer this evening sending e-mails.  Time went fast and we were late getting to bed.

 

Wednesday, July 28, 2004

 

                We went walking this morning.  We had breakfast and then did some planning on the things we needed to do today.  We were busy trying to get it all in.  We went out to the Relief Society work day at Guimba 1st.  We ate dinner at Cindy’s and them went back to the church and set up for the Guimba Pioneer Days program.  We showed the movie “Brigham Young” after the speakers.  There was a fair group that came out.  After the program and movie President Cabuyaban had us take him to visit Sister Dela Cruz on welfare maters.  It was 10:30PM when we got home.

 

Thursday, July 29, 2004

 

                We went walking although my back is hurting.  We got some Hydrocortisone cream the other day and it has really helped with the problem I have been having with sores in my crouch.  We get one item cleared up and now have problems in another area.  I wrote my cousin Mark and told him that I thought we are getting old.  This mission is certainly not the easiest thing I have ever done.  It has been very challenging in many ways.  The other day while at the bank, Mae got visiting with a lady and ITL (invite to lisson) her and she accepted to let the missionaries visit her.  Mae prepared a Book of Mormon with her testimony and picture in it and we took it over to San Jose so the Elders there could deliver it.  We ate lunch at Jollibees, bought a Allen wrench, some nylon rope to hang a picture and some jewelry that Mae saw and liked.  We got e-mails from Mike, Shellie, Jeff and a short note from Scott.  I went out to Guimba and met with President Cabuyaban for a few minutes, I had Elder go visiting with me to three homes on church business.  Mae stayed home and wrote the Family Letter.

 

Friday, July 30, 2004

 

                I woke thinking about the Guimba 1st branch and what can be done about it.  I am really quite at a loss as to what I would recommend if I am asked.  We didn’t walk this morning as we cuddled up for a few minutes and then had to hurry to be ready to pickup the Elder’s for meeting.  We enjoyed the District Development meeting and the Elders enjoyed the cake Mae brought them.  Before leaving the parking lot we had Brother Dalim come over and talked with us.  We ended up loaning him P180 to get a school uniform for his oldest daughter and some gas for his trike.  He talked to us about his niece that has kidney problems and needs dialysis but can’t afford it.  She isn’t a member of the church so we really don’t have any way to help.  There are so many people here in the Philippines that really need help but there is just no way to help them all.  We went to the Mercury Drug and bought the medication for Nomi, and then took them to the Dr. office.  We stopped on the way home at President Cabuyaban’s and had him correct some vouchers to be turned in.  We visited with him for a few minutes.  He seems depressed over the Branch also.  Marlyn had a nice dinner ready when we got home.  After lunch we took a nap.  Nida came about 3 PM and gave both Mae and I a treatment.  We encouraged her with her business plans.  We are still trying to decide just how to help her.  We drove over to brother Dimacio and took the vouchers.  We stopped at President Gajonera’s but he wasn’t home.  We went over to President Gasmenia’s to have him sign the check but it had already been signed by President Mercado.  We feel much better after the treatments today.

 

Saturday, July 31, 2004

 

                I woke about 2AM to use the bathroom and was thinking of the Guimba 1st Branch.  I felt the need to send a report to President Brimhall as to what is happening there.  I spent a couple of hours composing a ruff draft before going back to bed at 4AM.  We got up and went walking at the collage and then came home to shower.  I went back to sleep for a hour and then got up and ate.  Mae helped me compose the letter to President Brimhall.  At 11AM we drove over to Camiline to visit Elder and Sister Maharry and take a flower to Sister Maharry.  We went out to Cindy’s for lunch and then just went back to their place and visited for a while.  We got home at 5:15PM.  We ate some dinner then finished the e-mail to President Brimhall and the letter to our family.  Mae sent the e-mails off and I fell asleep.  This evening Mae worked on materials for Primary and YW.  It was 11PM before we went to bed.
