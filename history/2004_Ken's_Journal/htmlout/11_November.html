                I got up and read in the Book of Mormon for a while.  Mae woke shortly after 6 AM but I let her sleep as long as she would as it was mid-night that she came to bed last night.  We got ready and went out to Panique to their Y.W. program.  Elder Maharry called just before we left the house this morning and said that President Brimhall would like him to give the same presentation in the Camiline district conference as I gave in the Guimba District Conference.  They will come over Monday and I will give him a CD of my presentation.  When we got to Paniqui, they were already playing volleyball.  We watched them for a while and then I went inside and worked on a new presentation I am preparing.  I am not sure just why I am preparing it.  Mae did some last minute changes to her presentation.  I set things up for her and they started at 1:30 PM.  I felt that Mae did a very good job with her presentation but without speaking Tagalog, it is hard to say.  We did have some things we felt needed some attention in the teaching of modesty to the Y. W.  When we got home Mae wrote a letter to the District Young Women President addressing those concerns.  I warmed up some leftovers for dinner.  While Mae was finishing the letter I cleaned up the table and got the turkey bones cooking to make stew tomorrow.  Mae was tired and went to bed.  I got on the computer and did some work on our budget records and entered my journal entry for today.

 

Sunday, November 28, 2004

 

                Mae got up to do her hair but let me sleep in.  We made French toast for breakfast and ate late.  That way we could just eat twice today.  We prepared turkey stew from the turkey bones I cooked yesterday.  We stopped at Guimba 3rd Branch on the way to Paniqui and left a schedule for training starting next week.  I had a talk prepared for Sacrament meeting but the Primary took all the time with their program.  This was the first time President Manuel had conducted with a Primary program so he didn’t realize that it took the complete time.  That was ok with me but I was prepared.  I went in to the youth Sunday school class and was enjoying that when the counselor in the Primary came and asked if I would talk to the Primary children.  I did and then I went into the Y.M. Priesthood class.  That was a fun experience and I was asked to give some thoughts a couple of times.  Mae did some more training in the primary presidency meeting and I went into the Priesthood Executive Meeting.  It was almost 7 PM when we got home.  I heated the stew and set the table while Mae finished the family letter and got it ready to send.  It has been a busy day but a good one.

 

Monday, November 29, 2004

 

                We got up and went walking this morning.  Although it looked like rain, it didn’t, and we walked for over ½ hour.  We got home in time to shower and dress before Marlyn came.  Marlin Dator came and washed our car although it started raining shortly after he finished.  Mike, Jeff and Scott got on the instant messaging with us.  Elder and Sister Maharry came at 10:45 AM.  We went out to eat at Tony’s so they could meet him.  I gave Elder Maharry a copy of the presentation that I gave in district Priesthood Leader meeting that was held in conjunction with District conference.  We enjoyed being together.  Mae and I went grocery shopping after we left Tony’s, and the Maharry’s went back home.  After getting groceries we came home and I prepared a presentation for this evening. 

                We were planning to use my presentation and also show the video “Finding Faith in Christ” for the Family Home Evening with the Eligodo’s tonight, but as we were driving down the road to Bro. Deney R. Eligodo’s home and his parents, the lights went out and we realized there was a brown out.  Since we couldn’t use any of our technology, we went to plan B.  Mae had brought a beautiful picture of Christ, the Family Home Evening Resource Manual, some pictures of Christ and some handouts to give them.  I used my ball of string, which I have used several times to show the pre-existence, earth life and the here after.  While I was explaining that, Bro. Ronald Dator, his wife Kasanthra and his sister Ronnalyn came.  We think Bro. Denny invited them.  I talked about the life of Christ and Mae talked about lessons to be given during December and things to do to make this season more Christ centered.  It went well. 

                Sister Eligodo had made some delicious soup which they served us and we served the Brownies Mae had made.  We enjoyed visiting with them, they are special people.  Bro. Denny returned from his mission about a year ago.  He was an assistant to the president there.  He is a remarkable young man and is now a counselor in the district YM’s Presidency.  He is an only child.  His parents were active in the Church, but they wanted Denny to get his schooling in before he went on his mission.  Pres. Mercado, who is their neighbor, felt he should go now, so talked Denny into going before, which Denny wanted to do also.  Denny’s parents were so upset with Pres. Mercado and their son that they have not been back to Church even though he has returned.  He was hoping we could come and inspire them to go back to Church.  His mother was very receptive, so we hope it helped.

 

Tuesday, November 30, 2004

 

                We went walking at the collage and then came back and showered and cleaned up.  We had correlation meeting with the Zl’s.  I made up some forms for the prospective missionaries for each branch in the Guimba district and then Mae typed them up.  We went to San Jose and made our monthly deposit and withdraw, got our back pack repaired and bought some material to repair the toilet.  We had to go out to out to Guimba and get a report from President Baccay.  The power went off about noon and remained off the rest of the day.
