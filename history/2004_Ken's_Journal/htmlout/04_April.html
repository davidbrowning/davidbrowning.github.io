Saturday, April 24, 2004

Leadership Meeting.  Part I was at the Church and Mae was invited to go there.   I drove over to get the copies at the copy shop for Mae’s class and my training next week, but when I went to pay for them, I didn’t have my wallet.  They let me take them anyway and when Mae came home, I went up to pay them.  Mae said that she told the ladies, she could take 4 of them with her in the car, as they were waiting for trikes to bring them over.  There were lots more than 4 who piled into the car.  The sisters enjoyed the baking class and had a good time.  They also enjoyed eating the brownies and cookies and took some home to their families.  Sister Jean Gasmena had bought the ingredients for two cakes.  She told Mae that if you buy a cake or brownies that size, it costs P300, but she had bought the ingredients for the 2 brownies for less than P180 and she still had half of the ingredients left over.  She was excited.  The problem, however, is that they don’t have ovens to bake them in.   The R.S. President from Munoz 1, Sister Monta, said she would run right out and buy her an over.  She does have more money than most of the other sisters because her husband is a seaman and works on a big ship.  He is only home about 2 months a year.  She later asked Mae if she could borrow our oven to bake her son’s birthday cake next month.  Mae jokingly said “I thought you were going to run right out and buy an over.”  She said “first I’ve got to find the money to buy the measuring cups.”  They all laughed.  Mae furnished the brownies she made to show them how, and then divided them into two groups and one worked in the main kitchen and the other group in the cooking kitchen.  It worked well.  We borrowed a fan from the Elders as it was really hot.  I visited with Pres. Gasmena while Mae had the baking class.  Marlyn attended also and while we were saying “goodbye” to all the R.S. sisters, Marlyn and her sister-in-law were in the house doing the dishes, sweeping the floors, putting up the chairs, etc.  We sure appreciated it as we were so tired.  Marlyn is a gem, we are so grateful for her.  Afterwards, Mae & I relaxed a while before going to bed.

 

Sunday, April 25, 2004

S. class and enjoyed that.  I spent about ½ of the priesthood time visiting with the Young Men’s President about the need to spend time getting the youth out to their meetings.  I then went into Elders Quorum meeting and the first thing that was asked is “What is the main adversity that we have in the church?”  I raised my hand and told them it was laziness, I then expounded a few minutes.  That caused a pause in the lesson.  We came home and got some lunch, I then worked on my presentation for this afternoon and took a nap for a few minutes.  Mae went with me to the church and helped me get my equipment in.  She then took the car and went visiting some sisters.  It was 4:30 PM before we could get started as we were waiting for members of the P.E.C. to get there.  We did have everyone show up.  I think it went quite well, but I am not sure just how President Nieto took it.  I was quite straight forward in my presentation and at one point towards the end, Present Nieto got up and left.  I was not sure if I had upset him or not, but he then came back with treats at the end of the meeting.  I am not sure if he went to the tindahon to buy them or not.  My legs are really hurting me this afternoon and by the time I finished the presentation they were really hurting.  We then stood and talked to President Nuque for another half hour.  My feet and legs hurt until I am about ready to cry.  Mae helped me enter my journal before we went to bed.  She also rubbed my feet for me.  I fell asleep but Mae stayed up until after 11 PM writing a e-mail to our family.
