                We were late getting started this morning as I didn’t sleep very well.  We did go walking at the collage.  We met Maria and her husband as they were also walking together.  We visited a few minutes with them.  As there is a school break now, he and the children have a little free time.  Maria said that her children have seen us walking and told them that they, their children, hopped that they, Maria & her husband, will walk together when they are our ages.  As we completed our walk we go by their home so we stopped and asked if their family would like to see the movie, “Johnny Lingo”.  They were excited and want us to come a week from Monday.  Mae went visiting this afternoon and I went visiting with President Baccay and Brother Corpus.  It was after 9 PM when I got home.  We got on the internet to send an e-mail to President Brimhall.  Scott saw us on so he got on for a few minutes.  David then saw us on so he got on for a few minutes.  It was good to visit with both of them.  Gail had sent us a thank you note for sending her the information from my journal about their wedding.  It’s late and Mae is still working on her teeth.

 

                I will add in here the story that Brother Norberto Corpus told me of his mission call.  He said that when he filled out the missionary application form, all of his family told him that he should not go and that if he did none of them would help him.  He said this included even his mother, who is a member.  Only his father, who was not a member, and who was very sick, gave him any encouragement.  His father died right after he turned in his application.  He received his call one week after he turned it in.  All his family including his mother kept their word and would not help him in anyway.  His branch president paid for his medical and dental check ups, and gave him two pair of socks and two polo shirts.  A large Samoan Elder gave him a pair of pants.  Norberto is a very small person, so the pants were way to large for him, but a Sister in the branch altered them so they fit a little better.  The Mission President of the Angeles Mission, President Black, bought him two white shirts and some garments.  Someone else gave him a pair of used shoes and he left for his mission with his clothes in a plastic sack, as he didn’t have a suit case and didn’t have enough clothes to put in one anyway.  He said that none of his family ever wrote to him during the two years he was away.  I asked him if it was worth it and he said it was the best decision he had every made.  He also told me that as soon as he returned home he went to the Temple for his father and did his work for him.

 

Friday, October 29, 2004

 

                We got up and had to hurry to be ready and leave for Paniqui to attend District Development meeting.  From there we went to Moncada and visited Sister Vengco and her family.  They took us over to show how they extract the sugar from the sugar cane.  They were cooking rice in woven pouches in the hot sugar cane syrup.  It was interesting to see their press to extract the sugar pulp from the cane.  It was also very interesting to see their boiler, and how they use the remains of the cane stock for fire to boil the syrup.  They showed us how they use a palm leaf for the weaving material and how they weave the pouches.  They are very fast and use an assembly line to do the weaving.  We had a very enjoyable visit with them.  From there we went to Gerona to visit President and Sister Nadado.  They were very excited to have us come and visit.  Sister Nadado had prepared pansit (a fine noodle with vegetables and meat).  It was very good and we ate until we were full, then Sister Nadado insisted that we take the rest home with us.  We visited for some time with them before we came home; they were so hospitable to us.  Mae bought some souvenirs that Sister Nadado had made.  We will miss these people when we leave.  The driving in this traffic will be one thing I will not miss.  I just about go crazy by the time we drive very far.  We came close to a bad accident as a dump truck just pulled out into the traffic and made everyone else slam on the brakes.  The driver just didn’t care for he was the biggest.  You have to keep alert all the time for anything to happen.  After getting home I took a nap while Mae made a cake for Sister Gajonera’s Birthday and took it to her.  Roger Sylvia came by and we visited a while with him.  We had him stay and eat with us.  He helped me fix a problem with out computer and I loaned him some videos and a DVD to use in his seminary social tomorrow.  We had a couple of phone calls this evening that worried Mae for a call came in on the cell phone that no one would talk on and when she hung it up a call came in on the land line.  We even had Roger take the receiver and try to talk to the caller. 

 

                I will add a story that President and Sister Nadado told us today.  The Nadado’s had been married for some time but were struggling financially.  They had six children, when in the year 2000 Sister Nadado got an opportunity to go abroad to Russia to work.  President Nadado at that time was the Branch President of the Gerona branch.  He did not want Bessie to go but she insisted and especially with the encouragement of all of her extended family.  President Nadado said it was really hard to take care of the children and be Branch President, but he said he needed to stay in that calling in order to keep his sanity.  He said he asked the Mission President, President Black, to please not release him.  When Sister Nadado arrived in Russia, attended the branch of the church there, she was immediately assigned Home Teachers and Visiting Teachers, who were a couple missionary, Elder and Sister Frandsen from Sandy Utah.  When they found out that she had left six children at home they told her she needed to go home.  She said that she couldn’t afford to go, plus her family needed the money.  The Frandsens contacted their family and other friends and they raised the funds for her return fare, plus money for them to buy a palay combine to help earn a living.  At that time she was given an opportunity to accept a change of employment with a company in England which would give her a large increase in income, provide her with unlimited calls home, a bike and a paid trip home for two weeks every six months.  She said that she couldn’t make the decision alone, so went to her Branch President to ask him for his advice.  He told her that he didn’t know what she should do, but Heavenly Father knows.  They knelt in prayer and when they got up he told her that Heavenly Father wanted her to return home.  She also felt that was the answer, and so she came back to the Philippines.  She, of course, had also asked her husband and had him pray for the right decision also.  When her side of their family found out the decision she had made, they said that was the stupidest thing she could do to turn down such an opportunity, and they were angry with her.  We’re sure that part of the reason is that they expected her to give some of the money she made, to them.  But, both President and Sister Nadado still feel that it was the right decision.   She needed to be home to support, love and help her husband, and teach, train, love and help her children.  They have a wonderful marriage and a wonderful family.
