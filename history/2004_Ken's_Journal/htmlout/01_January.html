T. / V. T. Fireside for Saturday.  They left shortly after 9 PM.  We called Mike and are having him check to see if it is still possible to get the power point projector in time for Sandi to bring it with her.  If so we asked him to try to get it for us.  It was good to visit with him for a few minutes.  Brother Silvia has text us a couple of times today hinting that he needs money.  We text back and told him that we just couldn’t if for no other reason as it against mission rules.  We just wish we know just how to help these people.  It is already 10:30 PM and there is so much we need to get done.  I guess we just do what we can, good night.

 

Friday, January 30, 2004

 

                We received this letter from Mark today.

Dear Ken and Mae,

Enjoyed reading of your busy service.  You are doing a great work there and it certainly helps for Ken to have been raised in a country environment that helps you to cope from experience.  However, it is obviously more primitive than any of us have known.  Sandy told Bob she might come by today for a visit.  .  I didn't know they permitted family visits but obviously they do.  It will be a great experience for all of you.  We are happy for you.  Bob is sending hair spray and coral calcium thinking you might not have received the package of coral calcium sent in December which included vitamins and whey.

Bob, Carol, Irvin, Sandy and I attended Bernice's's funeral.  It was a good tribute and the bishop gave an outstanding gospel sermon.  We visited with Leslie, his wife and 2 of his children,  Aunt Wilma, Lee, Rich, Lois and daughter, Bruce (came from his work selling in Burley), Bill Hulet and daughter, Tammy.  Uncle Irvin seemed to be in shock.  He was perfectly stoic even at the cemetery.  We were told that at the viewing the night before he was leaning over the casket and talking to Bernice asking her several times to open her eyes and finally saying you don't have to.  He told me he would climb in the casket with her if he could.  He insists staying in his home in chubbuck.  Leslie is trying to arrange for a neighbor to daily check on him and feed him.  Irvin needs family with him constantly.  He has no desire to live.  He will probably stop eating soon.    It won't be long until he goes.  Wilma is taking it very hard.  The covers on your vehicles are staying on well.  There is still about 2 feet of snow on the ground.  The temperature has been in the 20's and a few days in the high teens for about 3 weeks but warmer the last three days with some snow melt.  We love you and pray for you. 

 

Love,

Bob, Carol and Mark 

 

                We attended District Development meeting with the Paniqui Elders this morning and really enjoyed that.  When we got home we went straight to San Jose to make a deposit and withdraw from the bank.  We also did some shopping for our groceries while we were there.  It was 2:30 PM when we got home.  Marlyn had called the landlord and he had some men come over and repair some lights.  We ate and then started work on the computer.  Mae got the H.T. / V.T. questions typed in Tagolog for tomorrow.  She also made the corrections to the Branch Organizational reports.  That took the rest of the day.  We got on the internet to send a letter to Sandi & Shellie, and saw the letter from Mark.  We didn’t think that Aunt Bernice would go before Uncle Irven.  We sure feel bad for Uncle Irven and are sure that he will soon go now.  Well it’s late and we still have some work for tomorrow’s presentations.

 

Saturday, January 31, 2004

 

                It was 1 AM before we got to bed, so we slept in until 6 AM.  We had to hurry in order to get to Guimba 3 by 9:30 so we could get set up.  However, when we got there the gates were still locked.  It really upset me that they (Philippine standard time) are almost always late.  We got started at 10:45 AM.  Our presentation went well.  The Elders got some of the members to roll play Home Teaching first wrong and then right.  Our tests seem to also go well.  We got home and went over to the Book Store to get more copies.  We came home and got something to eat, and then took a 15 minute nap, before going to Munoz 2nd Branch and giving the same presentation there.  It also went well and all those that participated did well and enjoyed it.  President Nieto wants us to do it again in a couple of weeks and he will try to get others to attend.  We came home very tired but felt like it was worth while.
