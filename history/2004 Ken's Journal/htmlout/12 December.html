Wednesday, December 01, 2004

 

                We didn’t have any power this morning but still got up and went walking.  When we came home, we had to take a “cold bucket shower” since our power is still off and when our power is off, so is our water.  We store water in our empty plastic whey buckets so we did have some water, thank goodness.  I am having a lot of pain in my neck and wish that I could get a treatment from Randy.  Mae called, and Nida will come tomorrow and give me a treatment.  The power came back on at 10 AM.  Mae finished the reports on the potential missionaries.  We got an e-mail from JaNae with some pictures of Dawson attached, they are sure good.  I worked on the budget and got Decembers started.  We drove to Tarlac and went out to eat with Elder and Sister Hyer at the Fortune café.  It was really good but we thought it was a little expensive, and then I figured the exchange rate and it came to less than $10.00 for the two of us.  We had to laugh at thinking P500 was expensive.  The Hyer’s also gave us some ideas as to where we want to tour in the Philippines before we go home.  We went to Paniqui where I gave some training to the Branch Mission Leader.  That went well and while I was doing that Mae attended the mid-week activity.  We got home about 8:30 PM.  I entered my journal entry.

                 At 5:17 PM we lost power and the storm is picking up.  Mae had me work on all the windows and get them closed just in case the storm gets worse.  We tried before the power went off to get an e-male to our family to let them know that we think we are safe but with the storm we couldn’t get through.  The winds blow all night quite hard but we are ok.  Mae and I set out on the porch and played rummy for a while, using out battery operated lantern.  (I won two games)  We are sure glad for that.

                We have had problems with ants every since we have been here.  I guess they are just common every place.  When we first got here and wrote about them, Georgia and Rick sent us some ant traps.  They have helped some.  We had them coming into the bedroom a few months ago and I realized they were coming in from the AC so I went outside and caulked around the condenser lines and that seen to have taken care of them.  Last week they again became a pest and again they were coming from the AC.  This time I think they just had a nest and I sprayed it heavy with bug spray and seem to have stopped them.  In the cooking room we have had a lot of them and found that they were coming in from a crack where the ceiling had been repaired but hadn’t been sealed, so I caulked that and hopefully stopped them there.  Both of these areas were large black ants.  Last night Mae got a candy bar out to eat.  She will get a candy bar and just eat a bit every day or so and then rap it back up.  Tonight she got it out and took a bite when she noticed small sugar ants all over her hand.  When she looked the candy bar was full of them.  She sure was spitting and washing her mouth out.  It is just amazing how those little things can smell sugar and get to it.  Oh well, another thing you just have to put up with in the Philippines.
