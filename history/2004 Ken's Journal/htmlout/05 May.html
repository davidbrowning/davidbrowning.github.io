Tuesday, May 18, 2004

 

                We got up at 3 AM and left at 4:15 AM for Clarkfield.  We got there by 6:00 AM.  We had interviews with President Brimhall for new Temple Recommends.  Elder and Sister Clark went with us to the Manila Temple.  We went through Sealing of Roberto Sagun and His brides marriage.  Elder & Sister Clark went on a endowment session with Elder & Sister Hyer and Elder & Sister Maharry.  Mae and I went out for pictures and a Wedding breakfast.  That was really nice.  They had a large group attending the wedding and many more attending the wedding breakfast.  We then went over to the distribution center and bought some Church supplies.  Mae also had ordered special embroidered temple envelopes for all the children & spouses, plus her and I. They are beautiful.   Mae met the lady & paid for them, plus 3 nativities from another lady.   It was a little confusing getting everyone together, but we all went to the Holiday Hotel where we had rooms reserved.  Our room was on the 27th floor and we had a wonderful view.  This is a beautiful hotel.  Thankfully, it only costs us $58.00 because we live here and have a Filipino driver’s license, otherwise, it would be about $150.00.  This evening we went to the Manila Hotel for dinner.  It wasn’t just dinner, it was “fine dining”.  Elder & Sister Hyer made arrangements for us to have a van and driver for tonight and tomorrow.  The hotel is on the waterfront and so there were ships in the harbor.  It was dark so we couldn’t see the ocean very well, but it was still neat.  The grounds, fountain and gardens were beautiful.  The food was excellent, but just us and another couple were there.  Soon the other couple left.  They had a 5 piece orchestra playing for us that were very good.  They played up on the stage, but soon came down to our table and serenaded us.  They asked for requests and they could play anything we requested.  It was wonderful.   We enjoyed dancing to their music.  The Hotel was very elegant .We were then given a tour of a gallery that had a lot of history of the Hotel and famous visitors who had stayed before.  This is where General MacArthur had his suite.  The host took us through a honeymoon suite and another suite for dignitaries and then took us into MacArthurs suite.  It was beautiful.  They had left the furniture and furnishings as it was then, along with the pictures on the walls, lamps, etc.  It looked like someone was living there.  It had a huge picture of General MacArthur on the wall behind his desk.  We took pictures of our husbands in front of the picture.  We went out onto the balcony and it was a beautiful view.  It was great to be there.  We got back to the hotel about 11:30 p.m, but it was a wonderful day.

 

Wednesday, May 19, 2004

 

We arose at 5:45 a.m. and met the Hyers, Clarks and Maharrys for breakfast.  The breakfast is paid for with the price of our rooms and it is wonderful – a buffet with all kinds of breakfast foods.  The chef makes omelets for us or cooks eggs anyway we want.   After breakfast, we drove to the PBO where we met the driver again and climbed into the van and we were off to the Bazaar.  They have this bazaar once a month and all the ladies at the PBO and Temple and other missionaries in Manila, plus other places go there.  We have been once before.  The men are not too crazy about this idea, but the women love it.  Mae bought wooded grandparents toys (to stay at our home for the grandchildren to play with when they come to visit.) plus she bought toys, etc., for the grandchildren.  We then went out to the war memorial.  There were 55,000 white crosses and some stars.  The stars were for the jews who were killed in the war and the crosses for the Christians.  These were all Americans who died in the war.  We felt like we were on holy ground with the blood of all these Americans in the navy, army, air force or marines.  They had acres and acres of land as it used to be a military base.  The grounds are beautiful and many people are hired to take care of it.  They have a beautiful monument with all the names of those who were killed in action craved in beautiful stone walls all around a huge circle.  In the middle of the circle is another monument.  We took lots of pictures.  I am proud to be an American and proud of what America stands for and how these men and women gave their lives for their country and for freedom.  From there we went back to the PBO where we split up and the Maharrys rode with the Hyers to go home and the Clarks rode with us.  After dropping off the Clarks at the Mission Home, we stopped at the duty free stores for Mae to get some more brownie mixes and cake mixes.  We stopped in Tarlac at McDonalds for something to eat and arrived home about 8:30.  It has been raining pretty steady since Angeles.  It got really heavy in Guimba and the rest of the way home with lots of thunder and lighting.  We were beat, but Mae put things away while I entered what we spent on our budget.  It was about 11:00 p.m. when we got to bed. 

 

Thursday, May 20, 2004

 

                It was about 6:15 AM when we got up and started packing to go to Baler.  We left at 9 AM and stopped for a minute in San Jose.  The road that we always take out of San Jose was closed and it took us a while to find a alternate rout.  The roads were wet and muddy and it seemed rougher than we remembered but we made fairly good time and got to Baler at 2 PM.  We checked in but there was no power through out Baler, but it came on about 3:30 PM.  We enjoyed a short nap.  At 4:30 PM we went to the meeting house and others came right after we got there.  President Go told us, when he got there, that the power was off because they had failed to pay the power bill and hadn’t receive a notice.  We met outside on the porch and had enough light to do fair.  We had planned on having the Elders Quorum Presidency and the Relief Society Presidency there, but we had President Go, his 1st counselor, and ½ dozen sisters there.  We did some training but it wasn’t quite what we had planned.  The 1 counselor in the Elders Quorum finally came.  When it got dark, we quite and closed the meeting.  We gave the 1st counselor in the branch presidency a ride home.  He lives 7.6 kl from the church house and said there are three other families that lives out that far but can’t afford to come to church.  I was rather discouraged at the way it went.  Mae took the sisters and she did fairly well. 

 

Friday, May 21, 2004

 

                I woke at 5 AM and couldn’t go back to sleep.  Mae slept until almost 7 AM.  We went walking on the beach and enjoyed that.  The day went fast and I soon went up for the training of the branch clerk at 2 PM.  Power still wasn’t on and President Go needed to go make some copies, so I waited.  I fell asleep waiting.  President Go informed me that the clerk couldn’t be there until 4 PM.  I went back and had some lunch and returned with Mae.  We really didn’t get started with the clerk training until 4:30 PM, so that put us behind on the other trainings.  The training did go well with all three groups.  Mae felt good with her trainings also.  I didn’t finish with the training until 9:30 PM.  At 9 PM I asked President Go if we wanted to stop and he said that we should continue with the training.  The Elders stopped in during the evening to see how it was going.  I visited with Elder Gooch about the possibility of a Basic Unit in the area north of the large bridge.  He will try to get some information on that area.

 

Saturday, May 22, 2004

 

                Although we got up at 5:30 AM we had to hurry in order to pack and check out and be at the Church by 8:30 AM.  Some members soon got there but President Go didn’t get there until 930 so we waited for him.  The presentation went well, we had President Go give the opening address.  The Elders quorum president gave a short talk taken from the First Presidency and the R.S. President gave a short talk from the Visiting Teaching message.  Mae and I then gave a presentation using the computer and projector on the need to sacrifice.  We broke up the group and went out Home Teaching and Visiting Teaching.  Mae and I went out with them.  After 1 ½ hours we all came back to the church and reported on our visits.  It really went well and they had a good time.  We had the Elders buy some treats for us to have when we got back together.  We then showed the video “How Rare a Possession”, we had a lot more come during the film.  Before going home Mae wanted to go down to see the ocean once again.  We left Baler at 3:30 PM and got home at 8:30 PM.  We were tired and had to unpack when we got here.

 

Sunday, May 23, 2004

 

                We attended meetings in the Guimba 1st Branch.  During the Sacrament meeting I was sustained as the 1st Counselor to President Cabuyaban.  I was asked to talk during the meeting.  After Sacrament meeting President Mercado set me apart in my calling.  Mae then went in to Primary and helped there.  I went in to Sunday School during which President Cabuyaban gave the lesson because the Sunday School teacher didn’t show up and the Sunday School President doesn’t feel that he can. Also during Priesthood meeting the Elders Quorum President didn’t show up so I conducted and President Cabuyaban gave that lesson.   After the block of meetings, I helped with the recording and counting of the donations.   We then took a break and Mae and I came home.  Mae had me take a short nap while she prepared some dinner.  I picked up President Cabuyaban on my way to the church.  He said he wanted to take a nap, but his children wanted him to play with them.  We had a short Branch Presidency meeting.  Then we held a P E C meeting at which only the President of the Sunday School and the Full time missionaries came.  That meeting went quite well, although I think I talked to much.  President Cabuyaban met with the Relief Society President and went over some welfare items.  While he did that I met with the District Young Men President, Norberto Carpuz, and we discussed some training that is coming up and also went over the organization of the Young Men Aaronic Priesthood program.  He felt that I helped him a lot.  President Cabuyaban, Brother Norberto Carpuz and I went over to visit a family that the husband is very sick and dieing.  It was 8 PM when I dropped President Cabuyaban off and came home.  Mae had been on the internet, so I couldn’t call her to let her know that I was on the way home.  It has been a very interesting day.  While we were visiting, flying bugs came in from every where.  Norberto explained that they were termites.  It is the migratory season for them.  When I got home Mae said that she heard something that sounded like rain on our bedroom window.  When she pulled back the drapes she saw all these flying bugs.  I explained to her what they were and we remembered them from a year-a-go.

 

Monday, May 24, 2004

 

                We slept in, so we didn’t go walking.  After breakfast we got on instant messaging with Jeff and Gail.  We don’t know what happened to the rest of the family.  I went with President Cabuyaban to send in the weekly reports and make the deposits.  When I got back the Land Lord was here trying to repair the kitchen area chandelier as it came lose from the ceiling and almost fall to the floor, but was stopped by the electrical wiring.  Mae and I went to San Jose and did some shopping.  We ate at Jollibees while there.  We like to go upstairs to eat so that we can watch the traffic of San Jose.  It is really quite amusing.  After getting home we put the groceries away.  I cut up two chickens, hoping that Marlyn will not cut it up smaller.  I worked on our budget until I became frustrated and gave up.  I entered my journal and we went to bed.

 

Tuesday, May 25, 2004

 

                I woke shortly after 3 AM and couldn’t go back to sleep.  Mae woke at 5:30 AM and we went walking at the collage.  We came home and showered, and then prepared breakfast for the Munoz Elders, Elder Bas, Elder Jenson, Elder Ebanza and Elder Holberg.  They came at 8:30 AM.  We enjoyed having them and visiting with them.  We made plans to go on Monday Evenings with them to have a Family Home Evening with selected families.  After they left we read a letter that Shellie sent and some pictures that she had prepared for us.  It was good to find out why she hasn’t e-mailed us lately, her computer is not working.  I took a nap and Mae worked on organizing our pictures.  I started a presentation on the Aaronic Priesthood Organization in the Branches.  I needed some pictures of a Branch Presidency, for the presentation, so we visited the Munoz 1st Branch Presidency.  It was good to visit with each family.  We think it is quite cute that we are often referred to as “the couple”, as we go around.  The children enjoy gathering around us and try to talk to us.  They get very excited when Mae ask them their names in Tagalog.  The people also really like to have their pictures taken and are always asking for a copy.  As our printer doesn’t do the best in printing pictures, we try to send them a copy by e-mail if they have a address.  When we got home Mae prepared a light super of cottage cheese and toast.  We worked on the picture organizing for a couple of hours before going to bed.

 

Wednesday, May 26, 2004

 

                I woke early and couldn’t go back to sleep.  When Mae woke we did go walking at the collage.  We went to the Dr. here in Munoz and had the nurse take Mae’s blood pressure.  It was a little high, but not to bad.  We went to San Jose to make our bank transactions and to pay our water bill at Aqua Soft.  When we got home I took a short nap, while Mae made us a lunch.  We left for Guimba shortly after 11 AM and went to the Dr. Office to pay for Nomi’s visit, but the receptionist was out to lunch.  We then met President Cabuyaban and visited a brother that gave us some pepper plant starts.  President Cabuyaban gave us some pechay starts and some squash seeds.  We met the Relief Society President and the Employment Specialist and went visiting families that are growing gardens as a preparedness program.  We were able to visit six families as well as President Cabuyaban and give them some plants and seeds for planting.  It was a good experience to be with these two sisters and visit the families.  We stopped at the Dr. Office again but they had no record to the last visit, so we didn’t pay them.  Sister Cabuyaban got sick last night and didn’t feel good today, so President Cabuyaban didn’t go visiting with us.  He also canceled the Branch Presidency meeting for tonight.  We came home and the power was off, so it was hot.  We were hot and tired so we laid down and rubbed each others feet and tried to take a nap, but it was to hot.  Mae got up to get a drink and found that the power had came back on.  After turning on the A/C we took a nap for a couple of hours.  We then stayed up until mid-night.  Mae worked on her training for this week end and I worked on a new budget program.

 

Thursday, May 27, 2004

 

                We were up late last night so didn’t go walking this morning.  I woke shortly after 5 AM anyway and didn’t go back to sleep.  We attended District Development meeting with the Guimba and San Jose Districts.  We are always impressed with the Elders and the great job that they do.  We went in with the San Jose Elders as we don’t get to see them very often.  We were very amazed with Elder Cropper, who has only been out 3 to 6 months.  I didn’t think he would make a very good missionary when he first came out and we were so impressed with him and his great abilities and knowledge.  President Brimhall was here interviewing the Elders.  We both wondered if he wanted to interview us, but he hasn’t had the time in the past, so we left.  He called us later and said he would have liked to visit with us, so we will see him tomorrow.  Elder Clark called and they will not be able to attend District Conference with us this weekend.  We were disappointed as we were looking forward to having them stay with us.  I got a new budget table set up that should make it a little easier to keep track of our budget.  I finished developing a presentation on the “Organization of the Aaronic Priesthood and Young Men’s program.”  I will have President Brimhall review it tomorrow.  I am hoping that it will be simple enough for the leaders here to understand the program.

 

Friday, May 28, 2004

 

                I woke at 5 AM but let Mae sleep until 6AM.  I then found out that she had stayed up until 11 PM on the inter-net.  I should have let her sleep longer but we had to pick up the Munoz Elders at 8 AM for San Jose / Guimba District Conference.  President Brimhall’s opening talk was on the Dispensations of time and apostasy, Sister Brimhall spoke on Guidance and Obedience.  Both gave excellence talks.  The conference lasted until 3 PM and then President Brimhall met with Mae and I for a short interview.  He also watched my presentation on the Aaronic Priesthood Organization.  He liked the presentation and wanted a copy of it that he might use it in a meeting tomorrow morning.  After taking the Elders home, we stopped by the Dr. office to get Mae’s blood pressure taken.  They were closed for the day.  We think that the reason that it was high the last week, is because she hadn’t been taking her medication.  The one thing we found is that her medication is working.  When we got home I sent the presentation to President Brimhall by e-mail.  We got a e-mail from Mark telling us that Bob was operated on and had a hart bypass due to 5 arteries being blocked.  He was in the operating room for 5 hours, but is now doing better and they will know more tomorrow.  We appreciate Mark keeping us informed.  President Cabuyaban called and wants to hold a Branch Presidency meeting this evening.  We met at 6 PM for a couple of hours and got a couple of things discussed.  I feel that our meetings need to last longer and we need to do more towards visiting and getting priesthood holders active.  One thing about it, we have only one way to go, because we are about as low as the branch can get.

 

Saturday, May 29, 2004

 

                We got up and went walking this morning.  We visited a gentleman from Mapal that is here studding plant science at CLSU.  When we got home we visited with Ed, our neighbor, and set up a evening on the 11th of June for the neighbor to get together for a evening.  Marlyn came this morning to work as she didn’t come yesterday.  We were still getting showered & preparing breakfast.  Brother Norberto Corpuz, the District Young Men President came at noon to see the Aaronic Priesthood Presentation.  We also invited him to eat lunch with us.  He commented that we had enough food for ten people on the table.  After lunch we did get a nap in.  We both did some work on the computer this afternoon.  At 2:45 PM we went to the Munoz chapel for District Conference meetings.  Elder Edgardo Fernando of the Seventies is the visiting General Authority with President and Sister Brimhall also on the program.  We took our fans, overhead projector and screen to the church for use this evening.  The AP’s helped us by using the mission van.  It was 8 PM before we got home.

 

Sunday, May 30, 2004

 

                District Conference was held this morning.  We were about late because I filled out the “Branch Organizational Reports” and printed them off for the District Executive Secretary.  President Mercado’s opening address was good.  President And Sister Brimhall both gave excellent talks.  A sister from Guimba 3rd Branch gave a talk and Sister Fernando gave talks but they were mainly in Tagalog so I understood very little.  Elder Fernando gave a very good talk and really gave some straight information for the members to consider.  There was fair attendance until you realized the membership that should have been there.  We stayed for the meeting with new members held after the main meeting.  I helped Mae get some dinner started and then I laid down and took a nap.  After lunch I went out to Guimba, picking up President Cabuyaban on the way and we held P. E. C. meeting.  After the meeting President and I went visiting.  We visited the Mother of the only missionary that is out from the Guimba District.  We then visited two brothers and their families.  This is where I am really having problems with not speaking the language as well as not knowing the people.  We also stopped to visit a mother that has a son that is mentally sick and is killing people.  Because of his mental condition the police can’t hold him in jail although that is where they have him now, but unless someone files charges they will have to let him out.  The family doesn’t know what to do with him as there are not facilities for the mentally ill here in the Philippines.  We took some medication into the jail for him at the request of the mother.  The pills are costing P100 per day and that is what the mother makes a day.  We then took a letter to the R. S. President, and then visited a family that the church is helping with welfare.  I explained that the policy of the Church is not to give the recipient of assistance cash, but to have the R. S. President of the Branch President pay for the items.  She accepted that answer and apologized for feeling bad towards President Cabuyaban.  President Mercado wanted to visit with me this evening but I was to late getting home.  Mae and I visited for a while and then ate some fried chicken and toast.  I filled out my journal and we went to bed.

 

Monday, May 31, 2004

 

                We got up and went walking although we were still tired.  At 6 AM the power went off and we were without power until almost 6 PM.  We were glad that we had put some water in storage as when the power is off, we are without water also.  We had to shower out of a bucket, but at least we had that water.  President Cabuyaban came at 6:30 AM with the financial reports.  He told us that as he had to leave early he tried to slip out without waking his family.  As he went to leave, Iven called out “Daddy didn’t you forget something?”  to which he asked what.  “You forgot to give momma and I a kiss.”  Iven is about 3 years old.  After breakfast we got on the instant messaging with Scott.  He is the only one that got on with us today.  We talked for about an hour when the battery went dead on the computer and shut us off.  We had tried to find out if the Munoz 1st Branch Relief Society was still planning on the baking class today.  We finally had to drive down to Sister Monta’s house.  Her phone had lost it’s charge and without electricity she couldn’t receive the calls.  They still want to come.  I brought Mae home for her to get ready.  I got her some groceries from the store in Munoz, and some LP Gas.  I then went to San Jose to mail some things at LBC and had planned on getting the rest of the groceries at CVC, but CVC couldn’t get their generator to run so had to close the store.  I did get some large nails and got my boots repaired.  Both pair needed the heel replaced.  Mae had 10 sisters here.  They had a good time.  I went over to President Gasmena’s to turn in the Branch reports.  This evening we went over to the Tobias’s home and held a FHE with them.  Thanks to Mae, that went well.  We got home at 9 PM quite tired.  We are glad to have the power back on and running water.
