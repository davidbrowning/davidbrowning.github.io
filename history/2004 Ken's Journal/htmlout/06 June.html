LETTER TO MIKE:

 

Tuesday, June 15, 2004

 

Dear Mike, Becky and family,

 

                I enjoyed visiting with you, Mike, yesterday on instant-messaging.  We were sorry you had to leave before we had time to ask about your family.  We appreciate Becky’s letters and hearing about you, Becky and the kids.  It sounds like the kids are growing so fast.  We can somewhat see what we have missed, being over here for two years, as we look at the youth here and see how they have grown.  When we do this we have a little sick feeling.  We hope that we can make up for some of this missed time when we get home.  We do know that this is what we were supposed to do and we are learning much, are having many experiences, and we love the people.

 

                Mike, we know that your time has many demands and hate to add to your burdens, but we need to ask a little more of you.  We were able to visit for a few minutes with you yesterday but maybe we can explain a little more about our plans with our home.  Before we accepted our mission call we had tried to sell the home, as you know, hoping to clear $50,000 that we could then use to build in Wyoming.  With the realtors fees and the way houses were dropping in cost, had we sold then we could not have cleared that amount.  That, along with the worry of Grandma Jensen, is what prompted me to go to the Temple seeking answers.  From there we put in our papers for a mission, and instead of building first, we went on a mission.   The realtor thought the prices of homes would go up, but they haven’t.  Scott just told us that our neighbors, the Banners, who had really fixed up their home by putting in oak railings, hard wood floors and many other improvements, had their home listed at $150,000 and they moved into his parent’s home who left for a mission.  They have been there for over a year and their home has not sold, so they just moved back into it this week.   The Parks lost their home and it sold for $120,000.  He has checked with other homes which have sold and even had the realtor, who sold the Parks home, walk through our home and he estimated it at $127,000.  Of course, we will have an appraisal right away to see, and hopefully, it will be higher.

                We are having Scott & Mishelle fix the back patio door as it had pulled away from the steps before we left.  Kip is going to do it for $1,000 (new door and cost of labor.)  We are also having them put in the new carpet now so the appraisal will be as high as possible, making it possible for their down payment to be added in.  We are having Mom’s friend at work send us the e-mail address where we can get some money out of our 401K as Mom has to have it out of there by the time she reaches 65 anyway.   The door and carpet we will pay for now before the appraisal, and then the price of the door and other needed repairs to the home, will be taken out of the price of the sale after we have received our $55,000. 

                We feel this is a good time to sell to Scott and Mishelle since they can get the interest rate at 5 ½ percent.  Also, we will have the money to begin working on the house soon after we get home.  We will eliminate the realtor’s fee this way, which takes a big chunk out.  As you can see, we should clear from $50,000 to $55,000 through this sale.  I do not have an exact estimate of the cost to build, but feel that we will do ok at that amount.  I think that this will also be a good buy for Scott and Mishelle.

 

                With you having power of attorney, we will need to have you intercede for us.  I have attached a copy of the letter to Scott & Mishelle for you and have told Scott that you will be the one to handle and sign all agreements.  We hope this will not cause you too much work and worry.
