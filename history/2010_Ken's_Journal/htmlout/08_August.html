Sunday, August 01, 2010

 

Dear Granddaughter Ashley,

                Grandpa had hoped that we might drive home to be with you on this special day in your life.  I am sorry that my health is stopping Grandma and me from doing that.  When we found out that I was going to have this operation and we were told that I could not work for a couple of weeks, we thought that it might work out so that we could come home for this special occasion but I guess Grandpa is getting to old for my body to heal that fast.  I guess it is like waking up on a school day being too sick to go to school but also to sick to get up and play. 

                May I still tell you some of my feeling as to what this special day means to me?  Of course this special day, that I am writing about, is the day you are BAPTIZED.  Although it was over 63 years ago that I was baptized, I still remember that day.  I am sorry to say that it was not because of my mother or father’s testimony, teachings or encouragement that I was baptized but because of the love of my primary teacher.  When she realized that my parent’s faith was not strong enough to teach me the importance of baptism into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints, she continued to teach me each week in our primary class and then, several months after my 8th birthday, she arranged for me to have an interview with my Bishop.  She then picked me up on the appointed day and took me to the Stake Baptismal Day which was held at our Stake Center over 10 miles away from where I lived, in the next community.  There is not much that I remember about that day except that I didn’t know anyone that was there, except my primary teacher.  I do not remember the talks or the music and I did not even know the one that baptized me.  I do remember that after being baptized that I felt good and I also remembered that my primary teacher loved me.  The next day was Fast Sunday and my Uncle Albert, who was also serving in the bishopric, confirmed me a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints.  I do not remember if anyone else in my family was in attendance at the meeting or not that day.

                When looking at how much I remembered about my baptismal day, some people may say, “Why do we baptize a child so young?  Why not wait until they are older, maybe 16 or 18 or even 21 when they are an adult and can decide for themselves?”  Yes we have many wonderful and faithful members of the Church that were not baptized until they were much older.  I believe that for some reason; only known to Our Heavenly Father, some of us were blessed with the privilege of coming to this earth with special parents and circumstances wherein we are blessed to have the privilege of being baptized as early in our life as 8 years of age.  We know from our scriptures that, Our Heavenly Father has stopped Satan from having power over the hearts of little children until 8 years of age.  I believe that because of the Great Love Heavenly Father has for each of his children, as soon as circumstances are right, that He wants us to have a very special helper, a personal guide and a comforter, to be with us as we go through the rest of our life.  Most of the time we refer to this helper as the Holy Ghost.  Ashley, for you that time is now. 

                May I testify to you, as one of many who loves you very much, that I know the Holy Ghost.  He has helped me make important decisions in my life.  He has given me answers to my prayers, often instantly.  He has comforted me when I was afraid and needed help when there was no one else that could help me.  The greatest experience to me was when I needed help and asked to know if the Book of Mormon was true and if Joseph Smith was a Prophet of God.  That answer came to me as I left my bed room, after I had completed reading the Book of Mormon and earnest prayer, as clear and certain as if someone had spoken to me in a loud voice.  I bear testimony that I know that the Book of Mormon is true and that Joseph Smith was called of God to restore the Church of Jesus Christ.   He remained true to that calling and sealed his testimony by giving his life rather than to deny his testimony.  By the Spirit of the Holy Ghost you or anyone can also have that testimony.

 

I love you!

Elder / Grandpa Browning

                I woke at 4:30AM and realized that I should not have put any sheetrock on the tub area at the Kimberlina Shop home.  I couldn’t go back to sleep so got up and got my own breakfast.  Mae got up just as I was finishing my breakfast.  She made me a lunch and I left for The Home Depot in Delano.  I decided to stop at the plant and leave the washer and dryer set that I had in the back of my pickup there.  I went on to the Home Depot and bought some sheetrock and underlayment and then returned to the Kimberlina Shop home.  Elder Shaw and I worked there all day and finished up about 5PM.  Mae had dinner about ready when I got home.  I then showered and shaved.  We watched another JAG series.  I entered this entry and played a game of solitary and a game of Harts before we went to bed.

 

Thursday, August 26, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                Mae got my breakfast for me.  I left a little early and got to work at 7:30AM.  We got started on the repairs.  At 8:45AM Mae came and got me and we went to the plant where we met with Merrill Dibble in his office.  He just wanted to meet with us to see how we are doing.  He would like to do this on a monthly schedule when he is available.  We just visited for almost an hour.  To show his appreciation for the work we are doing and the example we are he gave us a large bag of almonds and a bag of pistachios.  For our help with the lunching and dinner for the training meetings Carman had Lee give us some chocolate covered almonds, some chocolate covered prunes and a box of See’s candy.  This evening we tried the chocolate almonds and prunes and both were very good.  I worked until 5PM this afternoon and came home very tired.  Today we received two special E-mails, one from Tonie, Leah and Kenneth Garison and one from President Brimhall.  It was so good to hear from Tonie as it has been so long since we have heard anything from or about them.  They are in the United Kingdom.  Hopefully we can again establish contact with them.  President Brimhall let us know that he will be in Bakersfield on September the 18th at the Bakersfield East Stake Center.  When I got home Mae had dinner about ready and while we ate we watched a Netflex movie, “Mrs. Miniver” a movie of the families in England during the war with Germany in 1939.  It was really a moving story.  I filled in this entry and then took a shower before going to bed.  I need to just add, “That I sure love my sweet wife!”

 

Friday, August 27, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                We got up at 5:45AM although I would have preferred to sleep longer.  When I first got to work I took the quilting frames that Mae got from the Ward, to Lee’s shop and there did some repairing and strengthening of them.  Elder Shaw and I worked the day on the Kimberlina Shop home getting quite a lot accomplished.  Sister Shaw stopped at the home and I gave her the quilting frames.  She went to our home where she and Mae sewed a quilt.  I got home quite tired about 5:30PM.  Mae soon had dinner ready and then we watched the show “Rio Bravo” before going to bed.

 

Saturday, August 28, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                We got up at 6:30AM.  I gave Mae a hair cut this morning.  We took showers and I prepared breakfast while Mae put her hair up.  It was 9:30AM before we got away to Fresno.  We arrived at Roy and Linda’s at 11AM and hurried and changed clothes.  We attended the Fresno Temple with Roy and Linda.  It was a very enjoyable session and I again gained some answers to questions concerning the Temple session.  After the Temple we went out to the Home Town Buffet for lunch.  After lunch, we went back to Roy and Linda’s.  Roy and I got into their hot tub although the water wasn’t really very hot today.  It was 6:45PM before we left for home.  As we drove this morning Mae read the Church News and as we returned she read from the book “Seven Years in Hanoi”.  That really gives you an appreciation for the blessings that we have.  After unpacking I caught my journal up to date.  We watched a “Big Valley” episode before going to bed at 10:30PM.

 

 Sunday, August 29, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                I would like to have slept in but we were up early to get to church in time.  As it was we were there long before choir practice and had time to set the nursery room up.  Kim and Lee Brown were the speakers for Sacrament meeting and both did very well on the subject of Tithing.  Nursery went very well today.  We did have two Spanish sisters help us and Elder and Sister Shaw also helped us.  Today we took the children outside for a walk.  We have tried using the rope in the room but just wasn’t sure just how well it would work when we took them outside.  It worked very well.  After the block meeting was over the activity committee held a “linger longer” which was a pot luck lunch.  It went quite well and does give Ward members time to be together in an informal gathering.  After leaving the Church Mae and I stopped by Manuel Macias’s to make a Home Teaching visit but no one was home.  We came home and had some lunch and then took a nap.  We watched a DVD on the Life of President Tomas S. Monson “On the Lord’s Errand” that is very good.  We called Jeff and David, and visited with each for a few minutes.  Georgia called us to let us know that Aunt Beth died yesterday.  Her funeral will be held on Saturday.  We are sure glad that we were able to visit with her while we were in Utah two weeks ago.  It is sure a blessing for her to be able to go.  We received a e-mail from Leah Gerritsen.  We are sure glad that we have been able to reestablish contact with them.

 

Monday, August 30, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                Mae got up with me and got my breakfast and lunch ready for me.  Elder Shaw and I worked on the Kimberlina Shop home for a while.  I was informed that my truck was going to be sold and that I have another brown Ford assigned to me.  My truck has 150,000 miles on it and the Ford has 100,000 miles on it.  It really doesn’t matter to me.  It took a little while to transfer the tools from one to the other.  This afternoon we started work on the home that Stacy and Jolynn Shaw had lived in.  Mae had dinner ready for me by the time I showered and shaved.  She also went Home teaching with me to Brother Manuel Macias.  After visiting with him we went shopping for a few items and K-Mart.  I filled out this entry while Mae prepared my lunch for tomorrow.  We soon went to bed but it was still close to 10PM before we got to bed.

 

Tuesday, August 31, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                I woke at 4AM and couldn’t get back to sleep without dreaming that the alarm was going off and I would wake back up.  Finally it went off and then I didn’t want to get up.  Mae got up with me and got my breakfast and lunch.  Bob called to visit for a few minutes.  He said that the Doctor checked Carroll and said that she does not have Alzheimer’s but does have anxiety and he would like her to see another Doctor but Bob doesn’t think she will.  I worked on the Kimberlina Shop home and then went over to the Kimberlina housing where Elder Shaw and I worked all day on demo-ing it.  At 4PM Lee wanted me to follow him to a lock smith in Bakersfield to get a new lock put on my tool box.  After getting that repaired I stopped at a Sub-Way and got sandwich before going to Mike’s where I checked on what we would need to put an electrical outlet for their dryer.  It was 8PM when I got home.  After taking a bath and shaving Mae and I split the last half of the sub-way sandwich.  I entered this journal entry while Mae prepared my lunch for tomorrow.  I am sure tired tonight.
