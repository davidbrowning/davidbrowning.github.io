Friday, October 01, 2010

                I got up at 4:30AM and started the day.  It was 5:30AM when we got away to San Francisco.  I put the GPS on but Linda didn’t trust it and was afraid that it would take us the wrong and Linda had decided the places that we wanted to see today and we made all of them.  First we went to the Golden Gate Bridge, next to Lombard Street to the Worlds Crookest Street and drove down it.  We went to Pier 39 on Fisherman’s Wharf where we ate lunch.  Mae and I then took a cruise on the bay.  As we sailed away from the pier, there were lots of seals lying on floating docks.   We went under the Golden Gate Bridge and around Alcatraz Prison.  There was a narrative telling all about everything we saw and how it was in the 1800s.  It was very interesting and great to see these sights.  From here we went to Fisherman’s Wharf and walked around.  Linda and Mae were going to walk to Ghirardelli Square and Roy & I were going to pick them up there, but by the time we got there I realized it was way too far for Mae & Linda to walk so I called Mae and told them we would come pick them up.   We parked the car and went inside.   Linda bought some chocolates, she even bought a dark chocolate bar for Mae.  We then went to where we could ride a cable car.  Roy didn’t want to ride the cable car and said he would pick us up at the end.  We had a hard time finding a place to park so we could find out where Roy would pick us up so we could put it in the GPS and he could follow that to where we would be.  We had to wait 45 minutes so Linda stood in line while Mae made arrangements with us.  By the time Mae and I got back to Linda, she was pretty stressed as she was ready to board the cable car.  We had been longer than she thought we would be.  We were worried about Roy finding his way in all that traffic even with the GPS as he wasn’t used to one.  He looked pretty worried when we walked away.  Both Mae and I offered a silent prayer and it worked out just fine.  We had a great time on the cable car and when we called Roy, he was able to meet us.  The last place we wanted to go was to China Town.  It was dark by now and we were hungry so wanted to eat there.  We parked in a parking garage and walked until we found a Chinese restaurant.  The food was very good and so was the atmosphere.  It was fun to see China Town at night with all the colorful lights and decorations and shops.  San Francisco was great to see both day and night.  With the help of our GPS, w e drove to our motel in Oakland.  It’s been a great day, but we are all very tired.

                We were slower getting up this morning and then had to put the drops in my ears.  I really could have just gone back to bed and forgot work today.  Mae had to work at it but she finally got me off to work.  When I got to Shafter I realized that I had forgotten my phones, my badge and my wallet.  As I was already late I went on to work.  Mae had her time at the nursing home and then she brought my items and her lunch and ate lunch with me.  She is so special.  Elder Shaw and I worked all day tapping the house.  I was almost 6 hours on the stills today and when I quit I was tired.  We worked late, not quitting until after 4:30PM and then had to clean up.  I also stopped at Floyd’s for some sheet rock sand paper.  I had to hurry and shower and shave before the missionaries came for dinner.  As always, Mae prepared a very nice dinner.  Elder Day and Elder Bills were forty minutes late coming for dinner but Mae’s food held well so it was nice to relax for a few minutes.  We do enjoy having the Elders for dinner.  Tonight before leaving they sang the Hymn “My Savior’s Love” and then Elder Day led us in prayer.  I helped Mae with the dishes and then I laid on the couch and Mae put my ear drops in my ear.  I fell asleep there and slept for over an hour.  Mae listened to the last of some DVD’s entitled “Seven Miracles That Saved America”.  I made me some padding that should protect my legs while I am wearing my stills.  The dog collars that I bought yesterday worked well but they wore a sore on my legs.  Bob had sent us some information about the candidates that are running for elections and the amendments to the Utah State Constitution.  She read through much of that and we are still in the dark but did fill out an absentee ballot to send in.  It was 10:45PM and I then had Mae rub me with Thera-gesic pain relieving crème, before I went to sleep.  As late as it is, I had Mae sleep in the other bedroom so that I wouldn’t wake her when I got up. 

 

Wednesday, October 27, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                I slept fairly well but woke at 4:45AM.  I got up and read my scriptures and did my exercises before getting me some breakfast.  It is cold this morning and the furnace is coming on often.  I have a little extra time so I worked on my journal for a few minutes before going to work.  I enjoy Elder Shaw but he and I were taught differently as to craftsmanship.  I really have to bit my tong as we work together.   When I left the house, this morning the temperature registered 40 degrees and when I got to Shafter it was 43 degrees but back to 40 degrees at the plant.  Mae called me just after 2PM but I didn’t get the message until 2:30PM.  Mike and Becky are driving up to Logan to give David his Mission Call.  They will have him call us when he opens it.  I came home early and had time to shower and then had Mae put my ear drops in.  I got a short nap before he called at 4:50PM.  He will be going to Africa Abidjan (The Ivory Coast) Mission, but he is excited about it and will be speaking French.  He will enter the mission home on the 23 of February.  Mae had a nice dinner for us.  We watched the first half of “The Cowboys” and then decided that we had better watch the rest tomorrow evening as it is getting late.  Mae had a couple of calls during the show and Mike called us again just to visit about David’s call.  He has very mixed emotions from concern for his safety and the fact that all of a sudden his little boy is changing very quickly into a man.  How fast the time seems to go!

 

Thursday, October 28, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                We slept in as we have an appointment with Merrill at 9AM and I don’t want to start work and then get cleaned up for that appointment.  Elder Shaw and I continued to tape sheet rock.  I was tired when I got home.  Mae had dinner about ready when I got there but we called Tyler to wish him a Happy Birthday before we ate.  I also talked to Shauna and David for a few minutes.  David is upset that I am working so hard at my age, I don’t know, maybe he is right.  After dinner, Mae gave me a haircut and then I showered and shaved.  I added a story to my history and then Mae and I watched the last half of “The Cowboy’s” before going to bed.

 

Friday, October 29, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                This morning was fairly normal but I again forgot my wallet, badge and phone.  Mae went to the school today to help.  Elder Shaw and I continued to tape sheetrock.  I decided that we should quite early and call it a day.  Elder Shaw was happy to go along with that.  I talked to Travis and asked him if I could get another bag of almonds and he had a girl bring me a bag right up.  I got water and also filled my truck with gas before coming home.  I got home shortly after Mae had gotten home.  I made some almond milk, almond butter and ground some almonds for Mae.  She made some almond cookies and prepared things for Roy and Linda as they are coming to stay with us this weekend.  I took a nap and then when Mae finished with the things she was doing, she also took a nap.  Roy and Linda came about 6PM.  Mae soon had a nice dinner ready.  After dinner we just visited a little about a lot, but it was fun.  It was after 11:30PM before we went to bed.

 

Saturday, October 30, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                Before we got to bed Linda had a question for me and we ended up talking until after 1AM.  I think she is crying for help because she doesn’t know how to handle the fact the she has Roy 24/7 and he doesn’t know what to do with his time.  I don’t know how to help or what the answers are.

                We slept in and then went to Hodel’s for a all you can eat breakfast.  After that we went to Leson’s and purchased mainly bread as it is on sale know for $3.90 per loaf and Monday it will be $5.90 per loaf.  Mae bought a few other items.  We then drove up to Lake Isabella just for the ride and to see what is was like.  We had a very enjoyable time and ate a lunch at a park before returning.  We went to Wall-Mark for a shirt for Roy and some Thera-Gesic for me.  Mae and Linda found a few items they wanted.  After leaving Wall-Mark we went to McDonald’s where we bought their fruit smoothies and then came home.  We watched the movie “While You Were Sleeping”.  It was a cute movie but it did have some language in it.  Mae and I then took our showers before going to bed.

 

Sunday, October 31, 2010

Shafter, California

 

                We got up at 6:30AM so that we were ready for church.  We had understood that we would not be singing as the choir today but when we got there we were told differently.  As today is the 5th Sunday there was a special adult meeting after Sunday school ended.  The YW came in and finished the last hour of nursery so that Mae and I could attend the meeting.  It seemed a relief to be away from the children and to mingle with the adults for a little while.  Mae cooked a nice dinner when we came home.  We visited with Roy and Linda this afternoon.  I can’t really understand Roy and Linda’s situation but I am sure glad for what Mae and I have.  They left for home shortly after 6PM.  We watched two episodes of Big Valley this evening.  I tried to call Sandi but didn’t get them home.
