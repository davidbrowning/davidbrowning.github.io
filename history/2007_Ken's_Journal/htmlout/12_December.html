                We got up after 8 AM and soon started getting ready for church.  We attended meetings with Roy and Shellie.  The boys road to church with us.  Mae had asked me it I know where the church house was and I said that I did.  How ever as I started to go to the church Garrett ask me why I was going the direction that I was.  I was then told that they attended a new building since we were here last.  It was a good thing Garrett road with us as I think Quint and Kevis would not have noticed that I was going the wrong direction until we arrived at the wrong chapel.  We arrived just as the meeting was starting.  We enjoyed the spirit that was in the sacrament meeting.  The testimonies and speakers did a very good ob.  I was especially impressed with the Sister that spoke as she told of an experience that happened to her in her senior year at high school.  She was on the golf team and towards the end of the tournament her ball went into a sand trap.  It took her 14 strokes before she got out of the trap.  I can’t imagine still having the courage to finish the game.  Shellie teaches the Gospel Doctrine class and did a very good time.  Veldon and Pattie Seymore also attended Shellie and Roy’s ward.  We enjoyed attending the Gospel Doctrine Class as Shellie is the teacher.  She was well prepared and got good class participation.  We were very proud to be her parents.  After the meetings Shellie cooked a very nice dinner.  Roy’s parents also came over and had dinner with us.  Before they left Roy’s father read us an experience of his 2nd cousin who served as a helicopter pilot in the Vietnam conflict. It was a very interesting experience. 

 

Monday, December 31, 2007

 

                We slept in and after getting up I showered and shaved.  I had a call from Nike that we missed yesterday so I called it back.  I couldn’t reach either Nike or Sandi but Sandi soon called me back to let me know that the Flaming Gorge fishing had to be scheduled or loose it so they scheduled it for August the 7th.  We will try to work a trip out of it for more than Nike and I.   Shellie cooked hot cakes for breakfast.  Roy prepared his hot cakes and as he went to pour syrup over them the bottle separated for the lid and handle.  The bottle dropped onto his plate but syrup splashed all over Roy, Shellie and Mae plus the table chairs and floor.  Everyone stopped all activity and set very quiet but my mind remembered getting mad at the cow for kicking over the bucket of milk.  I jumped up with the bucket and slammed it over the back of the cow.  The milk that had remained in the bucket splashed back into my face.  Mike and David held their laugh for a minute and then roared with laughter.  That is just what I did with this instance and it did break the tension as I told my experience.  After breakfast I helped Roy empty the sewer tanks on the trailer and return it to the storage lot where he keeps it.  This afternoon Mae and I went to the grocery store to get a few items.  We then took the car to a car wash where we washed and vacuumed it out.  We came back home and waxed and polished the car.  It sure looks much better now.  Roy and Shellie went over to some friends for a New Years Eve party.  Mae and I stayed home with the kids.  The kids were playing video games so Mae and I put on a DVD.  It was one that we had watched before but we still enjoyed it.  I am disappointed that Shellie and Roy’s children just want to watch or play the selection of entertainment that they do.  They don’t watch TV because of the pour selection of shows on it but I am not sure that a lot of these selections are not just as bad.  I don’t know and only time will tell.  I came in and worked on my journal rather that watch the show Garrett had put on.  Garrett went to a party with some of his friends so I put the DVD of “The Times and Teachings of Joseph Smith” volume 1 on and we watched it.  Shellie and Roy came home and we played games until just before mid-night.  We then stopped and prepared for a count down and wished the New Year in with noise makers and a toast of non-alcoholic wine.  I finished up this journal entry and started 2008’s journal.
