February 27, 2006

 

My Dear, Dear Family,

 

I just want to send a big Thank You for all of you coming Sunday in honor of my Birthday.  It was special for all of you to make such a effort.  The gifts are really appreciated and I will remember each of you as I use and enjoy them.  I was never gifted with words and often when I start to express my feelings my mouth gets going before my mind does.  I hope you will remember that and forgive me and take what I say for what it is worth, but not offensively.  As we left to come home I thought of the evening and what I had said.  I am fearful that I didn’t express a proper appreciation, like I wanted to.  I also realized that I may have even offended some.  For example, when I opened the flash light I commented about some lights we had bought that didn’t work.  (Only one didn’t work, and perhaps it still will).  I hope I didn’t give the impression that I was disappointed in anyway with the one that was given to me.  These kind are great because you don’t need batteries.  I have used it already and put it in my truck.  The small light I put on my key chain as it will be handy when I put a key into a lock at night.  I have water proofed my fishing hat and am excited that it will shade my face, neck and ears when I’m fishing.  I bought some Velcro and have installed my fishing rod holders and am grateful that I now won’t have to worry about my poles getting broken.  I am wearing my socks already and appreciate them as I needed them.  Mom was saying a while ago that we both needed to buy socks.

 

As Jeff and Gail were getting ready to leave Sunday evening, thoughts and comments were expressed about their expected baby.  I had to just smile as Jeff expressed his concerns and even fears to David.  Then David explained that before his children arrived he had the same concerns.  I thought how interesting that we have to go through personal experiences in order to understand them.  I have always been amused that when Mom explains a recipe, she often does it the same way Grandma Bushnell or Grandma Porter or Grandma Jensen did.  To which she would respond, how do I know what is “Just until it is thick enough or sweet enough or moist enough?” I do hope that if Mom or I get too concerned or too anxious or give unwanted advice or are even over bearing that you would remember that our concerns are motivated by the love we have for each one of you.   Our Children, their companions, our Grand Children and Great Grand Children are our greatest joy, concern and happiness.

 

We love each of you,

 

Dad Browning
