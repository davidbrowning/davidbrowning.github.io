Friday, June 09 thru Saturday, June 17, 2006

                Lake Powell Vacation

 

                I didn’t take my Lap Top Computer with us so I am going to make one entry for this week.

 

                We got up early and packed the pickup and the boat to be ready to leave for Paige Arizona.  Mae and I then went over to Jeff and Gail’s and helped Jeff pack and move their items out of the basement of Gail’s Mother’s home where they have been living, into the garage, so it will be easier for him, his brothers & friend to move their furniture and other items tomorrow.  Gail was still teaching school so wasn’t with us.   Mae did the packing while Jeff and I carried items and furniture upstairs.  Mae is good at packing.  I guess that is because of all the practice I have given her with all the moves we’ve made.  We worked until after noon when Jeff took us over to Subway and bought us lunch.  We ate the sandwiches on the way as we drove.  We got to Paige about 8 PM and checked into Motel 8.  We then went to Wal-Mart and bought two gas cans. 

                Saturday morning we went back to Wal-Mart where we bought me a swimming suit, some shorts and some tee shirts.  We met Roy, Shellie and the kids at Denny’s at 10 AM where we ate breakfast before going to the lake.  We then went to Wahweap Marina to launch our boats and to meet with Butch who Shellie and Roy had made arrangements to rent his house boat for this week.  We transferred all our supplies to the house boat and left my boat to be towed behind the house boat while Roy, Shellie, Mae and I went in Roy’s boat to find a camp sight.  The boys and two friends stayed on the house boat.  We went to Last Chance area and found a place.  Butch did have a little trouble on his way out as the tow rope broke and he had to back track quite a ways to get my boat.  All turned out ok and we got our sight set up.

                We spent Sunday just relaxing and being together.  Shellie wanted us to take our boat and go touring.  I told her that I didn’t use my boat on Sunday and it upset them just a little.  The rest of the week went very well and we had a great time.  I got time in fishing, sometimes by myself, a couple of times Butch went with me and a couple of times Mae went with me.  We caught quite a few fish but I never found a boil of fish like Randy had told me about.  On Thursday morning with Mae with me we did get into a school of stripers and Mae and I caught fish about as fast as we could land them.  I wanted to get a picture of Mae catching her forth fish (these fish were from 21 to 26 inches long and quite heavy) so I laid my pole down to take the pictures when a large fish took my bait and pulled my pole into the lake.  All I saw was the bubbles as the pole sunk.  Besides my fishing everyone had fun with water sports of all kinds.  They would usually wake board, knee board and ski in the mornings so I didn’t get to see them doing it, but Mae went with them the mornings when she wasn’t with me, and she really enjoyed watching them.  She said Roy, Shellie & Garrett are really good and do all kinds of things on the water.  Quint wake boards some too, but Kevis is still a little nervous.  She got lots of pictures.  Roy and Shellie found a large tube that someone had left (probably it blow of a house boat) but when no one came back for it they brought it back and we all enjoyed riding on it.  Quint, Kevis & Quint’s friend, Mason, was riding the tube and Roy was pulling them fast and making the tube have some rough riding when Kevis got hurt.  We think he sprained his wrist.  After that he was too nervous to do any water sports, even ride again on the tube.  Of course, his wrist hurt for several days too.  Roy, Shellie, Mae and I took turns cooking the meals and we ate very well.  The weather was good although we did get some wind a couple of times.  We took my boat out touring on Thursday and went to the Arches Natural Bridge.  We had to walk 1 & ½ miles to get to that and it was quite hot, but it was worth the effort.  I was very pleased with the performance of our boat. 

                We packed most every thing up Friday evening and left early Saturday morning to come home.  This time Mae and I drove our boat back.  We got to the marina before 10 AM and had a couple of hours wait before the house boat got there.  That gave us time to clean our boats up and prepare to come home.  We again went to Denny’s to eat.  Last Saturday when we ate at Denny’s I walked out without picking up my Browning cap.  I figured I had lost that hat but when we came back, I saw the waitress that had served us and asked her if anyone had found my hat.  She remembered and said that she had put it away for me if I returned for it.  I was very surprised and glad for that.  Shellie had suggested that we take a walking tour to see a special sight that was advertised on the front of Arizona Highways.  After eating we drove out to the area but they wanted about $21 a person to take the tour.  We backed out of that.  Mae and I then left for home.  We talked of taking a few days to come home and visiting Aunt Joy, Uncle Van and Aunt Allison but then we remembered that Gale was to have her baby on Monday so we came on home.  We were held up with a traffic problem just after we passed the Highway Intersection 40, so after waiting for over a ½ hour we turned around and went back and took the other route.  Going route 40 allowed us to go by Cedar Breaks so we stopped there for a while and enjoyed see that area again.  It was after mid-night when we got home and we were very tired.  It has been a wonderful vacation and we enjoyed every bit of it.
