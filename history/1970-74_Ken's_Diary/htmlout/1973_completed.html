(I have some confusion in my Diary as to this next experience and what date it occurred on.)

At work I had one of my co-workers ask me if I know anything about Tape Library Systems?  I told him that I had designed and programmed a Tape Library System for Hughs Air West.  He went straight into my supervisor, who met with the Data Processing Manager, Mr. Wamper.  A little later I got a call to come into the Managers office.  There I was asked to explain my experience with the Tape Library system.  In the room were the manager, all three supervisors, which included Ron Shumway and the co-worker.  I told them that I had designed and wrote the Tape Library system now used by Hughes Air West, which Ron confirmed.   I was then assigned to compare my system with one that was used by the Hugh’s Tool Co.  Also I was asked if I could be ready to make a presentation by next Thursday.  I said that I was sure that I could.

Thursday, March 8, 1973

 

                At work the same group met in the conference room as met last week.  The Data Processing manager turned the time over to me and as I started my presentation, the senior supervisor spoke up and said that we didn’t need a new tape library system as our computer already had a system on it that kept track of the tapes.  The Department manager looked at me and said, “Is that right?”  I looked at him and said that I wasn’t aware that our computer could do that.  To which he just said, “It doesn’t look like you are very well prepared.  This meeting will be dismissed until next week and by then I hope you will be better prepared.”  I really felt embarrassed and deflated.  I went back to my desk and started research on our computer system.  Soon I found that it was true that whenever the computer generated a tape for storage the programmer had to give the length of time that the tape was to be stored before the tape could again be reused.  When a tape was inserted into the computer the first thing the computer checked for was that the data on the tape had expired the length of time that the data on the tape was to be kept.  But it did not and could not tell where the physical tape was located.  That was the purpose of a Tape Library System, so that the computer operator could find the physical tape when it was needed.  With-in a couple of hours, I had total evident that the Senior Supervisor was completely wrong.  My goof now was that I told another co-worker what I had found out.

We had planned that after I got home from work that Mom Porter would go to the Temple with Mae and I.  However Uncle Van and Aunt Allison invited Mom to come to their place for the evening.  Mae and I went to the Temple anyway.  Mae went through a session while I worked in the Baptistery.  This evening I was assigned to be the baptizer.  I preformed the baptisms for 602 people this evening.  After returning home Mae and I set up and visited until uncle Van brought Mom back home.

                I will insert an experience that “I believe” took place previous to this date.  I had bid a job to add a patio cover to a home.   The home set on a lot in which the lot tapered almost like a piece of pie.  The patio had already been poured with a rectangular concrete slab attached to the back of the house.  When I went to the city, too get a permit to do the job.  The city denied the permit because the cover would come to close to the property line.  In order to comply with the city code, the cover would have to follow the same angle as the fence line.  It left the owner with the option of not extending the roof to the full end of the house or build the roof to the same angle as the fence with a corner of the slab not being covered.  The 2nd was the option that the home owner chose, except he wanted the corner of the slab to be cut to the same angle also.  That we agreed on and I called a concrete cutting company to cut the slab for me.  After they had made the cut I went out to check the job and the cut was not a straight cut.  I therefore caulked a new line for the cut to follow and called the company back.  I told them that the home owner would not except such a crooked cut and told them they would have to re-cute, following my marked straight line.  When I checked the cut the next day they had done a worse job than before.  I first called the company and told them it was unexcitable.  They said that there was not enough slab left for them to cut it straight.   I called another company, explaining the previous situation and asked if they could re-cut the patio.  They said they were sure they could and came out and cut a very straight cut.  I paid the 2nd company and I built the cover which was accepted by the city and the home owner.  As we left home to go on the trip, I stopped at a service station to fill the car and motorcycle with gas.  The first fellow that I had hired to cut the concrete came over and demanded that I pay him for his work.  I told him that I would not pay him and he would have to get a lawyer if he wanted.   I would see him in court, before I would pay him. Now back to our trip.

Friday, May 25, 1973

 

                At work Ron had me go with him to Tomas mall in Phoenix.  He always went there to get his hair cut at Sandy’s Barber Shop.  He had talked to the owner, Sandy about some remodeling she wanted done.  He had told her that I did work on the side and might be interested in doing the work for her.  She needed a portion and a cabinet built.  She had previously had someone finish her shop in a Chinese Cheery Tree paneling and wanted this remodeling to match.  When I got back to the office, I figured the materials that I would need and made some calls about the materials.  I called several lumber yards for the material and some of them remembered the paneling but didn’t carry it anymore.  I finally reached one lumber yard that had 10 sheets of the paneling left.  I asked him to hold it for me until I could come over and see if it was what I needed and pay him for it.  He said that he would hold it for 1 hour.  I jumped in my car and went right over.  The lumber yard was on the north side of Phoenix and it took me 50 minutes to get there.  Another man was waiting there to buy all ten sheets but the owner told him that he had promised that he would hold it for 1 hour.  It was exactly what I needed and I bought 8 sheets of it at $80 per sheet.  The owner of the yard told me that as far as he know, this was all that was in Arizona and if I needed more, it would have to have the 1st ply of cheery wood manufactured in China, then shipped to Washington State for the other two ply to be attached and then shipped back to China for the finish before sending it back to Arizona.  Time wise would probably take 3 months and the cost would be close to $200 per sheet.  I paid for it and we loaded it into the back of my station wagon. I felt like I was carrying gold.

                This evening Mae and I went to the Lehi Ward Talent dinner and Dance.  It was very nice and we had an enjoyable evening.  It was 11:30PM when we got home and to bed.

 

Saturday, May 26, 1973

 

                This morning I worked at Opie’s for 2 ½ hours.  I came back home and built part of the portion for Sandy’s Barber Shop.  That was the hardest thing I think I ever built.  Each cut of the plywood was as nerve racking as anything I have ever done.  I would measure each cut of plywood paneling, carefully mark it and then reach for the saw.  Before I would turn the saw on, I would freeze up, go back and re-figure the cut and recheck the measurements.  I would do that two or three times before I could make each cut.  I really sweat each cut, thinking at what would happen if I made a mistake.  I would wish that, just for backup, I had bought all ten sheets of paneling. This weekend is our Stake Conference so I had two meetings to attend.  A Stake leadership meeting was held at 4PM and then at 7PM a meeting for all adults of the Stake.  (I have a short note that a film was shown about President Lee, but I did not indicate what it covered.)  After the meetings were over we got together with the Porter’s and the Fraizer’s for ice cream.  Just to keep things interesting, the water pump went out on our Ford Station wagon today.

 

Sunday, May 27, 1973

 

                We attended Stake Conference this morning.  I hadn’t indicated just how we got there, but I believe that Jim and Marion Frazer gave us a ride.  When we got home we had about decided not to go camping this evening as we had planned.  Our plans were that after our meetings today, we would meet the Braithwaite’s and the Shumway’s who had gone camping yesterday.  The determining factor was the station wagon.  Then Jim and Marion said that they would like to go camping with us.  (I am not quite sure how we got both families in their truck.  We didn’t have the seat belt laws back then.)  After attending meetengs we packed up and left for the camp site.  It was 9:30PM when we got there.  We had joined the camp site with the Braithwaite’s, the Shumway’s and the Nelson’s.  They had came out on Saturday morning.

 

Monday, May 28, 1973

 

                We got up early and took the children out fishing.  We didn’t have any luck at that.  We did enjoy riding the motor cycles and just relaxing and visiting.  Braithwaite’s, the Shumway’s and the Nelson’s packed up about 4PM and left for home.  The Fraizer’s and our family stayed for dinner and held Family Home evening before we left for home about 8PM.  It was about 10:30PM when we got home but we did have a good time.

 

Tuesday, May 29, 1973

 

                After work I bought a rebuilt water pump and put the Ford Station Wagon back together.  At 9PM I attended Bishopric meeting, getting home at mid-night.

 

Wednesday, May 30, 1973

 

                After work all my family went to Dr. Opie’s for treatments.  Jim and Marion Fraizer went with Mae and I.  we thought that we might find some peaches but that was a wild goose chase.  Not having found peaches we stopped and got ice cream.  We enjoy being with Jim and Marion.  This evening I figured bills.  That isn’t a very enjoyable thing to do.  We got to bed about 11:30PM.

 

Thursday, May 31, 1973

 

                After getting home from work and eating dinner I then worked on the Partitions for Sandy’s Barber Shop.  Hal & Mollie Ashcraft stopped over just to visit.  Mae and I set with them on the patio and just enjoyed the visit.  This evening Mae and I talked a while before going to bed.  We are quite sure that Mae is pregnant.  That is ok with both of us.

 

Friday, June 1, 1973

 

                This evening we attended a Ward Budget Dinner that went well.  The only problem is that it is the same people that always come and they are also the same families that support the budget fund whether or not we have a Fund Raising activity.  Well, I guess it is a good time to mingle closer with these people.

 

Saturday, June 2, 1973

 

                After breakfast this morning I mowed the lawns.  I then worked on the partition for Sandy’s Barber Shop.  This afternoon all our family went to a Stake pot luck dinner, held at the building sight for the new Stake House.  That was an exciting event but the weather didn’t corporate with the event as the wind blew quite hard.  This evening we watched some TV shows as a family.  Mae and I talked quite late before going to sleep.  She is sure a wonderful wife.

 

Sunday, June 3, 1973

 

                Some of us attended Sunday meetings this morning as Mae isn’t feeling well today.  We think it is just morning sickness.  Fast and Testimony meeting was very Spiritual and appropriate today.   As Executive Secretary I was given the responsibility to take care of the sale of tickets for the upcoming Church Education Week.  I had several people contact me for tickets today.  This afternoon the Bishopric came to visit us, they asked me to speak in Sacrament meeting next week.  Lois and Deloy Porter came over to visit for a little while this evening.  Mae has been sick with morning sickness most of the day.

 

Monday, June 4, 1973

 

                I bought me a new belt sander through the tool room at Air Research, paying $40.00 for it.  The kids and I got into a pillow fight this evening when I got home from work.  Mae is feeling quite a bit better this evening; however Sandy isn’t feeling very well now.  We held Family Home Evening and then put the children to bed.  Mae read for a while but I fell asleep.

 

Tuesday, June 5, 1973

 

                After work I went over to Sandy’s Barber Shop and worked for a couple of hours.  After getting home, Mae and I went over to Larry and Tammy’s.  We ended up taking the Honda motorcycles for a ride along the canal bank.  Mae isn’t feeling the best but she is sure a good sport at doing things.

 

Wednesday, June 6, 1973

 

                I didn’t have much to do at work today, but made up for that after work.  I helped Ron Shumway haul a load of sand to a job site.  I came home and took a shower.  I then went Home Teaching.  While there I sold a ticket to the Education Week.  I then got my ticket information compiled and got Mae to take them to the Stake Executive Secretary’s home and turn them into him.  I then joined the Bishopric at the office where we held Priesthood Home Teaching reports.

 

Thursday, June 7, 1973

 

                I only worked ½ a day today and took off so I could attend Education Week classes.  We really enjoyed the classes we were able to attend but we were very tired when we got home.  Mae is quite the gal!  She has been very sick with being pregnant, but she doesn’t complain.

 

Friday, June 8, 1973

 

                I only worked a half a day today so that I could attend Education Days.  When I got home I found that Mae was been quite sick all day.  We decided that I should go to the Education Days classes with the Frazier’s and then if Mae got feeling better she could come up and join us.  Mae later came up, saying that she was feeling better.  I think that was a lie but she sure tried to keep from showing how she felt.  She is sure a wonderful wife and with her joining us it made it a wonderful day.

 

Saturday, June 9, 1973

 

                I accomplished quite a bit this morning before taking the rest of the day to attend Education Days.  We are sure enjoying these classes and feel that they are so worthwhile.  Mae isn’t feeling very well but is trying not to show it.  I sure do love her.

 

Sunday, June 10, 1973

 

                We attended the morning meetings.  When I got home I started studying for my talk and ended up rewriting it.  As I gave the talk in Sacrament meeting, I felt the Spirit of the Lord with me.  This evening was spent together as a family.

 

Monday, June 11, 1973

 

                Mae and David went up to Dr. Opie’s for a treatment after dinner this evening.  We held Family Home Evening tonight and it went fair.  After we all went for ice cream cones.  I finished working on a cabinet top and got the car loaded with the materials for Sandy’s Barber Shop.

 

Tuesday, June 12, 1973

 

                After work I went over to the Thomas Mall where I worked on Sandy’s Barbershop.  I started on some of the areas that were not in use until they closed the Barber Shop. The sink had been a self hanging sink and I had built the cabinet top so that the sink could easily be set into the cabinet.  I did not have to disassemble the faucet at all form the sink with the exception of disconnecting the water supply lines and the drain. When I reinstalled the supply lines and hooked up the drain pipes, turning on the water, I had water leaks at almost every joint in the faucet and drain.  I was puzzled as it was leaking where I had never even disconnected a joint.  It was already late and the night watchman kept looking in the windows to see what I was doing there.  Finally I gave up and left signs on the sink that they weren’t working and so I would come back when they opened in the morning and try to find the problems.  It was 1:30AM when I got home, tired and discouraged.

 

Wednesday, June 13, 1973

 

                I went into work as normal and then I went over to the Thomas Mall to see if I could get the sink working for Sandy.  To my surprise Sandi was washing a customer’s hair in the sink.  I asked Sandy what she had done to stop the leaks.  She said that when she saw my signs, she just turned on the water supply and couldn’t see a leak either on the drain or on the faucet, so she used it.  I didn’t have any answers.  Sandy was very satisfied with my work.  I sure felt good about that.  When I went back to the office I found out that Ron Shumway had turned in his resignation.  He has been given an offer with the Tanner Brothers Construction Co.  This afternoon we took all the family to Dr. Opie.  I attended Bishopric meeting this evening, not getting home until 9:30PM.  We found out that Mom Porter has been in the hospital.

 

 

Thursday, June 14, 1973

 

                After coming home from work and having dinner, I went to the Temple where I worked in the Baptistery.  I was assigned as the one doing the baptizing this evening.  We performed baptisms for 591 deceased individuals.  I really enjoy working at the Temple weekly but I am also concerned as to the time I am not spending with my family.

 

Friday, June 15, 1973

 

                After getting home from work and having dinner, we took all our family to a movie that our Elder’s Quorum sponsored.  It was held in the recreation hall at the church house  We all enjoyed the evening.  After returning home we got the children put to bed.  I then helped Mae shampoo the carpeting.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife, Mother and Sweetheart.   One of my greatest Blessings!

 

Saturday, June 16, 1973

 

                I received a check in the mail from Thomas Mall for the amount of $167.00 (this must be wrong or I didn’t record a previous check for materials as the plywood alone cost me $640.00) for the work I did for Sandy’s Barbershop.  Mae and I took $150.00 of that money and bought food storage supplies.  That really felt good as our food storage has been about deleted since we left employment with the bank.  This evening Mae and I went out to dinner with Larry and Tammy.  It was good to spend the evening with Larry and Tammy.

 

Sunday, June 17, 1973

 

                I attended Stake Priesthood meeting at 7AM.  When I got home Mae and my children met me with hugs & kisses & a Father’s Day song.  After Sacrament meeting this evening, DeLoy and Lois Porter came over.  We visited, sang around the piano and then played a game of Rook.

 

Monday, June 18, 1973

 

                This evening we got a call from Mae’s parents.  It was good to hear that Mom Porter is feeling much better.  She is home from the hospital.  ( From our memory, this must have been the time when Mom had an appendicitis attack and her appendix burst before they got her to the hospital). We held Family Home Evening after dinner.  We had a nice time together.

 

Tuesday, June 19, 1973

 

                I received a phone call from work informing me that a main transformer had gone out so I should not come into work today.  We turned the alarm off and slept in.  I had a couple of small items to finish at Thomas Mall so took Mae with me.  After doing those items I took Mae out to dinner.  When we got back home I build some shelves in our storage room.  I then took all my family swimming and had a good time.  It has been a great day.

 

Wednesday, June 20, 1973

 

                When I got home from work and we had eaten dinner, we went over to Larry’s where I picked up the Honda 350cc motorcycle that I am going to buy from Larry.  When we got it home I took all the children for a ride on it.  Doug and Joan Caldwell came over for a few minutes this evening just to visit.  Mae and I were up until mid-night getting things ready for Shellie’s Birthday tomorrow.

 

Thursday, June 21, 1973

 

                I rode the motorcycle into work this morning.  I took off work early this afternoon so that we could take all the children to the park for Shellie’s Birthday.  Larry, Tammy and their children all joined us.  After eating I went to the Temple to work in the Baptistery.

 

 Friday, June 22, 1973

 

                I rode my motorcycle into work this morning.  Ron Shumway officially quite Hughes Air Research today.  I am very sorry to see him leave but hope this job with Tanner Construction will work out for him.  After I got home from work and we had dinner, Mae and I went to the Temple.  While I worked in the Baptistery, Mae went through a Session.  After we both finished we went into the dining room and had soup.  We really enjoyed the evening.

                 I don’t know if I have explained the situation at Air Research or not so I will insert it here.  Air Research is one of the many companies owned by the Howard Hughes Corporation.  As far as I have gathered Hughes Air Research developed and manufactured turbo jet engines.  The Data Processing department, where both Ron and I worked, consisted of a Planning Department that coordinated between the Data Processing Department and the users throughout the rest of the company.  We had three sections of programmers, each having a Supervisor with about 5 programmers under them.  Ron had filled the position of supervisor of Group 2.  I work in Group 3, under John Brown as my supervisor, and then there was the Supervisor over Group 1.  Over the three programming Supervisor, was the Data Processing Manager.  I never did understand what authority the Group 1 supervisor had but he seem to be the one that had most of the authority, even over the D.P. Manager.  I never did feel that Ron felt like he had much authority and so when this position with Tanner’s opened up Ron jumped for it.

 

Saturday, June 23, 1973

 

                I didn’t do a lot except putter around the house and yard.  I did repair some drawers for our Ward’s Library.  I guess I got teasing Mae and went too far when sprayed her with the hose.  She got quite upset with me.  This afternoon we went to the Kino swimming pool swimming.  Mae and I watched a good movie on TV before going to bed.

 

Sunday, June 24, 1973

 

                This morning we attended our regular Sunday meetings but this evening we attended the Mesa 19th Ward.  Jeff Bushnell gave his Missionary Fare Well address as he is leaving to accept a mission call.  The meeting was also a Fare Well to all the rest of the family as Uncle Van has accepted a position at Cedar collage.  They will soon be moving to Utah.  Susan came home with us so she could spend the night with Sandy.

 

Monday, June 25, 1973

 

                When I got home from work there was a call asking me to return to work.  I did and I didn’t get back home until 10PM.  Mae took the children to the Temple Visitor’s Center for Family Home Evening.

 

Tuesday, June 26, 1973

 

                After getting home from work, all my family went over to Dr. Opie’s for treatments.  After dinner this evening Mae and I went over to visit with the Premak’s.  We invited them to learn more about the Mormon Church.  They excepted the invitation and we set up an appointment for next Sunday at 7PM.  I attended Bishopric meeting this evening and didn’t get home until late. 

 

Wednesday, June 27, 1973

 

                I left work about noon so that we could go Camping with the Braithwaite’s, the Shumway’s and the Nelson”s.  We were packed and left about 4PM and got to the camp site about 8PM.  We had some problems with our car over heating on the way.  With this Arizona heat and the car being old, that wasn’t too surprising. 

 

Thursday, June 28, 1973

 

                We slept well and got up to a beautiful day.  After breakfast we went riding on the motor cycles.  We returned for lunch and then road some more.  There was a nice creek nearby so most of us went in swimming and used a little soap while there.  The Warner’s came up and joined our group this evening.  David was also quite sick with Flew like Simpsons this evening.  He kept us up quite a bit during the night.

 

Friday, June 29, 1973

 

                This morning after breakfast all the men took the motor cycles out for a ride.  We came upon a herd of elk and so started chasing them.  At one time I looked at the speed-o-meter and I was going over 30 miles per hour.  It was surprising that none of us got hurt or damaged the bikes as we were going through some very wild timber.  The elk soon found a canon and disappeared over the edge.  We also did some riding this afternoon.  We got playing around the creek and Sue Warner ended up being thrown into the water.  Ron Shumway and Joe Nelson drove into town this evening for some supplies.

 

Saturday, June 30, 1973

 

                This morning for breakfast we had sausage, biscuits and gravy.  Most of us went out riding motorcycles and came back to camp for lunch.  We again went out riding after lunch.  We stopped at a creek and got in a water fight, with most everyone getting wet.  We packed up and left camp about 7PM, getting home about 10:30PM.  We were tired but it has been a great time.

 

Sunday, July 1, 1973

 

                We slept in this morning, still being quite tired from the trip.  I took Sandy and Shellie up to Sunday school and then I went into the Bishops office where I took care of some work there.  Mae came up to the Church in time to attend Sacrament meeting with us.  Following the meetings we came home for dinner.  After dinner we put the younger children down for a nap and Mae and I also laid down.  At 7PM the Premaks came over and met the full time missionaries and lessened to the first lesson.

 

Monday, July 2, 1973

 

                After work Mae and I had our picture taken as Mae had a special offer from Olan Mills photography.  When we returned home we held Family Home Evening with our children and had a very nice time.  We then spent the evening playing with the children.  After putting the children to bed Mae and I stayed up and wrote letters to our parents.  We are seriously thinking about moving back to Utah.

 

Tuesday, July 3, 1973

 

                (I made an entry in My Diary, that I called and talked to Carroll and Sandy from work, but didn’t give any more explanation.)  This evening we didn’t do much as is so hot outside.  We did find a couple of good movies on TV to watch.  Sandy was asked to baby sit this evening.  I believe this is the first time she has done that for pay.

 

Wednesday, July 4, 1973

 

                Mae, Mike and I went up to the church, at 5AM, for the Flag Raising ceremony and breakfast.  Sandy hadn’t got home from Baby Sitting until mid-night, so we let her, Shellie and David sleep in.  Mae and I took a nap when we returned.  Mae, Sandy and Shellie went shopping this afternoon and I stayed home with the boys.  This evening we took all the family over to Tempe to see the Fire Works. 

 

Thursday, July 5, 1973

 

                This evening I went to the Temple as normal to work in the Baptistery.  When I went to the East door, where we normally enter, no one answered the door bell, plus the lights were off.  I therefore went around the Temple to the main entrance.  I walked through the hallway to the Baptistery and as I passed the Temple Secretary’s office I gave a “Good Evening” and went on to the Baptistery.  All the lights were off and no one was in the area.  I presumed that I must just be a little early so I started getting out the carpets and towels to be ready.  The Temple Secretary then appeared and asked me what I was doing here?  I just answered with, “What I normally do on Thursday evening.  He then asked if I didn’t get the call that no one was coming this evening for baptisms.  I told him that I hadn’t been informed, so I would put things back then and go home.  A couple of minutes later he reappeared and said that a couple from Philadelphia had just came to the Temple to do some work for deceased family.  They would need to do the Baptisms and Confirmations tonight, before they could take care of the rest of the ordnances tomorrow.  I therefore got thing ready.  I performed the baptizing, with the Temple Secretary and I signing as witness, then I Confirmed membership and gave the Gift of The Holy Ghost, and again the Temple Secretary and I signing as witness.  With that work done, I then cleaned up the area, turned off the lights and went home.  What a wonderful experience that was.  I spent the rest of the evening relaxing with my family.

 

Friday, July 6, 1973

 

                Mae called me at work to let me know that we are overdrawn on our checking-account.  I took off work and came home to see what we can do.  We are also having troubles with our car.  I think the problem is in the ignition switch.  This evening we attended a session at the Temple with Jim and Marion Frazer. 

 

Saturday, July 7, 1973

 

                I went to work today to make up for the day off on the 4th.  After getting home I tried to find the trouble with the car.  For some reason the wiring to the ignition switch is heating up.  Our TV also went out, so I tried to fix that and only made it worse.

 

Sunday, July 8, 1973

 

                I went to the Church Office fairly early and didn’t get home until 1PM.  We attended Sacrament meeting this evening as a family.  This evening after getting the little ones to bed, Sandy played the piano while Mae and I did some reading. 

 

Monday, July 9, 1973

 

                After I returned home from work and after having dinner, we held our Family Home Evening.  We had a very enjoyable time.  After the lesson we played games and had refreshments.

 

Tuesday, July 10, 1973

 

                David is quite sick this morning.  Mae took all the children into Dr. Opie for treatments.  He feels that regular adjustments is a key to keeping good health, I’m not sure about that.  Danial Price called and would like me to look at a remodel job he needs done.  I met him out at the job site in Apache Junctions.  He bought a home that was damaged by a flash flood.  His masonry crew had repaired the walls, but he now needed a couple of rooms re-sheet rocked.  I took on the job.  This evening I asked Lanny Owens to assist me in Administering to David.  We don’t know where else to turn to for help but the Lord.  This evening I mailed out some resume’s to Utah.

 

Wednesday, July 11, 1973

 

                Mae and I were up most of last night with David.  We had a company put a thermostat on our air conditioner today.  They charged us $50 for the installation.  This evening I had planned on going Home Teaching but Bishop Rice call, being concerned that we were late getting our report into the Stake, so I went up to the office.  Everything was ok.  This evening we held our monthly oral evaluation with the Priesthood leaders.

 

Thursday, July 12, 1973

 

                This evening I went to the Temple to work in the Baptistery and Mae went to the Genealogy Library.  She met me when I finished and we went to the cafeteria at the Temple and had a bowl of soup together.  Sandy tended the children while we were gone.  She is becoming a very good baby tender.

 

Friday, July 13, 1973

 

                Work went much better at work today as I had some work to accomplish.  Since coming to work for Air Research I have had very little to do and that has been difficult for me to just waist time.  This evening Mae and I again attended the Temple as it was scheduled as our Ward Temple night.  There wasn’t very many from our Ward that attended but for those that did attend, after the session we met at Phil Allen’s home and had watermelon together.

 

Saturday, July 14, 1973

 

                I worked 10 hours for Danial Price today sheet rocking the house in Apache Junction.  Mike and Shellie attended a Birthday party today.  Mae was busy most of the day cutting material for her some maternity clothes.  I went to bed early, being quite tired this evening.

 

Sunday, July 15, 1973

 

                We attended our church meetings throughout the day. Mae cooked a very nice Sunday dinner for us today.  This evening we, as a family, walked over to Primac’s for a few minutes, just to visit.  I played a game of Rummy with Sandy before she went to bed.

 

Monday, July 16, 1973

 

                After coming home from work and eating dinner I went to up to Dr. Opie’s for a treatment.  Mae took the children over to Larry and Tammy’s for Family Home Evening.  They went swimming.  After my treatment at Dr. Opie’s I went out to Bishop Price’s house in Apache Junction and worked on it until 1 AM.

 

Tuesday, July 17, 1973

 

                I had planned on working for Larry this evening but he wasn’t able to get things lined up for me.  It seemed that almost everything I tried to do this evening didn’t work out.  I have been quite discouraged today.  Mae is sure a wonderful wife and does so much to help me.

 

Wednesday, July 18, 1973

 

                After work this evening I bought an ignition switch for the car.  I replaced the switch when I got home.  That took care of the problem with the car starting.  Mae took David into Dr. Shill.  Dr. Shill is the Bishop of the Lehi 3rd so we know him very well.  Mae told me that when she got to the Dr. office the nurse took Mae and David into the room and had her strip David off.  She then said that when Dr, Shill came in he said Hi to her and the next words were “That child has tonsil and annoy problems.”  He said that they would have to be removed and set an appointment for August 28th.  He also told Mae that David had to be well for at least 2 weeks prior to that date.  Mae explained that we didn’t think David had been well for two week at a time since he had been born.  Dr. Shill said that we should not let visitors in the house or take David out of the house for the two weeks prior to the operation.  I took all my family out to dinner this evening.  We also received my families letter today. 

With our moving to Arizona, that has left Mom and Dad Jensen living in Idaho, Bob’s family and Margaret’s family living in Utah, Roy’s family in California and Irven’s family in Washington.  We therefor started the idea, of writing a (rotating family letter).  When you received the letter, you would remove the last letter that you wrote, read all the rest of the letters, insert a new letter you would write and send the letters on to the next family.  This way we would be able to keep in touch and up to date with all our family.  Today the letter came to us, making the first complete circle.  We sure enjoyed reading all the letters.

 

Thursday, July 19, 1973

 

                Tammy had been asked to be a leader at the Ward’s Girl Camp this year and so had asked Mae if she would tend their children while she was gone.  Of course Mae had said that she would and so Tammy had brought Lori, Holley and Jody over.  Lonny stayed with Larry.  Larry would take Lonny with him to work.  Lonny enjoyed being with his dad.  This evening I worked at the Temple.  Tonight I was asked to be one of the Witnesses to the Baptisiams.  That possession is probably the least enjoyable as you just listen to the wording and see that the proxy is completely submerged at the end of the prayer.  I have trouble keeping awake.  On the way home from the Temple I stopped at the Oppie’s to figure the materials I would need to do a small remodeling job for them. 

 

Friday, July 20, 1973

 

                I took off work an hour early so that I had time to pickup materials for the Opie job.  This evening we took all the children to a Ward movie night.  The movie was “Pollyanna”.  We all enjoyed the movie, but it was after 11pm when we got home.

 

Saturday, July 21, 1973

 

                I worked at Opie’s about 9 hours today.  After dinner this evening I laid down for a nap.  At 9pm Mae went with me and we went over to Air Research, where I checked on one of my projects.  I called and visited with Bob and also Mom while I was there.  On the way back home, I stopped at a Big Boy and bought Mae a treat.  I sure enjoy being with Mae, no matter what we are doing.

 

Sunday, July 22, 1973

 

                We attended meetings this morning.  We had invited the full time missionaries over for dinner with us, but when we got home we found out the meat hadn’t cooked.  I called it an emergency and ran over to Kentucky Fried Chicken and bought a bucket of chicken.  Dinner went well.  This afternoon when it was time to leave for Sacrament meeting, David, Mike and Jody were still asleep.  I stayed home to be with them and let Mae, Sandy and Lori attend Sacrament meeting.  Mae was in charge of the Primary Graduation program this evening so she needed to be there. Mae reported to me after returning home that the program went well.

 

Monday, July 23, 1973

 

                This afternoon we took the family over to Larry and Tammy’s for and went swimming.  We came home for a light dinner and held Family Home Evening.  It was after 9PM when we finished.  Being that we were all tired we soon went to bed.

 

Tuesday, July 24, 1973

 

                After eating dinner this evening, all the family went up to Dr. Opie’s for treatments.  This evening Sandy and the rest of the children put on a 24th of July parade, and did a very good job with it.  I took David with me to Ernest Johnson’s where I replaced a door for them.  Mae and the rest of the children played game while we were gone.

 

Wednesday, July 25, 1973

 

                Mae and I believe Marion Fraizer, went out to a grape farm where they were able to glean grapes from the vineyard.  Mae picked 165 lb of grapes.  After eating dinner, we picked the grapes from the stems.  We worked on them until almost 10PM. We put most of them upon the roof to dry for raisins, the rest Mae put in the freezer.

 

Thursday, July 26, 1973

 

                I worked at the Temple this evening.  We performed the baptisms and confirmations for 741 individuals this evening.  Larry talked to me and would like me to come to work for him.  It would mean quite a large increase in pay.  I am not sure just what I should do.  I am quite unhappy with the work situation that I am now in at Hugh’s Air Craft.  It has turned quite bad since I stood up to one of the supervisors.  I don’t know what power he has over the Data Processing manager, but my usefulness for the company has sure come to a halt.  I guess I teed off the wrong person.

 

Friday, July 27, 1973

 

                I have thought about Larry’s offer most of the day.  I visited with Ron about it, while at work and he didn’t have any suggestions for me.  After dinner this evening I went Home Teaching with Travis Reis.  I stopped and had a talk with Bishop Reis, about Larry’s offer and he couldn’t give me much to think about either.  I am not sure but I don’t think I will do it.

 

Saturday, July 28, 1973

 

                I figured bills this morning and they aren’t looking very good.  I went up to Opie’s to work but I couldn’t get the material that Sister Opie wants, so couldn’t get much done there.  Mae and I talked about our bills, my job at Air Research and the offer that Larry is making us.  We are just not quite sure but maybe that would be for the best.  We went to the Ward 24 of July party.  Sandy got all the children dressed up and they entered the parade contest.  They won 1st place.  We were very proud of them and felt that Sandy deserved all the credit.

 

Sunday, July 29, 1973

 

                We attended our meeting this morning and evening, although Mae isn’t feeling very well.  I was in the office, at the church house, most of the morning.  After dinner, Mike, David, Mae and I took naps.  This evening we went over and visited with Jim, Marrion and Malinda.  I still have an undecided feeling about the job offer with Larry.

 

Monday, July 30, 1973

 

                After I got home from work, Larry came over and helped me move our freezer into the boy’s bedroom.  We then went over to Larry and Tammy’s for dinner and Family Home Evening.  We then went for a swim in their pool.  They have a large plastic pool that is quite large.  Larry and I talked more about me coming to work for him.  I just wish that I could get a good feeling about it.

 

Tuesday, July 31, 1973

 

                This morning we made the decision to accept Larry’s offer to go to work for him.  I turned in my two week notice of quitting Air Reasearch this afternoon before leaving work for the day.  I sure hope we are making the right decision.  I played with the kids on the floor this evening before putting them to bed.

 

Wednesday, August 1, 1973

 

                After getting home from work, I went up to Opie’s and set a couple of windows.  Mae went to Phoenix today to select some proofs from a photographer that took pictures of our children.  She also bought some shoes for the children.  I went to the Bishops office where we held Oral Evaluations with the Priesthood Leaders.  It was 11pm before I got home.  Gratitude

 

Thursday, August 2, 1973

 

                When I got home from work, I found that the power was off all over the area.  Mae told me that from what she had found out, the power will be of for the next 24 hours.  I made arrangements with Larry to borrow a generator.  However when I got back with a generator, the power was back on.  I went to the Temple and worked in the Baptistery, as normal this evening.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

 

Friday, August 3, 1973

 

                After work we took the children over to Larry and Tammy’s for the week end.  Mae and I then drove to Lake Side, Arizona to attend the Joseph Lee Robinson Family Reunion.  We were able to stay the night in a trailer at Ben and Fran Hansen’s.  Mom and Dad Porter were there and they had made the lodging accommodations for us.

 

Saturday, August 4, 1973

 

                When we got up, we joined the Joseph Lee Robinson family reunion.  Joseph Lee Robinson was Mae’s Great Grandfather on her Father’s side.  After a very nice breakfast, they held a Family Meeting.  Elder LaGrand Richards of the Quorum of Twelve Apostles presided over that meeting and was the main speaker.  What a thrill that was to be there.  A few weeks ago, as Mae and I were visiting, we commented that most everyone in the Ward were related one to another except for us.  At this reunion we found that there at the family reunion we know many from our Ward and also from our Stake.  Following the meeting, there was a fun rodeo and then lunch.  Mae and I took a nap after lunch.  This evening we enjoyed a dance and a nice program.

 

Sunday, August 5, 1973

 

                This morning they held a Priesthood meeting and Relief Society meeting, followed with Sunday School.  Elder LaGrand Richards again spoke for most of the time and of course that was thrilling to be there.  We followed that meeting with a testimony meeting.  We then had a nice lunch and after that we broke up and everyone started for home.  Mom and Dad Porter followed us home.  After getting home I helped Mae get things packed for her and the children, then I packed everything into our station wagon, plus a few things in Mom and Dad’s car.

 

Monday, August 6, 1973

 

                This morning Mae, my children, Mom and Dad Porter left for Utah.  Mae drove our station wagon and follow Mom and Dad.  This way they have plenty of room for everyone.  It will also give Mae a car while she is in Utah.  She is going to help with Georgia’s wedding plans and preparations.  It will also give us transportation to return to Utah when I Fly up for the Wedding.  I have an uneasy feeling about this trip but just hope that it is just me.  After getting home from work, I worked at Opie’s for a while.  Larry called me this evening and told me that if I come to work for him, that the insurance he carries with his company, will cover David.  Insurance coverage was one of the concerns we had about going to work for Larry.

 

Tuesday, August 7, 1973

 

                After work I went to Dr. Opies for a treatment.  When I got home I tried to call Mae but the phone just rang busy.  I then went over to the Caldwell’s and cut some doors off for them.  When I got back home I again called Mae and was able to get thru to her that time.  We had a good talk.  It is sure lonely here without my family.

 

Wednesday, August 8, 1973

 

                Work went well although I am just trying to finish up projects that I have been working on.  This evening I went over to the Opie’s and worked on some remodeling for them.  When I got home I tried to work on our TV but I didn’t have any success.  This house is sure lonely.

 

Thursday, August 9, 1973

 

                This evening I went over to Larry and Tammy’s.  Larry, their children and I went in for a swim.  Tammy had to go to a Shower, but soon came back.  She said that she just dropped off a gift and then came back to be with us.  We got playing around and I throw Tammy into the pool.  It was a fun evening.  This afternoon I bought 50 lb of flour for storage.

 

Friday, August 10, 1973

 

                I quit working for Air Research today.  The department employees gave me a $30 gift certificate towards some Red Wing work boots.  I stopped on the way home and bought the boots.  I also bought a saw and some work clothes on the way home.  I took our TV into a shop to get it repaired.  This evening I went over to Larry and Tammy’s where we watched some TV and took a short swim.

 

Saturday, August 11, 1973

 

                I went to work at Opie’s at 5AM, working until 8:15 AM when I came home to get some breakfast.  While home I called Mae.  I sure miss Mae and the children.  I went back to work and worked until 2PM.  When I got home I cleaned up the house and washed some clothes.  I went back over to Larry and Tammy’s were we went for a swim and watched some TV.

 

Sunday, August 12, 1973

 

                I attended morning meeting as normal.  Doug and Joan Caldwell invited me over for dinner and a nice visit with them.  After attending Sacrament meeting, Jim and Marion Frazer invited me to their place for a nice super and a visit with them.  I was there until 9:30 PM before going home to sleep.

 

Monday, August 13, 1973

 

                I started working for Larry Braithwaite this morning.  Work went well and I feel we accomplished quite a bit.  This evening Deloy Porter and I were invited to join the Opie family for dinner and Family Home Evening.  Deloy’s family are also out of town and the Opie’s took pitty on the two of us.  After a nice picnic dinner at Finch Park, we played racket ball.  That was a first for me.  When I got home I had a letter from my sweetheart and family in the mail box.  I sure miss having them home.

 

Tuesday, August 14, 1973

 

                Work went well today although we were held up due to not having enough doors on the job.  On the way home from work, I picked up our TV from the repair shop.  I watched some TV and also wrote a letter to my family.  I sure miss having them being away from home.

 

Wednesday, August 15, 1973

 

                Work went well today.  Larry and Tammy invited me over to their place for dinner this evening.  I am afraid our refrigerator is about out.  It is not keeping food cool anymore.  I don’t know where I will get the money to buy another one.  I sure miss Mae and the children.

 

Thursday, August 16, 1973

 

                Work went well again.  After getting home from work, I cleaned up the house and then mowed the lawn.  I called Dad Jensen to wish him a happy Birthday.  Mom wasn’t there as she had gone to Utah because Margaret is having problems again.  I am sure lonely without my family at home.

 

Friday, August 17, 1973

 

                Work went well today.  After I finished work I went to the lumber yard and picked up materials to build some cabinets for Sister Hasting’s.  (I believe that Sister Hasting’s is the Sister that works at the Temple as the cook in the cafeteria.)  This evening Larry and Tammy; Ron and Audry; and I went out to eat at the Golden Coin, a restraint here in Mesa.  I am sure lonesome without Mae and my children.

 

Saturday, August 18, 1973

 

                I started work at Sister Hasting’s at 5:30AM.  I put a new counter top on the kitchen cabinets and built a Spice cabinet for her.  I didn’t finish the job until 9PM.  I was sure tired when I got home.

 

Sunday, August 19, 1973

 

                I attended meetings this morning.  Jim and Marion invited me over for dinner with them.  They are sure good people and friends.  After Sacrament meeting I drove up to the hospital and visited Carrie Essley.  I believe that the Essley’s were one of my Home Teaching families.  I then went back to the church house and worked in the office quite late but went home before 9PM.  Mae called me at 9PM.  I sure enjoyed talking to her and missssss her lots!

 

Monday, August 20, 1973

 

                After work I stopped and bought me a new watch.  My old one just simply stopped working.  This evening Larry and Tammy invited me to have dinner and Family Home Evening with them.  Afterwards, Larry and I went in for a swim.  I sure miss not having my family with me.  I don’t think we will do this again, at least I hope not.

 

Tuesday, August 21, 1973

 

                After getting home from work, I cleaned up the refrigerator and took it into the shop to see if they could repair it.  I then went over to Larry and Tammy for dinner.  I then helped Larry work on his car and truck.  It was 11:30PM before I got home and to bed.

 

Wednesday, August 22, 1973

 

                After finishing work, I went over to Larry’s and again helped him with his car.  I cleaned up the house when I got home and then packed my clothes so I can for Utah in the morning.  I am sure getting excited to see Mae and the children again.

 

Thursday, August 23, 1973

 

                This morning I caught the 7AM flight to Salt Lake on Hughs Air West.  Mae and my children met me at the airport shortly after 9AM.  From the Airport we drove into Salt Lake where we had an appointment with Dr. Stutsnigger, a family dentist.  He worked on all of us.  From there we drove to Bob and Carroll’s home in Sunset.  The children and I stayed there and visit while Mae went to a hair appointment.  This evening Mae and I went to the Salt Lake Temple to be with Georgia.  Georgia is taking out her own Temple Endowments this evening.  It is sure great to be back with my family again.

 

Friday, August 24, 1973

 

                This morning we again attended the Salt Lake Temple again to witness the marriage of Georgia and Rick.  Georgia Porter and Richard Tew were married and sealed as Husband and Wife for Time and All Eternity with Hartman Rector Jr. of the 1st Council of 70’s officiating.  Elder Rector was Rick’s mission president in Germany.  Elder Rector gave them great counsel prior to performing the ceremony.  It was good to be there and realize that each of us should take that counsel personally.  Following the Temple ceremony, pictures and a wedding breakfast, we returned to Mom and Dad’s home.  I then went over with Bob at his bakery and tried to give him some help.  I was probably more in the way than a help.  This evening there was a very nice reception given in Honor of the marriage of Rick and Georgia.  Mae, Sandy and Shellie were also honored to be invited to stand in the reception line.  What a wonderful day.

 

Saturday, August 25, 1973

 

                We slept in until 8:30AM before getting up.  We then went over to Bob and Carroll’s about 10:30AM.  Mom and Dad Jensen, Margaret and Sandy were already there.  Bob had gone into his Bakery about 4AM as normal, so I went over to see if I could help him.  When I came back, Linda, Jerry, Irven, Rich and Lois had arrived.  We had a good time mainly just visiting along with playing a few games.  I think we had left our children at Mom Porters today.  This evening Mae and I went back to her parents and packed the car for the return trip.  It was 11:30PM before we got to bed.

 

Sunday, August 26, 1973

 

                We had planned on leaving for home by 6:30AM but we slept in and didn’t leave until 7:30AM.  We then couldn’t get gas until almost 9AM.  That was one thing we had left out of the planning as we should have filled the tank yesterday.  We stopped several times during our trip home.  Besides bathroom breaks, fuel stops, I think we also made a couple of recreation stops.  We had a nice and a safe trip home getting there about 11:30PM.  It was just good to bring my Family into our home and put everyone down to sleep in their own beds.

 

Monday, August 27, 1973

 

                Mae got Sandy and Shellie registered into school, Sandy in the 6th grade and Shellie in the 1st grade.  We took David into Dr. Shield to give him an examination.  He instantly recognized that David needed to have his Tonsils removed.  He scheduled the operation for 18th of September.  He said that David had to be completely well for a two weeks prior to the operation.  I told Dr. Shield that David had never been well for two weeks at a time since he was born.  He said that we should not take him out of the house and not let anyone come to visit until after the operation.  One thing that I recognized this evening is that it is sure good to have my family home!  We had a nice Family Home Evening tonight and at the end, I gave Sandy and Shellie a blessing.  Mae also felt that she would like one, so I was honored to give her a Blessing also.  Just before putting the children to bed, David dropped a can of vegetables on his toe.  I had to take him into the hospital to get it lanced.

 

Tuesday, August 28, 1973

 

                After work I went over to the shop and got our refrigerator.  It cost $59.00 to have it repaired.  After dinner I took a nap for a little while before going to a meeting at 9PM.  Mae also had a meeting this evening.

 

Wednesday, August 29, 1973

 

                After coming home from work, Mae and I went grocery shopping.  We then stopped at a drive in and bought sea burgers for dinner.  When we got home I found that the kitchen sink was plugged up, so I went to work on that problem.

 

Thursday, August 30, 1973

 

                I came home from work early but spent the rest of the day trying to unplug the drains in our house.  I did go out Home Teaching and was able to visit all my families for the month.

 

Friday, August 31, 1973

 

                After getting home from work and having dinner, I took Mae out to a show.  We had a very nice time together.  Mae is sure a wonderful person and couldn’t be better to me.

 

Saturday, September 1, 1973

 

                We had a relaxing morning.  I mowed the lawns and Mae came out and trimmed for me.  I built some toys for the children this afternoon.  I also cut Mike and David’s hair.  This evening we watched TV as a family.  It has been a very enjoyable day relaxing and being with my family.

 

Sunday, September 2, 1973

 

                To day I taught Sister Essley’s Sunday school class for her.  This afternoon in Fast and Testimony meeting Mae, Sandy and Shellie each bore their testimonies.  We invited the Fraizer’s over for the evening.  Mae lost the cap on her front tooth. 

 

Monday, September 3, 1973

 

                Although today is Labor Day, I went to work feeling that I couldn’t afford to take a day off.  After returning home we took the children to the park where we ate dinner and enjoyed the evening as a family.  When we returned home I went to bed feeling quite tired.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

 

Tuesday, September 4, 1973

 

                After coming home from work, I went up to Opie’s where I work for a couple of hours.  Mae went into the dentist and had her tooth fixed.  To our surprise it he only charged us $8.00.  We spent the rest of the evening watching TV.

 

Wednesday, September 5, 1973

 

                After returning home from work and after dinner, I went up to the chapel where we held Priesthood Personal interviews.  I didn’t get home until 11:30PM.

 

 Thursday, September 6, 1973

 

                I worked 10 hours today.  Larry gave me a $25.00 a week raise starting next week.  This evening I went to the Mesa Temple where I work in the Baptizer each week.  Tonight I was assigned to be the Baptizer and we performed Baptisms for 384 names.  Mae had hurt her back while I was gone this evening.

 

Friday, September 7, 1973

 

                When I woke this morning, it felt like I had something in my eye.  I had Mae look into my eye but she couldn’t find anything.  It bothered me enough that I stopped at the hospital and had them look for something.  They also could not find anything.  My eye bothered me all day.  This evening we took our family to the movie.  We saw two shows, “One Little Indian and The Lady and The Tramp”.  We enjoyed both features.

 

Saturday, September 8, 1973

 

                I went to work for Larry this morning and worked for 3 hours.  On the way home I stopped at Dr. Opie’s office and got a treatment.  After I got home, Mae and I went looking at some homes for sale.  This evening Mae and I went out to eat with DeLoy and Lois Porter.  We had a very enjoyable evening.

 

Sunday, September 9, 1973

 

                We attended our meetings as normal today.  I taught the Sunday school class for Sister Essley again today.  This evening we went for walk as a family.

 

Monday, September 10, 1973

 

                This evening we had Larry and his family over for dinner.  Mae had prepared a very nice dinner.  We enjoy the friendship we have with Larry, Tammy and their family.  After they left for home, we held a nice Family Home Evening before putting the children to bed.  Mae and I watched a show on TV and then we visited until after 11PM.  I sure love my sweetheart and wife.

 

Tuesday, September 11, 1973

 

                Doug Caldwell came over this afternoon and took Mae and I to see a home in our area that we could trade our home equity onto it.  We were quite impressed with the home.  We then looked at plans on a new home, but we feel it would be out of our range.  This evening I went Home Teaching.  Later Doug returned and we wrote up an offer on the home he had shown us earlier. 

 

Wednesday, September 12, 1973

 

                After I got home from work, Mae and I took a walk over to look at the house we are interested in buying.  We then took our family to join with the Braithwaite’s at Pioneer Park for a Birthday Party for David and Jody.  This evening at 8PM Brother Bailey came over to our home to talk to me about a fellowshipping program.  Doug Caldwell stopped by at 9PM with more information and papers on the house.

 

Thursday, September 13, 1973

 

                On the way home from work I stopped and bought paint for the inside of our home.  Mae has been cleaning out closets as well as the rest of the home.  She then attended Primary.  This evening I again worked in the Baptistery at the Temple.  We put our home up for sale in the news paper today.

 

Friday, September 14, 1973

 

                After getting home from work, I worked on repairing the closet doors in the children’s bedrooms.  We then all relaxed and watched a program on TV.  Jim and Marion stopped by with a pizza and salad to shear with us.  We visited for a while with them.

 

Saturday, September 15, 1973

 

                We got an early start painting the inside of the house.  Sandy helped us all day long and was sure a good worker and she was also very tired when she quite to go to bed.  Mae and I continued and didn’t finish up until 1AM.  It’s been a long day.  I am concerned about Mae being ok.

 

Sunday, September 16, 1973

 

                This morning I went into Executive Leadership meeting and then to Priesthood meeting.  (I am going to add an experience that I had in one of the High Priest Quorum meeting, not sure when it occurred but it did take place while we lived in Lehi.  The Brother that was giving the lesson commented that Damnation was not Eternal.  I questioned the statement asking what he meant.  He then said that we didn’t know how long it would last but it would not continue forever.  I again questioned that “Eternal Damnation” meant just that, forever is Eternal.  We got in quite an argument and it became a little heated, until I finally just shut up and let it go.)  After those meetings ended I came home and we put the house back together.  Larry and Tammy’s then invited our family to come over for dinner.  While the kids were playing outside, Mike fell on their trampoline and cut his head.  We had to take him into the hospital where they took three stitches above his eye.

 

Monday, September 17, 1973

 

                I left work 2 hours early so we could take David into the Hospital.  My entry for this day doesn’t give much detail and we are not quite sure what happened.  To the best of our recollection, this was for a Tonsillectomy.  Dr. Shield would have been the Doctor that operated on David.  However, there are some details that doesn’t work out quite right.  I spent the evening with David.  Mae brought the children up to the hospital to see David about 7:30PM.  David didn’t want to stay the night but when we explained that it was necessary, he accepted it without a fuss.

 

Tuesday, September 18, 1973

 

                I took the day off work so I could be at the hospital with David.  At 8AM they came and got David for the operation.  By 10:30AM they brought him back to the room.  Mae had come to the hospital shortly after he had gone into the operating room.  She stayed the afternoon with him.  I went home to be with the other children and then I came back at 7PM to stay the night.  Mae went home to be with the other children during the night.

 

Wednesday, September 19, 1973

 

                We were able to bring David home from the hospital this morning.  I finished mowing the lawn this evening.  Brother Bailey gave Mae and I the 2nd Finding lesson tonight.

 

Thursday, September 20, 1973

 

                Larry has me working on the West side of Phoenix, so we leave for work at 5AM, getting home about 4:30PM.  It makes for a pretty long day.  This evening I went to the Mesa Temple to work in the Baptistery.  Tonight I was selected to confirmer.  We did the work for 896 individuals. 

 

Friday, September 21, 1973

 

                Mae let Sandy go with the Braithwaite’s camping this afternoon and tomorrow.  When I got home from work, I went over to Jim Fraizer’s and irrigated his field for him.  Jim and his family has taken a trip for a few days and his irrigation turn came up today, so I did the irrigating for him.  Brother Freeman and I also went to pickup paper supplies for a Ward Project.

 

 Saturday, September 22, 1973

 

                I went up to Delbert Opie’s and worked for about 7 hours.  I attended a Stake Leadership meeting this evening.  After returning home, I took Mae out to a show.  We saw “The Sound of Music” and although we have seen it before we still really enjoyed the show.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and I do enjoy being with her!

 

Sunday, September 23, 1973

 

                I attended Ward Executive Meeting this morning.  I then came home so Mae and the older children could attend our Stake Conference.  I stayed with David as Dr. Shield recommended that we not take David to any public meetings for 2 or 3 weeks.  This afternoon Mae stayed home with David while I attended the meetings.  This evening we went for a short drive.  We like to drive east of town on University Drive, through the citrus groves.  When you get into the orchards, the road conforms to the landscape with lots of hills and draws.  At 40 MPH the children gets a thrill like a rollercoaster ride.  It is also such a good smell to have the windows down so the air circulates with the fragrance of the citrus trees. 

 

Monday, September 24, 1973

 

                After I got home from work and we had dinner, we held a nice Family Home Evening.  We then watched a show that Sandy had been assigned to watch by her school teacher.  David is having quite a lot of pain tonight, so Mae was up most of the night with him.

 

Tuesday, September 25, 1973

 

                Mae had dinner ready when I got home this afternoon.  Later she took Sandy and Shellie to the movie “Sound of Music”.  I stayed home with the boys.  David is still not feeling very well.

 

Wednesday, September 26, 1973

 

                Larry had me working on a job in Tempe today, so that wasn’t so far to travel.  After work I went to a Mrs. Woolf’s where in installed some thresholds for her.  When I got home, Mae told me that David has been in quite a bit of pain today.  I also feel under the weather this evening.

 

Thursday, September 27, 1973

 

                David still isn’t feeling very good today.  When I got home I also am not feeling very well so I didn’t go to work in the Temple tonight.  Mollie Ashcroft and her children came over this evening to visit.  Doug Caldwell came over and had an offer for us to trade the equity in our home at 233 W. Hunter for the down payment on the home at 1608 N. Freeman, just two blocks away.  We decided to make the trade.

 

Friday, September 28, 1973

 

                This evening Mae and I attended a Wedding and the Reception for Elaine Morgan’s.  (I am not sure of the last name.  Neither Mae nor I remember the event.)

 

Saturday, September 29, 1973

 

                I worked at Opie’s this morning for 5 hours.  I hung a closet door and was trimming it out.  I was in a very tight area and as I reached for my finish nail gun, I hit the trigger, it fired.  I felt it hit my leg but it didn’t really hurt.  I stood up and pulled the nail from my pant leg and could see it was covered with blood.  I checked and it wasn’t bleeding very much so I just ignored it and continued working.  After going home Mae, our children and I went with Larry’s family to a Circus.  After the Circus, we took the children home.  Then Larry, Tammy, Mae and I went out to eat.  By the time I got home this evening my leg was beginning to get quite sore.  Mae was concerned about infection so I poured some alcohol over the red spot before going to bed.

 

Sunday, September 30, 1973

 

                My leg felt much better this morning so we were glad that it wasn’t worse.  We attended all Sunday meeting as normal.   It was good to have all of attend meeting together for a change.  The Fraizer’s invited us over to visit this evening.  After getting home and putting the children down for the night, Mae and I did some reading before we went to bed.

 

Monday, October 1, 1973

 

                When I got home from work, Mae had our dinner already packed and we took our family to Pioneer Park to eat.  We also held our Family Home evening there.  This Park is such a peaceful area, even thou it is right in the middle of Mesa.  The park sets just north across Main Street in front of the Mesa LDS Temple and is just a perfect setting.  There is lots of play equipment that the children to enjoy, as well as tennis courts and horse shoe pits.  Often we would see an older gentleman practicing there by himself.  He would throw 4 horse shoes from one end, making a ringer with each, and then going to the other end, doing the same act again.  I watched him do this for quite a while, never missing.  I finally asked him if he ever missed when playing someone else.  He said that he seldom ever missed but then those that he played against also seldom missed.  I asked him how when playing that well, how do they determined the winner.  He said it was the one that won the starting coin toss.

 

Tuesday, October 2, 1973

 

                After getting home from work we went over to Jim and Marion Fraizer’s and helped them put in their garden.

 

Wednesday, October 3, 1973

 

                The Appraiser came this morning with the appraisal on the house we are looking at but we still haven’t heard what the appraisal is on our house.  I went up to the church office this evening and finished the Home Teaching report for last month and got it turned in.  We finished the final lesson this evening on Finders. 

 

Thursday, October 4, 1973

Monday, November 05, 1973

                Today at work, we finished up the job we have been doing in Sun City, Arizona.  That has been an interesting experience but it has also been quite a drive each day.  The job consisted of remodeling a Church there in Sun City.  Sun City is a retirement village and so has only elderly people that live there.  All facilities are designed for the Elderly including recreation center, golf course, etc.  Golf carts are a normal thing to have on the streets and they mainly have the right-a-way.  This chapel is the only chapel that I have seen.  With the residency, there are many funerals held there as well as many social activities.  The building was original constructed in somewhat of a U shape, with the chapel running east and west and wings extending to the south.  The wings contain office and class rooms extending southwards about 100 feet.  There was about 80 feet of space between the two wings.  The remodeling was to build a large room in between these two wings, closing it in, making a recreation center out of that space.  When Larry sent me out there, they had just set the trusses between the two wings.  Crews had laid roofing sheathing on the east side trusses and were now carrying sheathing from the east side to the west side.  I reported into the superintended who was busy getting crews lined up for the day.  Seeing his schedule, I started helping the crew carry the sheathing, (sheets of 4’ X 8’ X 1/2” plywood) up the east side to the crew that was installing it to the west side.  As I walked up the roof, carrying the sheathing, I noticed that by the time I got to the ridge of the roof, there was a very notable bounce to the roof.  I had only worked for a short time when the superintended assigned me to take three men with me and go to the west side of the chapel and start framing a canapé that was to extend from the building to the curbing of the street.  He gave me a set of plans for that section of the remodeling.  As we started to lay out the structure, it was definitely easy to realize that the pillars, which had already been poured of concrete, were way outside the measurement of the planned roofing.  I went back to the superintended and told him that there was a problem.  When I pointed out the problem, he reassigned me and the men to start working on a build up beam that was to cross between the center of the two wings and then from the center of that beam to the south wall of the chapel.  From these beams were to hang ten foot byfold doors that would section the large room into three rooms.  As I went over the plans and the engineering of the beams, I told the superintended that the beam would never hold up the weight of the doors.  I explained that the nailing design would pull apart, plus I felt (because of the bounce I felt when carrying sheathing across them) that the trusses were not strong enough to hold that weight.  He assured me that the trusses were engineered for the weight.  When I explained the nailing design, with 16 penny nails driven up vertically would pull out as they were designed to hold horizontally, he considered that thought and asked what he thought we should do.  I suggested that we build it to plan, plus we have steal 4 inch angle iron plates made and bolt then into the framing.  Then instead of just nailing the finish paneling onto the framing, that we also use construction glue to secure the ¾” paneling.  He agreed with my ideas and said that he would get the angle iron plating and for me to go ahead with the framing.  I told him that I was still afraid that the beam would not hold that weight.  We framed the beam as to engineered design and then bolted the angle iron braces into place before gluing and nailing the paneling as to engineered nail pattern.  Completing the interior beams completed the portion of the construction that Larry had been asked to do.  (Several months later, after we had moved back to Utah, I got a call from Larry, asking if I remembered working on the Sun City job.  Of course I did and he asked if I built the interior beams as to Engineered Specification.  When I said no, I could almost hear his heart stop.  I then added that we had built the beam exactly to plans, but then we added the angle iron bracing and the gluing of the paneling.  His heart again started beating.  I then told him, “The beam is coming down isn’t it”.  He said it was. The doors were hung and within a week they were dragging on the floor.  The door company came back and cut the doors off shorter.  Again a second time they were called back to cut the doors off but when the third time they were called, they refused to cut the doors.  A law suit was now taking place and he was asked if the beam had been built to engineered speciations.  I told him that if they would pull a straight string down the roof ridge, or sight down it, they would find that the roof sagged down where the beam was attached underneath.  It was several months later that I again had the opportunity to ask Larry, how the law suit ended up.  He said that when he told them of how I had added steel reinforcement to the design, but that the engineered plans had been followed exactly, he never heard any more.

                I went into work this morning but when I got to the job site, the materials were still locked up and there was no power available.  Maybe I should explain here.  Because of the great amount of theft of building materials in most construction areas, contractors have found it necessary to put up 10 foot chain link fencing with bobbed wire topping and locked gates.  Some also release watch dogs into the area.  This morning the security had not been removed.  Also because of the high demand of new construction the power company no longer installs temporary power supplies and so either the contractor or each crew has to furnish generators for the crew.  As a result that neither power nor materials were available this morning our crew just went home for the day.  Having the day off (without pay) it gave me time to finish the painting and re-hanging the door in our house.  This evening Mae and I went Christmas Shopping.

                I went back to work this morning although I was tired.  This evening I took Sandy over to the Stake Presidents office where she got her Temple Recommend signed.  Mom Porter’s has some friends that are spending the winter here in Sunny Arizona.  When they came down from Utah Mom had sent a box of fruit with them.  Mom had bottled the fruit last fall, for us.  After getting Sandy’s recommend signed, we drove over to their place and picked up the box.  We are sure going to enjoy those perseverates.  This evening I attended the Stake Oral Evaluation whit the Bishopric.  After returning home this evening we had a salesman give us a demonstration on a water softener.  It would be nice and with the hard Arizona water, we could us it, but we can’t afford one now.

Wednesday, November 28, 1973

                Work went ok today.  After getting home and having dinner, Mae and I went over to Dr. Opies for a treatment.  When we returned we rested for a while and then played games with the children.  When bedtime came for the little ones, Mae put them to bed.  Sandy and I played a game of marbles while she did that.  Mae and I then went over to the Allen’s just to visit with them.  When we came back home we watched a show on TV before going to bed.

Thursday, November 29, 1973

                When I got home from work and had some dinner, Sandy and I went to the Temple.  Sandy acted as proxy and was baptized for 10 sisters.  I was assigned as the baptizer this evening and so was able to baptize Sandy.  After finishing this evening and as Sandy and I drove home, we had a good visit as to the importance of Temple work for the Dead.  Mae and I again watched a show on TV before going to bed.

Friday, November 30, 1973

                This evening after dinner I played with the children on the floor until they had me worn out.  With this being the last day of the month, I still needed to visit the Essley’s as their Home Teacher.  I took Mae with me to be my partner tonight to make the visit.  We did have a nice visit with them.  We then picked up our proofs for the Ward Directory.  From there we went over to Larry and Tammy’s and visited for a while.

Saturday, December 01, 1973

                I worked today to help make up some of my time that I lost during our Thanksgiving trip to Utah.  After getting home, I cleaned up and then we took our family shopping for a Christmas tree, but there were no lots that have opened up yet.  To overcome the disappointment we stopped and bought hamburgers for dinner, then went to the park where the children enjoyed playing for a while.  When we got home we let the children stay up and we watched some TV for a while.

Sunday, December 02, 1973

                This morning was a busy morning for me.  Starting with Bishopric meeting, then Priesthood Executive meeting, followed by normal Fast Sunday meetings.  I also started preparing for yearend Tithing Settlement appointments with the Bishop.  After meetings we had invited Jerry Hanselman to our place for dinner.  Mae as normal had a lovely dinner.  Jerry had brought some of his slides that he had taken while in Europe to show us.  Jerry is a great friend that I have enjoyed being with every since we met at Hugh’s Air West.  It has been quite a nice day, but we went to bed early and quite tired.  ** I may have already explained about the appointments that I have been making for Tithing Settlement this year, but I am not sure and so I will enter some explanation here.  Last year in early November, the Lehi Ward, which we lived in, was divided into the Lehi 1st and the Lehi 2nd wards.  Bishop Price was released and Bishop Carl Reese was sustained as Bishop of the 1st Ward and Bishop Shield was sustained as Bishop of the 2nd Ward.  A week or two after the reorganization Bishop Price came into the office asking for Bishop Reese.  I was there and told Bishop Price that Bishop Reese was in his office but he was in an interview and could not be disturbed right now.  Bishop Price explained that due to Ward division being late in the year, Salt Lake Presiding Bishopric office had sent the Tithing Settlement Receipts to his mailing address.  He therefore devided the receipts up according to the new Ward boundaries and he gave me our Wards receipts to give to Bishop Reese.  When Bishop Reese was finished with the interview and came out of his office, he saw the package.  He asked what the package was and I told him that it was his surprise for the year.  I then explained that for a new Bishops, there first Tithing Settlement was usually a surprised.  He replied, “Brother Browning, I have lived in this ward for many years and I know these people, it will not be a surprise to me!”  Following the end of last year tithing settlement and at the first Ward Council meeting Bishop Reese’s opening comments were “Brother Browning told me that tithing settlement would be a surprise to me.  I told him that it wouldn’t be a surprise to me.”  He then added, “Was I wrong and was I surprised!”  Nothing else was said about it until this year when he got this years, Year End Tithing Settlement receipts; he told the Ward Council that last year he had to sign the majority of the Tithing Settlements Receipts as signed by Bishop.  This year he wanted at least one person from each family in the Ward to come into his office and report for that family.  His plan was to announce in each meeting for two weeks that Signup Sheets for Tithing Settlements are posted at the Bishop’s Office.  After the two weeks, and he turned to me, you will call each family that has not signed up and make an appointment for them to come into the office and meet with him as Bishop. 

Monday, December 03, 1973

                When I got home from work, we had dinner and then we had a Family Home Evening lesson.  I had to go up to the church to be in a picture with the clerks of our Ward.  When I got home we had out refreshments and then we all loaded into the station wagon and went up town looking for Christmas trees but the trees still hadn’t arrived.

Tuesday, December 04, 1973

                I was late getting home from work this evening.  Mae had dinner ready and when I finished eating I went up to the church house to help with Home Teaching Oral Evaluations between the Priesthood Leaders and the Bishopric.  I didn’t get home until 10:45pm.  Mae was busy with Primary preparations.

 

Wednesday, December 05, 1973

                When I got home from work, Mae informed me that David had woke with a cough and has been sick all day.  I set down with the kids and watched a show on TV with them before dinner.  I then met with our Ward Librarians, at the church, to demonstrate the new copy machine that our Ward reassembly purchased. 

Thursday, December 06, 1973

                I finished up the job at Fort McDowell, which I have been working on for the past while.  I came home a little early.  Mae and the children were at Primary when I got home.  When they got home Mae told me that David was feeling much better today.  This evening I went to the Temple on my regular assignment.  After returning home I found Mae busy with Primary preparations.  I watched a show on TV while I waited for Mae to finish her preparations so that we could go to bed together.

Friday, December 07, 1973

                Mae has been busy all day preparing for a Primary Christmas Lunching with all the Primary leaders.  When I got home we took all the family out looking for a Christmas tree.  This time we were successful and found a nice tree for $13.50 that we bought.  After getting home we had dinner and while the rest of the family watched a Christmas show on TV, I got the tree into the house and got it decorated.

Saturday, December 08, 1973

                After breakfast I went up to the Opies where I finished the remodeling I have been doing for them.  When I got home at 3pm I took my children Christmas shopping.  At 4:30PM I attended the first session of our Stake Conference, which I believe was for Bishopric’s and Ward Clerks.  If my memory is correct Elder Tom L. Perry was the visiting author, at that time I believe that he was an Assistant to the Twelve.  He talked to us as to what was the most important date we were to put on our calendar as we made our monthly plans.  He opened it up for comments and got several ideas.  After some thoughts were presented he said “The most important date to calendar was a weekly date night with our wife’s.”  “Some time needs to be planned for spending one on one time with our Eternal Partner”.  Marion and Jim Fraizer went with Mae and I to the 7PM session.  Following that session the four of went out to eat.

Sunday, December 09, 1973

                We had Stake Conference this morning so that took the place of today’s meetings.  Mike didn’t feel well this morning so Mae stayed home from conference with him.  After conference was over and we had dinner, I went up to the church house where I worked on Tithing Settlement and also preparing for the sale of tickets for the upcoming “Know Your Religion” classes.  I also called both Rich and Irven about my concerns with the construction work down here.  I just let them know that I was putting out feelers for what I might be facing.  This evening we went over to the Fraizer’s just to visit.

Monday, December 10, 1973

                After getting home from work today, I trimmed the palm tree way back as it isn’t looking very good.  After dinner this evening we held our F.H.E.  That went well and every seemed to have a good time.  We then discussed Christmas plans.  After putting the little ones to bed, we left Sandy in charge while Mae and I walked over to meet Don Studson and his family.  They are a new family that has just moved into our sub-division.

Tuesday, December 11, 1973

                When I got home from work, I finished building the fence around the air-conditioner.  Larry and Tammy came by to visit for a few minutes.  After dinner I went up to the Church House where I was busy in the office until 9:30pm.  (I believe tonight I received instructions from Bishop Reese that I should start making appointment for those that had not sign up for Tithing Settlement.)  When I returned home, Mae was watching a show on TV so I set with her until it finished at 10PM.  We then visited about our concerns over finances and how stable work will be with Larry and the building slump we are in.  It seems that I always get into the building trade just before a big slump takes place.

Wednesday, December 12, 1973

                This evening after returning from work and having dinner all the family got into the station wagon and we went to the Owen Mills Portrait Studio at the Thomas Mall in Phoenix, where we had pictures taken.  Following the pictures, we decided that as we were already at the Mall, we took the time to walk through the mall and see the Christmas Decorations.  This evening I made more appointments for Ward members to meet with Bishop Reese for the year end tithing settlement.  Mae sure is a supportive and wonderful wife. 

Thursday, December 13, 1973

                When I returned home from work, Mae and the children were at Primary.  Mae drove me up to the Temple tonight as she has need of the car.  While there I visited with the supervisor over the Baptistery and explained that Mae and I felt that with my calling as Executive Secretary and with the stress of work, we felt that I should be released from my Temple Calling.  He expressed appreciation for my work but felt that he could realize my needs.  This evening we had an extra large group of proxies, but it went well.  When Mae came to the Temple to get me, we took the time to go to the cafeterias where we had a bowl of soup.  I will certainly miss this experience of each week being in the Temple. 

Friday, December 14, 1973

                At work today there wasn’t enough work for me to put in a full day.  I got home early and built some drawers for Larry.  This evening at 7PM Mae and I attended a Christmas party but on by the Bishopric in honor of the Ward Clerks.  It was a wonderful evening and I felt it a great privilege to be able to work with these Brethren. 

Saturday, December 15, 1973

                This morning Mae, Shellie, Mike, David and I went with Larry and his family, to the mountains above Pine, Arizona to get firewood.  It is also a chance for the children to play in the snow.  Sandy stayed home as she wanted to go to a production “The Nut Cracker Ballay” with Lori Premack.  After returning Mae and the children went to a Ward Christmas party.  This evening Mae and I went to the Know Your Religion classes.  It was a great evening!

Sunday, December 16, 1973

                This morning I attended Ward Executive meeting.  I reported to Bishop Reese that in making the calls for the appointment to Tithing Settlement, I had one person refuse to make an appointment and when I put some pressure telling her that the Bishop wanted to visit with her, she slammed the receiver down hard.  Bishop Reese, after a moment turned to Brother Opie, asking, aren’t you her uncle?  When Brother Opie said Yes, Bishop asked him to contact her to make the appointment.  The rest of the morning was busy as normal.  In Sacrament Brother Robert VanBeekum was sustained as 2nd Councilor in the Bishopric.  This evening we had invited Deloy & Lois Porter over.  I have a note that we attended an open house at Brother and Sister Leon Wright’s this evening.

Monday, December 17, 1973

                This evening we joined with the Braithwaite’s for Family Home Evening at their place.  We had a nice dimmer and following that the children under the direction of Sandy and Lori put on a Christmas Nativity.  It was very well done with all of the children participating in the program.  Following that we watched slid pictures that brought back many great memories.  It was a very enjoyable evening.

Tuesday, December 18, 1973

                On the way to work this morning the Chevrolet broke down.  I didn’t indicate what went out on the Chevrolet or how I got to work or back home! Mae picked up some food storage today.  Following dinner this evening we went to the Whitman Elementary school where we enjoyed a concert.  Sandy was singing in the chorus tonight.  This evening I again work on making Tithing Settlement appointments. 

Wednesday, December 19, 1973

                After work I had to stop and get some parts and some tools so that I can repair the car.  Larry wanted me to stop at his place for a few minutes and he suggested that I buy a van for work.  That way my tools could be inside where they were locked up for security and I would have a heaver vehicle for work.  When I got home Mae ahd I went over to Phoenix to pick out picture proofs.  This evening we took all our family Christmas Caroling especially stopping at selected widows homes. 

Thursday, December 20, 1973

                I took off work early today so that I could look for a van.  I also had some errands that Mae wanted me to do.  At 6pm I found a van that looks pretty good, but I want to think about it.  I had to hurry to get to the church house to help with Tithing Settlement.  The first three appointments did not show up.  When the fourth appointment showed and was taken into the Bishops office, before the Bishop closed the door he gave me instructions to call the first three families and tell them that he, Bishop Reese, had been waiting for them for over an hour and he expected them to make a new appointment with him and keep it.  The first one that I called said, “He is serious, isn’t he!”  I said that he was.  They re-scheduled and I believe the word soon go out as from then on everyone kept their appointments.  Bishop Reese told me that he would sell me his Suborn at Low-Book.  I wish I could but the price would still be out of my range.  It was mid-night when I got home and to bed.

Friday, December 21, 1973

                As closing time came for work, Larry came around and gave me a new $100.00 bill and a nice size ham as a Christmas Bonus.  I stopped at the car lot and got the papers on the van, taking then to the Credit Union and applied for a loan on the van.  They approved the loan and gave me a check which I went back to the car lot and picked up the van.  I then went home and took my family for a ride in the van.  We then went up to the church house where my family had an appointment with Bishop Reese for our yearend report, it went just as normal as any other Tithing Settlement we have attended.  I have often wondered what Bishop Reese said to the others when they came in.  Whether it was the fact that he just visited with every family or if it was something that he said, but this much I know that in the future our wards statics rose dramatically.  After meeting with Bishop Reese, we took the children home where Sandy tended them.  Mae and I went to the store to do some shopping and then we went out to eat.
