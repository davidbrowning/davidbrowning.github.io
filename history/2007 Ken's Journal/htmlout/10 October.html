                I got up at 6 AM but Mae was not in bed.  That means the she didn’t sleep very good.  She came in from the front room where she had slept in the recliner.  I had her go back to bed and sleep longer.  She had already prepared my lunch and had breakfast set out for me.  She is sure special.  I disconnected my trailer and left it in the street in front of the house.  Bob parked his car in front of the trailer so no one could hook on to the trailer while I am gone.  I went over to Aunt Beth’s and got the fans and returned them to the rental company.  Aunt Beth gave me a check for the rental amount.  I didn’t charge her for my time.  I went to Roofer’s Supply in Ogden where I purchased the shingles for Grant’s job.  I then went to Riverdale and picked up my boat from Peterson’s Marine’s.  I had them winterize it and change the oil.  I next stopped at The Home Depot and purchased a 10 foot step ladder so I could get to the ceiling in Grant’s home.  It cost me $200.00 but if I were to fall that would be cheap.  I worked at Grant’s and got the two areas of ceiling repaired.  When I was repairing the ceiling in the small bedroom I bumped the ceiling fan and it fell.  Grant had installed that fan and hadn’t put proper bracing to carry the load.  I added bracing to the ceiling so that it will hold the fan this time.  After putting the first coat of mud on the patches I carried the three bundles of shingles up to the roof so I can start shingling tomorrow.  It was 5 PM when I left the job.  I was very tired when I got home.  Mae prepared dinner while I showered and shaved.  After eating we watched two shows on cable TV.  We didn’t have anyone come Trick-or-Treating this evening but that is normal for where we live.  I filled out some invoices and my journal before we went to bed.  Mae was updating her Birthday Calendar and commented that the month has sure gone by fast.
