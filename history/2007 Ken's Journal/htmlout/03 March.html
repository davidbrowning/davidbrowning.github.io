Saturday, March 17, 2007

 

                We were up early and had breakfast at 7 AM and loaded the bus by 8 AM and off for another day.  We went first to Mount Carmel and oh what a beautiful area.  This is the sight where Elijah challenged the priest of Baal.  Off course we do not know the exact location where the alters were placed but do know that we were in the area with in a hundred yards.  It was so inspiring to look down from that area on the valleys below and know that we were on sacred ground.  On the way up the mountain, and that was quite a ride up there, Brother Barrett explained about a city there where the Druse are active.  They are a break off from the Israelites that believe in reincarnation, they believe that Christ will yet be born of a man.  They do not proselyte but are very secretive about their beliefs and organization.  A person must wait until they are an adult and then he has to come to them to get information about their organization and beliefs and then go through a couple of years of testing before he will be accepted into their faith.  Brother Barrett also pointed out several Kuibutes which is a communal village.  They are no longer like the original were but are a form of socialism.  We next went to Tel Megiddo.  (A Tel is a hill made up of one city being built on top of a previously existing city.)  Also in Israel all of the towns and cities are built on the mountain tops and sides so the valleys can be used for agriculture.  (This makes good since too me.)  At this dig (a dig is an archaeological uncovering of ancient ruins.)  I believe that here they have found possibly 9 civilizations that have existed in this spot.  From the city Megiddo, we overlooked the Valley of Megiddo, where the last great battle Armageddon, will commence at the second coming of Christ.  It seems so incredible to be in this area. 

                Our next stop was at Nazareth at a KFC where we had lunch.  Nazareth is now a very large city that is well over 2,000 families.  In the Old Testament Nazareth was not even mentioned and in the New Testament it was a very insignificant village of maybe 200 families.  I couldn’t help thinking of the song “I Walked Today Where Jesus Walked” and the verse that mentions “those little paths, they have not changed” and couldn’t help thinking “those little path, oh how they have changed”.  We went to an area that has been preserved and rebuilt to show how Nazareth would have looked.  I am not sure if the buildings are from a dig or just reconstructed.  We were taken through by a guide that explained the olive trees, the vineyards and the buildings.  There was an olive press, a synagogue, houses and also sheep and donkeys.  There were people dressed in the clothing styles of New Testament time and they gave us samples of unleavened bread and olives.  I really enjoyed this area and it gave me a good feeling as to how Nazareth would have been at the time of Jesus youth.  We think of Joseph being a carpenter but a carpenter was also a worker of stone.  Stone is the main building material in Israel with wood only being the trim and roofing materials.

                After leaving the village we went to the Church of the Annunciation.  This is a Catholic Church built in the 1st century.  It was built to commemorate the announcement to Mary and later to Joseph, by the Angel Gabriel, that Mary would be the mother of Christ.  Brother Barrett said that he was first shown this sight by Father Raphael, a Catholic Priest.  It is interesting that the chapel has a baptismal font in it.  We next went to the Church of Joseph where it is believed was the place where Mary and Joseph lived.

                We stayed in a very nice hotel in Nazareth.  It is neat to think that we walked, ate slept in the town where Jesus lived but oh how it has changed!

                We had a big day ahead of us so we were up early and was on the bus and left just after 7 AM.  We left Jerusalem where the altitude is 2500 feet above sea level and traveled south to the Dead Sea about 10 miles distance and dropped to 1300 feet below sea level.  When we reached sea level M-- stopped the bus and we got off.  There was a man with a camel there and for a couple of dollars I had Mae ride the camel.  We traveled on down to the valley floor and along the side of the Dead Sea south to Masada.  This is a fortress built by Herod the Great.  The Masada was built on a bluff that is several hundred feet higher than the valley floor.  We took a tram to get to the top and to come down.  Some brave hikers hiked up the winding trail.  Shellie, Quint and Kevis did hike down it.  On the top Herod had build a three-tiered Northern Palace, storerooms, administration building, large water cisterns, Roman bath houses and exercise area.  On the North side still remains the siege ramp built by the Romans.  The Romans were only successful at building this ramp because they used captured Jews as slaves and when they got with in striking distance, the Zealots in the fortress would not kill their own people.  When the Romans did break through all the Zealots inside had committed mass suicide rather than be captured.  It was an amazing sight to see and my first comments were, “It could not have been possible for an enemy to take such a fortress.”

                We next stopped at the Dead Sea and most of us went in for a float.  The water is so salty that there is no sinking in it.  We were warned that to swallow some of that water could be deadly.  I got one drop splashed into my mouth and it was very bitter.  As we came back north we stopped at Ein Gedi.  Here you can see some caves that in 1949 was found some pottery that contained what is now know as the Dead Sea Scrolls.  These were found by a Arab boy who was herding his sheep in the area and trying to locate wandering sheep, threw a rock into a cave to scare the sheep.  Instead of hearing sheep he heard pottery breaking.  Many ancient writings were found including the first five books of the Old Testament.  Sadly scholars offered rewards to those that found parts of these scrolls so the finders would break the scrolls into smaller pieces to receive more rewards. 

                We next went to Jericho.  In Jericho is a dig where has been uncovered possibly the oldest remains in the world.  It is believed that it is part of the original wall of Jericho that fell when Joshua marched around the city and the walls came tumbling down.  The archeologist that found this site said that the site proved that the Bible was false because she dated the wall as being 400 years before Jericho.  Another archeologist said he didn’t want to disagree with her but it didn’t prove anything except that the wall was built before Jericho.  As we came back to the parking lot Mae tripped over the curb and not only scrapped and hurt her knee but through her back out of place.

                From Jerusalem to the Dead Sea Valley the terrain is what I expected the whole area of Israel to look like.  It is very desolate and baron.  This area is the wilderness of Judia where Jesus fasted after his baptism for 40 days.  Also to the East of Jericho is possibly the Mt. of Temptation.

                On the way back to Jerusalem we stopped in Bethany and went to the tomb of Lazarus.  Although a church has been built over the tomb, by going around to the back side of the church is the original entrance to the tomb.  To get to the tomb is a narrow passage but then to get into the tomb you have to crawl in.  It was very interesting to get a different prospective of the tomb. 

                At some shops near by we purchased a sling shot and some jars of Frankincense and Mir and some mustard seeds.

                It has been a wonderful day.  After dinner Mae and I took showers and cuddled in bed and watched a show on TV before going to sleep.  I have worn a blister on my left foot today.

 

Wednesday, March 28, 2007

 

                Each morning as we got on the bus a couple would give a short devotional.  This morning Garrett, Quint and Kevis took their turn and did very well.  I think all the adults were very impressed. 

                We started at a museum that displayed some of the Dead Sea Scrolls.  Also at this area was a model of Old Jerusalem that was very good.  It was large enough to take up about ½ acre.  I tried to take pictures but am sure that they will not do it justice.  If I understood Brother Barrett right this model was built by a man for his son.  Originally its site was on the west side of the present city of Jerusalem.  Later it was disassembled and relocated in this museum.  I would have liked to had more time to study the model.

                We next went to the Yad Vashen (Hill of Remembrance).  This area was built in 1957 as a memorial to the 6 million Jews that were killed by Adolph Hitler and the Nazi Regime during the 2nd World War.  As we viewed the many displays one visual show really made me feel bad to see a news reel where the U.S. refused to allow a ship load of Jewish families trying to find refuge, to enter into our country.  We were at the memorial for over two hours and verily began to see all of it.  What a sad time in the history of man kind.

                We returned to the hotel where I stayed and rested as my feet were hurting too much to walk anymore.  Quint also stayed in the hotel and read a book.  Mae went with Shellie, Roy, Garrett and Kevis.  They went to the Mount of Olives and to the Garden of Gethsemane, back into the Old City and did some more shopping.  I watched three movies while waiting for them to return.  After Mae returned we did some packing, went to dinner and when we got back to the room finished our packing before going to bed.

 

Thursday, March 29, 2007

 

                We woke before 4 AM and were packed and on the bus by 5 AM.  Madgi drove us to the airport at Telivive.  That was the start of a long day on airplanes and in airports.  We had been prepared for a difficult time with inspections at Telivive but that went fairly well.  Free bottles of water were passed out to waiting patrons.  At London I came away with a much different feeling.  There security treated us like animals or criminals.  Why don’t they prosecute the guilty instead of persecuting the innocent?  We had to go through two or three security checks before arriving at our depart area.  As we walked down the ramp way Mae saw some hand lotion that she wanted.  We bought two bottles of it and went on.  Later we found that to be a mistake.  We flew from London to Chicago.  At the Chicago airport we had to go through custom, then back through check-in and through security check.  We were tired and again guards shouted orders.  The one area was roped back and forth for a crowd but there was no one in line a head of us, so one person suggested that we just duck under the ropes instead of walking back and forth.  When he attempted to do that, guards immediately screamed orders to stop.  Then to add to the rest, when we went through the security gates, they stopped Mae and said that she was trying to take something through Security that was illegal.  It was the two bottles of lotion that she had bought inside the secured area in the London airport.  They were liquid and each bottle weighed 5 oz.  Had she bought three bottles of 3 oz. it would have been ok, but because it was 10 oz. in two bottles they gave us the choice of paying to have it held in security until we drove back to get it, pay for postage to have it mailed or throw it in the trash.  The first two options were more expensive than what the produce cost us so I told them to throw it in the trash.  What a humiliating experience this Homeland Security is becoming.  The seating that we got on the next plane was in front of an emergency exit so the seats would not lean back.  We were very uncomfortable and tired when we got to Salt Lake.  When we went to get our baggage we were missing one suit case.  Others of our group were also missing luggage and one couple were short their entire luggage.  We had to fill out a report and were told that they would get our luggage tomorrow and would deliver it to us.  we waited for quite a while before Bob and Carroll came for us.  They had been waiting at the waiting area but our flight never did show up on the arriving schedule.  It was 1 AM when we got home.

 

Friday, March 30, 2007

 

                Today was quite lost.  We tried to sleep in.  Mae made some Angle Food cakes and we went to the Clark’s this afternoon.  We road with Grinn and Kathleen to Provo, where we attended a Philippine Angeles Missionary reunion.  It was good to see President and Sister Brimhall and many of the Elders and Sister Missionary’s that we know while we were there.  Brother and Sister Charles Hyer were also there.  it was a good evening but we were sure tired when we got home.

 

Saturday, March 31, 2007

 

                I woke about 4:30 AM and couldn’t go back to sleep so I got up and started entering my journal notes that I took while we were in Israel.  Mae got up later as she couldn’t sleep either.  I did go back to sleep before 8 AM and slept until Bob had breakfast ready.  He cooked biscuits and gravy for us.  Mark Browning had came over last night and slept here as he had brought his girl friend from collage with him for the weekend.  Bob invited Irven, Sandy and Sarah (Marks girl friend) to also come for breakfast.  We finished breakfast just in time to turn the first session of General Conference on.  We really enjoyed the conference messages.  Jeff came over and returned my truck keys.  He said that he and Trent had a good time last Saturday with the snowmobiles.  He said the machines ran great.  After the first session I downloaded our pictures to the computer files.  I made an error on the second chip and lost all those pictures.  There would have been about 180 pictures on it.  I called Best Buy’s technicians and was told that they were sorry but it was an unrecoverable error.  I was successful at downloading the other two chips and we still have about 1,000 pictures that we took while on our trip.  We watched the second session of conference and after that I took a nap.  Mae woke me in time to get cleaned up and I attended the Priesthood session of conference with Bob, Grant and Mark.  Mae worked on getting many E-mails taken off the received file.  Roger Silvia again wrote asking for more money.  Mae replied and told him we can’t continue to help him.  We went to bed about 9 PM and started watching a movie, but by 10 PM we were so tired that we turned it off and went to sleep.
