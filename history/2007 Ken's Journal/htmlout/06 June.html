                I got up at 6 AM although I had wanted to sleep longer.  As I studied 2 Nephi 32 I felt great sadness and worry over Jeff and David.  I decided to fast for them but as often happen this evening we planned to go to dinner and the Temple with the McPhie’s.  I did fast until then.  I was busy getting things ready to go tomorrow.  I changed the bladder in the Big Mable tub so we can use it Thursday and Friday.  I also got other camping items ready.  I went over to Durk’s and got a couple of items and asked some more questions.  On the way home I washed the car.  I showered and changed and at 5:15 PM we picked up Brother and Sister McPhie.  We ate dinner at the Temple cafeteria before going through a session.  There were 25 from our Ward there in the session this evening.  After the session several of us went over to Farr’s ice cream and after getting our order we set out side at the tables and visited.  It was 10:30 PM when we got home.  I filled out my journal and also down loaded the pictures that were on our camera.  Mae got some more things ready for our trip tomorrow.  Bob called me to tell me that Sheryl had called to let us know that she will be going through the Temple Saturday afternoon.  I am sorry that we will have the conflict with Brad being baptized at that same afternoon.

 

Thursday, June 28, 2007

 

                I woke at 6 AM so we could get ready to go.  We were packed and ready to go by 8:30 but Rick and Georgia weren’t there yet so I ran down to Checker Auto and got a hitch pin.  Rick and Georgia were there when I got back so we hooked the boat on to Rick’s pickup and he pulled it.  We stopped to get gas and then came on up to Camper World at Coleville.  After setting up camp we ate lunch.  We enjoyed visiting and playing games.  We went to the swimming pool and enjoyed that for a couple of hours.  We cooked hamburgers for dinner along with salads and chips.  Rick cut some willows and we cooked some marshmallows before coming into the trailer and playing more games.  It was after midnight before we got to bed but we have sure had a good day.  We were sure glad that we had the furnace as the evening got very cooled.

 

Friday, June 29, 2007

 

                I woke up at 8 AM and went to use the rest room.  The rest were still asleep.  When Georgia got up she went to take a pill.  I was typing a letter on my computer and wasn’t really lessoning to her but she said something about Oscar Meyer being made out of pig gut.  I said “No, they don’t make them with pig gut any more.”  Georgia came back with, “Yes they do, I looked them up on the internet and that is what it said”.  I then said, “No they haven’t made hot dogs with pig gut since I was about 10 years old”.  Georgia then said “What are you talking about hot dogs for; I’m talking about thyroid pills”.  We got quite a laugh over our mis-understanding of Oscar Meyer.  We had fish, fried potatoes and eggs for breakfast.  After breakfast was over Rick and I went up and got the boat.  Mae and Georgia prepared a lunch to take with us and we went to the reservoir.  We blow up the Great Big Mabel float tub and really had a good time.  We did have some problems when we tried to get away from the dock as the wind was blowing us into the dock.  Rick and Georgia got on the tub and I asked Mae to let the tow rope out as I tried to maneuver the boat away from the dock.  With the wind blowing I was busy trying to keep control.  Mae got the rope tangled around her foot and while trying to get it undone it pulled her hand into the fishing pole holders and tour the skin quite deep.  It was good that we had a first aid kit on the boat.  We were able to get the wound cleaned and covered.  Later while Georgia and I were on the tub and Rick was driving the boat, Rick turned a 360 and when we hit our own wake it really jolted both Georgia and I.  after boating for a while we dropped the anchor and ate lunch out in the middle of the lake.  Mae and I actually set on the Big Mabel while we ate.   After eating we took the tub back and deflated it and put it away and then went boating for a while.  We then came back to the camp ground where we stored the boat.  We went swimming in the swimming pool and took showers.  For dinner Rick and I roasted hot dogs over a bond fire while Mae and Georgia got the rest of the food ready.  We ate dinner and cleaned up.  We again played games until after mid-night.  This has really been a good day.

 

Saturday, June 30, 2007

 

                I woke up about 7:30 AM thinking that Rick must have an alarm that was going off.  I don’t know where it was coming from but I was concerned that he wasn’t getting up.  Mae finally spoke to him and he said he didn’t need to leave until almost 9 AM.  He has a Singles Ward activity to be to this morning.  Rick took the boat down with him and the rest of us stayed longer and ate breakfast before finishing packing to return home.  When we took Georgia home Rick was through with the activity and cleaning up.  I hooked the boat on behind the camp trailer.  Georgia had us stay for lunch.  When we crossed the Rail Road tracks the boat trailer came disconnected for the camp trailer.  I am not sure as the lock was still hooked but this is the second time this has happened.  Two men near by helped me hook it back us and we came on home.  We had to hurry in order to get ready to go out to Tooele to attend Brad Carrie’s Baptismal service.  That was very special and most of our children came to support him.  Brad invited every one to come to their home for hamburgers.  I volunteered to cook the hamburgers for them.  It was a very nice time and they had many from their ward come to support them.  Mae and I were the last to leave as we enjoyed visiting with Brad and JaNae so much.  It was late and I had asked Mae earlier if she could drive home as I was so tired.  When we started to leave I realized how dark it was so I told Mae that she should stop and I would drive.  Mae insisted that she was ok and continued to drive.  At the next stop sign I again told her to wait and I would trade her places.  She again insisted that she could do it and went on.  It upset me and I thought to my self that I would just let her.  But at the next stop sign I felt instance that I should drive so I got out of the car.  Again she said that she could drive at least to Tooele.  I just walked around the car and opened the door for her.  I drove from there.  Just as we came into Tooele a large car sped from a parking lot on the left side of the road, in front of an on coming car and right towards our car.  I saw the car coming out the side of my eye and instinctively whirled the wheel to the right.  Our car started to skid and I saw obstacles and curbing coming to my right and had to swerve back to the left.  The car made it between me and the oncoming car and went on with out stopping or even slowing down.  All was ok but I shook for the next mile or so amazed at how close we came to a serious accident.  How greatful we were that I had felt prompted to take over the driving.  We felt that Mae, with her bad night vision, probably could not have maneuvered the car to have avoided an accident.  We came on home but at the Lagoon area the traffic came to almost a complete stop for road construction.  We were another ½ hour getting past that area.  We got home just before mid-night and I had to do some more unpacking from the camping trip and then move the truck and boat off the lawn so the water can come on.  We were tired when we got to bed.
