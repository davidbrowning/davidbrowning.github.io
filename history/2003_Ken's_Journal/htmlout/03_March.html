            Elder and Sister Clark prepared breakfast for us and we ate at 7:30 AM.  They then took us to the Bank where we were able to exchange our cash and traveler checks for Philippine currency.  Had we not had Elder Clark with us, we wouldn't have been able to exchange the traveler checks.  Elder Clark drove the car and I was glad for that.  They then took us to Munoz.  What an experience that was.  Driving in this traffic is crazy.  We also saw so much poverty.  I have never seen anywhere in the U.S. as such total poverty as we saw everywhere here.  Nogales Mexico is the only place that compares but the population here on this island is 80,000,000 and the island is the size of Arizona.  As we drove and saw the congestion and poverty I just became sick to think of what I brought Mae into.  We arrived late for the Zone meeting, in fact they were just concluding with a testimony meeting.  Sister Erma Santiago and her family had prepared wonderful box lunches for us.  After eating and meeting and visiting several of the Elders, Elder & Sister Clark and four Elders, took us to our home.  We were very impressed with the home, but it is still a change.  The home is fairly clean, but we will still have a lot of cleaning to do.  It has an iron rod fenced, with locked gates around it.  A little grass in front, a covered front patio and part of the car port is covered.  Inside there is a large Great room (living, dinning, and kitchen), two bed rooms, a food preparation room and a bath room.  The bath room is all tiled so it is also the shower.  Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita, the Zone Leaders, took us shopping; they were absolutely great in their help with us.  We were until 8 PM shopping.  They left us for the night and walked home.  I offered them a ride, but they said it wasn't very far.  We worked quite late trying to get some what organized.  We finished eating some left over box lunch and went to bed.  We then realized that we had forgotten our pillows when we left the mission home.  New Experiences!

 

 

Friday, March 28, 2003

 

            Mae wasn't feeling the best this morning so I had her stay home while I went with Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita to District Meeting.  It was really inspiring to see these young elders conduct and lead the meetings.  I understand more the comment that President Kimble made years ago when he said "If it wasn't for the missionary system the Church would fail".  These young men are being taught Leadership not only for now, but will be the leaders when they return home.  After meeting I dropped the Elders off at their place, and then came home.  Mae and I had lunch, and then I took a short nap.  Mae is feeling better; I think it is still Jet Lag, and the New Life style.  Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita came by and the four of us went out visiting members so Mae & I could meet them.  We visited the Branch President of the Munoz 2nd Branch.  He was not at home but we visited with his in-laws Brother and Sister Cortez.  We then visited Brother and Sister Noque and Brother and Sister Umipid.  It was a very enjoyable experience.  We ate dinner after getting home, and then stayed up late cleaning and putting things away.

 

Saturday, March 29, 2003

 

            We attended a Branch Teachers Development meeting at 9 AM, But the only ones that showed up was the instructor and two Sisters.  We waited for a while, and then came home.  We came back home, did some planning with the Elders, then after the Elders left we took a nap.  This afternoon Mae and I went shopping in San Jose.  We enjoyed being together and getting around by ourselves.  Mae was tired so I had her lay down, but she first did some straighten up.  We had a water dispenser delivered this afternoon, along with another bottle of water.  It was late again before we got to bed.  We had found some pillow while shopping, so it was good to have something a little more comfortable to sleep on.

 

Sunday, March 30, 2003

 

            We were up before 5 AM but were busy trying to get things done, such as recording our finances and catching up my journal.  Mae felt we should be studying Tagalog and reading our scriptures.  That had her upset.  We were late getting to the Munoz 1st Branches combined Priesthood / Relief Society lesson.  The lesson was given on the importance of holding regular Family Home Evening.  After meeting, we came back home to eat.  I took a 5 minute nap.  We picked up the Elders and attended the Munoz 2nd Branches full Block.  Mae was asked to introduce you selves and bear our testimony during Sacrament meeting.  We were sure made welcome from both Branches'.  Almost everyone shook our hands.  After the block we visited a while.  We next attended Ward Counsel and enjoyed that meeting.  The Elders took us over to meet the District President before we went home.  When we came home the drive way was blocked so it gave us an opportunity to meet our neighbor across the street, his name is Ed and he is Catholic but we got to wait for a few minutes.  Our next door neighbor also came home before we went in, so we got to meet him.  We prepared some dinner, ate, cleaned up and went to bed very tired.

 

 

Monday, March 31, 2003

 

            Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita took us shopping at the palanki to get fresh meats and fruits.  That was quite a new experience.  Marlyn Pilipa came over to interview to be our house maid.  We settled on 750 Paso per week.  She will start tomorrow and will come at least three days per week.  The Elders then went with us to a mall in a town about 300 kilometers South of Munoz.  That was another new experience.  We met many Elders there on P day, also four Sisters.  We looked at buying a new range for 14,000 P, but I didn't feel we should at this time.  Mae & I did some shopping while the Elders went bowling.  On the way home we stopped to get our Philippines drivers license and after all the paper work, drug test, eye test and paying out about 1,000 each they wouldn't issue them to us with out a A.C.R. report.  Elder Kendall called the Elders in the Mission Office and requested that they get the report for us.  We visited a couple of families after getting back to Munoz, then went to a Internet Cafe and tried to send a E-mail to our children, but didn't get it to go through.  We came on home and ate dinner.  I cleaned up the dishes while Mae took a short nap.  She then got up and typed a letter to our family.
