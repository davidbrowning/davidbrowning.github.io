Sunday, November 23, 2003

Monday, November 24, 2003

 

            I woke early but let Mae sleep until 7:30AM.  I got on the computer and tried to catch my journal up to date.  We went through the e-mails that Mae had written last night, and then sent them off.  We ate breakfast, and then got on the instant messaging with David, Jeff, Mike and Scott.  We enjoyed that, although it gets confusing at times.  We were with them for over an hour.  At 1PM Monday our time and 10PM Sunday, Utah’s time, we called Sandi.  We visited with her and Shellie (who has came up to be with her sister) for over an hour.  It was so good to talk to our daughters.  Sandi is doing well with the help of her siblings, family (JaNae, Brad and Chelci), friends, Ward support and Terry’s son’s and their wives.  Sandi was excited at the suggestion of President Brimhall’s, for her to come over here after things settle down there. Tuesday, November 25, 2003

 

            We slept in, so didn’t go walking.  After breakfast we designed a time-scheduling form for the schools that are participating in the Health Fair.  We took it to President Neito.  He was at the city municipal building waiting to visit with the city mayor.  He asked us to go with him.  We were given time with the Mayor and explained the problem that was caused with the change in the schedule for the Health Fair.  We also recommended that we change our location to Central Elementary and he agreed with that decision.  He invited Sister Browning and I to attend the activities that are scheduled from Dec. 4th through Dec. 16th.  He also had his aid to escort us to the principles office at the school and clear the arrangements with the school.  They were very gracious and accommodating with everything being approved.  As we looked at the facilities we think this is a better venue anyway.  We next went out to Guimba where we met with Elder Brown and Elder Sovic and we visited the Doria family, to see how the boys are doing.  Sister Doria showed us some artwork they have done that is very good.  Armando wanted to give the picture to Sister Browning and when she accepted it, then Fernan also wanted her to have his.  That was special and we will treasure those drawings.  We dropped the Elders off at their apartment and then went to the Guimba District Hospital where we visited with Rosemarie (Rose) A. Bautista the head nurse, about a service project of painting the hospital beds.  She is excited and feels the 24th and 30th of December would be good days as most people do not want to be in the hospital during the Holidays.  She also showed us the tire to the only wheelchair that the hospital has and the tire is worn out.  They are running the chair on the bear rim.  We came back to the Elders and picked up the turkeys for Thanksgiving dinner.  They are quite small turkeys.  We came home and got some lunch and then laid down for a nap.  Mae only slept for about 15 minutes and then got up and took e-mails off the computer.  We got e-mails from Bryce and Deanna, Kim and Jeanenne and from Sandra Peterson.  I slept for about an hour.  We went shopping in San Jose for our Thanksgiving dinner and some misc. items.  This evening we went over to a wedding reception for Alvin and Christine Pilien.  It has been a busy day.

Sunday, November 30, 2003

 

We got dinner started this morning, and then left to meet President and Sister Clark at the San Jose Stake Center where they were asked to speak in Stake Conference.  Whenever they come our way, we invite them for dinner.  We had planned to go visit some of our branches in Paniqui today, but President Clark called on Friday to tell us they were coming, so we changed our plans and went to San Jose.  When we walked in, President Apao visited with us and asked Dad and I to sit on the stand along with President and Sister Clark.  We shook hands with many of the people first, we love doing that and seeing their beautiful smiles.  Soon after, a young man pinned a corsage on Dad and a young woman pinned one on me.  Dad said “Oh, Oh, I have a feeling this means we might be speaking also.  Sure enough, we were asked to speak.  They had a huge crowd, but the Lord blessed us as we spoke and we felt his spirit.  President and Sister Clark’s talks were great, as well as the stake presidency and others who spoke.  They had a single adult choir and they looked great in their white blouses or shirts and dark skirts or pants and they each had corsages on also.  They sang beautifully.  After conference Sister Lorna Carurucan Delos Santos visited with me about the problems she and her husband are having.  She had visited with me a few weeks ago at the Munoz 2nd Branch Conference.  Knowing that she was aware that her husband to be was legally married to another woman they falsified the license.  She is getting a cancellation of the marriage, as it was not legal.  I told her that if that was what she wanted she should not try to get the church leaders involved in it but should just try to put her life back in order and not worry about trying to get revenge. 

We enjoyed eating and visiting with these good friends, the Clarks, and then President Brimhall called and asked if President and Sister Clark would leave and go to the Guimba chapel where people were waiting to have their temple recommends signed.  As they left, Sister Clark said “I hope we’ll be friends forever.”  I assured her that we would.  We are looking forward to the 19th and 20th of December as we will accompany them to BelAir where we will attend their branch conference and the four of us will give training to the Priesthood and Auxiliary leaders.  BelAir is by the ocean and they say it is beautiful there.  They were telling us that BelAir has a cute hotel on the beach and a restaurant with wonderful food.  It should be a great weekend. 

After they left, Mae and I took a nap, then went to the church for another Health Fair Meeting.   After it was over, Mae was talking with the District President’s wife, Sister Karen Mercado, and she asked her when we were going to invite their family to our home?  She said other families have told them that they have been invited to our home to watch the “Finding Faith in Christ” video and now the “Joy to the World” video and how Mae had made brownies or other desserts for them.  Mae told her to find out when her husband was available since he works in Manila all week and is so busy with district business and meetings on the weekends, and we would be happy to invite them.  We had to chuckle about that when we got in the car.  She is a darling lady, but one who just says what she is thinking.  We try to invite one family a week over to watch one of these good videos as we know the active families need to watch these uplifting videos as well as less-actives.  We took the zone leaders home with us and we all ate leftovers for supper.  They stayed and visited longer than we had hoped, as we needed to get on the computer and make the corrections for the district report, which needs to go to President Brimhall tonight.  We had received the branch reports from the Elders in each of the branches and needed to get this done.  Mae finished the reports, but I fell asleep, so Mae decided to finish the family letter.
