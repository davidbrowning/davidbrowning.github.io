items during the night.  It was quite a challenging and stressful day, yet it was a great day.  First of all, the Elementary school sent over all of their 4th, 5th and 6th graders, which were 1,200 students.  We had scheduled, with the principal, to send over 270 for each session.  We took part of them through the Exhibits and then we had the “Opening Ceremony” where the mayor and other Thursday, December 11, 2003

 

We woke with the alarm at 4:30 a.m. so we could be at the Central Elementary venue again this morning at 7:00 a.m.  It was hard getting up as we were still so tired from yesterday.  When we got to the school, the Elders were already there.  Junie, the supervisor for the CLSU students came soon after with her student volunteers.  Carolyn Rubio, the Exhibit Group Leader, came soon after and together we got the student volunteers registered and assigned to their station.  I decided we needed to put papers stating the section # so it would make it easier for the volunteers and the participants so I quickly typed them up, cut them and took masking tape to tape them on.  Each volunteer (or tour guide as they are called) explains 5 of the lighted panels to a group of about 20 students.  They have 3 minutes to explain the 5 panels and then the students go to the next section and that tour guide explains their 5 panels and they continue this going to the right until the students have gone to all 60 panels and also to “Muscle Man” and “Smoking Sam”.  They are great-lighted panels about drug abuse, the effects of alcohol and tobacco on the body, health and hygiene, etc.  We have to have 14 tour guides at all times.  It was mainly these college students from CLSU who helped us with the exhibits (which is the lighted panels).  They did a great job.  There were about 5 yesterday afternoon who left their stations to rest and visit and this caused us some anxiety, but we told future tour guides to always stay at their station when we had students going through.  It took 45 minutes for the students to go through all these sections.   Junie was wonderful to work with.  She was so friendly, cooperative and went the extra mile by bringing students and making sure they were doing their job, then going back to the college to recruit more for the afternoon or next day.  We had a student named “Mark” who had such a cute personality.  He seemed to be the “leader” or really popular.  He was out going and kept asking Mae & I questions and teasing us, etc., as he would ask us to speak Tagalog.  The others would laugh or clap depending on what we said. 

At 8:00 a.m., National High School brought over their students.  They brought 270, but the ROTC from the college came also and there was about 200 or 250 of them.  They were all in their uniforms (hot uniforms, Mae & I couldn’t see how they can stand to wear them as it is too hot over here for those kind of uniforms).  They had been standing at attention in the hot sun waiting to come in while the students came in first.  They came to give blood for the “Blood Letting”.  We didn’t have room for them, but we couldn’t make them stand in the hot sun so we brought in more chairs and crowded the high school students together.  We knew there was going to be a “blood letting” and that ROTC students were coming, but we had no idea that all of them would be coming at once.  The Red Cross was doing the “blood letting”, but they were not supposed to be there until 9:00 a.m. (they didn’t arrive until after 9:30 a.m.) but the ROTC students were there by 8:00 a.m.  We were also having a “Medical Mission” in connection with the Health Fair and this took place this morning from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. (Although Sister Umipig’s 2 daughters who are doctors and 1 daughter who is a nurse stayed until about 4:00p.m. to examine people who came.)  Sister Umipig pointed out to Mae a mother with her two sick children and said “How could we turn them away when her children are so sick” ?  She pointed to an elderly man who was sick and in pain and said “How could we turn him away?”  She pointed to others and said “How could we turn them away”?  Sister Umipig and her daughters are so Christ like.  Sister Rubio is another person who is Christ like.  She saw how many people were waiting to go to the medical mission and saw that there were only 3 doctors so she called her friends in Cabanatuan who are doctors and asked if they would come to which they responded, they would.  She also called a friend who is a dentist and she also came.  We had one other dentist.  There were many people who came to the medical mission.  In the afternoon Sister Rubio noticed an old lady who seemed sick so she went to her and asked if she was ok.  The lady asked Carolyn if she would get her a drink of water as she was about to pass out.  Carolyn quickly did so and the lady told her how she and her grandson had gotten up really early as they had to walk here and it is a long way as they are in another providence.  The lady is 81 years old.  They went to the Science City Gym (where we were first scheduled to have it) and waiting two hours until her grandson, who was about 12, found out that it was at the Central Elementary Gym.  They walked over here.  By that time she was about sick as they had had no food or water since the day before.  She asked at the information table where to go and they sat down in that area.  Many were in front of her, but when Carolyn found all this out, she quickly went to buy food for this grandma and her grandson.  She got them water and chocolate milk also.  She then talked to Sister Umipig and Jean Gasmenia about getting her in to see the doctor right away.  They did and also gave her free medicine.  This sweet little grandma was so grateful.  Carolyn gave her money for trike fare for her and her grandson and flagged down a trike for them.  Carolyn was telling Mae about her and pointed her out, as she had not left at this time so Mae went over and met her and expressed her love to her.  Mae was very grateful that Carolyn was observant and also that she was in tune with the spirit to see this little lady as none of the other Activities volunteers had noticed her, and there were several over in that area.  Carolyn’s responsibility as being the Exhibit Group Leader was on the other side of the venue.

We had several other challenges during the day such as the schools not following their schedules, so they were either early or late bringing the students over and sometimes they would have the correct amount of students, but many times they brought less or else they brought way too many.  The Elders quickly called me to take them to their apartment as it was being robbed.  Several crowded into the car with me and the Elders as two women came in saying the Elder’s gate was open and they saw people in their house.  The police was called and appeared at the house shortly after we did.  It turned out that it was Bishop Mensia who works for the mission.  He was bringing bunk beds for the Elders as at the end of the month they will have two more Elders living with them.  The President is giving us 2 more since we are a small zone and we need two elders for each Branch.  The Bishop would have been in bad trouble had we not arrived when we did.  Others had known to be shot on the spot by the police.  The Bishop was nervous and really sorry that he had not called or txt the Elders to let them know he was coming.  He had assumed that Elder Karin had let them know.  Another challenge was             First, Balar is the most remote part of our mission.  It is the only area, in our mission, that is in contact with the ocean.   There is a high mountain range that separates that area, and probably there are people there who have never been outside of that valley.  You go through some really steep terrain on both sides of the mountain, on a very narrow road that often has mud slides during the rain storms, so they are always having to clean off roads and rebuild the areas.  We bought a map that showed us a little different way to get there than what President and Sister Clark had taken when they have gone there before with President and Sister Brimhall, however, maps in the Philippines are not always accurate.   President and Sister Clark picked us up and we went in the mission van, because the roads are too rough for light passenger cars.  We went from Munoz to San Jose and from San Jose to Rizal, an area which we have traveled several times before.  From Rizal, we had planned on going to Bongabon, but ended up going almost the opposite direction to Pantabangan.  In Rizal we had seen a highway sign, which is rare to see, that pointed the direction to Balar.  Then when we questioned the road, we stopped and asked a man for directions, he pointed to the way we were going.   We asked directions in the next village, when we didn’t see any signs showing the name of the village, the way to Balar.  They pointed out the direction so we continued on.  The thing that had us concerned was that we were never getting to Bongabon.  The road was not too bad and we just kept on going hoping that we were going in the right direction.  Had we had positive knowledge that we were going in the right direction, we would have felt ok, but not knowing for sure, kept us in an uneasy feeling. 

We finally stopped at a nice place in the road with shade trees, etc., to eat our lunch.  Sister Clark had made hoagie sandwiches, potato salad, etc., so we were enjoying lunch, when we noticed that across the road were some stairs leading up the mountain.  We thought that we were in a desolate area, as far as people are concerned, but we were in the jungle.  We soon began to recognize little bamboo huts quite frequently among the foliage.  That was ok until Dad recognized that one of the fellows, coming down the steps towards where Sister Clark and I had gone, so Dad could take a picture of us partway up the steps, was carrying a pretty large rifle.  Then came a second man and he too had a pretty large rifle.  Sister Clark and I soon came back down to where Dad & President Clark were.  We were a little nervous, as you can imagine, but the men waved and went on down the road.  We were relieved and finished our lunch then headed back on our journey.

It is sure a different Christmas.  We didn’t get up until 6:30 AM.  Mae felt quite a lot better, so we called the Elders and invited them for breakfast.  We planned on eating at 8:30 AM, but Elder Smith, got a phone call from his parents, just as they were leaving their apartment, so they were late getting here.  The Elders brought us a very nice fruit basket for a Christmas present.  We had a very enjoyable morning.  After breakfast we had a short program, we sang some Christmas songs and read the Christmas story from Luke 2 while Mae used the flannel board.  Mae then read a story that had been sent to us, “The Christmas Carol”, (yes that is spelt right).  We enjoyed visiting for a little while, and then sent them off to work.  Mae and I took a nap, at least I did, but Mae only rested for a little while.  I got on the computer for a while.  I wrote e-mails to our children and did some with out pictures.  Mae started Christmas Dinner, to which we had invited Roger & Heidi Silvia and their children Karen & Darin.  They came at 5 PM.  They also brought Heidi’s younger sister, Irene.  We had a very nice time with a very nice dinner.  We again held a short program.  Mae read her Christmas Carol story and we gave the flannel board reading of the Saviors birth.  We then watched the DVD of “Joy to the World”.  Heidi’s sister is not a member of the Church, but she seemed to enjoy the program.  We took some pictures and Roger helped me figure out some problems with our camera and the transfer program from the camera to the computer.  It was almost 9 PM when they got ready to leave.  I realized that they didn’t have a way to get home.  As it is Christmas Day, most of the transportation stopped at 5 PM.  I guess they had just planned on walking home.  I was not going to let them do that so I took them home in the car.  Roger commented that this was his children’s first ride in an automobile.  He also asked me how old I was when I learned to drive.  He was quite surprised when I told him that I was 10 or 11 years old.  He said he still didn’t know how to drive a car, only a bike.  Mae and I finished sending e-mails to our children.  This time we sent each one a personal e-mail.  We also sent an e-mail to both of our families.  We got an e-mail from both Jeff and Mike.  They were just starting their Christmas Day.  It’s been a good Day, but a different Christmas.

 

Friday, December 26, 2003

 

                We didn’t get up quite as early as we should have, so we had to hurry to be ready to pick up the Munoz Elders and go to Guimba where the District Development meet is held.  We enjoy meeting with the Elders and watching them in their leadership roles.  After meeting the Elders invited us to have lunch with them.  We got home at 1PM and Marlyn had lunch ready for us.  We had text her, but she hadn’t brought her phone with her, so missed out message.  Mae took a nap, but I got on the computer and figured on the budget.  We went over to San Jose to the bank to deposit our check and draw out some money.  We looked for Mae a dress for her Birthday, but couldn’t find anything.  We did some shopping at C. V. C. and then came home.  I warmed up some food for super.  I took a nap, then got up and completed our budget.  At 10 PM I went over to the Elders and borrowed their telephone, so we could have two phones to talk on when our children calls us at 11 PM.  Our family called at 11 PM our time and 8 AM Mountain Standard Time.  We talked for a few minutes when the line disconnected.  Mike called back on his cell phone to let us know that the weather had shut off all the power in the area.  They will try to call back when the power comes back on.  Sandi wasn’t going to be able to make it for the call because of the weather.  They are getting heavy snow at the time they called.  We were very disappointed to have the call cut short.  I guess we will go to bed and hope they call a little later.

 

 

 

Saturday, December 27, 2003

 

                It was 12:30 AM when we got to bed, hoping that our family could get back through the phone, but it didn’t happen.  We got up a few minutes after 6 AM and got ready to leave before 8 AM.  We went to Paniqui 3rd Branch and gave President ------- some training.  We really enjoyed his humble spirit and the receptive attitude he showed.  We were over two hours in the training.  As we gathered things up he took all items and carried them to our car for us.  As we started towards the parking lot gate, he ran to open it for us.  What a humble servant of the Lord.  We went to Camiline where we met Elder and Sister Maharry at Cindy’s for lunch.  We went to the church house where we participated with leadership training in connection with District Conference.  Although the training went well and we really enjoyed the evening meeting with President Brimhall’s talk, we were tired when we left for home.  It was 8:45 PM when we got home, and I especially was exhausted.  I started my journal entry, but gave up and went to bed.  We got a phone call from Elder Ragasa about 10:15 PM and woke us up.  He wants to schedule a H.T. / V.T. Fireside, but we got cut off before Mae found out when.  Mae had trouble getting back to sleep, and then she said the phone rang again about 11 PM but only one.

 

Sunday, December 28, 2003

 

                We got up about 6AM but that left us short on time as we wanted to attend the meetings in the Moncada 1st and 2nd Branches.  We got to the Moncada 1st at 8:50 AM, but meetings started at 8:30 AM.  We enjoyed the rest of the meetings.  I visited with the Elders about some problems and questions they had concerning Branch 2, during the Sunday School time, and then went in with the Young Men during Priesthood.  Mae attended Primary and Y.W.  Elder Ellison invited us for dinner again with them.  We sure enjoyed that.  They gave me a bag of California Pecans that I sure enjoy.  We went back and attended the Moncada 2nd Branch, this time getting there in time for the opening of the meetings.  Again we enjoyed our time visiting the various groups.  Traffic was slow getting from Moncada to the Guimba turn.  It was almost 6 PM when we got home.  We just had leftovers for dinner.  I checked the e-mail and we got a nice letter from Becky.  We are tired tonight so went to bed shortly after 9 PM.  David called about 11 PM.  At first we couldn’t figure out what it was.  We sure enjoyed visiting with him.  Both Tyler and Emily talked with us for a few minutes.  They will be going home this morning.  It’s morning there.  David said they are thinking of moving to Utah with his company.  It is a reduction in pay plus an increase in cost of living.  Mike wants him to move down by him.  That would be good in some ways, but again more expense.  It seems incredible, but he will loss more in this move that Mae and I ever made in a year.

 

Monday, December 29, 2003

 

                We slept in until 6 AM, but still got up and went for a long walk at the collage.  School is out for the Holidays so it was quite peaceful over there.  After getting home, showering and eating breakfast it was time to get on the inter-net to visit with our children by instant messaging.  We sure enjoyed that.  Mae then got on the inter-net and e-mailed some letters.  I took a nap, until I work with acid reflex.  After lunch we went to San Jose and got a few groceries and got some glass cut to fit the end tables.  Mae was sure happy with that.  She had quite a headache so I rubbed her back and neck and then had her relax and sleep a while.  I later woke her and we went to bed.

 

Tuesday, December 30, 2003

 

                We got up at 5:30 AM so we had more time to walk.  We went a new direction just to see some new areas.  It is cool this time of the year and very peaceful with school being out.  We really enjoy walking together.  We spent most of the day on the computer planning on training for the new Branch Training program we are putting together for the couple missionaries.  It is surprising how fast time goes by.

 

Wednesday, December 31, 2003

 

                We got up and went walking.  Mae made chocolate chip cookies this morning so she would have some for today when Pres. and Sis. Clark come and most of them for the Elders tomorrow when we will be finishing the service project for the Guimba District Hospital – painting beds, etc.  President and Sister Clark came shortly after noon.  We played games, watch church videos, visited and ate.  We had a good time.  We always enjoy being with these good friends.  We went to bed about 10:30.  We didn’t know if we would be able to sleep or not because everyone sets off lots of fireworks on New Year’s Eve and they had been doing it all day, plus families get together and celebrate and sing on the karaoke machine.  We slept pretty well, but I still heart a man behind our home singing and he sang like he was sick.  We’re sure he was drunk – most are.  The fireworks were loud.
