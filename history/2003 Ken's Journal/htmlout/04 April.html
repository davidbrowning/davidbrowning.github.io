Tuesday, April 1, 2003

 

            We had Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita over for breakfast.  They then worked with us on Tagalog for about 1 1/2 hours.  We then went to San Jose shopping for a mirror, a chain for the gate, some clothes line wire, a toilet seat, a tape recorder, keys for the house and gas for the car.  We came back for lunch and took a short nap.  This afternoon we signed up for telephone service to our home.  That will probably take a week to get a phone installed.  We then went visiting several families.  Some have nice homes, but most have a very modest home and your heart just aches for them.  We visited Brother Grandes last.  His circumstances really humbled me.  His wife works in Hong Kong in domestic help.  She came home after being gone for 7 years, then came back for 3 years, then has been gone 2 years.  She will come home on the 24th of this month.  Brother Grandes works six days a week from 8 AM to 6 PM, then takes care of his family, does all the house work, cooking and shopping.  Before leaving, I felt impressed to ask him if he would like a Priesthood Blessing.  He did, so I was spokesman.  We left the Elders at 8 PM and came home.  We were beat by the time we got something to eat and got ready for bed.

went over to the internet cafe, and found that none of our e-mails went through last night.  Mae kept working on the internet, while I went back to the Elders apartment to get them.  The pouch mail and letters from the mission home came today.  We also received word that Becky had given birth to a baby girl, born Friday 28 March, they named her Jessica Joe Browning, and she weighted 8 LB 15 oz and is 19 1/2" long.  She has lots of dark hair.  Both Mother and baby are doing well and are home from the hospital.  What great news and great blessings.  We also got a letter from the Social Security stating that they have suspended my social security payments because we are living in a country that the U.S. don't have an agreement with.  We tried to call Social Security office in Manila but it was closed for the day.  We will have to try again tomorrow.  We went with the Elders to visit an elderly couple age 68 that have a very nice home.  He is a farmer that raises "palay" or rice.  His farm is about 5 acres, but I guess that is a good size farm for here.  I ask him quite a few questions about his farm.  They also have a lovely family of nine children, 3 sons and 6 daughters, all Thursday, April 3, 2003

 

            Elders Kendall and Irorita came over and worked with us on teaching us Tagalog.  I feel so weak in this ability.  Mae is doing so much better than I and yet is feeling the frustrations of not being able to communicate with these humble people.  That frustration is heightened with me.  I don't know what he Lord would have me do?  We were able to reach the Administer of Social Security and found that another letter is on its way telling me to ignore the last letter.  Benefits are ok, but they still need some information from us on our address. He will send us the forms.  After lunch Elders Kendall and Irorita had us go with them to the city if Kimba where we did companion splits with the Elders in that area.  Mae & I went with Elder Welsh to visit members to become acquainted.  The Branch President of the one Branch was not at home.  We drove to his home in the farm area.  The humble constancies these people live here is just indescribable.  Many of the homes in this area are just bamboo shells with thatched roofs and dirt floors.  Water is pumped by hand from wells.  Elder Welsh explained that the inactivity of this area is so great that they are not allowed to proselyte here but can only try to activate.  While in that area we did visit one Sister and her Daughter.  Although she could speak very little English, and Mae and I very little Tagalog, the visit was very delightful.  Her husband is a police officer and is required to be at his job full time every other week.  He is then home for a week and they can attend church together.  They were baptized three months ago and are doing well.  From there we went into town to the Church House.  We met the two counselors in the Branch Presidency.  Both are fairly young and neither is married.  They cold speak broken English and we enjoyed meeting them.  We next visited the home of a recently returned missionary, Brother Ramazaros.  He was very delightful to visit with and will really be a great strength to the branch.  At his home was a young man, Jereck, 16 years old, who was just visiting.  After visiting there, the young man wanted us to visit his family.  As we left, he ask if he could ride in our car.  He introduced us to his mother, Facitas and two sisters, Julie and Jennalynn Dupitas.  Again though we had the language beerier, we had a delightful visit.  The family has just recently been baptized.  The father was working while we were there, but as we were staring to leave in the car, returned home.  The young man stopped us and wanted us to meet his father, Christao Dupitas.  What a delightful experience.  We then left the area with Elder Kendall and Elder Irorita to return back home.  After dinner, I was so tired that I laid down for a nap.  Mae stayed up to study.  About 10:30 PM I woke, just as Mae was coming to bed.  She said that she had tried to wake me, but couldn't.  I said prayers with her, and then stayed up writing this journal entry.   How to help this people is beyond me.  They need leadership, but what am I to do to help, that is my concern.   I can surly see the truth to the statement that President Kimball made several years ago, "With out the missionary system of the Church, the Church would fail." It is not the lack of converts that would be the problem, but the lack of leadership developed in the young men as they go through the training of being a missionary.  As this people gets returning missionaries to take leadership the Church will become strong here.  What to do until then, I do not know.

Elder Kenneth H Brownings Testimony

Tuesday, April 29, 2003

 

            At the request of Matt Price, from the Country Creek Ward, Layton Utah Holmes Creek Stake, I am writing this Testimony.

 

            I bear my Testimony that I know that God lives, that He is the Eternal Father in Heaven, the literal Father of our Spirits and that He loves each one of his children.  I know that He has an Eternal plan, that when followed, will allow each of his children, that will be faithful to those eternal laws, the privilege of returning home to where he dwells.  I know that Jesus Christ is Gods Eldest Son in the preexistent world and is His only begotten Son upon this earth.  He was chosen to be the central figure in the eternal plan of Our Heavenly Father, to come in the Meridian of time to this earth, to be born of an earthly Mother, Mary.  To grow day by day learning much the way each of us do, the experiences of earth life.  I know that He lived a perfect life, not making even one mistake.  I am sure that because of His Father that He was often tutored by Heavenly Angles.  I know that when he reached the age that allowed him to begin His ministry, that He organized His Church in its fullness here on earth.  I know that He set for us a perfect example of how we are to try to live.  He lived in perfect harmony and obedience to His Heavenly Fathers Eternal laws and ordinances.  I know that because Our Heavenly Father and Jesus love for each of us, that Jesus was allowed to suffer the most unjust trial and then was crucified on the cross.  Jesus is the Christ foretold by all the Prophets of God from the beginning of time.  Through His selfless sacrifice He became the First to be resurrected and made it possible for everyone that has been or that will be born to be resurrected from the death of the body.  Also that because of His love for us,  He was willing to suffer and paid the Eternal price for all the sins of those that are willing to take upon then self His Name and be obedient to His commandments.  Through this Atonement for our sins, we will be able to be cleans from our sins and return to live with Our Heavenly Father and receive Eternal Exaltation.

            I know that there was a falling away into total Apostasy from Christ True Church after His death and the Death of His chosen Apostles.  The authority from God for man to act in His name was taken.  Although many men tried to find the truth and many brought about great forces of change, it wasnt until April 6, 1830 that The Church of Jesus Christ was restored through Joseph Smith Jr. and subsequently followed by the restoration of all the ancient authority and gifts that God had given to the Prophets of old until there was a fullness of the Gospel as given by Christ Himself.  I know and bear Testimony that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints is that Church and we have been and am today lead by A Prophet of God, even until the present, with that Prophet being Gordon B. Hinckley.

            I pray with all the desires of my hart that I may always bear this testimony and be obedience all of my life.  I bear this testimony in the Name of Jesus Christ.  Amen.
