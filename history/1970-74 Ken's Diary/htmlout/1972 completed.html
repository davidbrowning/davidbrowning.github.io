                Previous to this calling, shortly after we moved into the Lehi 2nd Ward, we had the Bishopric come to our home to visit us.  During that visit I was asked to teach the oldest youth class in Sunday School.  I was excited at this calling as my experience while in Clearfield, though I was serving in the Bishopric, I enjoyed being called to teach this age during Sunday School when the instructor had to be absent.  I had found this age to be eager to learn and discuss the principles of the Church.

                Also during this visit, Mae was asked to teach the Laurel class in the Young Women’s program.  She too was excited for this and anticipated it with excitement. 

                During my first Sunday School class I was greatly surprised and came home determined to do a better job in my lesson preparation, as my lesson certainly did not go over with the youth I taught.  Mae came home from her first M.I.A. class in tears.  She said that the girls showed no respect to her or to each other.  Over the next several months neither Mae’s nor my class showed any improvement and I would come home discouraged and Mae would come home in tears. I finally had enough and asked to be released.  Mae however continued to persist with her calling and after 6 months things started changing for the better.  She finally earned the respect of almost all of the girls.  Over the years she saw most of these girls get married and most were married in the Temple.  One of the girls a few years later, when we had moved back to Utah and then moved to Arizona a second time, even became one of Mae’s councilor in the Primary Presidency.

                On November 21, 1971 I indicated that I visited with the Sunday School Superintendent and asked to be released.  Actually it was a demand to be released, as I quit!  The problem started first during Junior Sunday School.  Brother Marsdon, was the advisor to the Adult Aaronic Priesthood group and I had been called to assist him.  We had gotten permission for those, that we could encourage to attend the priesthood meeting and class, to be able to pass the Sacrament during Jr. Sunday School.  Today we had one Brother attend the Priesthood class and he expressed interest in passing the Sacrament today.  I volunteered to assist him.  During the Sacrament, I became quite upset at the two Priests that prepared and blessed the Sacrament as they made (in my opinion) a mockery of the Sacrament.  A member of the Bishopric was in attendance and did nothing about the problem.  Had he not been there, I think I would have dismissed the two boys and found another Priesthood holder and we would have completely re-preformed that Sacred Ordnance.  With a member of the Bishopric in attendance, I didn’t feel that it was my place.  I left the Jr. Sunday school quite upset and went to my Sunday school class.   During my Sunday school class the youth were again very disrespectful.  But that wasn’t bad enough; as when I saw a boy and a girl petting, I came unglued.  I walked out of the room and slammed the door.  Just a few feet away was the Sunday School Superintendent visiting with the Bishopric Counselor.  I walked up to the Superintendent, handed him my manual and said “I quit”.  I added that I would never prepare those youth another lesson and then I left.  This evening we attended Sacrament meeting.  Prior to the meeting starting, the Sunday School Superintendent came to me and asked if I could take the class one more week, to give him enough time to get a replacement for me.  I told him, “If he wanted me to baby sit them during class time, I would do that, but I would not waste the time to prepare a lesson for them”.  He agreed to that.  The following Sunday I finished with the Adult Aaronic priesthood class and I went into my Sunday school class room.  I was the first there so I just set down by the door and started studding my next Sunday’s priesthood lesson.  As students started coming into the room I wouldn’t even look up.  When the last seem to have come in I looked up as not a person was saying a word.  I told the class that I hadn’t prepared a lesson but was there just to baby sit the class.  I went back to reading.  Without anything being said, I again said that I had no lesson for them, but if anyone had a question we could discuss that.  No one said anything so I started reading again.  Finally one girl cleared her thought and said Brother Browning, I have a question. That opened up a discussion and the rest of the class went well.

                 May I also include another experience I had with this Sunday School class, just a short while before my call to be Executive Secretary.  Again I am unable to pinpoint the date.  At this time on Fast Sunday, a Sunday School class would be assigned to give the Opening Exercises to the meeting.  One member was to give the opening prayer, two class members were to give talks on the Scripture that the class had chosen and then the entire class was to stand and five a Scripture Recitation.  The class was given a month advance notice so there was plenty of time to prepare.  My class had been chosen.  I had tried to get the youth to participate but I could not get them too corporate at all.  As the month went by I tried to find ways but to no success.  I finally got one girl to agree to give the opening prayer.  I asked a second girl if she would give a talk and I asked the Bishop’s son if he would give a talk. I also got one girl to agree to just stand and read the assigned scripture.  On that Sunday Morning I was notified that the Bishops son wasn’t going to be there as he didn’t feel well.  I thought that as my lesson was on the ability to open the Scriptures, pick a Verse from the page and give your ideas on that scripture, so I thought I would do that.  The girl that was going to give the opening prayer, decided that she wouldn’t come up to the stand and didn’t even though the member of the Sunday School prescience announced her name. I was also new to the calling as he had just been put into the calling a couple of week earlier.  This frustrated him and he had to call on an adult to open the meeting with prayer.  Then, the girl that was going to give the talk wouldn’t come up to the stand.  I got up to give my talk but instead I let the parents of the Ward have it with both barrels and really told them that they needed to teach their children better respect.  I don’t know just what I said but Mae had taken David into the cry room and from there she said that she could really see the anger in the Bishops wife, who was the music chorister and was setting behind me.  Well, opening exercises were dismissed and my Sunday school class didn’t go very well, with what few there was in attendance.  On Fast Sunday, Sacrament meeting follow Sunday School with Fast and Testimony meeting.  The first person to take the stand where the time was opened for the bearing of testimony said that our children were good and Brother Browning had no right to criticize them the way I had.  The next person was the Scout Master and he said that Brother Browning was right, and our children needed more parental discipline.  Then a parent disagreed with Brother Browning, then the Primary President agreed with Brother Browning and that is the way it went the entire meeting went.  I was glad to get out of that meeting.

                When the call came to me to be the Executive Secretary, I felt that in reality the call was just to give me something to do beside be with the youth.  The position of Executive Secretary was a relatively new calling and as of yet had little training information as to what the position detailed except to fulfill what the Bishop needed to be done.  The Executive Secretary was to attend all meetings that the Bishopric attended. 

                I do not want to, in anyway, downgrade Bishop Price, he was a Brick Masonry Contractor by profession and worked very hard during the week.  Also, the position of Bishop, which he had fulfilled for over 4 years, required many unseen hours of time and responsibility and so it is easy to understand that after being in meeting from early morning until after noon, bishop Price would start the Ward Council meeting and then would fall asleep.  His first counselor would just pickup and continue the meeting.  Each person in the council, in turn would make reports, requests or receive assignments.  Then it would be the next person’s turn.  I always sat next to the Bishopric and would be the last to report.  When it came my turn I would be asked if I had anything for the meeting.  As executive secretary, I had no assignments unless given to me by the Bishop or his counselors.  During the first three weeks, I had never been given an assignment and so I would just say that I had nothing to report.  During the first three weeks, I was beginning to feel embarrassed to have nothing to report.  However, I began to see a trend in the meetings.  The first person to give a report, was the Ward Clerk and he would read the minutes of the last meeting.  In those minutes, assignments would have been noted but I realized that very few of them ever surfaced again as far as a check up to see if the assignment had been completed.  Deciding that I should do something, I got a note book and wrote down the meeting, the date and the assignment given.  For example: it had been requested that the High Priest Group Leadership were asked to check on a family that had some serious sickness.  The Elders Quorum Presidency was asked to check on a new family that had been reported to have moved into the Ward.  On the next meeting when it was my turn to report, I said: last week the High Priests were asked to check on a family about their health, has that been done?  The High Priest Group Leader, cleared his throat and said “I forgot, we will do it this week.  I then said, Last week the Elders was to check on a new Family that moved in, has that been done?”  The Elders President reported that they had gone by but no one was at home but they would go by again.  I then said that was all I had.  The meeting was closed and we went home.  At the next weekly meetings, I again would bring up past assignments and if completed, I would cross them off, but if not completed they would remain in my note book.  On the second week when I again reminded the high Priest that they were assigned to visit a family two weeks ago, the Group Leader turned to the Elders President and asked he had a piece a paper and a pencil, adding “he expects me to do this, so I better write it down.”  I continued with this note book as long as I held this position. 

                About a year later, the 2 Lehi Wards were divided and the 3rd Ward was formed.  The week prior to the division, I was called into the office to meet with our Stake President.  When I was called into the office, the Stake President was sitting behind the desk, with Carl Reese setting to his side.  I knew Carl as he had been the 70’s Group Leader in our old ward.  I entered into the office and the President welcomed me.  He then informed me that the information that I was going to be told about was strictly confidential and was not to go outside that room until the action had been announced from the pulpit during a public meeting.  I told him that I understood.   He then asked if I knew Carl Reese.  I replied that I did.  He then told me of the upcoming ward division and that Carl Reese had been called to be the new Bishop of our ward.  He asked me if I could support that calling.  I assured him that I could.  He then told me that Bishop Reese had asked that I be extended the calling of Executive Sectary to serve with him.  I told him that I was willing to accept that calling.  At that the Stake President turned to Bishop Reese and said, Bishop Reese, do you have any instructions for your new Executive Secretary?  Bishop Reese then said “yes! You know that note book you have been keeping”.  I said “yes”.  He told me that that was the main job he wanted me to continue to do.

                June 1970 I went to work for Browning Arms Co. in Morgan, Utah.  I had been out of the Data Processing field for 2 ½ years and felt that I needed to find a company that would let me back in.  I found that when most of the companies I applied with, looked at the time I had been out of the field, that would end the interview.  When I got the opportunity to apply with Browning Arms, I left the wage question blank.  During the end of the interview, the Data Processing Manager told me that I hadn’t filled in the wage that I requested.  I told him that many were concerned about my time out of the field and I asked him if he could give me an offer.  He said that he couldn’t and that I had to fill it in.  Being almost desperate for the job, I put $600 per month.  He said: “Even if you can’t program, I could give you a broom and dust pan, as I pay my janitors more than that.”  He then said “If in 90 days you can prove your abilities I will bring you up to where you should be paid.”  I accepted the job on those terms.  It didn’t take long until I realized that we could not exist on $600 a month.  Bishop Murdock put me on Welfare and he also had the McKay Dee Hospital write off the bill from the birth of David. 

                I was working 60 plus hours a week on salary and I had been called as First Counselor in the Clearfield 1st Ward’s Bishopric.  Anyway, this became a very stressful period in our lives.  I kept thinking that as soon as the 90 days were up I would get a raise.  Well as things went, just before the 90 days were up, Mr. Browning sent out a memo to all personal that he had put a freeze on all wages and that no more than a 1% increase in pay would be given during the next year.  That was really a blow to me.  It was early spring and had been a very wet spring.  I came home very discouraged and down and told Mae that I needed to get away.  Mae asked me where could we go this time of year?  I said that I didn’t care but I couldn’t take it any longer.  The thought then came to me of Larry and Tammy now living in Mesa, Arizona.  We questioned where we could get the money for gas.  Mae visited with her mother, and because Uncle Van and Aunt Allison also lived in Mesa, she said that she would pay ½ the traveling expenses if she could go with us.  We, therefore, took a week’s vacation time and drove down.  While there with Larry and Tammy and attending Church with them, Larry introduced me to a neighbor and member of their Ward, Ron Shumway.  Ron asked me to come over to his home after meetings as he would like to talk to me.  I did and he asked about my computer programming experience and abilities.  He said that he would like to bring me a programming manual on the language they were using and have me write him a short program in that language.  He said that he didn’t want me to work on it while I was on vacation but when I had time when I got back home.  During the remainder of our trip I did do a little studying in my spare time.  When I got home I rewrote a short program in the new language.  I got one of my associates there at Browning Arms to key punch the program.  As I didn’t have a computer compiler, I just packaged it up and asked Mae to mail it to Mr. Shumway the next morning.  When I came home from work the following day Mae said that Mr. Shumway called and wanted me to call him back.  I was surprised as I was sure that the program had not had time to get to him that fast.  I called and he was not home but they would give him the message when he got there.  The next day Mr. Shumway called me at my work number and asked if I could come in for an interview.  The day of the week was then Thursday.  I said that it would take me about 16 hours to drive down, to which he said, no! I don’t want you to drive.  He said that if I could go to the Hugh’s Air West ticket counter at the Salt Lake Airport the next morning, tickets would be waiting for me.  I told my supervisor, Roger, that I needed Friday off.  He said that I could.  Mae took me to the airport early Friday morning and I flew down to Phoenix.  Ron met me at the airport and took me to his office.  There he had some of his staff interview me.  They then took me out to lunch.  We returned to his office where he offered me a job at $900 a month.  I asked him if I could talk it over with Mae and call him back.  He said that it was ok with him.  I flew back home very excited.  We thought it over and I visited with Bishop Murdock and he also felt good about it although he wasn’t excited about us moving.  Monday morning I went into Roger and told him that I wanted to turn in my resignation.  He said that he was afraid that that was why I had taken off work.  He asked how much he would have to offer me to keep me there.  I said that if he could give me ½ of the increase that I was offered I would stay.  He said that he would soon be back to talk to me.  I went back to my office to wait.  Morning break came and went, noon came and went, and then afternoon break came and went.  I had given up on Roger even coming to talk to me when he walked into my office and said “If you don’t take that job you are the dumbest person I have ever met.”  He had been in Mr. Browning’s office since he had left me that morning but could not give me the raise.  Two weeks later I was on my way to Arizona.  We had decided to have Mae and the children stay until school was out as we didn’t want to take Sandy out of school and put her in a new school for such a short time.  That also gave Mae time to sort out things and pack.  I stayed with Larry & Tammy for that length of time.

                Ron Shumway was one of the greatest people I have ever worked for.  He expected a lot out of you but he also saw that you were rewarded for the effort that you gave.  Just a couple of days, before the day Ron was demoted, I was in Ron’s office talking over a project that I was working on.  A phone call interrupted our visit and I sat quietly while he took the call.  The call went something like this.  “Hello Mr. Bair.” (I believe that was the name of Ron’s boss.)  “No, Mr. Bair, I don’t think that would be legal”.  “Mr. Bair you can go to Hell if you think I am going to jail for do something like that”.   And that ended the call.

 

Wednesday, March 1, 1972

 

                Work went fairly well today but I really don’t think much work got done.  Moral is really low with everyone in the department.  I took time and went over to American Express to check on any openings for employment.  I didn’t find much there.  Mae had M.I.A. this evening so I stayed home with the children.

 

Thursday, March 2, 1972

 

                This morning I flew to San Francisco on company business.  Ron Shumway also was there on company business.  This evening Ron took me out to see the town.  We first went to Fisherman’s Warf where we ate.  Ron then took me to a street that was very steep.  The street dead ended and so we had to turn around.  I really didn’t think the car could tip that far without rolling over.  Next Ron took me to North Bay Street.  We parked the car and walked a couple of blocks.  We saw many women offering them self for money.  We also were walking behind a couple in front of us.  The one in a dress and the man with his arms and hands all over her.  The only problem was that the one in the dress had a very deep base voice.  As a teenager I had to go downtown in Ogden and pull or carry my sister Margaret out of a bar on lower 25th Street.  This was the first time I had ever seen so much sin and filth.  I was glad when we left and went to our hotel.

 

Friday, March 3, 1972

 

                I finished my work in San Francisco and flew back home.  Mon and Dad had arrived at our place about mid-night last night.  Mae and Mom came to the airport to get me about 2PM.  When we got home I took a nap for about 1 ½ hours.  We were going to attend a session at the Temple but Dad had miss-placed his wallet and so didn’t have a Temple Recommend.  After some searching, Mom and Dad insisted that Mae and I go without them.   We got to the Temple just after the last English speaking session closed.  The Brother at the desk invited us to attend the Spanish speaking session which we did.  That was quite an experience as we just had to watch for every clue as to where we were in the session.  All in all it was an enjoyable experience but one that I never want to do again

 

Saturday, March 4, 1972

 

                I took Mom and Dad Porter and the children to the Lehi Day Treck.  Later all of us went up to Pioneer Park for lunch and to play shuffle board.  We then went back to the Lehi Days.  We came home for dinner.  We then left the little ones with a baby sitter.  Mom, Dad, Sandy, Mae and I then went back to the Lehi Days where we enjoyed a play and a dance.  Sandy is becoming good dancer.

 

Sunday, March 5, 1972

 

                Today being Fast Sunday I was in meetings from 6:30AM until 2PM.  Mae had a nice dinner ready for me when I got home.  in the evening we went for a ride and stopped at Mr. Swiss for a treat.  We then went to Ron Shumway’s to get my check.  There is a mistake in my pay check.

 

Monday, March 6, 1972

 

                After I came home from work we took our family to Pioneer Park for dinner.  We then went for a walk around the park looking for all the things we could see that was part of Nature.  This all fit in with our Family Home Evening lesson we had this evening.  We had a wonderful evening and I sure feel blessed.

 

Tuesday, March 7, 1972

 

                After dinner, I attended Bishopric meeting, getting home about 10:30 pm.

 

Wednesday, March 8, 1972

 

                This evening Mae attended M.I.A.  Dad and I went to the Golden Health Spa for a couple of hours.  Mom Porter took care of the children for us.  After returning home I filled out my monthly Home Teaching repot but was too late to turn it in tonight.

 

Thursday, March 9, 1972

 

                After work I met Mae, Mom Porter and the children at the Pioneer Park.  We had dinner there and then played shuffle board for a while.  The little children just enjoy the park.  I think everyone had a good time.

 

Friday, March 10, 1972

 

                I drew $20 out of the credit union this afternoon.  This evening Mae and I took Mom and Dad out to eat at the Old Miner’s Camp.  The Old Miner’s Camp is a restraint in Apache Junction, east of Mesa.  They serve dinner on tin plates and metal cups on old wood tables.  It is very rustic but a fun place to go.  We had a very enjoyable evening.  David has a bad cold and stuffy nose this evening.

 

Saturday, March 11, 1972

 

                I had a job interview this morning but didn’t qualify for what they wanted.  Mae and Mom went over to the spa and then shopping.  I didn’t feel very good so Mike and I laid down and took a nap.  This afternoon all of us went over to The Lakes and saw the model homes there.  This evening Dad Porter and I attended the Saturday Evening Stake Conference session.

 

Sunday, March 12, 1972

 

                We attended Stake Conference this morning.  We didn’t do much else as Mae, Mike, Davie and I all have bad colds.  Mon & Dad Porter, Sandy and I did attend Sacrament meeting.

 

Monday, March 13, 1972

 

                I had an interview with the 1st National Bank of Arizona today.  It went well except I don’t know if it is enough money.  This evening we took our dinner to the park.  After dinner we held Family Home Evening and Sandy led us in a activity.  We finished with ice cream for dessert.  Mae and I are still sick and it looks like Sandy is coming down with the same thing.

 

Tuesday, March 14, 1972

 

                I stayed home from work today.  Sandy and I are still not feeling well.  this evening I went to Bishopric meeting any way.  It was about 11 PM when I got home.  Mae put a spice plaster on both Sandy and I before we went to bed.

 

Wednesday, March 15, 1972

 

                Sandy is feeling much better this morning but I am not.  I stayed home from work and continued to doctor up.  Mom and Dad Porter left about 11 AM to go home.  It has been nice having them here.  I think we have all enjoyed their stay with us.

 

Thursday, March 16, 1972

 

                I took off work about an hour early to get home and help Mae pack to go to Fresno California.  We flew out of the Phoenix Airport at 8PM and Roy met us at the Fresno Airport about 9AM.  We enjoyed visiting for a little while before going to bed.

 

Friday, March 17, 1972

 

                This morning we got things ready for Roy, Steve and I to go deep sea fishing with Roy’s Elders Quorum.  We still had some time left so Roy and Linda drove us around Fresno to show us the town.  Roy took us to a large sub-division and as we were driving down the road, I looked behind us and an airplane was following us.  It about shook me up and I shouted at Roy that he must be on a runway.  He just laughed and told us that this sub-division was built on a private air-port and the roadways were taxi ways so the planes could taxi right to the homes.  They had hanger car ports attached to the homes.  After seeing some of the area, Roy drove to one of his friends, Dee McMillian.  There Linda and Mae let us off, as we will ride with Dee to Morro Bay this evening.  Mae, Linda and all the children, except Steve, will stay at Roy and Linda’s tonight.  Dee invited us to go for a swim in their pool.  This afternoon Dee, Roy, Steve and I left for Morro Bay.  We got there about 9:30PM and met up with others from their Ward.  We all slept in sleeping bags on the beach.

 

Saturday, March 18, 1972

 

                We got up about 4AM so that we could be to the boat dock by 6:15 AM ready to board.  Someone in the group was passing out a sea sickness pill, and because I woke with a stuffy nose, I felt it might be wise to take a couple.  Roy and Dee thought it was nonsense and that sea sickness was all in one’s head.  It was 7AM when the boat left the pier in quite a windy day.  There was some that were sea sick before we even got under way.  I believe that the boat had room for 74 fishermen aboard and we only had 71 from our group so the captain let 3 other men on.  They took their cases of beer up to the bow of the boat and kind of kept to themselves.  The ocean was very rough with waves that I estimated being possibly 40 feet high.  The winds continually pushed the boat sideways.  I had to enjoy watching the three men on the bow.  They would drink down some beer and then hang over and through it up again.  With all the beer they consumed I doubt if any of it made it to their blood stream. Dee and his son were soon sick.  Roy did ok until he had to use the rest room.  When he came out of the little room he was green.  As he would lean over the railing and heave Steve would ask, what is wrong daddy?  And then he became sick and he would say, I don’t want to go fishing Daddy.  There was one man that kept running around offering everyone crackers, saying that they would help them.  However; before we got back to shore I saw him heave up everyone he had eaten.   I believe that everyone on board, except the crew and myself, got sea sick.  The captain said that he had never before taken a group out in that bad of weather.  I don’t think there were very many fish caught, though I did catch 4.  Everyone had put a dollar into the pot for the largest fish caught during the day.  I don’t think anyone even checked that when we got to the dock.  Everyone just wanted off the boat.  It was about 8PM when we got back to Fresno.

 

Sunday, March 19, 1972

 

                We attended Priesthood and Sunday school with Roy and Linda.  After dinner we caught the 2:30PM flight to Phoenix.  It has been a very nice weekend.  We had Sandy tend the children while Mae and I went out for something to eat.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

 

 

Monday, March 20, 1972

 

                After I returned home from work, we held our Family Home Evening at the Pioneer park with Larry and Tammy’s family.  It was good to be together with the two families. 

 

Tuesday, March 21, 1972

 

                Mae has been busy getting things prepared for a M.I.A. party.  This evening I attended Bishopric meeting getting home about 11:30PM.  When I got there Mae was waiting up for me.  She sure is a beautiful and wonderful doll!  I sure love her.

 

Wednesday, March 22, 1972

 

                Mae had her M.I.A. party and was very pleased with the way it went.  I stayed home and tended the children this evening.

 

Thursday, March 23, 1972

 

                Work went well today.  Brother and Sister Sherman brought me some tomato plants that they had extra.  I planted them in our garden this evening.  Mae went over to a Shakily meeting this evening.  I stayed with the children.

 

Friday, March 24, 1972

 

                Mae and David went to the Doctor today.  He said that he thought that David would out grow the limp and everything looked ok with Mae.  This evening I went Home Teaching.  After I went over to Brother Judd’s where we took care of some church work.

 

Saturday, March 25, 1972

 

                This evening I made a bid for a fellow to do some remodeling for him.  He wants to convert a patio into a extra bedroom.  I then went over to Brother Judd’s and hung 4 doors for him.  He paid me $30 for the job.  I took Mike with me.  This evening all of went over to Larry and Tammy’s for dinner and to visit.

 

Sunday, March 26, 1972

 

                I had meetings until 12:30PM before going home.  We met Dale and Peggy Dransfield at the park for lunch and had a very nice time.  This evening in Sacrament meeting Mae sang with 6 other Sisters.  Deloy & Lois Porter came to our home and we played Rook.

 

Monday, March 27, 1972

 

                This evening we went over to Dale and Peggy Dransfield’s for dinner and we held Family Home Evening.  We got home about 9:45PM.  It has been a very enjoyable evening.

 

Tuesday, March 28, 1972

 

                After getting home from work and eating dinner, I went up to the Church house where we met in Bishopric meeting.  It was around 10:30PM when I got home.

 

Wednesday, March 29, 1972

 

                This morning I helped Ron Shumway pour concrete.  We started at 6AM and by the time we finished and I got cleaned up and to work at Air West, I was ½ hour late.  Mae had M.I.A. this evening so I stayed home with the children.

 

Thursday, March 30, 1972

 

                After work I stopped at and employment service to see what is available.  When I got home Mae was defrosting the freezer and the outside refrigerator.  I had a meeting attend this evening.  David and Mike are both coming down with colds this evening.

 

Friday, March 31, 1972

 

                Sandy went to work with me today.  This evening we let the kids color Easter Eggs.  Mae and I then went grocery shopping.  When we got home we cooked and colored more eggs.  The boys still have colds.

 

Saturday, April 01, 1972

 

                After we ate breakfast, the children went outside to fine Easter Eggs.  When they had found all the eggs and their Easter Baskets, we all worked together in cleaning up the house and the yards.  We then had fun making chocolate, marshmallow Easter eggs as well as other items for Easter so that we were ready for tomorrow.

 

Sunday, April 02, 1972 Easter

                         

                I had my regular Sunday meeting to attend.  Larry, Tammy and their children attended our Wards Sunday school and Fast & Testimony meeting’s with our family.  Mae had invited them for dinner with us.  She also invited Larry’s brother Melvin, his wife Doddy and their family for dinner.  She had prepared a lovely dinner and then we visited and played games.  It has been a very enjoyable day.  Mike and David were much better today but Shellie still isn’t feeling the best.

 

Monday, April 03, 1972

 

                When I got home from work we decided to go for a ride for F.H.E.  We stopped at an amusement called “Smash-n-Dart” and took everyone for a ride.  Shellie was going to drive one by herself but then became frightened so I took her on another ride.  When we got home we had 7 up floats for refreshments.

 

Tuesday, April 04, 1972

 

                Mae did quite a lot of work in her flower garden today.  This evening I attended Bishopric meeting.

 

Wednesday, April 05, 1972

 

                Today I put a application in for employment with Air Research. I also had an interview with Valley National Bank but they can’t offer me enough for me to make a change.  Mae hasn’t felt very well today but she went to M.I.A. anyway.  Larry, Tammy and I went shopping this evening and bought the groceries for a trip we are going on tomorrow.  After returning home I filled out the Ward’s monthly Home Teaching report.

 

Thursday, April 06, 1972

 

                I left work at 1:30PM and came home.  Larry and I changed the refrigerator in his camper.  We left on our trip about 5PM.  We hadn’t gone far when we had trouble with the car overheating.  We stopped at a gas station and checked the water fluid. After adding coolant to the radiator we went on.  As it got dark we realized that we had some troubles with the electrical connections to the trailer so we had to stop to work on that.  By then we were all getting hungry. We found a restaurant and stopped for super.  We then found a place where we could pull off the highway and camp for the night.  We were about 30 miles from Tucson.

 

Friday, April 07, 1972

 

                We got up and prepared breakfast.  After eating we again started out.  We hadn’t gone far when the car ran out of gas.  We began to wonder if this trip was going to be worth continuing.  However, we had made it to a trailer park where we had a fellow help us with enough gas to get to a filling station.  We arrived at Nogales, Arizona about noon.  We were able to find a place where we could park the trailer for the night.  We then crossed the border into Nogales Mexico.  We did some shopping but mainly touring.  Larry had a young boy ask if he could shine our boots for a quarter.  Larry asked if he meant both Larry’s and my boots for a quarter.  The boy said yes.  Feeling that it was a good deal, we let him shine our boots.  When Larry handed the boy a quarter, he said no, a quarter each.  Larry told him that is not what he had agreed on but the boy refused to take the quarter.  Finally Larry told him either he took the quarter of nothing.  He finally took the quarter but then followed us down the street, about two blocks, calling “Cheep sons of a bitch”.  After shopping we drove into some of the housing areas. The difference between the Mexico and the U.S. residential areas were so completely opposite.  Where the U.S. side was beautiful homes and yards, the Mexico side was shacks, without doors or windows and no yards except bare dirt. After a while we returned to our camp on the U.S. side of the border.  We prepared dinner and ate, and then we again went across the border.  About 8PM a band began to play and couple’s began coming out from everywhere to joined in street dancing.  The amazing thing that we noticed is that the girls were all wearing very beautiful white dresses or white blouses.  From what we had seen of the housing earlier, we wondered how they could get so clean or even where they could keep clothes so beautiful.  At 10PM the band stopped and everyone went home.  It was a special experience to be there.

 

Saturday, April 08, 1972

 

                This morning we drove across the border and then continued south.  We soon realized that if a semi-truck was coming towards us. you pulled over to give them the right-of-way.  Several times we almost had head on as they took the entire road.  We first went and stopped at the city Magdalena, which from the highway just looked like a dirty Mexican town but when we turned off the highway and went into the town was a very beautiful community.  There was well kept stores with a well kept board walkways.  The road way was mainly cobblestone and the area was very clean.  A wedding was just finishing as we looked across a very clean grassy park to the Catholic Cathedral.  The bride and groom were getting into a decorated carriage to leave.  The whole scene was almost like out of a fairy tell.  As we walked around town we went past a Buick Automobile dealership.  We went in and Larry asked a salesman the price on the new car.  It was much higher than it would be in the U.S.  When Larry asked why the salesman explained that the prices in the border town were cheaper because most items there were manufactured in Mexico for sale to the tourist but all items manufactured in the U.S. had to be imported through Mexico City and then trucked back up to the northern towns which that cost had to be add to the item.  After we left the dealership we heard the clock on the cathedral tower strike noon.  We then noticed that all the stores closed and everyone went to the park square, set down under a tree and took a siesta.  We then left and drove further south.  When we to Santana it too looked very dirty and when we drove into the town it was nothing like Magdalena, but it was a dirty Mexican own.  We didn’t stop to look around but got back on the highway and drove back to Nogales.  We got there about 3PM and did some more shopping for a while.  We then went back across the border to the trailer.  Larry and I worked on his car for while Tammy and Mae prepared dinner.   After eating the four of us played Rook.  This had been an enjoyable game that we had often done together.  However this time

Larry had determined that if a person always took the bid, whether or not he had a good hand or not, in the end he would win the game.  He therefore would out bid any other player even if he just throw the hand in and went back the bid.  The game lost all fun and after that trip we never played another game together.

 

Sunday, April 09, 1972

 

                We got up about 7:30AM, ate breakfast and got ready to leave by 9:30AM.  We stopped about 30 miles north of Tucson and fixed dinner and rested before going on.  It was about 5PM when we got home.  We spent the rest of the evening with the kids, not making it to Sacrament meeting.  Mom Jensen called this evening to see if it would be ok if they came down tomorrow.

 

Monday, April 10, 1972

 

                I left work at Air West at 12:15PM in order to pick Mom and Dad Jensen up at the airport.  I had planned on taking them home and returning to work but on the way home the front bearing went out on our car.  I spent the rest of the afternoon working on the car.  This evening Mom, Dad, Mae and I were up until 11PM just visiting and playing Rook.

 

Tuesday, April 11, 1972

 

                Today was a very busy day at work.  This evening we had planned on taking Mom, Dad and our family to the Pioneer Park to eat but the wind was blowing so hard that we stayed home.  After eating I went with Larry to look at a remodel job and get things lined up to do it.  as soon as I got back for that I went up to Bishopric meeting.  I didn’t get home until 11:20PM, very tired.

 

Wednesday, April 12, 1972

 

                I left early this morning for San Francisco, where I was to be in a meeting at Hugh’s Air West headquarters.  When I got there I was told that the meeting was rescheduled for this afternoon.  I was able to take care of some other business until noon.  I went out for some lunch and when I came back, I continued to find things to do, waiting for the meeting.  At 2:15PM I was told that the meeting was canceled until Friday.  I made arrangements to flight back to Phoenix on TWA.  This evening we just relaxed and enjoyed being with Mom and Dad.  We again had a game of Rook and as usual enjoyed having a cup of hot Postum and ginger snap cookies.

 

Thursday, April 13, 1972

                Today was a bad day at work.  Several of my systems had problems that I spent a lot of time working on.  Mom and Dad Jensen flight left about 11AM for Fresno.  I was so busy that I didn’t make it to terminal to see them off.  When I got home I found out that Mae also had a very bad day.  I decided to take her out to dinner to cheer her up.

 

Friday, April 14, 1972

 

                I worked late today because of problems.  The meeting that I had been told would be scheduled for today was re-scheduled for next Wednesday, April 19th.  I did get some good news that off set my other problems.  My supervisor, Scott Fry, called me for performance review and a $53.00 a month raise, retroactive to April 1st.  After work I went to Chandler to measure and figure materials to give a Lady a bid to install two doors for her.

 

Saturday, April 15, 1972

 

                Ron Shumway and I work 10 hours for Bob Willis, building an addition  to his home.  after getting home and cleaning up I took my family out to dinner.  After eating we went to Dash & Smash, it is am entertainment center where they have bumper cars.  We all had a lot of fun riding them. It has been a nice day.

 

Sunday, April 16, 1972

 

                Ron and Audrie Shumway and their older children planned a trip to Nogales Mexico for the day.  They asked if Mae and I would tend their baby, Ronda which we agreed.  She is about the same age as David.  I stayed home from Sunday school with David and Ronda, while Mae took the rest of our family.  Ron and Audrie got back home about 5PM which left us time to attend Sacrament meeting.  After that meeting we took the children and Sandy tended them while Mae and I went to visit Lois and Deloy Porter.

 

Monday, April 17, 1972

 

                Ron and I worked two hours, from 5:30AM to 6:30AM on William’s home before we went into work at Air West.  We went home to clean up and change and then Ron came by my place and we road together into work.  On the way home this afternoon I had Ron stop so I could go into Nationwide Finance where I paid of our loan with them.  Mae had bought material for new drapes for our kitchen so I helped her with them.  Sandy started with piano lessons this afternoon.  We held Family Home Evening tonight and it went very well.

 

Tuesday, April 18, 1972

Note of addition:

 

                One thing that I have left out of this Diary, is very much detail of my everyday work.  I am making this insertion here as I cannot remember or find the exact date of an important occurrence while working at Hughes Air West. 

                I had indicated that shortly after coming to work for Hughes Air West that I had been assigned to be the programmer responsible for the Air Freight Cargo system.  I soon found that it was a very poorly written system and was a great challenge to keep it running and in balance.  Also, I found out that the three previous programmers, that had that responsibility, had been terminated because of the system.  When I first found that out I went to my supervisor, Scott Fry, and said to him, “I thought you liked me but it looks like I am an escape goat if the system fails.”  I told Scott that getting this assignment was like being handed a rope with thirteen loops in the knot.  The systems main runs were on the 15th or mid-monthly and the month-end run, at the end of the month.  Our computer had the ability to operate two systems simultaneously, one program in foreground and the other in background.  When the Freight Cargo system started to run, it held up the simultaneous operation and took up the complete computer time.   The mid-monthly run would take approximately 8 hours to run and the month-end-run would take 16 to 24 hours to run.  90% of the time the runs would be out of balance, in which case the programmer, in charge of the system, had to find the error or errors, restore the files to the original information and then start the system running over.  Probably the greatest concern, caused by this run, is that when it ran it took up 100% of the computers time, and all other systems were on standby until it was finished.  That meant that all flight records and especially the flight maintenance reports were unavailable.  This was a great concern as every plane in the fleet had exact schedules to be met and scheduled.  In other words, the entire airline could be shut down or at least fined for not meeting the required maintenance.  Therefore, 8 to 24 hours is critical but to have to rerun it again is unforgivable. 

                During the next few months I continued to work with the system and often had to spend many extra hours.  I also got an assignment to develop a tape library system that would keep track of the data tapes that are kept with various data files on every system controlled by the computer.

                As I worked with the Freight Cargo system my lead programmer and I looked at what it would take to get the system to work.  We finally concluded that it would be easier to completely write a new system rather than try to correct this one.  We went to Ron with our proposal but he turned us down.  He said that he could never get it approval to rewrite a working program, no matter how bad the system is, when he had so many other systems yet to be written.  That ended the conversation and I just had to continue to baby sit the Freight Cargo system

                Then somewhere around the first of April, I got a call from Ron asking me to come to his office.  I went down and when I walked into the office, there were several men sitting around Ron’s office.  I recognized one of them as being the head of Air Line Planning.  The others I don’t think I had ever seen.  As I entered, Ron just handed me an envelope.  I looked inside of the envelope wondering if there was a pink slip in there.  It wasn’t pink so I removed the letter and read it.  It was a letter from the Federal Department of Airlines, if I remember right.  The letter required every Airline in the United States to supply the total Freight Cargo Air Mileage to that Department by a given date.  They also asked that each airline send a representative from each Airline to a meeting in Washing DC on May 21st, 1972.  After reading the letter, I folded the letter back up and handed it back to Ron.  He said, well?  I said well, what?  Ron then asked if we had a system that could provide the information that was being requested.  I told him, no, we didn’t.  Ron then asked, “Do we have a system that could be modified that would provide the information?”  To this I said, no, we don’t.  You are referring to the Freight Cargo system, but it will hardly operate now, not alone asking for additional information from it.  I then added, that if we rewrote the system we could get the information that was wanted.  The Freight Cargo System is where the information should be retrieved from.  Ron then said that he would give permission to do the rewrite.  He then asked how much help I needed.  I told him that for now I only needed Jerry Hanselman, I would let him know when I needed more help.  Ron then said, “You are it!”  I asked him what he meant.  He said that the letter requested a representative from each airline and said “You are that person.”

Thursday, May 4, 1972

 

                This morning I needed to go to San Francisco on business.  When I fly on Air West Airlines, it takes me most of the day, because of flight connections, to get there.  I then have to stay overnight to finish my work.  Last week I checked with other Airline’s and found that for $16.00 (Air Line Employee cost), TWA has a nonstop flight that leaves Phoenix about 7AM.  That would put me in our San Francisco office with plenty of time to get my work done and instead of having to stay overnight in a hotel; I could catch an afternoon flight on Air West and be home for the evening.  I had presented the idea to Ron a few days ago and he approved it.  I, therefore, had the secretary make those reservations for me.  Having flown quite a few times since being with Air West, I have become familiar with the flight patterns.  However, this morning, after takeoff and the plane had reached the first climb level and the stewards had started serving breakfast, I noticed that the stewards started picking up any empty cups and wrappers and seemed to be encouraging everyone to finish eating.  Also the plane leveled off to a no climb position.  Then the captain came over the speakers and asked all passengers to please help the stewards to finish up with breakfast as quickly as possible.  This was very unusual.  Within just a few more minutes the captain again came over the speaker  and said, “I would like to inform you that shortly after we left the Phoenix airport. A male person called the TWA office and said, “you had better get the TWA Airliner that just left the Phoenix airport back on the ground, as there is a bomb on that plane.”  The caption then continued to explain that there was a problem as the person did not say which plane and there were three planes that took off within five minutes of each other.  Therefore all three planes were to land at the closest airport that could be available.  Our destination would be the LA Airport.  That announcement caused some tension on the plane with the passengers.  One lady that was sitting near to me exclaimed, “Oh no, this is the third time I have done this since getting on my flight with TWA in London.”  Anyway the plane remained at the low altitude but moved up to normal traveling speed.  As we came closer to LA the plane did not go into the standard flight pattern, nor did it decrease in speed until just as the plane wheels touched the runway, then all breaking systems were applied and as soon as the plane slowed enough to turn, the pilot pulled the plane off the runway, completely away from any buildings or other planes.  As the plane completely stopped a truck with stairs pulled up to the plane.  However the stairs were pulled to the galley door and had to be backed up and brought around to the opposite side to the front passenger side.  We were instructed not to take anything, including personal items off the plane but were instructed, starting with the front passengers, to move off the plane as quickly as possible.  We were then lead to a terminal that was completely off by itself.  I noticed that as we proceeded to the terminal that plain clothed F.B.I. persons moved into the line as though they were regular passengers.  As we entered the terminal they were making notes, of anyone that used a telephone or who was visiting with someone else.  I also noticed an older coupler that had become quite excited from the first announcement of the bomb scare.  A shuttle car came within a few minutes and picked that couple up and took them away.  I later found out that this couple was flying to San Francisco because they had a grandson in a San Francisco hospital that was dying and they were trying to get there before he passed away.  We were at that terminal for quite a while.  Finally, person by person was called up.  We went out and identified any carry on item that belonged to us and then we were taken to where the entire checked luggage was stacked.  We picked up out luggage and took it to a table some distance from anything else except an armed guard.  He then instructed us to place one piece of luggage at a time,  put it on a table and open it up.  We were then to remove all contents, one item at a time and let the guard examine the item.  After all items had been removed from the luggage, the guard then searched completely through the piece of luggage.  We then put all items back into that piece of luggage, moved the luggage to the side and then proceed to our next piece until all our luggage had been cleared.  We then had to wait for everyone else to be cleared before they put us on another airplane and flew us to San Francisco. All in all it was 4PM before I made it to the office and so I had to spend the night in a hotel.

He know that it was a programming error.”  Well! They immediately picked up and left.  I have never seen Ron so mad at anyone before.  I don’t know who or how the problems will get solved, but at least I don’t have to worry about it anymore.  Tonight for Family Home Evening the entire family camped out in the back yard.  We sure had fun.

I began to think more seriously about Ron’s advice.  Anyway, we had a wonderful evening.  It is great being with Mae.

 

Tuesday, May 23, 1972

 

                We had breakfast in the hotel dining room and then I caught the city bus out front of the hotel.  It let me off just across the street from the building that the meeting was to be held.  When I got there several other people were starting to gather.  At first we were just gathering in a lobby but finally they took us into a large conference room.  There was quite a few as every Air Line in the U.S. was to have a representative at the meeting.  By the time we got settled and the ones from the US Aviation Commission got there it was 9AM so they had us take a coffee break.  They supplied coffee or orange juice and sweet rolls for everyone.  Well it was almost 9:30AM before the meeting got started.  Those in charge were vague as to what was to be reported.  They seemed unorganized as to who was to give us direction and after stumbling around they finally opened it up to questions.  Again they really didn’t have any concrete answers to the questions that were presented until everyone quit asking questions.  One of the men finally asked if anyone else had a question for them.  No one raised a hand so I finally raised my hand.  When I was recognized I bluntly said, you haven’t been able to answer anyone’s questions yet so why should we ask any more?  They admitted that they weren’t very well prepared for the meeting.  They assured us that if we would all give them an address and a phone number where we could be contacted, they would get more information to us shortly.  The meeting was then dismissed. 

                I was back to the hotel before noon.  I found Mae in the back garden reading and preparing for her talk for Sacrament Meeting on Sunday.  We got a quick lunch and then went down town Washington D.C. sightseeing.  We had picked up a tourist guide pamphlet at the hotel.  We took the bus to the White House, which wasn’t open to tourist.  We walked the outside of the fence and then down the block.  We continued down the street where we saw a art museum that was open.  We went inside and looked around at various arts on display.  The main thing I remembered about the museum was a large room that was empty except one large picture that took most of a wall.  The picture was framed, all white background with one red spot, about the size of a baseball in the center of the picture.  There was nothing else on the painting or in the room.  From there we walked to the Lincoln Memorial, which we found very impressive and spent some time there. To stand at the entrance of the Memorial and look east, you see the Capital Building at the far end.  About half way between the Lincoln Memorial and the Capital Building stands the Washington Monument. The landscape in that area is mainly beautiful well kept lawn with gardens and water ponds.  The Washington Monument was constructed in 1848 to commerate the first American President, General George Washington. It is 555 ft tall.  There is an elevator that will take you to the top on the monument where you can look out to see over the city.  At that time they would let you ether ride the elevator back down or you could walk down the stairs.  I convinced Mae that we should walk down the stairs, just to be able to say that we did.  After all it was all down so that would be simple.  Well I don’t know how many stairs there are but by the time we got to the bottom, we were tired.  From there we walked south east to the Smithsonian Institute.  By the time we got there it was 4:45PM.  We just got inside the front door when they announced that closing time was 5PM so please exit the building.  We then walked to the Capital Building but it too was now all locked up.  After walking around the building we went to a bus stop on the north side.  There we waited for quite a while for a bus.  I finally decided to see if we could find a pay phone.  Just then we saw a bus approaching and we were happy as it was getting late.  When we got on and found that this bus would take us to our hotel, we set behind the driver.  The bus stopped at several more stops where people got on the bus with none getting off.  After a while, as the bus was about filled, Mae whispered to me, do you realize that besides the driver, you and I are the only white people on this bus.  I told her that I had noticed that.  When we finally got to our stop for our hotel, we were across the street from the hotel.  Also, there were many people standing in groups around barrels with fires in them to give off warmth.  When the bus stopped I took Mae by the hand and we ran across the street and into the hotel.  It was a relief to feel safe.  We sure enjoyed the experiences of the day.

                I worked at Wilis’s for a while and then came home and changed the oil and greased our car.  At 5:30PM we went over to Larry and Tammy’s.  We ate dinner, visited and played Rook.  Our children all seem to enjoy playing to gather.  We got home about 11PM.

 

Tuesday, May 30, 1972

 

                It was a busy day at work today.  I had to get ready to go to San Francisco in the morning.  I came home from work and ate dinner.  I then went Home Teaching before going to Bishopric meeting.  It was mid-night before I got back home.

 

Wednesday, May 31, 1972

 

                We went into the Phoenix Airport to meet the flight from Salt Lake this morning.  Mom Porter arrived on the 5AM flight.  She came because Tommy Bushnell, the son of Van and Allison Bushnell died Monday morning after a long fight with Leukemia.  I also flew to San Francisco this morning to have meetings about the Freight Cargo System.  I believe the meetings were very beneficial.  I didn’t get home until 7PM.  Mae had M.I.A. to attend this evening.  Mom Porter went with Uncle Van and Aunt Allison to Jeff Bushnell’s High School Graduation.

 

Thursday, June 1, 1972

 

                The school year ended today for Sandy.  She was able to tend our children for us as Mae and I attended the Viewing and Funeral Services for Tommy Bushnell.  The services were well attended with many members of the ward, friends and family of Uncle Van and Aunt Allison.  The services were also very appropriate for such a brave little boy.  Mae and I attended classes of the Church Education Week, this afternoon and evening.  We really enjoyed the classes we attended.

 

 Friday, June 2, 1972

 

                First thing this morning I went thru all the water taps in the house and ether replaced washers or replaced the complete water tap.  This water here has so much mineral and chemicals in it that it is very hard on plumbing.  Mae and I then attended Education Week classes the rest of the day.  The classes were so excellent and educational.  We were very impressed with all we learned.  The only problem I had was the realization of my human weaknesses.  I guess that is why we were sent here to this earth, that we might learn and develop Faith in Our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ.

 

Saturday, June 3, 1972

 

                I feel that I have had some success with my Goals for today, but still have plenty of room for improvement.  I built some shelves in the boy’s bedroom closet, before Mae and I left to attend more Education Week classes.  Again the classes were very enjoyable and gave me many more good ideas.  The cost and the time were sure well worth it, for all we have been taught.

 

Sunday, June 4, 1972

 

                I tried making a written plan of my day, when I got up this morning.  We were taught this in the classes that we took in Education Week.  I felt like it really did help.  Rhis evening Deloy and Lois Porer came over to visit and play some games.

 

Monday, June 5, 1972

 

                I got up early this morning and went to work for Ken Groscost, he had talked with me a few days ago about helping him on the house is going to build.  I told him that I would be glad to help him at home for no charge, but if I went on to the job sight I felt that I would have to charge him $10.00 per hour.  He said that he had another person that said that he would only charge him $5.00 per hour.  I told him that it was fine with me if the other person helped him, but to take the time away from my family, I just felt that my time should be worth the $10.00 per hour.  I spent two hours this morning with my Transit, helping him lay out the corners where the footings were to be dug.  I explained that he could now run a string from stake to stake and then he should drive a stake about every 8 to 10 feet along the string for the backhoe driver to follow.  I gave him the corner elevations and told him that by using a straight 2 X 4 with a level on it he could drive each stake along the string level.  He was concerned at the amount of time I was spending there this morning at $10.00 per hour.  He felt that he could follow my instructions for there, so I left. I went home to change and then into work.  When I got home Mae had a very nice dinner ready for us.  We then held Family Home Evening and Mae had the lesson prepared.  I was quite tired so went to bed early.

 

Tuesday, June 6, 1972

 

                I flew to San Francisco this morning on business and didn’t get home until 7:30PM.  I stopped at home just long enough to say Hi to my family and then went right up to the Bishops office, not getting home until 11PM.  David didn’t sleep very well last night.  We are not sure why but Mae and I were up several times during the night.

 

Wednesday, June 7, 1972

 

                I got my Home Teaching report turned in this morning before I went to work.  Mae had M.I.A. this evening so I stayed home with the children.  I read a Bible story to the children and then put the little ones to bed.  Sandy and I played a game of Jacks before she went to bed.  I then read until Mae came home.

 

Thursday, June 8, 1972

 

                After getting home from work and eating dinner Mae and I went grocery shopping.  I then attended a special meeting at which all Priesthood holders were invited to attend.  The meeting was called to discuss our Ward’s finance situation, which isn’t very good.  The Bishopric recommended that we hold a special Dinner for all the adults in the Ward to attend and suggested that a donation of $100.00 per plate be given.  All in attendance supported the Bishopric recommendation.

 

Friday, June 9, 1972

 

                I didn’t feel that I had accomplished very many of my goals today.  This evening Mae and I went over to Larry and Tammy’s just to visit.

 

Saturday, June 10, 1972

 

                After breakfast I installed a new muffler and tail pipe on our car.  I had just finished when I got a phone call from our Ward Elder’s Quorum President asking me if I could drive a pickup car to Anaheim California, to pickup several Brothers in our Ward that are driving Rental car to California for Avis Car Rental.  The Elder’s are doing this as a fund raiser for the Quorum.  I told him that I would have to quickly clean up and then I could leave.  He told me that the others had already left in the rental cars and he also told me where to go to pick up a station wagon from Avis.  When I got to Avis Car Rental, they had a new 1972 Plymouth Station Wagon all ready to go.  I was told that some of the drivers had left as early as 12:15 PM.  I figured that the others had at least an hour head start on me so I hurried.  That car could go.  In as much as I have never been to California I just followed the directions that Avis had given me.  I am not sure where or when I passed the other drivers but when I got to Anaheim I passed the address and had to go around the block.  Two cars pulled into the lot just ahead of me and the rest soon arrived. After checking the cars in at the Avis dealer, all the drivers got into the car with me.  We left Anaheim in the late evening.  I drove while the rest went to sleep.

 

Sunday, June 11, 1972

                 

                I didn’t drive quite so fast on the return trip home.  We got to the Mesa Avis Rental at 4AM.  I started with Bishopric meeting at 7:30 AM.  The Stake Presidency called for a special meeting at 730 PM at which time the Lehi 1st Ward and the Lehi 2nd Ward were divided making a Lehi 3rd Ward.  Carl Ries was called as Bishop of the 3rd Ward with Delbert Opie as 1st Counselor, Brother Tryon as 2nd Counselor, Brother Shield was called as the Ward Clerk and I was called as Executive Secretary.

                I did not record the night that I was interviewed by the Stake President so I will add the event here. We were told last Sunday that there were plans to divide our Ward.  I was asked to meet with the Stake President; I believe it was last Tuesday evening.  When I was invited into the Stake Presidents office, not only was the Stake President there but Carl Ries was also there.  Carl has been our 70’s Quorum President in the Lehi 2nd Ward.  After being invited to set down, the Stake President informed me that on Sunday a new Ward would be formed.  He said that Carl Ries has been called to serve as Bishop of the new Ward.  He then asked me if I could sustain Carl in that calling.  I told him that I could.  He then said that Bishop Ries had requested that I serve as his Executive Secretary and asked if I could except that calling.  I told him that I would.  He then turned to Carl Ries and asked him if he had any requests of his new Executive Secretary, to which Bishop Ries said:  “I want you to keep that note book”. 

                When I had been called to serve as Executive Secretary in the Lehi 2nd Ward, I soon found out that I really didn’t have anything to do, unless the Bishop gave me an assignment.  We would hold our leadership meetings each Sunday.  After a few minutes of comments and assignments by the Bishop, the time would be turned over to one of the organizational leader for their input.  Each organization would take their turn for any comments.  That process would continue around the room until it would reach me. Usually the Bishop would fall asleep within the first couple of reports and the First Counselor would finish the meeting.  Some assignments might be given but most of the time there was never any follow up reported.  When it got to be my turn, I had not been given an assignment, therefore, I had nothing to report.  Prayer would be given and the meeting adjourned for that week.  After the third such meeting I decided that I should be doing something, so for the next meeting I carried a note book with me.  I would enter the name of the meeting and the date.  I would then make an entry for each assignment that was made.  The next week when it came my turn I read the assignment, the date made, and to whom the assignment was given.  The first time I did this, I read that last week the High Priests were assigned to visit a certain family and then asked if that assignment had been completed.  The High Priest Group leader reported that he had forgotten and didn’t make the visit.  I then read that the Elders Quorum president had been assigned to visit a new Family that had moved into our Ward.  He reported that he had gone by but did not make contact as the family was away.  I said that was all I had to report and the meeting was closed and we adjourned.  The next week when it was my turn I again brought up the assignments from the two previous weeks.  When I asked the High Group Leader about the assignment he had been given two weeks ago and hadn’t completed last week, he turned to the Elders Quorum President and asked if he had a pencil and paper so he could make a note, as that guy really expects us to do those assignments.  That type of report continued until the assignment had been completed or the Bishopric canceled the assignment.  We had one assignment that was given to the Relief Society President that remained on the book for over a year.  It was not necessarily her fault but never-the-less it had not been completed, so it remained in the book and was asked for a report each week.  It got so that prior to the start of the meeting, the Relief Society President would speak to me privately and report that it had not been completed, so please don’t read it in the meeting today.  When it finally did get completed she was so happy to report that the assignment had been completed.  It was surprising what that type of reporting did for our Ward. 

 

Monday, June 12, 1972

 

                This evening we held Family Home Evening.  After getting the children down for the night, Mae packed clothes for her and the children to go to Utah tomorrow.  I got a call from work and had to go in to solve a problem.  By the time I got back home, it was almost mid-night before we got to bed.

 

Tuesday, June 13, 1972

 

                I took Mae and the children into the Phoenix Airport so they could catch the 7AM flight to Salt Lake.  I went from there to the office.  Work got hectic today and as the day went on things got worse.  I felt that Satan was right with me.  This evening I attended the Mesa Temple that I might rid myself of the feelings that I have felt today.

 

Wednesday, June 14, 1972

 

                After work I went to a Mrs. Bell and gave her a bid on some work she would like me to do for her this Saturday.  I then went up to the church house and worked on membership records and other supplies.  I didn’t get home until mid-night.

 

Thursday, June 15, 1972

 

                Ron Shumway and I did some work for Larry this morning from 5AM until 9AM.  We then went to work at Hugh’s Air West.  After getting home this afternoon I laid down for a nap and slept until 8:45PM.  I went up to the church and worked on records until 11:30PM.  I really don’t like being home alone.

 

Friday, June 16, 1972

 

                Before going to work this morning I gave Mae a call to see how they are doing and too let her know how much I missed her and the children.  I had some problems at work today and didn’t get off work until after 5:30PM.  I was still able to get to the Temple.  Our Ward attended as a Temple Day.  Following the Temple attendance several of our ward went to the Brother and Sister Opie’s for dinner.  I attended and enjoyed it but missed not having Mae with me.

 

Saturday, June 17, 1972

 

                I was able to get the job done for Mrs. Bell this morning.  I made $49.00 after expenses so that will help.  I then went home and cleaned up the house before flying up to Utah.   Mae, Bob, Carroll, Irven and Sandy met me at the Salt Lake Airport.  We then went out to a Chinese Restraint for dinner and a good visit.  It was almost 1:30AM before we got back to Mom and Dad Porters and got to bed.

 

Sunday, June 18, 1972

 

                We attended Priesthood meeting and Sunday school with Mom and Dad Porter this morning.  Mom Porter had a very nice dinner when we got home from Sunday school.  Bryce and Deanna and Jean came out for dinner also.  This evening we attended Sacrament meeting at the Clearfield 1st Ward.  It was sure good to see so many of those we grow to love while we lived here.  This evening Irven, Sandy, Mae and I went over to Bryce and Deanna’s.

 

Monday, June 19, 1972

 

                Mae and I left for Salt Lake at 7AM.  Mom P. agreed to tend our children for us.  I had an appointment with Dr. Stutsnigger to have my teeth checked.  After that we went to the Salt Lake Temple and attended a session there.  We went through the Visitors Center while we were there.  We then went out to eat at Harmon’s on North Temple.  We have many memories of that restraint when we were dating but it is not as nice as it was then.  This afternoon I went over and visited with Bob and Carroll.  They are much happier now they have moved back up to Utah.  At 9:20PM I with all my family flew back to Phoenix together.

 

Tuesday, June 20, 1972

 

                After I got home from work, we took all the family swimming.  I then went up to the office at the church and worked on supplies.  Mae spent the evening getting things ready for Shellie’s Birthday Party tomorrow.  We were late getting to bed. 

 

Wednesday, June 21, 1972

 

                Mae had a nice Birthday Party for Shellie today and Shellie seemed to have a very fun day.  It rained this evening so we didn’t go swimming as we had planned.  Mae received a call to be 2nd counselor in the Ward Primary Presidency this evening.  I am not sure why but for some reason I feel just a little funny about that calling.

 

Thursday, June 22, 1972

 

                The rain continued all night and through the morning.  It has caused a lot of flooding in the area. The rain stopped this afternoon and it turned off quite nice.  After I got home from work we all went swimming.  I went Home Teaching after eating dinner.  When I got home Mae and I went for a ride.  Mae sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart.

 

Friday, June 23, 1972

 

                When I got home from work we took our family swimming again.  Larry and Tamy and their family also joined us.  Mae had bought a family summer pass to the swimming pool at the Junior High School that is not very far from us.  It sure makes it nice to be able to use it whenever we like. Larry and Tammy’s family also joined us at the pool.  After swimming we all went to Larry and Tammy’s for dinner.  We stayed late mainly just visiting and the children playing.

 

Saturday, June 24, 1972

 

                I worked at Willis’s today installing cabinets.  We attended the Ward Banquet this evening.  The dinner raised about $7,500 for the budget and operating fund.  After getting the children down we left Sandy to tend them while Mae and I went over to visit the Porters.  We are enjoying our relationship with DeLoy and Lois.

 

Sunday, June 25, 1972

 

                Because we were in Utah last Sunday, this morning is the first time we met as the Lehi 3rd Ward.  Meeting went very well and it is sure surprising how many new faces there are out to meetings.  One can sure see the need to divide the Wards when they get large.  The division gives so many more opportunity’s to participate in the program.  We are looking at a small economy car to purchase.  This evening we drove it over to visit with Uncle Van and Aunt Allison and their family for a little while.

 

  Monday, June 26, 1972

 

                Work went fairly well today but I was sure tired when I got home.  I stopped and looked at a couple of used cars on the way home.  Mae sure had a nice dinner prepared when I got home.  We enjoyed a nice Family Home Evening after eating and cleaning up the dishes.  We spent the rest of the evening just playing with our children.

 

Tuesday, June 27, 1972

 

                After getting home from work, I took some Shakly vitamins over to Larry.  He has been sick for a while and we hope these will help him get to feeling better.  I also started taking them myself as I don’t want to catch whatever is going around.  This evening I was set apart as Executive Secretary by Bishop by Bishop Ries.  I then spent some time at the Bishops helping get things organized.

 

Wednesday, June 28, 1972

 

                I left work a little early this afternoon so I could stop and pickup some parts for the car.  When I got home we all went swimming.  We had to come home in time for Mae to attend M.I.A.  I worked on the car getting it running pretty well.  I mainly changed the plugs and points.

 

Thursday, June 29, 1972

 

                After work this evening I tended the children as Mae went up to the church to help put make up on the youth for a Road Show.  When she finished with that she attended a Primary Leadership meeting.  I changed the oil and filter in the car.  I also wrote Mom and Dad Jensen a letter.

 

Friday, June 30, 1972

 

                After I got home from work this evening, Mae and I went to the Temple.  When we got home from there, I figured our budget.  It really doesn’t look very good but somehow we will get through this.

 

Saturday, July 1, 1972

 

                This morning the DeLoy Porter family invited us to go with them tubing down the Salt River.  This is a great sport with many in this area.  We saw several that were diving off high cliffs into the water below.  Many groups would tie their tubs together and have a party as they floated.  Often there would be coolers tied into the center of the groups filled with everything from pop to beer and liquor.  We had a very enjoyable time but we did come home with some Arizona sub burn.  After getting home I went back into work for a while.

 

Sunday, July 2, 1972

 

                I started early with Bishopric meeting and with other meetings and working in the office, I was at the Church all morning.  Mae had dinner ready when I got home and after dinner we put the children down for a nap and then Mae and I joined them.  This evening Larry and Tammy stopped in for a visit.  Before going to sleep this evening Mae and I visited with each other quite late.

 

Monday, July 3, 1972

 

                After I got home from work and we had dinner, we then enjoyed a nice Family Home evening.  Mae and I set up and read for a little while.  We then drove over to Kent Marsden’s and paid him $235.00 for a 1965 Ford Taunus.  (It is interesting that neither Mae nor I remember this car.)  On the way back home we stopped at a post office and bought some stamps and then at a store for milk.

 

Tuesday, July 4, 1972

 

                We took all our family to the church at 6AM for a Flag Raising ceremony followed by a breakfast.  I had some work that I needed to do at my office so after eating I went into work.  Sandy wanted to go with me so I took her.  I showed her around the computer area and the offices, then we also went into the aircraft maintenance area.  This evening when I went to bed I wasn’t feeling the best.

 

Wednesday, July 5, 1972

 

                I woke at 4AM quite sick.  I stayed home and slept most of the day.  This evening we did take our family to see the Road Shows, held in the Recreation Hall at our Ward building.

 

Thursday, July 6, 1972

 

                I worked late today and when I got home Mae and the children had gone up to the church house to watch and enjoy the Road Shows.  When she got home and we got the children down to sleep, she and I sat and talked for a while.  I then went up to the church office and did some work on Home teaching.  It was almost mid-night when I got home.  Mae and I again set up and talked for about another hour before we went to bed.

 

Friday, July 7, 1972

 

                After getting home from work and having dinner, I worked outside and repaired the lawnmower.  This evening Mae and I vacuumed and shampooed the carpet.

 

Saturday, July 8, 1972

 

                I got out and mowed the lawns while Mae prepared breakfast.  We then took the children tubbing down the Salt River.  After returning home I tended the little children while Mae and Sandy went shopping.  This evening Mae and I went out to eat.

 

Sunday, July 9, 1972

 

                I spent most of the day at the office at Church.  This evening Mae and I went over to the Porter’s where we played Rook with DeLoy and Lois.

 

Monday, July 10, 1972

 

                When I got home from work this evening Mae had a nice dinner ready.  We then took our family and went to the Arizona State University Experimental Farm.  It is just south of our place to Baseline Drive and then West into Tempe a couple of blocks to the farm.  Uncle Van was the one that told us of the farm.  It was a very enjoyable experience for all of us.  We stopped on the way home and bought everyone ice cream.  After getting home, Ken Groscost called and came over to visit with me.  They are going to build themselves a new home east of Mesa.  He had some questions and also wanted to know if he needed some help building the home if I would be willing to help.  I told him I could help with plans and advice at home almost any time, and I could do some evening and Saturday work.  He asked what I would charge for that help.  I told him that work at home I would not charge him anything, but I felt that I would need to get paid for time away from my family and for that time I would charge $10 an hour.  He hesitated and then said that he had another fellow that would help for $5 an hour.  I told him that it was fine to have the other fellow help him but I felt that my time from my family should be worth the $10.

 

Tuesday, July 11, 1972

 

                I worked 2 hours this morning for Ken before going to work at Air West and then another 2 hours this evening.  Using my transit, we got his house laid out on his property.  I also told him how to stake the footing lay out for the backhoe operator to dig the footings and then how to set the grade stakes to know the elevation of the concrete when he poured the footings.  David has quite a bad cold today and Mae also isn’t feeling very well.

 

Wednesday, July 12, 1972

 

                Mae had MIA this evening so we had Sandy tend the children and I took Mae to the church.  I stayed at the office and took care of some paper work.

 

Thursday, July 13, 1972

 

                After dinner this evening all our family went over to Jerry Hanselman’s.  Jerry had invited us over to watch some of his movies.  It was a very enjoyable evening and Jerry is a great host.  He had pop corn and ice cream refreshments.  The children really enjoyed being with him. 

 

Friday, July 14, 1972

 

                I got some of our camping gear packed in the car this morning before going to work.  I caught a ride with Ron Shumway to work.  Mae finished packing the car and the children and met me after work.  We left from there to go camping at Oak Canyon.  Someone at work had suggested that Oak Canyon was a good camping area.  It was a lot greater distant to travel than I had thought, plus in the Arizona heat and a station wagon without air-conditioning, along with three tired, hot children it seemed forever.  When we got to the canyon we found that everyone else had got there ahead of us.  We traveled from the bottom entrance on the South to the upper exit on the North without finding even enough room to pull a car off the road even if it were an emergency and we were in a long line of traffic going both ways that we also looking for some place to stop.  It was mid-night by the time we got to the south side of Flagstaff.  We turned South on the freeway going back to Phoenix where we found a rest area.  Mae and the kids slept in the station wagon and I slept on a sleeping bag beside the car.

 

Saturday, July 15, 1972

 

                We woke early and to a beautiful morning.  We prepared a nice breakfast using the tailgate of the station wagon for a table.  After eating we tried to find a stream to go fishing.  We ended up back at Oak Canyon but just drove through the canyon and then on home. The canyon was still packed.  This evening Mae and I attended a High Priest social.

 

Sunday, July 16, 1972

 

                I was busy at the Church most of the day.  This evening, Mae and I took our children and went up to the park to relax and let the children run and play.

 

Monday, July 17, 1972

 

                I was called into work early due to some problems with the Freight Cargo system.  Later this afternoon more problem arose with the system and I ended up working until 10PM.  The Freight Cargo System is a very poorly designed system and I have recommended that it needs to be totally rewritten. Ron just tells me that there are so many systems that are waiting to be written that he cannot allot any time to rewrite a system that is working, no matter how badly it works.  Mae and the children had to have Family Home Evening without me. 

 

Tuesday, July 16, 1972

 

                I went to work for Larry right after I finished at Airwest.  We worked until after midnight.  When I got home I found out that there was some problems at Airwest so I went back in.

 

Wednesday, July 19, 1972

 

                This evening Mae went to M.I.A. and I went to Bishopric meeting.  The M.I.A. had a water fight as a closing social.  As a Bishopric we went over to see how the social was going.  We ended up getting involved with the social and got wet also.

 

Thursday, July 20, 1972

 

                I flew to San Francisco this morning for a business meeting.  I got back home about 7pm.  I felt that I had accomplished quite a bit while at the head quarters today, but was sure tired when I got home.

 

Friday, July 21, 1972

 

                I slept in and went into work a couple of hours late.  When I got home this evening, Mae and I went grocery shopping.  Brother and Sister Allen brought their children over this evening.  Mae is going to tend their children for a couple of days, while they take a week end trip.

 

Saturday, July 22, 1972

 

                I went to work for Larry this morning and worked late getting home about 5PM.  Mae has had quite a busy day with the addition of the Allen’s children.  We watched TV this evening.  Althouge Mae was tired she sure is a wonderful wife and sweetheart to me.

 

Sunday, July 23, 1972

 

                I was up to the Church most of the day but only attended the opening of Priesthood.  The rest of the time I was in the office working on records.  I came home for dinner and stayed this evening with the children so that Mae and Sandy could attend Sacrament meeting.

 

Monday, July 24, 1972

 

                Mae went shopping for school clothes today.  She also looked for a Colman camp stove.  Ron Shumway and I worked for Larry this evening.  Mae took Shellie into the Doctor. She may have a bladder infection.

 

Tuesday, July 25, 1972

 

                I went Home Teaching this evening.  I then went up to the Bishops office where I joined the Bishopric in an Oral Evaluation with the Stake President.

 

Wednesday, July 26, 1972

 

                I took off work at Air West so that I could pick up some materials to build a case that would hold all our camping equipment.  This evening I attended Bishopric’s meeting.  I worked on the camping case after getting home and worked on it until after mid-night.

 

Thursday, July 27, 1972

 

                After work Ron and I went out a job for Larry.  We worked for 5 hours.

 

Friday, July 28, 1972

 

                This evening after getting home from work and having dinner, Larry and Tammy invited us to go to a movie with them.  We saw the show, “What’s up Doc”.  We really enjoyed the show and evening.

 

Saturday, July 29, 1972

 

                Ron, Larry and I went to work, for Larry, at 5am and worked until 12:30 pm.  When I got home, being quite tired, I lay down for a nap for a couple of hours.  When I woke up I worked on the camping case until mid-night.

 

Sunday, July 30, 1972

 

                We weren’t quite so busy at church today.  Georgia arrived here at 9:30AM and will stay with us for about 10 days.  We are excited to have her come and visit with us.  This evening Georgia went to a Young Adult Fireside.  We had also invited DeLoy and Lois Porter to come over and play Rook with us.

 

Monday, July 31, 1972

 

                After getting home for work and having dinner, I went to work for Larry for a couple of hours.

 

Tuesday, August 1, 1972

 

                Before going into work this morning I worked 1 ½ hours for Larry.  This evening I went up to the church for a while.  Mae sure is a wonderful in her support in the things I do.  I am concerned that I am not spending enough time with her.

 

Wednesday, August 2, 1972

 

                I went into work early this morning.  After work I went up to Lynn Johnson for a treatment.  I don’t remember how Mae became acquainted with Lynn but he reads your eyes and worked on your feet.  The one problem I find in him is he starts talking and his hands stop working, but the clock doesn’t stop.  (This next entrance I do not understand what I meant when I wrote this next sentence.)  Mae hurt her back while tubing on the river today.

 

Thursday, August 3, 1972

 

                I went into work early this morning.  Scott Fry had me come into his office before I left for the day.  He gave me a work evaluation and said that they were very pleased with my work and he had been given permission to advance me to a Senior Programmer and of course it also means an increase in salary. I worked for Larry for a while this evening.  Mae, Georgia and the children went to “The Big Surf” in Phoenix today and had a good time.

 

Friday, August 4, 1972

I am going over here to fish.  You can fish where you want.”  She decided that if she was to fish she needed to bait and remove her own hook.  While mom and I were fishing, between the two of us we mom was going back home after dinner and we wouldn’t have a car.  I roped a small pine tree and bent it down, then put all our food items in a burlap sack, tied it to the top of the tree and let the room was filled with several men, who were all looking at me.  My first thoughts were “Oh Boy, what have I done!”  Ron just handed me a envelope, which I slowly opened.  As I looked inside the envelope, I could see that it wasn’t a “pink Slip”.  I pulled the letter out and read it.  The letter was from the Federal Aviation Commission in Washington D.C. and they are requiring certain data to be sent monthly to them, concerning Freight Cargo transportation from every Airline in the United States.  It also requires that a reprehensive from each Airline be in attendance at a meeting to be held in Washington D.C.   I then folded the letter and put it back inside the envelope and handed it back to Ron.  Ron then said “Well!”  I responded back “Well what?”  Ron asked if we had a system that could provide that information.  I told him “No we do not”, to which he asked if we had a system that could be modified to supply that information.  I again replied “No, we do not.  I told him that the system that should supply that information was the Freight Cargo System, but that system could not run properly even now, but if it was totally rewritten it could meet all the requirements that were needed for the operation of Air West as well as the new requirements requested by the Federal Aviation Commission.  Ron then looked at the other men in the room for a minute, then looked back and asked if it could be done by the given dead line.  The Dead Line was approximately 1 year from the date of the letter.  I told him that I was sure that it could.  He then told me that I had total authority to rewrite the system and would be given whatever assistance I felt I needed.  Then he added “Your it.”  I responded back “I’m it what?”  To which he told me I would also be the representative for Hugh Air West to attend the meeting in Washington D.C.  I was then excused from his office.  Wow! What did I just get into?  From work this evening I went over to the Health Spa and worked out for a while.  After dinner this evening, Mae and I watched the last of a show on TV.  After the children went to bed, I explained to Mae what had taken place at work today.  She totally gave me her support and confidence that I could do the job.  She certainly is a wonderful wife and sweetheart!

Saturday, October 7, 1972

 

                It finally quit raining this morning.  Dad Jensen and I went to the Health Spa for a while this morning.  After we got home, all of us went to Dale and Peggy Dransfield’s place.  They had invited us out.  They took us out to a very large cattle feed yard.  I and Dad Jensen were quite impressed with the operation.  From there they took us to the Air Force Base in the Queen Creek area.  That is the base where Dale is receiving Pilot Training.  The airplane that he is training in is really a very light weight rocket to deliver bombs to a target with a pilot to guide it.  The fuselage is aluminum and holds the jet engine.   The cockpit sits almost on the engine like a saddle on a horse, except the pilot leans back farther.   The wings are Styrofoam with a very thin covering of aluminum sheeting covering.  That sheeting is so thin that the pilots are instructed that when they fill out their reports, they are to use a clip board and not put the paper on the wing as the pencil point might poke a hole in the wing.  Under each wing were mounted 3 or 6 rockets (I don’t remember) with war heads.  Inside the wings are the fuel tanks.  They hold enough fuel for                 I am going to add some information at this point, concerning things going on with my job.  I had not entered this information into my Diary due to so little space.  My work with Hugh’s Air West has been very challenging but very enjoyable.  I have really enjoyed Ron Shumway, not only as my boss, but also as a personal friend.  A few months ago, while I was visiting with Ron in his office, about the Freight Cargo System, a phone call came on Ron’s phone.  He answered it and recognizes Mr. Bared (I am not sure if that the correct name but it will do for now.  Also I was only hearing one side of the conversation, so that is all I will put here.) “Yes Mr. Bared; I don’t know about that. Mr. Bared; I don’t think that we can do that.  Mr. Bared; I don’t think that is legal.  Mr. Bared; You can go to Hell, but I am not going to go to Jail for you.  And he hung up the phone.”  Needless to say, within two days Ron was demoted from Data Processing Manager to Data Input Supervisor.  His replacement was from the planning department.  He was a retired Sergeant from the Army and he was usually smoking a big cigar.  When he came in the room you felt like he wanted you to jump to attention.

                Also at this period of time, the Air West Mechanic Union had called for a strike of all mechanics.  Just as they started the strike, President Richard Nixon had made an executive decision and put a freeze on all union raises.  Without any real financial benefits to gain, the Union wanted to hold off any settlements until the President’s freeze would be released.  So that the Union members could go back to work, they went on a slow down work until the freeze was lifted.  That in itself, was a circus and caused all kind of confusion with the airlines.

                Being that the programming department, along with the accounting department, were non-union employees, plus were salaried employees, that  left us open to fill in where workers were needed.  With long hours and bad management, moral fell.  With my work on the Freight Cargo going well and about to conclude, I was still managing pretty well - until one morning.  Jerry Hanselman, my co-worker, came back in our office after having been called into the manager’s office.  When he walked in, I could tell that it hadn’t gone well.  I asked him what had happened.  He said that he was told that his vacation leave had been canceled.  When he asked why, he was told “I might need you”.  Jerry had scheduled this vacation over a year ago.  He had made plans to take his parents to Europe, where he had spent 3 years in the military, and show them around.  All plans had been made in detail with reservations already made and paid for.  His parents were at that time already on a flight to Phoenix where he would meet them and then he and they would leave tomorrow.  That really upset me and I suggested that he go to Mr. Bared and try to get the decision overturned.  Jerry did and was given 1 week off instead of the 2 weeks he had planned on.

                From the time of Ron’s release as manager and the new manager took over, almost every time a conversation started in our department, the subject became centered on the puzzling question as to what to do.  Many were looking for other opportunities of changing employment.  Several in our department had talked about joining a Union but no one had the nerve to do anything about it.  I was in the final dead line stages of having a new Freight Cargo up and running.  Because Jerry and I had our own office, with most of the rest being in a large room with partitioned area, our office seemed be a place where individuals would come to vent their frustrations.  My work was being hampered by others.   Being upset, I called the union office.  As soon as I told them that we might be interested in joining a union, I was told that I needed to be very careful and sure before making such a decision.  I was warned that if this information was to get out before a union was officially in place, I would be fired.  I was told that I would need over 50% (that % may have been 75%) of the department agree to hold a meeting which would be held off company property, for the Union to explain what they could do for us, should we decide to form a union.  At that meeting we needed to have over 50%, in attendance, vote in favor of the union.  I would need to furnish, to the office of the Union, a list of every person in the department, by full name and complete address, that worked in our department.  From that list the union would send to each person’s home a card requesting them to sign and return by mail, indicating their willingness to hold the meeting.  The card also indicated that under no circumstances, even should the case be taken to the Supreme Court, could anyone, including company attorneys, ever get access to the cards to see who had signed the card.

                Although I was careful as I visited with every person in the department, excluding 3 (as I knew that they would not be in favor of the union) and got a very positive approval, if someone would make the initial venture.  On one particular day someone found out that I had started the process and made a slip about it.  At that time I said “No more! If this couldn’t be kept quiet now, I wasn’t going any further.” This individual and I had a private discussion for well over an hour before I was convinced to continue.   Over the next few days I gathered the needed information and sent it into the Union Office.  The card arrived at my house with Friday’s mail delivery.  When I went into work on Monday morning, I noticed one of the 3 that I suspected, standing in front the Managers office door waiting for the manager to come to work.  He had the card from the Union in his hand.  Within minutes, from work starting time, everyone in our department was called into a meeting.  Supervisors were stationed outside the doors to the meeting room and all phones within the room were disconnected.  The Manager, with his cigar smoking and being chewed, informed us that someone was trying to organize our department into a union.  He said that we had each received a card from the union and that if any one signed the card they were to clean out their personal belonging and leave the company property as they were fired.  We were then dismissed to either go to work or to leave.  As we left the room, almost everyone threw their card into the trash can.  I later found that out of 36 (I believe,) only 3 plus myself sent out cards into the Union office.

                At that point management started looking to find out who was the instigator.

                After getting up we met Ron and Audry and again went to the Fruit Buffet for breakfast.  We then took another walk on the beach, did a little more sightseeing and then turned the car back in before noon.  We checked out of our hotel rooms and went to the airport.  I had taken 3 or 4 rolls of film with our camera but when I loaded the next roll, for some reason I looked inside the empty camera and clicked the shutter.  Nothing happened!  I repeated the process several time but each time the shutter remained closed.  We didn’t know when the camera had failed but after returning home, we developed the 4 rolls of film and gound that we didn’t have one picture of our trip.  We left the Hawaii Airport at 3pm.  We got us hotel rooms in San Francisco, rented us a car and went to the Warf where we had dinner.  We did a little sightseeing before going to our rooms for the night.

Monday, November 6, 1972

                I took Ron and Audry to the airport where they had to fly back to Phoenix as Ron had a meeting he had to attend at work.  Mae and I remained in San Francisco going back to the Warf for breakfast and more sightseeing.  We rode the trolley on a tour through San Francisco until we returned back to the Warf.  We then drove throw China Town, then up a steep hill that Ron had shown me on a previous business trip in which it came to a dead end.  The problem was that the road was so steep that you were afraid that the car would roll over when you turned it around.  We then started for the Golden Gate Bridge, but was having trouble finding directions from our map.  I stopped the car to the side of the road and before I could even look at the map a police officer was at my window asking if he could be of assistance.  He showed me on our map, where we were and drew out the directions to the Golden Gate Bridge.  We drove there and then drove across the bridge.  On the way back we had the windows down and we passed some crews that were sand blasting the bridge and repainting it.  As we passed them Mae felt something fly into her eye.  We couldn’t get it out, so it was a little uncomfortable the rest of the day for her.  This evening we flew back home and it was good to get back home and get our children around us again.  It was a remarkable and unforgettable trip.

Tuesday, 7, 1972

                I went back to work this morning.  Mae had to go into a eye specialist, this morning and have a piece of steal removed from her eye.  That is what blow into her eye yesterday when we drove across the Golden Gate Bridge.  Both Mae and I went into the voting precinct and cast our ballets after I got home from work.  We haven’t studied the candidates and issues very well and so weren’t very informed, but I always remember what my Grandmother Hill told Uncle Lee when she asked him if he had voted one election.  He remarked that he didn’t know anything about the ones running for office, so why waste his time to vote.  She replied, if you don’t vote then you have no right to complain when thing don’t go the way you think they should.  I attended Bishopric meeting this even.  When I got home, Mae had me look at a lump on David’s chess.  She had noticed it before but it is getting larger now.

Wednesday, November 8, 1972

                When I got home from work, all the family went into Dr. Opie for adjustments.  While there we asked him what he thought the lump on David’s chess was and he didn’t know.  This evening Mae and I went to the Mesa Temple and went through a Endowment Session.  Afterwards we stopped at a Pizza shop and bought a pizza.  We had left Sandy to tend the children.  She is getting old enough and she wants to tend them.  The children prefer that Sandy tend them than us getting another sitter.  We told Sandy that if she did a good job tending the children, then we would pay her what we normally would pay a baby sitter.  Sandy protested, saying that she didn’t want to be paid.  We compromised, saying that if she had an opportunity to tend someone else’s children but we needed her to tend, then we would pay her.  She felt that that would be ok then. 

Thursday, November 9, 1972

                After working today, I went from Air West to the Spa for a work out.  I then came home and Mae had dinner ready.  After eating I worked on the Church Magazine subscription drive.  I then called those, which had been suggested by the Bishopric that was to help with the drive.  Almost all of those called agreed to help with the drive.  It was late when Mae and I got to bed.  Mae is sure a sweetheart and a wonderful wife and mother.

Friday, November 10, 1972

                Today will be my final day working for Hugh’s Airline.  I got all my work turned over to others and finished signing all my final papers before quitting.  Working for Hugh’s Airline has been one of the most enjoyable jobs I have had, with the exception of the last few weeks.  I have never enjoyed working under the direction of anyone as I have working with Ron Shumway.  For the most, my team workers have been special friends.  Scott Fry, my Programming supervisor and Scott ??? my Sr. Programmer, have been great to work with.  Jerry Hanselman who shared our small office together, became a great friend.  At lunch time all the Programming staff took both Ron and I out to lunch.  I left Hugh’s Air West office about 2:30pm with a heavy hart.  (It is interesting that the 2nd Scott, I cannot recall his last name.  He was the one that convinced me to try getting the Union in and the first one to throw his Union card into the trash can when we were threatened with being fired if we signed those cards.)  I stopped at an automotive supply store and bought some parts for our car.  Mae has been busy all day with kids and house work.  After dinner we took all the children and went to a good family show.

Saturday, November 11, 1972

                This morning Ron Shumway and I lay out and formed a patio that Ron will pour next week.  We then looked at two other jobs and figured a bid on them.  When I got home I put new spark plugs in our Chevrolet, also changed the oil, filters and lubricated both of our cars.  This evening we took our family over to Larry and Tammy’s.  This is one of the delights all of our family enjoys.  Later this evening Mom and Dad Jensen arrived at our place.  This time they had to drive down as with me leaving the Airline, they also lost their Fling privileges.  We will all miss that privilege.

Sunday, November 12, 1972

                I spent all day at the Church, either in meetings or involved with Executive Secretary assignments.  Mae, Mom, Dad and the children spent a Sunday without me.  This evening Mae and I attended a Young Married Fireside.

Monday, November 13, 1972

                I started work with Air Research, as a computer programmer.  Air Research is one of the companies that also belong to the Howard Hugh’s Corporation.  Their main production is producing turbo jet engines.  My starting salary will be $950 a month, the same as my ending salary with Hugh’s Air West.  Ron Shumway also started work for Air Research as a programming supervisor.  I will not be under Ron’s group, but my supervisor is John Brown.  There are three Supervisors, each with their group of programmers.  I found out today that Scott Fry also left Hugh’s Air West and will also be a programmer under the 3rd supervisor.  After finishing the day at Air Research, I went to a remodeling job in Tempee.  I worked about 5 ½ hours on this jobs, getting home about 11PM.  Mae, Mom, Dad and the children held Family Home Evening without me and the children were to bed when I got home.  I visited with Mom and Dad for just a few minutes before we all went to bed.

Tuesday, November 14, 1972

                Work went well today although I mainly studied the features and capabilities of the new computer.  This computer has more memory and greater capacity than the one at Air West.  I have my own desk cubical, which makes it a little more privet than at Air West.  After finishing at Air Research I went directly to my job in Tempee.  I finished that job and got home at 8:30PM.  Mae had gone out Christmas shopping.  After eating, I worked on the magazine drive getting it ready to go.  Mom and Dad went over to some friends from Idaho Falls, that come to Mesa every winter.  They had invited Mom and Dad to stay with them this winter.

Thursday, November 16, 1972

                At work I feel much like I did when I went to work at Air West, there I was left to study for a few days until I went to my supervisor and asked for an assignment so that I could get busy.  That is the way I feel now, like I am useless and I don’t like that feeling.  This evening we went over to visit Mom and Dad Jensen and to become acquainted with their friends.

Friday, November 17, 1972

                Mae took David into a Pediatrician today and asked him about the lump on David’s chess.  He didn’t feel that it was anything to worry about and said that the lump was commonly referred to as a chicken breast.  I had to buy a new battery for the Tannus, this is a car that was given to us by a family that lives in our Ward.  This evening Mom and Dad Jensen came over to visit.  I think that Mae, Mom, Dad and I played some Rook and probably had Postum and Ginger Snap cookies.  That was a favorite snack while we played Rook.  We had an enjoyable evening.

Saturday, November 18, 1972

                I went to the Mesa Temple to help with performing Baptisms for the Dead.  (I believe that on this occasion, our Ward was asked to bring our Young Men and Young Women to the Temple to perform Baptisms for the Dead.  I volunteered to help with transportation.  When we got their and took our youth into the Temple, the officiator asked if any of the men would like to assist in the ordinance?  I indicated that I would like to and so they had me dress in white.  During the ceremony I got the opportunity to be mouth.  The Brother in charge, in checking that everything was going properly heard me giving the Confirmation prayer.  He later asked me if I had participated in the ordnance before, to which I told him I had worked in the Logan Temple as a substituent while we lived in Ogden.  He asked if I would like to work in the Temple again and I told him that I would.)  After finishing at the Temple I went to the Spa for a work out.  Mae took the children to town so they could do some shopping for Sandy a Birthday present.  This afternoon I dug up our garden area.  Larry came by and helped me clean out my shed.  I think the main reason that Larry stopped over was to finalize plans for me to ride with them to Utah on Wednesday.  With me leaving AW, the cost of flying to Utah is a little hard on our budget.  This way it will save one adult fare.  This evening we stayed home and prepared for Sandy’s Birthday party.

Sunday, November 19, 1972

                Today was a normal Sunday with meeting most of the day for me.  This evening Mae baked and decorated a Birthday cake for Sandy.  While Mae did that I made up a Treasure Hunt for the children to enjoy.  We are celebrating Sandy’s Birthday tomorrow afternoon as Tuesday Mae and the children are flying up to Utah for Thanksgiving.

Monday, November 20, 1972

                Today was very slow for me at work.  I don’t like just studying but would rather have an assignment to get started on.  I remember that that is the way it was when I started working at Air West so finally I went to my supervisor and asked him for an assignment.  He gave me one and I learned quickly how the computer works.  That seems to be the way I learn, doing!  I may have to do the same here.  When I got home Mae said that she had been busy all day, getting ready and haveing a Birthday Party for Sandy, along with packing for the trip to Utah for Thanksgiving.  Mom and Dad Jensen stopped over for a few minutes this evening.  They enjoyed having some cake and ice cream for Sandy’s Birthday, with us.  After that we went over to Larry and Tammy’s as Bob and Pauline Hein’s had un-expectantly came down for a visit.  It was late when we came home but after putting the children to bed, Mae and I stayed up and packed.

Tuesday, November 21, 1972

                I took Mae and the children to the airport where they flew up to Salt Lake.  Mom and Dad Porter will meet them at the Salt Lake Airport.  I then went into work.  After work I went to the Spa and worked out for a while.  When I got home I finished packing my things for going to Utah.

Wednesday, November 22, 1972

                Work went much better today at work for I was given an assignment to work on.  I left work a little early and at 3pm I left with Larry and Tammy and their children for Utah.  We arrived at Ephraim Utah at 1am Thursday morning, at Larry’s parents, where I was invited to stay the rest of the night.

Thursday, November 23, 1972

                This morning I enjoyed a nice breakfast with Larry’s parents.  After breakfast Lynn, Larry’s father, Larry and I went out pheasant hunting for a little while.  At 10:45am, Bryce and Deanna came for me and we drove to Mom and Dad Porters in Clearfield.  At 1PM we joined Mae’s family for a wonderful Thanksgiving Dinner.  I believe that all of Mae’s family was there.  If I remember correctly we set the dinner table up in the family room downstairs using the Ping Pong table as the dining table.  It was a very enjoyable day.

Friday, November 24, 1972

                We slept in and when I got up I went for a haircut.  We then went over to Bob and Carroll’s for another Thanks Giving dinner with their family.  After dinner, Bob took me to Salt Lake where I had a job interview with Skaggs.  With me not working for AW and not having fighting privileges, Bob would like us to live closer.  The interview went ok but no offer or commitment was made on either side.  When we returned to Bob’s, Irven and Sandy were there and we spent the evening playing games.  I believe that this might have been the time when my Browning felt Sports Hat got wrecked.  When we came into the house I had removed my hat and set it up on the back of a chair.  Bob seeing it there was concerned that one of the children might knock it down and it would get crushed, he therefore put it on top of a floor lamp, this way being out of reach of the children.  As the evening came on someone flipped the light on and within a few minutes we could smell smoking felt and leather.  The hat was totally ruined.  Bob sure felt bad at that.

Saturday, November 25, 1972

                Mom and Dad Porter took Mae and I to the Ogden Temple where we enjoyed doing an Endowment Session.  Being that we had moved to Arizona during the time the Temple was erected, this was the first Mae and I had the opportunity to go into the Ogden Temple.  It is sure a beautiful Edifice to Our Lord.  Following the Temple Session we stopped at Bishop and Sister Moulton’s and enjoyed a short visit with them.  This afternoon Dad Porter, Bryce and I went into a health spa for a short workout.  This evening Irven, Sandy, Bryce, Deanna, Mae and I went to a Clearfield Stake Dance.  There we enjoyed meeting many of our previous friends and acquaintances.  We sure enjoyed the dance and when we got home we packed for our return trip home.

Sunday, November 26, 1972

                Bob picked Sandy and I up at Mae’s parents at 7AM and drove us to Ephraim to Larry’s parents.  From there we drove back to Arizona with Larry and Tammy and their children.  We left Ephraim at 10AM.  We stopped a few time along the way, but got home at 11PM.

Monday, November 27, 1072

                Mae called to let me know that the flight from SLC to Phx was full so they got bumped from the flight.  She will have to try again tomorrow morning.  Sandy said that she would be ok to stay at home and would call me if she needed anything.  I then went into work.  It went well and when I got home this afternoon, Sandy and I went shopping for some Christmas ideas.  We then went out to eat.  It was good to have some “one and one” time with Sandy.  Mom and Dad Jensen stopped over to visit for a few minutes this evening.

Tuesday, November 28, 1972

                Mae and the kids were able to catch the morning flight to PHX and so I took off work and picked them up at 11:30am.  I took the rest of the day off and it is sure good to have all my family together again.  This evening Mae went with me Home Teaching.  After that I went up to the Bishops office for our regular Bishopric meeting.

Wednesday, November 29, 1972

                After finishing a day’s work at Air Research I went over to a Dr. Young’s home in Mesa.  His wife had called me yesterday, having had someone refer me to her that I did handyman work on the side.  They had a back door to their home that needed replacing.  When I got there, she explained that someone had broken into their home two or three week ago.  The police suspected that they were looking for drugs.  She said nothing else was taken and they didn’t find any drugs, as her husband never kept drugs in their home.  Her husband had gone to a door shop and purchased a solid core door and new lock sets.  She also said that they had hired a carpenter to replace the door.  When the carpenter came he started work on the door.  He had removed the old door, measured the opening and cut the bottom off the new door.  At that time she had an appointment in which she had to attend and the fellow indicated that he would be ok without her there.  Before she left, he was getting ready to drill the door for the new lock sets.  When she returned from her appointment, the old door was re-hung and a new door that had not been cut or bored was leaning against the wall.  She said that there was no note of explanation but the fellow never returned.  When they tried to contact him, he would not return their phone calls.  A friend then gave them my name and they called me to do the job.  I started on the job, first removing the old door and as it did fit the door frame opening, I used it for a pattern to lay out the new door.  As soon as I laid the old door, on the new door, I immediately realized what had happened with the other carpenter.  Only one side of the old door was cut straight and square.  The other three sides were all custom cut to fit the frame.  I presume that the other carpenter had just measured the height and width and then cut the door.  With the door being cut square, it now would not fit the opening.  He had wrecked the door and now he had to go to town and buy a new door.  That would have cost him everything he would have made from doing the job, so he just bought a new door and went home.  The job still took me four hours to get it installed but when I finished the door fit and operated properly. 

Thursday, November 30, 1972

                After getting home from work, eating dinner with my family, I went into the Mesa Temple where I worked in the Baptistery.  Tonight I was assigned to be the Baptismal Font Recorder.  Working in the Temple Baptistery is an experience that I look forward to.  Mae is in the Primary Presidency and this afternoon they held a special 100% Attendance Day and they were quite excited that today they had all but two children in attendance and those two children’s mothers called and said that they were both sick.

Friday, December 01, 1972

                This evening Mae had invited Mom and Dad Jensen to come over for dinner.  After eating Mae and Mom went over to the Spa for a while.  Dad and I enjoyed visiting while they were gone.  This evening I figured our budget and it doesn’t look like we are doing very well.  Mae and I have been discussing the possibilities of getting an offer with Skaggs Drug Store Co.  I think Mae is against moving to Utah.

Saturday, December 02, 1972

                This morning, at 7am I went into the Spa to work out.  At 9am I went to the Mesa Temple to help perform Baptismal Ordinances for the Dead.  We never finished up until 3pm.  When I got home I took a nap and then we took our family to the Primary Christmas party which started at 7pm.

Sunday, December 03, 1972

                This morning I attended a Bishopric meeting at 7am.  During Sunday school time I taught Brother Porter’s Sunday school class for him.  Mae had invited Larry, Tammy and their family to have dinner with our family and to spend the evening.  Later this evening I went with Bishop Rees and Deloy Porter to administer to someone in our Ward.

Monday, December 04, 1972

                Mae was tired when I got home from work so I suggested that we go out to eat.  We took all our family, plus we invited Mom and Dad Jensen to go with us.  Larry came by this evening to let me know the outcome of a project we have been looking into.  Larry had approached me with an idea a couple of weeks ago.  A fellow in his ward had approached Larry asking if he could build shipping crates for the company he worked for.  The company, which this fellow worked for, machined parts for N.A.S.A.  These parts are machined to a very fine tolerances and are very susceptible to be being damaged during shipping and handling, so each part had to be placed into a special shipping box in which the part is padded and could not move or be jarred during transportation and storage.  Presently they have a crating company that produces a crate for them, but charges over $250 per crate and would only produce 1 to 2 crates per month.  The machining company needs more than 12 crates per month.  Larry and I figured material and labor to manufacture the crates and we put a bid in to build them for about $175 per crate and could produce whatever quantity they needed.  Our thoughts were that I would run the shop, with Larry backing it with his financing.  We figured the tools we would need and even located a building we could rent for the shop and even took some tools over to the building.  This project could get us initially started and we could expand from there.  I even started contacting other companies that might need our services.  Larry wrote up a proposal and turned in our bid.  We have been waiting to hear back before making any solid investments and commitments.   However, Larry received word back today that a Boys Youth Training Program, that was Government subsidized, had put a bid to provide the crates for $45 each, so we lost out.  We had figured that the materials themselves would cost more than that.  Oh well! That’s life.

Tuesday, December 05, 1972

                This morning I was given a call from Skaggs Drug company giving me a job offer.  I had interviewed with them while we were in Utah for Thanksgiving.  Since the interview had ended without offers from either side, Mae and I have discussed, prayed and Fasted concerning the move.  Mae is especially against the move.  We decided against the offer and so thanked them for their offer and consideration.  This evening we took our children to town to see Santa Claus, but found out when we got there, that we were too late.  We will have to work that in another night.

Wednesday, December 6, 1972

                After I got home from work, Larry and I went over to the building that we had rented.  We removed some of the equipment that we had put in the building.  We talked to the person that we had rented the building from and explained the loss of the contract that we were planning on.  He was understanding and let us out of our rental agreement.  We thanked him and returned the keys to him.  This evening after dinner I went to Bishopric meeting.  It was 11:45pm when I got home.  Mae and I set up for a while talking about my job and trying to decide just what we should do. 

Thursday, December 07, 1972

                After returning home from work this evening and having dinner, I went to the Temple.  Tonight in the Baptistery I was assigned to be the baptizer.  Tonight I baptized youth for the Dead, performing ordnances for approximately 200 persons.  While I was gone this evening Mae and the children attended a Ward dinner and Christmas party.

Friday, December 08, 1972

                After returning home from work, I put the outside Christmas light up.  We then took our children downtown to buy a Christmas tree.  We were able to find a tree that cost us $12.00.  Mike hasn’t felt good most of the day and is running a slight fever.

Saturday, December 09, 1972

                With the concerns about my employment, Mae felt like she would like to go to the Temple.  I stayed home with the children so she could go.  We brought the tree in and got it stood up in the stand while Sandy prepared breakfast of hot cakes.  She is getting to quite a young lady.  Mom and Dad Jensen came over about noon.  Dad asked if I could change the oil and grease their car.  While Dad and I worked on the car, Mom and Mae made a cake and some homemade candy.  Mae and I went Christmas shopping for a little while this afternoon.  This evening I went to an evening Stake Conference meeting.

Sunday, December 10, 1972

                This morning we attended the Morning session of Stake Conference.  This afternoon Mae and I went into AirResearch’s Employee Christmas party.  We didn’t stay very long and came home to attend Ward Sacrament meeting this evening.  At 7:30pm Mae and I left Sandy in charge of the children and we attended a fireside.

Monday, December 11, 1972

                After I got home from work, Sandy and I picked the oranges off our tree.  We picked them because the weather report indicated that we were likely to have frost tonight.  After dinner this evening we held Family Home Evening, discussing the purpose of the Christmas celebration.  After butting the children to bed, I wrote a letter to Bruce and Mae did some Christmas sewing.

Tuesday, December 12, 1972

                I am really not been very excited with my job at Air Research.  As I transfer my “Diary” information into a more detailed “Journal” format, it is evident that I haven’t entered much about my job.  My time at Air West was exciting and I felt that I was contributing to a worthwhile project, but at Air Research I am accomplishing almost nothing.  This evening after dinner I took the children shopping for their Mothers Christmas presents, of course we took Mae with us also.  On the way home we stopped at a ice cream store and bought each of us a ice cream cone.  After putting the children to bed, Mae filled out Christmas cards and I figured bills.

Wednesday, December 13, 1972

                At work today Christmas Bonus’s were passed out to all the Employs.  I don’t know how the bonus was figured but I received a check for $15.00, which is ok.  After dinner this evening I did some assembling of Christmas bikes.  This evening was also Bishopric meeting and I didn’t get back from that until 10:45pm.

Thursday, December 14, 1972

                This evening I worked at the Temple.  Tonight I was assigned to perform conformations.  We performed about 300 ordnances this evening.  When I got home, the children were in bed and Mae was sewing on Christmas clothing.  I went to work on assembling bikes again.

Friday, December 15, 1972

                Mom Jensen came over today and helped Mae make Christmas candy.  After dinner this evening we enjoyed watching a Christmas show on TV with the children.  After putting the little children to bed, Mae and I went grocery shopping.  When we returned I took Sandy over to Larry and Tammy’s where she staid over night with Lorie.  When I returned home Mae and I watched a show on TV.

Saturday, December 16, 1972

                I went to work for Larry today, doing drywall tapping.  I worked 11 hours and by the time I returned home I was very tired and sour.  When I got home I showered and changed, then Mae and I went out with Larry, Tammy, Ron and Audrey for dinner.  We went to the Last Eskimo Restaurant, it was sure good. Today both Mike and David have been down with colds.

Sunday, December 17, 1972

                I attended early Bishopric meeting along with the Priesthood and Sunday school.  Mae stayed home with the boys.  After dinner I took a short nap.  Mom and Dad Jensen came over this afternoon and ask Mae and I to go with them to look at a home that is for sale.  With this warm Arizona winter weather, I think they are giving some thought about moving down here.  This evening I stayed home with the boys while Mae and the girls attended Sacrament meeting.  When they came back home, we had our normal peaches and toast for super and then we relaxed and watched a TV show.

Monday, December 18, 1972

                After getting home from work, Mom and Dad Jensen came over and spent the evening with us.  After a nice dinner we held our Family Home Evening.  The lesson was based around the meaning and purpose of Christmas and giving of gifts.  We had a nice time and enjoyed having Mom and Dad here with us.  Mike and David are still sick today and David is quite a bit the worse.  Mae and I took turns being up with David.

Tuesday, December 19, 1972

                Mae took David into Dr. Opie this morning.  When I got home from work, I took him back into Dr. Opie for another treatment.  I also took Sandy Christmas shopping this evening.  Mae made a batch of homemade candy to take into work tomorrow.  David slept quite a bit better tonight.

Wednesday, December 20, 1972

                After getting home from work I went Home Teaching.  While visiting the Essley’s, Sister Essley gave me a copy of a talk given at a BYU Devotional, entitled “The True Story of Christmas”.  After finishing my Home Teaching I went up to the Bishop’s office and attended Bishopric meeting.  I got home a little after 10PM and when I came in I found Mae busy sewing for Christmas.  While she worked I sat beside her and read the story to her.

Thursday, December 21, 1972

                Today at work they held a Christmas party, which I really didn’t enjoy and when I could I slipped out and came home.  After getting home I went up to the spa and worked out for about an hour.  I then went to the Temple where I worked in the Baptistery.  When I got home I had quite a bad headache so I soon went to bed.  David is still sick.

Friday, December 22, 1972

                The company gave us the day off.  I spent most of the day assembling bicycles for Shellie and Mike, prior to Christmas Eve.  We invited Jim and Marion Frazier and their girls over this evening.  We enjoy the friendship we have developed with them.  While we were visiting, our door bell rang and Santa Claus (Brother Lannie Owen) came in and visited with the children.  We had a very nice evening although David and Mike are still sick.

Saturday, December 23, 1972

                Both Mae and I worked for Larry today on a home he is finishing up.  We did painting, putting and installing door hardware.  Mom and Dad Jensen came over and stayed with the children for us.  After dinner this evening I did some Christmas shopping.  When I returned home we watched a Christmas show on TV as a family.  We then put the children to bed and Mae and I stayed up until after mid-night working on Christmas peperations.

Sunday, December 24, 1972

                We attended Sunday morning meetings.  This afternoon Mae sang in our Ward Choir as part of the special Christmas program held in conjunction with our Sacrament meeting.  It was a very enjoyable and Spiritual meeting.  We invited Mom and Dad Jensen to spend Christmas Eve as well as Christmas day, with us.  This evening Uncle Van and Aunt Allison and most of their family stopped by to wish us a Marry Christmas.  It has been special having them living down here with us.  Uncle Van is finishing his Masters Degree at Arizona State University; this will be his last year before graduating.  We got to bed about mid-night knowing that the children will be up early.

Monday, December 25, 1972

                We got up about 5:30am to open gifts.  Our main family gift was a stereo.  Shellie and Mike were especially excited with bicycles and being in Arizona it wasn’t long before they had them outside to ride.  Mae had sewn pajamas, dresses, pants and pajamas for most of us.  The children were all excited with their gift exchanges to each other.    Although it wasn’t an elaborate Christmas we all enjoyed it.  We are still doing a Gift Exchange with my siblings and this year Roy and Linda had our name.  They sent us a nice box of Cheeses.  I am always in favor of cheeses although Mae doesn’t enjoy them that much.  After enjoying a very nice Christmas dinner we took all the family to visit Larry and Tammy’s family.  We stopped over and visited the Shumway’s and Uncle Van’s family.  It has been a great day with everyone tired and ready for bed this evening.

Tuesday, December 26, 1972

                Air Research also gave us today off for the holidays.  We enjoyed a leisure morning.  After breakfast I gave both Mike and David haircuts.  I then gave Mae a hand with the house work and vacuumed the floors for her.  Sandy and I then went outside and played ball for a while.  I took a 2 hour nap after lunch.  Then I trimmed and cleaned up our roses.  It is a beautiful day and so pleasant.  This evening we invited Mike and Donna Young, neighbors that live across the street from us, over to play some games.  We also got out the box of cheeses to snake on.  In the conversation, Mae commented that she really didn’t like cheeses and when they got ready to leave Mae offered then some cheeses to take home.  I am not just sure how the offer was taken but they took the hole box and left.  I just stood there with my mouth open.  Mae apologized to me after they left as she only expected them to take a couple of small packages, not the whole box.

Wednesday, December 27, 1972

                Work went quite well although I didn’t feel the best.  I was glad to return home and after dinner I soon went to bed.  I am concerned about getting a head cold.  Mae has been busy all day working around the house.  I sure love that girl!

Thursday, December 28, 1972

                I felt much better this morning and went to work.  Work went better today and I feel some better about that.  When I got home, Mae seemed to have had a good day.  She and the children have been sewing most of the day.  Mae made her a blouse and me a couple of neck ties.  Work went some better today and I am feeling a little better with my job.  This evening Larry, Tammy, Ron, Audry, Mae and I went out to dinner for Mae’s Birthday.  We probably went to a Chinese Restraint East of Mesa.  Ron had found this restraint a couple of months earlier and all of us really enjoyed.  We would ask for the owner to take our order and when he came we would just ask him to bring us dinner for as many as were in our party.  Whether it was on this occasion or another time, when the food was brought to our table the owner would use a little pen light and shine on each plate and tell us what is was.  On the one occasion as he ended the pointing and telling what the food was, he said that this is our Dog Food.  We all got quite a chuckle over that as recently someone reported that they thought one of his foods contained car food.  When we asked him if the article hurt his business, he responded that if another article like that came out he would have to open another Restraint to handle the custumers.

Friday, December 29.1972

                Work went well today.  Mae had dinner ready soon after I got home.  I figured our budget and then joined Mae and the children in playing games.  At 9:30PM Mae and I went up to the Chapel where the Lehi 3rdward was hosting a dance.  After getting there we changed our minds and returned home.

Saturday, December 30, 1972

                I worked for Larry for a while this morning.  When I came home I trimmed more of our rose bushes.  After dinner this evening we were going to take our Family to a movie.  We were unable to find a movie that we thought was worth watching so instead we stayed home and watched a show on TV with our family.  Mae is sure a great wife, mother and sweetheart!

Sunday, December 31, 1972

                We attended Church meeting as normal.  This evening Mom Jensen came over and tended the children for us, just staying the night.  Larry, Tammy, Mae & I went out to eat and then came back to Larry & Tammy’s where we visited and played games until 2:30AM.  Happy New Year!!
